## Notes

- Habash front-loads the **Athanasian Creed** ("unity and Trinity and Trinity in unity is to be revered") and uses Garrigou-Lagrange's gloss to thread the needle: *unity in Trinity* preserves the threeness, *Trinity in unity* preserves the oneness. The structural form is the same one mind-monism reaches for: the substrate's unity does not dissolve the alters' real distinctness, and the alters' distinctness does not fragment the substrate's unity. Bridge to Kastrup (alters within mind-at-large; dissociation-without-fragmentation) and to Hoffman (multiple conscious-agent perspectives on a single underlying agent network).
- **Q.31 a.2's Arius/Sabellius needle** — Arius lost the oneness (three substances), Sabellius lost the threeness (no real distinction) — is the central methodological move of the day. Habash's careful list of forbidden terms ("diversity, difference, separation, division") and required terms ("distinction") is teaching-grade Trinitarian discipline. Bridge to mind-monism's analogous discipline: at the substrate level, no diversity-of-natures (anti-Arius), but real-relational-distinction (anti-Sabellius). - The **Hilary-of-Poitiers quote** — "it is sacrilege to assert that the father and the son are separate in Godhead" — Habash reads aloud as part of Aquinas's argument. ASR rendered "Hillary"; previous QC pass restored proper-noun "Hilary" (St. Hilary of Poitiers, 4th-c. anti-Arian theologian).
- **Q.32 a.1 — Trinity not knowable by natural reason** — is the most important methodological point of the entire 6-day Trinity arc (Days 12-17 in the transcript ordering, but the Trinity arc proper is 15-17). Aquinas explicitly distinguishes (a) *we know God's existence* by reasoning from effects to cause, but (b) *all created effects are common to the whole Trinity*, so creation does not betray the relational distinction. This is the **strongest epistemological claim** in the day: there is a known limit to natural-reason's reach. Bridge to Stump (faith-as-knowledge-of-persons is the *receptive* mode of knowing required where natural reason cannot reach) and to Wright (critical realism: tradition-embedded knowing is the right epistemic mode for revealed mysteries; mind-monism analogously must *receive* substrate-scale extrapolations as productive coherence rather than derive them).
- The **shadows-of-revelation** passage at Q.32 a.1 ad 2 — Aristotle on prayers/sacrifices in threes; Plato on "the word in the beginning"; Moses' triple-mention in Genesis 1:1-2 (God / God said / God saw); Habash's reading is gentle. Bridge to Wright on second-temple wisdom-traditions and to Rohr on the universal-Christ pattern visible-in-shadows-everywhere.
- **Q.32 a.4** "various contrary opinions of these notions" — Habash explicitly uses this article to gloss the **Filioque** and the East/West split. The structural claim — opinions on the notions are licit unless they imply heresy — is itself the central-theme thread on perspective-limitation: my limited perspective does not warrant me to enforce my notion as dogma where the church's defined boundary is not in play.
