## Notes

- Q.53 a.1 is one of the day's most striking physics moves — Habash explicitly reads quantum superposition as a contemporary vindication of Aristotle's actuality/potentiality account of motion: "when my hand is in motion from A to B it is in one way neither at A nor B but also in another way both at A and B as well — there's your explanation for quantum superposition." This is a strong claim and worth flagging on the synthesis: Wolfram on discrete-step motion at the substrate, Arkani-Hamed on the amplituhedron's positive-geometry computation of scattering amplitudes — both are the contemporary frame, and both partially endorse and partially complicate Habash's reading. CROSS-053 and CROSS-002 are the relevant index entries.
- Q.53's Zeno-paradox treatment is unusually thorough — Habash devotes substantial time to the bisection argument (430 BC), Aquinas's reply, and the continuous-vs-discontinuous distinction. The medieval *motion-as-continuum-of-actualizations* answer is structurally similar (not identical) to the modern measure-theoretic answer to Zeno (uncountably many points, finite measure). The synthesis can mark this congruence without overclaiming.
- Q.53 a.3's distinction between *continuous* angelic movement (passing through intermediate places, like ours) and *discontinuous* angelic movement (ceasing-to-affect-A and beginning-to-affect-B, more like teleportation) is structurally important: it preserves the body-mode for embodied movers and gives a separate mode for purely intellectual movers. Bridge: Hoffman's interface theory makes spatial location an interface-feature; for an alter that doesn't share the human interface, "moving" is reassigning the coupling-scope.
- Q.54 a.1–3 lands the *creature/Creator* metaphysical distinction at the intellectual level: in God, knowing IS being; in angels (and in us), they come apart. This preserves the directional-asymmetry frame — the substrate's act-of-knowing is its being; alters know-by-acquiring. Stump's "knowing as second-personal" is the analytic interlocutor here, not the metaphysical lead.
- Q.54 a.4 is the day's most epistemologically dense moment: Aquinas applies Aristotle's active/passive intellect distinction. The *passive intellect* receives intelligibles; the *active intellect* abstracts intelligibles from sense data. The synthesis bridge: Friston's hierarchical generative model (top-down priors generating, bottom-up errors updating) is the modern formal cousin of active/passive; Hawkins HTM is the modern instantiation of concept-formation-by-abstraction-over-particulars. CROSS-011 the relevant index entry. *Mature empirical-and-formal home for the cognitive structure*.
- Q.54 a.5's *angels know by intellectual knowledge only* + Q.54 a.4's *angels don't need active/passive intellect at all because they receive intelligibles directly* together commit the angelic-cognition picture to a non-sensory, non-abstractive substrate. Whether anything in contemporary cognitive science models this directly is doubtful (most cognitive science presupposes sensory input); the synthesis should mark this as a place where the medieval ontology runs ahead of the empirical anchorage.
