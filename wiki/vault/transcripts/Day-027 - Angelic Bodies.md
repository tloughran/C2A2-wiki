- Q.51 a.2's air-condensation account of how angels "assume bodies" is the most empirically vulnerable part of the day's content — Aquinas inherits this from late-antique angelology, and Habash flags it as Aquinas's own conjecture rather than dogma. - Q.51 a.3 strictly limits angelic "bodily" function to *appearance*, not real eating or speaking — Tobias is the canonical anchor ("when I was with you I seemed indeed to eat and drink but you were seeing a vision"). This is the spine of the day's metaphysics: place-as-coupling-scope rather than place-as-spatial-coordinate.
