## Notes

- Habash explicitly frames this as a "mental break" episode — single-article-only questions. Worth noting that his pedagogical pacing makes room for a non-substantive episode at the structural midpoint of the seven-day account. The synthesis is free to lean hard on the theological climax (image of God; *very good*) without mirroring the brevity.
- The structural-symmetry argument Aquinas gives — *distinction* days 1/2/3 mirrored by *adornment* days 4/5/6, in middle-mirrors-middle / lowest-mirrors-lowest pattern — is the *temple-cosmology* architecture Wright and Walton trace to second-temple-Jewish exegesis. Bridge candidate: this is precisely where the medieval structural reading and the contemporary scriptural-historical reading agree most cleanly.
- Habash flags a real puzzle in Aquinas's text — the apparent tension between vegetation-as-adornment (day 3) and vegetation-as-not-yet-adornment (implied by day 6 being the "completion" of adornment). He notes the tension without resolving it. The synthesis treats the puzzle via the temple-cosmology *hierarchical inhabitant* lens (first-stage vs. full adornment).
- The Genesis 1:26–27 *image of God* / *dominion* passage is the keystone Christian-anthropological claim of the whole seven-day account. Habash reads the text but doesn't elaborate on its theology in this episode — that's the synthesizer's job to develop via Wright's royal-priestly framing and Hahn's cosmic-temple reading.
- The Genesis 1:31 *very good* judgment closes the adornment sequence. Theologically loaded; the synthesis develops this as the substrate's loving-ratification of the relational structure.
- *Be fruitful and multiply* recurs at days 5 and 6 — animal blessing and human blessing. Levin's morphogenetic-teleology reading of reproduction-as-form-propagation is a strong contemporary bridge.
