## Notes

- Q.41 a.1 is the relation/notional-act identity argument: *clearly the reality expressed is one; the differences are exclusively in the way of signifying between the relations and the notional acts.* The Gilby translation Habash quotes flags the medieval *modus significandi* distinction. Bridge: Kastrup on substrate's-self-presentation-under-multiple-aspects without ontological multiplication.
- Q.41 a.2 — *the Father begot the Son not by will but by nature; not by necessity either* — the Faith of Damasus formulation. The synthesis should preserve Habash's careful gloss: *just as we naturally will our happiness, God Wills by his nature to be God and to be Father, Son, and Holy Spirit*. The voluntary-by-nature shape is the medieval form of Levin's *agency-as-pattern-following-form*.
- Habash skips Q.41 a.6 ("whether the act of begetting can refer to several persons") — proceeds directly from a.5 to a.7. The transcript marks the skip; the synthesis can engage a.6 if a contemporary frame has something to say, but must mark the skip.
- Q.41 a.7's *three possibilities exhaustively cover the relations of origin* — *to be without origin / to be from the origin alone / to be from the origin through another.* This is a tight logical argument for *exactly three* persons. Bridge: Wolfram on graph-theoretic-completeness of three-node-types; Friston on three-positions arising from two operations.
- Q.42 a.2 — *eternity excludes the principle of duration but not the principle of origin.* A precise distinction: origin-without-time-priority is the shape of substrate-internal relations.
- Q.42 a.5 (*the Son is in the Father and conversely*) is *circumincession / perichoresis* — mutual indwelling. Habash carefully sets aside spatial imagination. Bridge: Rohr on *The Universal Christ* perichoretic-cosmology; Kastrup on dissociation-within-monism without spatial separation.
- Q.42 a.6 — Hilary's formula *the Son so acts of himself that he does not act by himself* — the one operation of the one nature. Important for the synthesis: the medieval one-operation-per-nature rule sets a constraint on any *substrate-acts-through-distinct-aspects* mind-monist reading.
