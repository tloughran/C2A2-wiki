## Notes

- Habash flags Lagrange's reading of Q.39–Q.40 as *recapitulation-and-synthesis* of the Trinity treatment. The 8+4-article density and the meta-character of these questions are why this day overruns the medium-tier guideline; the substantive linkage to mind-monism's language-rules-for-substrate-with-internal-structure is load-bearing.
- Q.39 a.1 — Aquinas's one-paragraph summary of the entire Trinity treatment is itself a small marvel. *Relation as referred to the essence does not differ therefrom really but only in our way of thinking; while as referred to an opposite relation it does have a real distinction.* The two-aspect grammar is the medieval form of *substrate-with-real-internal-relations-but-no-substantial-divisions* — the Kastrup analytic-idealism cousin.
- Q.39 a.3 — the *adjective vs substantive* distinction (three wise beings vs three Gods) is a precise piece of medieval philosophy-of-language. Bridge: Wolfram on language as compression-with-perspectival-loss; the move that fails when *abstract essential names* are substantivized is a precise category-error.
- Q.39 a.4–6 — the symmetric pair *God begets the Son* vs *the godhead does not generate the godhead* is the medieval rule preventing tritheism. Important guardrail for the synthesis on substrate-language.
- Q.39 a.7–8 — *appropriation* is the move by which essential attributes (power, wisdom, goodness) are *fittingly* assigned to particular persons (Father, Son, Spirit) for the manifestation of faith, even though all attributes belong to the single essence. Hilary's pattern: *eternity → Father; brightness → Son; use/fruition → Holy Spirit.*
- Q.40 a.2 — *better to say the persons are distinguished by relations than by origin* — Aquinas's preferred order. Note for the synthesis: this orders the doctrine in a *relational-priority-over-causal-priority* shape that maps cleanly onto Kastrup dissociation-as-relational-not-causal.
- Q.40 a.3's *abstract paternity* counterfactual — *if we abstract the paternity nothing remains of the Father* — is the medieval form of the strong claim that relations are constitutive of the person, not accidents added on. Bridge: Levin on the substrate's pattern-as-constitutive-of-identity.
