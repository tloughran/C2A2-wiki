# Day 77 — Delightful Sorrow (Contemporary)

## Frame

Q.32 (cause of pleasure) and Q.33 (effects of pleasure) seem on first read a tidy taxonomy. But Q.32 a.4 detonates a paradox at the center of Aquinas's affective theory: *sadness is the accidental cause of pleasure*. Sorrow, the passion of resting in present evil, brings with it — by the same operation that registers the evil — an apprehension of accompanying goods. The grief at a friend's death is also the *presence* of the friend in memory; the discomfort of past evil now recalled is also the *relief* of its being past. This is not a Stoic detachment-trick. It is a structural claim: in a creature whose appetite reads the world through the lens of the good, no privation is felt as pure non-being.

The contemporary version: under predictive-processing accounts of affect (Friston), valence is *prediction-error gain modulation* over a generative model that always includes counterfactuals — the felt absence is the *signal* of what could be present. Sorrow and delight are not pure opposites but co-arising values in a single inference. The question for the synthesis: what does this co-arising imply about *limited-perspective* agents and about loving unity as a computational task?

## Problem

The problem is *the affective phenomenology of privation*. Aquinas inherits an Aristotelian framework in which pleasure follows the union of an appetite with a perceived good, and pain follows the union of an appetite with a perceived evil. But human experience is shot through with mixed valence: bittersweet recollection, grief that contains gratitude, contemplation of a tragedy that elicits a deep aesthetic peace, "tears of joy." Either the framework is wrong, or — Aquinas's actual move — privation is *never* pure for an appetite-bearing creature, because the very registration of the absence already activates the good as counterfactual.

Under conscious-realist monism the problem deepens. If the agent is individuated by its perspective-limitations, and if those limitations are the *condition* of being an agent at all, then loss-of-good is structurally constitutive: the limited perspective always lacks what a less-limited perspective would have. The question becomes: how does the agent compute its way *through* the felt sorrow toward the loving unity that is the synthesis's central theme?

## Resource

**Friston's active inference** (Friston wiki; PRS-04 organism-as-active-modeler, PRS-06 multi-agent coherence) supplies the formal backbone. In active inference, expected free energy decomposes into pragmatic value (closing the gap to preferred states) and epistemic value (resolving uncertainty about the model). Sorrow corresponds to a *high* expected-free-energy state with respect to the lost good — but the same inference also generates *epistemic* value: knowing more about what mattered, what now needs revision, what the good actually was. Sorrow and delight are then *components* of a single free-energy computation, not opponents. This is the formal home of Q.32 a.4.

**Fredrickson's broaden-and-build** (Fredrickson wiki) is more subtle here than at first glance. The 2025 Fredrickson & West RCT (PRS-09, PRS-10) found that weak-tie positivity resonance — moments of connection with strangers, *not* with close intimates — produces measurable increases in intellectual humility, prosocial belief, and civic efficacy. PRS-08 establishes that *high-quality listening* enables these moments, with sensory connection and perceived safety as the proximal mechanisms. This is the direct empirical correlate of Q.32 a.5's "praise from those we trust gives greatest pleasure." The pleasure is not flattery; it is the cognitive update — *I am known* — that comes from being heard by a credible witness. The same mechanism explains why sympathetic friendship in sorrow (Q.38 a.3, anticipated for Day 80) is the chief remedy: the heard sorrow becomes available for revision.

**McGilchrist's hemispheric framework** (McGilchrist wiki, dispatch with Friston on precision-weighted attention) helps with Q.33 a.3 (pleasure hinders reason). Affective intensity recruits attention; attention is precision-weighting of prediction errors; high precision narrows the inferred world. The pleasure-hindrance-of-reason isn't a moralistic warning but a description of how affective gain modulates the inferential space.

**Kastrup's analytic idealism** (Kastrup wiki) lets the synthesis name the deeper move. In the alter-within-mind-at-large picture, sorrow is the alter's registration of dissociated content it cannot integrate. Grief, lament, and mourning are then *not* failures but the available routes by which an alter loosens its dissociative boundary and re-admits content from the broader mind. *This* is why sorrow can carry delight: the same loosening that admits the loss admits the wider field of which the loss is part.

**Wright on Romans 8** ("the sufferings of this present time are not worth comparing with the glory that is to be revealed in us") and **Rohr on second-half-of-life** integration both supply the theological articulation: the sorrow that contains delight is *eschatologically structured* — already participating in the resurrection-pattern, not yet fully realized.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

**(a) Shared with Aquinas.** The synthesis preserves Q.32 a.4 intact: in any actual creature, no privation is registered as pure non-being. Pleasure and sorrow are not symmetric opposites but co-active values in the appetite's reading of the world. Aquinas's *appetite* framework, with its dual openness to present good and present evil, is structurally preserved.

**(b) Where it reframes.** Two reframes. First, Aquinas locates the co-arising of pleasure-in-sorrow in the *adjacency* of goods to evils (the friend now lost was a good; that good is "still there" in memory). The contemporary reframe is mathematical: the co-arising is in the *generative model itself* — every prediction error is a comparison against a counterfactual, so every registered loss simultaneously instantiates the lost good as model-state. This is a stronger claim than adjacency. Second, Aquinas treats wonder (Q.32 a.8) as a "kind of desire for knowledge" linked to hope; the contemporary frame reads it as *epistemic value* in expected free energy, with formal expression. Wright/Rohr add the eschatological structure that Aquinas leaves more theologically constrained.

**(c) Where evidence is firm vs. speculative.** The mixed-valence empirics are *strong*: bittersweet emotion has been documented in psychology since Larsen & McGraw (2011), and predictive-processing models of valence are increasingly robust (Joffily & Coricelli 2013; Solms 2021). The Fredrickson/West weak-tie findings (PRS-09, PRS-10) are *strong* and recent. McGilchrist's precision/attention bridge is *formally suggestive* but not yet a settled empirical claim. The Kastrup/idealist reading of grief as alter-loosening is *speculative metaphysics* — internally coherent, theologically congruent with the Wright/Rohr eschatology, but not falsifiable in current frameworks.

## The central-theme thread

The central claim — that conscious agents are individuated by perspective-limitations, and that loving unity is a computational task — is *especially* visible here. Q.32 a.4's delightful sorrow is the affective signature of an agent whose generative model *holds* both the present privation and the larger good in which it is contained. The agent who can only feel the loss has a narrower model than the agent who can feel the loss *and* the persisting reality of the good. The path from one to the other is the perspective-widening that the whole project tracks: contemplative practice, sympathetic friendship, Fredrickson-style cultivation of positivity resonance, Wright's eschatological re-framing of present suffering against new-creation hope, Rohr's second-half-of-life integration of loss into a larger pattern. Each is a *computational resource* by which a limited agent integrates more of the substrate that, in Day 76's frame, was already there.

## Bridges

- **Friston PRS-06 (multi-agent coherence)** — collective active inference frames *shared* mourning (Q.32 a.5, Q.32 a.6) as alignment of generative models around a shared model-state; *empirical, growing literature*.
- **Fredrickson PRS-08, PRS-09, PRS-10** — high-quality listening (Q.32 a.5 praise/honor) and weak-tie connection as the empirical mechanism for Aquinas's "pleasure from being-known"; *empirical, high confidence*.
- **McGilchrist precision/attention bridge** — Q.33 a.3 (pleasure hinders reason) as affective gain on precision-weighting; *formally suggestive*.
- **Kastrup analytic idealism** — grief as alter-loosening admits more of mind-at-large; *speculative metaphysical*, theologically congruent.
- **Wright, Romans 8 + Rohr, *Falling Upward*** — eschatologically-structured suffering; the mixed valence as the affective form of "already / not yet"; *theological, well-attested in tradition*.
- **CROSS-005 (master)** — love as ontological category: the same field that grounds delight grounds the dignity of grief; without ontologically-anchored love, mixed valence has no home.

## Where this leaves us

Day 78 (Q.34–35) reverses ground: from the goodness-and-malice of pleasure to pain and sorrow *as such*. Q.35's distinction between bodily pain (apprehended externally, present-only) and sorrow (apprehended interiorly, regards past/present/future) maps cleanly onto the predictive-hierarchy distinction between exteroceptive and interoceptive prediction errors. The open thread we carry forward: under perspective-limitation, *higher-level* errors (sorrow) have larger downstream consequences than peripheral ones (pain) — Aquinas's claim that inward pain exceeds outward (Day 78's Q.35 a.7) gets its mathematical home.
