# Day 69 — Contemporary Parallel: *The Layered Control Architecture of Execution*

Day 68 articulated the deliberation-and-approval register that precedes choice. Day 69 takes Aquinas through Q.16 (use — 4 articles) and Q.17 (the acts commanded by the will — 9 articles), the *execution* register of the human-action pipeline. Habash follows Gilby's translation of *usus* as **application** — the will applying the chosen mean to execution. The contemporary mind-monist frame engages this as the *layered control architecture* of how command (reason) and application (will) coordinate to produce executed action, with active inference's hierarchical-control structure and Levin's multi-scale agency as the leading resources.

## Frame

Q.16 a.1: application is *to refer the object of application to the obtaining of something else* — the will applying itself to execute the means. Aquinas: *first and principally application belongs to the will as first mover, to the reason as directing, and to the other powers as executing the operation, which powers are compared to the will which applies them to act as the instruments are compared to the principal agent.* Q.16 a.4: application precedes execution but follows choice.

Q.17 a.1: command is an act of reason presupposing an act of the will — *the reason by its command moves the power to the execution of the act*. Q.17 a.3: command precedes application — the stick-analogy: *one does not apply a stick before doing something with the stick; but command is not simultaneous with the act of the thing to which the command is given*. Q.17 a.4: command and the commanded act are *one human act*, just as a whole is one yet in its parts many — formal/material analysis applied to action.

Q.17 a.5: the act of the will can be commanded. Q.17 a.6: the act of reason can be commanded only on *uncertain* matters; necessary truths (non-contradiction, sufficient reason) are not subject to command. Q.17 a.7: the sensitive appetite (passion) is commanded only *indirectly* — by managing what enters apprehension, since *passions follow immediately upon the apprehension of some sensible good*. Q.17 a.8: the vegetative powers (digestion, heart-rate, growth) are *not* commanded directly. Q.17 a.9: the external members are commanded directly — Augustine's striking observation that *the mind commands a movement of the hand, and so ready is the hand to obey that scarcely can one discern obedience from command*.

## Problem

The problem (P): the alter's volitional architecture issues in *executed action* in the world, but the alter is not transparent to itself in this execution. Different parts of the alter respond to command differently — voluntary motor obeys directly, imagination is gated indirectly, passion is influenced through apprehension, vegetative is not under command at all, intellect is commandable only on uncertainties. What is the structural account of this *layered control architecture*?

## Resource

**Friston on hierarchical active inference and layered-control architecture.** Active inference formalizes layered control: high-level priors (terminal goals) cascade through middle-level priors (goal-policies, action-policies) to low-level priors (motor policies, autonomic regulation). Different layers have different controllability — the high-level prior is the locus of the alter's deliberate command, lower-level layers respond automatically to higher-level signals, and the lowest layers (autonomic, hormonal) operate on their own internal regulation. The medieval *substrate-of-command* map preserves remarkably:

- **Will commanding itself** (Q.17 a.5) → the alter's terminal-prior shaping its own next-state.
- **Reason commanded only on uncertain matters** (Q.17 a.6) → epistemic policy-selection where multiple priors are admissible; certainties are not policy-targets.
- **Passion influenced via apprehension** (Q.17 a.7) → emotion-priors shaped by perceptual content (Fredrickson's cognitive-appraisal frame).
- **Vegetative not commanded** (Q.17 a.8) → autonomic and homeostatic loops outside voluntary control.
- **External members obey directly** (Q.17 a.9) → motor cortex direct projections.

**Levin on multi-scale agency and layered control of body.** Each scale of the body has its own agentic dynamics with constitutive autonomy. The medieval *vegetative powers not commanded* preserves cleanly: cellular-level agency operates on its own goal-structure, only mediately responsive to the personal-scale alter. *Reason has no control over the nutritive and generative power* (Gregory of Nyssa, quoted by Aquinas) is structurally the multi-scale-agency commitment.

**McGilchrist on hemispheric account of willed action.** Right-hemispheric integration sets the gestalt-of-the-action; left-hemispheric analysis articulates the command. The unity-of-act in Q.17 a.4 — *command and commanded act are one human act* — is the bi-hemispheric integration at the action-level.

**Hawkins on HTM hierarchical motor control.** Cortical-and-subcortical architecture: action-priors are encoded at multiple levels; the alter's command at the highest level cascades through the hierarchy with corresponding adjustments at each layer.

**Fredrickson on cognitive appraisal of emotion.** Q.17 a.7's *passions follow apprehension* is precisely cognitive-appraisal theory of emotion: emotions are not commanded directly but are shaped by what the mind attends to. The alter's *indirect command of passion* is the management-of-apprehension, which the alter *can* command directly. *Broaden-and-build* research provides the empirical anchor.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.16-17: the alter is a *layered-control system* with constitutive heterogeneity in the controllability of its layers. The alter's command is real and effective at the layers where it is constitutive — terminal-prior, deliberate-policy, motor-execution; the alter's command is *mediated* at layers where the body's own dynamics are autonomous — passion, autonomic, vegetative. The medieval *one human act* that integrates command-and-commanded preserves under the hierarchical-active-inference frame as the integrated multi-layer policy-selection that *is* the alter's action.

The Q.17 a.6 case — reason commanded only on uncertain matters — is structurally important: *certainties are not policy-targets*. The alter cannot, by deliberate command, change its assent to non-contradiction or sufficient-reason. The alter *can*, by deliberate command, change its assent to under-determined matters (where epistemic policy-selection is admissible).

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the layered-control structure; the heterogeneity of controllability across layers; the indirect-command-of-passion via apprehension; the unity-of-command-and-commanded-act.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas's frame is faculty-psychological-and-Aristotelian, the contemporary frame uses active inference, multi-scale agency, hemispheric coordination, HTM, and cognitive-appraisal.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Friston, Levin, Hawkins all empirically grounded; Fredrickson's cognitive-appraisal of emotion is empirically robust; the substrate-of-command map preserves with remarkable structural fidelity.

## The central-theme thread

Today's day articulates the *layered-execution* register of the central theme. The alter's *salvation in fear and trembling* under perspective-limitation operates through a layered-control architecture in which the alter's deliberate command reaches some layers directly (motor), some indirectly (imagination, passion via apprehension), and not at all to others (autonomic, vegetative). The work of becoming-aligned with the loving unity must take this layered architecture seriously: *the alter cannot directly command its passions* — but it *can* command what it attends to, and the attended-content shapes the passions. *The alter cannot directly command its autonomic state* — but Fredrickson's positivity-resonance research shows that relational-affective practices reliably shift autonomic-regulation patterns over time.

This is the daily-task structure of *spiritual practices*: deliberate management of attention shapes apprehension, which shapes passion, which over time shapes habit, which over time shapes character. Stump's *Wandering in Darkness* second-personal-knowing intervention works precisely by surfacing the alter's apprehension to the diagnostic that the alter could not surface from inside.

Wright on *renewing the mind* (Romans 12:2) as the deliberate management of apprehension as the leverage point on the layered-control system. Rohr on contemplative-practice as the deliberate cultivation of the attention-pattern that shapes the lower layers. Fredrickson's empirical work on positive-affect-induction practices that reliably shift the layered architecture.
