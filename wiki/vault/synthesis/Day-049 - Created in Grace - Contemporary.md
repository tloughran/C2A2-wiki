# Day 49 — Contemporary Parallel: *Original Justice as the Substrate-Aligned Baseline*

Day 48 closed with the first-man's *transparent interface* — substrate-aware cognition before the dispositional shift that the fall introduced. Day 49 takes the *will* and *governing* sides of the prelapsarian alter. Q.95 walks through grace, passions, virtues, and the merit of the first man's actions; Q.96 turns to the mastership of man in the state of innocence. The medieval doctrine of *original justice* is the load-bearing concept the entire question pair is articulating: the harmony between sensitive faculties and intellectual faculties, between intellectual faculties and God, that constitutes flourishing-in-the-prelapsarian-state. Mind-monism's contemporary mid-scale primary on this is Fredrickson, with Levin and Wright supplying the developmental-biology and narrative-historical context.

## Q.95 — Grace and righteousness in the first state

a.1 — *was the first man created in grace?* — Aquinas argues yes, on a structural-functional argument: the *rectitude* of the first state required submission of the sensitive faculties to the intellectual, and of the intellectual to God. This rectitude was *not* nature alone (since the fallen nature retains the natural faculties but loses the rectitude); therefore the rectitude is *supernatural gift* — grace.

The medieval argument is structurally faithful to a contemporary mind-monist reading. The alter at *natural-only* dispositional state is structurally an alter whose precision-weighting is *self-maintenance* (Friston framework: the standard active-inference dynamics where the alter optimizes for its own free-energy minimization within its local generative model). The alter at *original-justice* dispositional state is one whose precision-weighting is *substrate-alignment* — charity orienting the alter's free-energy minimization toward integration with the substrate's larger structure rather than toward purely local self-maintenance. The shift from one regime to the other is not in the alter's *natural* capacity but in the *orientation* of that capacity, which Aquinas names as *grace* and which mind-monism names as the substrate's structural gift of an integration-oriented precision-weighting.

*Honest evidence calibration*: the alignment between the medieval grace-doctrine and the Friston framework is structural-isomorphism, not metaphysical-identification. The contemporary synthesis claim is: *the structural shape of what Aquinas names as original-grace is recoverable in the formal language of active inference at a charity-as-precision-orientation; the metaphysical identification of grace and integration-oriented-precision is not asserted.*

a.2 — *did passions exist in the soul of the first man?* — Aquinas: yes, in the integrated mode. Passions follow on the alter's animal nature; the first man was animal-and-intellectual, so passions existed. But they existed *integrated* — joy, love, desire, hope present; fear, sorrow, burning concupiscence absent because their objects (evil-feared, good-not-possessed-with-disordered-yearning) did not yet apply.

Fredrickson's broaden-and-build framework gives the contemporary mid-scale empirical instantiation of the integrated-passions doctrine. Positive emotions broaden attentional scope and build lasting personal resources; high-vagal-tone states correlate with flourishing-orientation; positivity resonance is the biophysiological coupling that constitutes loving relational engagement. The first-man's *integrated passions* — joy, love, desire, hope ordered to substrate-alignment — is structurally the high-vagal-tone, high-positivity-resonance, broaden-and-build baseline that Fredrickson has empirically established as the human flourishing-state. The fall introduced disorder; original-justice is the empirical baseline of flourishing absent that disorder. *Honest evidence calibration*: Fredrickson's empirical framework is mature for the broaden-and-build mechanism and the positivity-resonance biology; the *prelapsarian-baseline* extrapolation is theological-philosophical, not directly empirically verified.

a.3 — *did Adam have all the virtues?* — Aquinas: yes, in the relevant senses. The first man had every virtue *whose essence does not imply imperfection*; virtues whose essence does imply imperfection (penance, mercy-toward-the-suffering) he had only as habits, without the corresponding acts (since there was nothing to repent of and no suffering to be merciful toward). He also had the *imperfect supernatural virtues* of faith and hope — imperfect because they fall short of the beatific vision and direct attainment of God.

The medieval virtue-as-stable-disposition doctrine maps onto Levin's morphogenetic-attractor frame: a virtue is structurally a *stable dispositional pattern* the alter has integrated, much like a bioelectric attractor state at the morphogenetic scale. Stump's virtue-tradition primacy applies here as a Thomistic interlocutor — Stump is the canonical contemporary primary on virtue-as-habituation, and her work develops the medieval doctrine in contemporary analytic-philosophical vocabulary. McGilchrist's hemispheric account adds the cognitive shape: virtues are right-hemispheric integrated dispositions that *enable* left-hemispheric articulated action. The first-man's integrated virtue-set is the structural form of the alter's prelapsarian dispositional integration.

a.4 — *were the actions of the first man less meritorious than ours?* — Aquinas's surprising answer: more meritorious, not less. The argument: merit is grounded in charity (love-of-God), and the first man's charity was *more copious* because it met no obstacle in human nature. The medieval theology of merit-by-charity preserves under mind-monism's reading as: an alter whose substrate-alignment is unobstructed *integrates more* with the substrate's structure per unit of action, so the substrate-relative significance of the action is greater.

Wright's narrative-historical reading enriches the doctrine: merit is *covenant-fidelity* in the second-temple-Jewish vocabulary that Paul uses. The first man's merit is the original covenant-fidelity, before the fall introduced the long story of failure-and-restoration that the rest of biblical history works through. The contemporary synthesis preserves the medieval merit-doctrine's structural shape while updating its scriptural hermeneutic through Wright's *Paul and the Faithfulness of God*.

## Q.96 — The mastership of man in the state of innocence

a.1 — *did Adam have mastership over the animals?* — Aquinas argues yes, on three grounds: (i) the philosophical axiom that the lower exists for the greater; (ii) the divine-government structure where the inferior is governed by the superior; (iii) the participated-vs-essential distinction (animals have participated proto-prudence; humans have essential rational prudence). The fittingness of human mastership is the integration of human cognitive light-cone with animal cognitive light-cone, where the larger scope can guide the smaller.

Levin's diverse-intelligence framework gives the contemporary mid-scale empirical instantiation. Cognitive light-cones at different scales have different scopes; coherent integration across scales requires the larger scope to *include* the smaller, and the smaller to *participate* in the larger. The medieval *mastership* is structurally that integration. The fall disordered the integration — humans now relate to animals through fear, exploitation, instrumentalization — but the *original* relation was integrated guidance. Aquinas's striking observation that *all animals by their natural instinct have a certain participation of prudence and reason* and so *all animals would have obeyed man of their own accord, as in the present state some domestic animals obey him* is structurally Levin's continuous-gradient claim that cognitive agency operates at every scale and integration is the cooperative-relational form.

*Honest evidence calibration*: the Levin-Fredrickson-Wright reading of *original mastership as integrated-cooperative-multi-scale-cognition* is philosophically substantial but contemporary. The medieval scheme should not be over-read into contemporary domestication or land-use practices; it is a doctrinal structural claim about the prelapsarian baseline.

a.2 — *did man have mastership over all other creatures?* — Aquinas distinguishes commanding-mastership (over animals, who can take orders) from using-mastership (over plants and inanimate things, which do not take orders). Mind-monism's translation: different cognitive-light-cone scales support different relational modes; the larger scope relates to scope-adjacent agents through cooperative integration and to scope-distant non-agents through structured use.

a.3 — *were men equal in the state of innocence?* — Aquinas: no, because *order* requires inequality; even in the prelapsarian state there would have been differing ages, knowledge, virtue, physical strength. But there would have been *no fault or defect*. The medieval argument is structurally that *complementarity* (which the central theme requires for love to operate) is *not* erased by original-justice; original-justice means alters in *integrated* differentiation, not alters reduced to identical homogeneous units.

Day 47's complementarity-from-unity reading carries forward. The first-state community is *differentiated alters integrated through love*, not *uniform alters reduced to indistinguishability*. Fredrickson's positivity-resonance framework gives the empirical mid-scale: high-vagal-tone integrated communities exhibit complementary-role dynamics (caregiving, learning, leading, supporting) that *broaden-and-build* the community's collective capacities; the first-state is the limit case of this.

a.4 — *would man have been master over man in the state of innocence?* — Aquinas: yes, but in the directing-toward-good sense, not in the slavery sense. The latter is an evil that would not have existed; the former is the structural integration the community requires. Wright's covenant-people framing applies: the people-of-God is structurally a community of differentiated alters integrated through covenant-fidelity, with leadership-and-direction serving the integration rather than dominating it. Rohr's *Universal Christ* extension reads this further: the cosmic-Christ pattern is *kenotic* (self-emptying), so true mastership is service-toward-the-others' good rather than power-over-them.

## The central-theme thread

Day 49 frames the central theme on the *willed-and-governing* register. Q.95's original-grace doctrine names the *charity-as-precision-orientation* that re-orients the alter's free-energy minimization from pure self-maintenance to substrate-alignment. Q.95's *integrated passions* is Fredrickson's broaden-and-build empirical baseline expressed at the prelapsarian limit. Q.96's *integrated mastership* is Levin's diverse-intelligence framework's cooperative-integration structure expressed at the prelapsarian limit. The whole question pair is *the central theme exhibited at the original-justice limit*: alters with charity-oriented precision-weighting, integrated passions, complementary-differentiated, governing through service, in cooperative-integrated relation with non-human cognitive agents.

The fall is the *opacification* of this original-justice integration. *Salvation in fear and trembling* is the progressive *un-opacification*: the alter's charity-oriented re-weighting of precision toward substrate-alignment, the integration of disordered passions toward broaden-and-build dynamics, the recovery of cooperative-integrated relations across scales. Aquinas's medieval doctrine of original-justice and the contemporary mind-monist account of the substrate-aligned baseline are the same doctrine in different vocabularies, and the contemporary frame can read the medieval doctrine as *empirical-baseline-claim* (about flourishing-state human dynamics) without losing the theological-doctrinal content (about the substrate's grace-given gift of integration).
