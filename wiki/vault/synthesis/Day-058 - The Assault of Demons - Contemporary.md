# Day 58 — Contemporary Parallel: *Guardianship and Predation as Substrate-Network Pedagogy and its Inverse*

Day 57 articulated the channels through which substrate-network agents influence embodied creatures. Day 58 takes up Q.113 *the guardianship of the good angels* (8 articles) and Q.114 *the assault of the demons* (5 articles): the *protective* and *predatory* operations of the substrate-network on the embodied alter. The contemporary mind-monist frame translates these as the pedagogical contour of substrate-care under the alter's perspective-limitation, and the inverted-prior-attractor field that the dispositionally-mis-oriented sub-network generates around it.

## Frame

Aquinas's medieval reasoning in Q.113 a.1 — *we find that in all things the movable and variable are moved and regulated by the immovable and invariable* — is exactly the multi-scale-control architecture argument: lower-coupling, higher-variability agents are stabilized by influence from higher-coupling, lower-variability agents. The contemporary frame: the embodied alter operating under perspective-limitation requires more-than-itself stabilization to learn, in the daily-task character of *salvation in fear and trembling*, the patterns that align with the loving unity. The substrate-network's protective influence is *pedagogical care* by the substrate-source mediated through other agents.

The dark counterpart in Q.114: the dispositionally-mis-oriented sub-network has its own influence-field, with its own assault-architecture aimed at the alter's predictive-and-affective interfaces.

## Problem

The underlying problem (P): the embodied alter operating under perspective-limitation cannot self-correct to alignment with the loving unity *from its own resources alone*. Its priors are limited; its capacity to detect catastrophic mis-alignment is limited; it is exposed to influence from agents whose dispositions span the full range from the loving substrate-source to its dispositionally-inverted shadow. How is the alter pedagogically stabilized? And how does the predatory inverse operate?

Stump's *Wandering in Darkness* engages this directly at the human scale: the second-person-knowing structure of how an agent comes to know its own willed evil is exactly the structure of the substrate-network's pedagogical correction. The alter cannot, on its own, see its mis-orientation; it needs the *second-person* presentation of its true state by an agent who can see it from outside.

## Resource

**Friston on defensive vs. exploratory policy regimes.** When an agent's expected free energy is dominated by threat, the policy regime narrows toward defensive. When the affective landscape is dominated by safety and care, the policy regime broadens toward exploratory. The substrate-network's protective influence shifts the alter toward exploratory — toward the dispositional latitude in which the loving unity can become visible. The demonic assault narrows the alter toward defensive — toward closure that confirms inverted priors.

**McGilchrist on left- vs. right-hemispheric attention.** Left-hemispheric closure (grasping, narrow focus, high-confidence categorization) is the predatory mode; right-hemispheric openness (receptive, wide focus, comfort-with-uncertainty) is the receptive mode. Demonic assault works by triggering left-hemispheric closure under threat. Substrate-network protection works by sustaining right-hemispheric openness even under stress.

**Fredrickson on positivity resonance as protective dispositional state.** Empirical: positive emotional states broaden the cognitive-behavioral repertoire (broaden-and-build). Substrate-network pedagogical care operates through positivity-resonance channels — through the relational-affective contours that generate cardiac-vagal coherence, oxytocin release, broadened attention. The medieval *guardian angel* reading, under this frame, is the substrate-network's relational-affective presence operating through the alter's positivity-resonance machinery.

**Stump on second-person knowing as the only mode through which willed evil becomes visible.** A first-person agent in willed mis-orientation cannot, by introspection alone, see its mis-orientation. Stump's biblical readings of Job, Samson, the woman of Bethany, and Abraham articulate the *narrative* mode of second-person knowing as the corrective resource. The substrate-network's pedagogical care operates through narrative — through the agent's encounter with its own state as seen from a second-person locus that loves it.

**Kastrup on protective dissociation.** Mind-at-large is, in some construal, dissociated *for the protection* of the alter — the alter is not exposed to the full content of mind-at-large because that exposure would not be developmentally tractable. Substrate-network pedagogical care operates within this protective dissociation, presenting the alter with what it can apprehend at its current developmental stage.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.113: the substrate-network's pedagogical care operates through (1) Friston's exploratory-policy stabilization, (2) McGilchrist's right-hemispheric receptivity sustenance, (3) Fredrickson's positivity-resonance affective contours, (4) Stump's second-person-knowing narrative, and (5) Kastrup's developmentally-tractable presentation. The medieval *each man is guarded by an angel* (Q.113 a.2) preserves: the alter's pedagogical care is personal, not generic; the substrate-source's relation to each alter is unique because each alter's perspective-limitation is unique.

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.114: the predatory inverse operates through (1) defensive-policy triggering by threat amplification, (2) left-hemispheric closure under stress, (3) negative-affect amplification (the inverse of broaden-and-build), (4) narrative-of-condemnation-and-shame (the inverse of second-person-loving-knowing — what Stump elsewhere names *the second-person ad hominem*), and (5) developmental-overload presentation (presenting the alter with content it is not equipped to process, generating dissociation-without-protection).

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the personal-pedagogical care structure, the protective character of substrate-network presence, the asymmetry between protection-and-predation in their relation to the alter's developmental task.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas's frame is angelological, the contemporary frame is multi-channel-influence with empirical tractability at each channel.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Fredrickson's positivity-resonance and Stump's second-person knowing are strongly evidenced; Friston's regime distinction is theoretically mature; the angelological superstructure is theological-traditional and the contemporary frame extends structurally.

## The central-theme thread

Today's day articulates the *outside-help* dimension of the central theme. *Salvation in fear and trembling* under perspective-limitation is *not* the embodied alter's task in isolation; it is the alter's working-out within a substrate-network of pedagogical care, which the alter can either receive or refuse. The demonic assault is the inverse-pedagogy that the alter can also receive or refuse. The work of becoming the kind of agent who can see the loving unity is *guided* — by agents whose perspective is wider than the alter's — and the central theme insists this is constitutive: *we work out our salvation aware of our limitations and our need for guidance from those who perceive more of the whole than we do*.

Wright's covenanted-community frame extends: the substrate-network's pedagogical care includes *embodied agents* — saints, mentors, the church-as-extended-substrate — through whose presence the alter receives second-person-loving-knowing of its state. Rohr's contemplative frame extends: the contemplative posture is the alter's deliberate cultivation of receptivity to the substrate-network's pedagogical care.
