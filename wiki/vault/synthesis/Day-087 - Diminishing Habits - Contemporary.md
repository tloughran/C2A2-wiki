# Day 87 — Diminishing Habits (Contemporary)

## Frame

Q.52 takes up the *increase* of habits — can habits grow stronger, and if so how? Aquinas distinguishes carefully. Habits are *qualities*, not *quantities*, so they do not increase in extension (more parts) the way a quantitative thing grows. They increase rather in *intensity* and in the agent's *participation*: the subject comes to share more deeply, more readily, more efficiently in the quality. The example is striking — fire increases both by spreading and by intensifying; one's knowledge of Latin grows by extension (more vocabulary) and by intensification (more rapid, fluent use). Acts cause this growth, but only when performed with *full intention* and *due intensity* — perfunctory acts neither generate nor strengthen the habit.

Q.53 takes up the inverse: *diminution* and *corruption* of habits. Aquinas argues that habits decline when acts contrary to the habit are performed; when right acts are omitted; or when the conditions that sustain the habit's exercise are removed. The exception is *infused virtue*, which cannot be diminished by partial acts but only by acts directly contrary to it — because the infused virtue's *cause* is not the agent's own acts. Two principles emerge: (i) habits are sustained by congruent acts and undermined by contrary acts or omission; (ii) virtue's diminution is specifically a moral matter, not merely a forgetting.

Under conscious-realist monism the Q.52-53 dynamics map almost verbatim onto the contemporary literatures on practice-induced plasticity, extinction, disuse atrophy, and consolidation. The strikingly correct mechanical content of the medieval account — *intensity not extension*; *full-intention acts only*; *contrary acts as the specific mechanism of corruption* — recovers as precision-weighted plasticity, attentional gating of consolidation, and counter-conditioning. The episode is, in this respect, a small triumph for the medieval method: armchair phenomenology + careful conceptual analysis gets the structure right that the experimental literature would later validate.

## Problem

The shared problem: **what are the dynamics of habit-stabilization and habit-destabilization, and what mechanisms account for each?** The Q.52-53 articles isolate three substantive claims that any complete theory of habit-dynamics must vindicate. First, that habit-growth is *intensive* rather than *extensive* — habits do not get bigger; their subject becomes more deeply patterned by them. Second, that *only full-intention acts produce growth* — distracted, perfunctory, or coerced acts neither grow nor maintain. Third, that habits *decline by contrary act, omission, or environmental disruption*, with infused virtue as a special case whose dynamics differ.

The contemporary problem is the same, and it now has substantial empirical answer. Practice-induced plasticity is real but not flat: gains scale with attentional engagement (Treisman, Posner), affective stake (LeDoux, Pessoa), and prediction-error during the act (Schultz, Friston). Extinction is real but does not erase — the original disposition persists below threshold and can be reinstated (Bouton). Counter-conditioning is more durable than mere disuse. The dual-process distinction between habitual and goal-directed control (Daw, Niv, Dayan) lets us specify *which* stabilizations resist disruption and which can be undone by deliberate counter-action.

The deeper question Q.53 raises — what does it mean for a *virtue* (as opposed to a mere skill) to diminish? — sharpens under the central theme. A diminished virtue is not just a forgotten habit. It is an attenuation of the agent's disposition toward loving unity under conditions of perspective-limitation. The contemporary frame must say *how* this attenuation works mechanically and *why* certain dispositions admit attenuation that others (the infused virtues) do not.

## Resource

**Friston's precision-weighted hierarchical learning** supplies the formal machinery for Q.52 a.1's *intensity not extension* claim. In a hierarchical generative model, learning a domain does not add a new model; it sharpens existing priors. Precision-weighting concentrates the agent's processing on the relevant variables. Full-attention acts produce strong prediction-error signals that drive consolidation; perfunctory acts produce attenuated signals and weak consolidation. Friston PRS-04 (organism as active modeler) and the precision-weighting literature directly underwrite the Aquinas claim that *full-intention* acts grow the habit while perfunctory acts do not.

**Fredrickson's longitudinal practice data** provides the empirical foothold for both Q.52 (growth) and Q.53 (decline). Fredrickson PRS-01 on loving-kindness meditation documents the dose-response of sustained practice on vagal tone, attentional defaults, and positivity resonance — and the *return* toward baseline when practice lapses. The data confirm that habitus is sustained by ongoing congruent act and decays under omission. Fredrickson's "upward spiral" (positivity-resonance → broaden-and-build → more positivity-resonance) gives the *positive feedback* version of Aquinas's "habit-strengthens-with-act" dynamic.

**Hawkins's reference-frame consolidation** is the cortical-level mechanism for Q.52 a.2's "habit grows by repeated act with due intensity." Reference frames consolidate with repetition under attention; without attention, the same repetition produces less consolidation. Hawkins's account gives a clean cortical-circuit story for the medieval "due intensity" requirement.

**Levin's bioelectric pattern dynamics** provides the morphogenetic-scale account of pattern stability and destabilization. Bioelectric patterns can be re-routed; they can also stabilize a new attractor under sustained perturbation. The Q.52-53 dynamic of "stabilize-by-congruent-act; destabilize-by-contrary-act" recovers at the cellular-tissue level as bioelectric attractor switching.

**McGilchrist on attentional defaults and characterological diminution** supplies the hemispheric-level dynamic. The agent's habitual mode of attention (left-instrumental vs. right-contextual) is itself a habit, modifiable by sustained practice. *Disuse* of right-hemispheric attention produces a left-hemispheric-dominant character; *contrary use* (sustained instrumental focus) produces the same drift more rapidly. McGilchrist's clinical account of attentional drift maps onto Q.53's habit-decline mechanism.

**Stump's analytic-Thomistic work on virtue maintenance** is keystone for Q.52-53 read as moral psychology. Tom's PRS-form re-description of Stump's account of virtue covers the will's role in sustaining virtuous habit; the Stump-tradition wiki treats virtuous habit as essentially relational (faith-as-knowledge-of-persons, charity-as-second-person union). The contemporary frame can extend her account by reading the relational substrate as the wider participation under conscious-realist monism — without contradicting her position.

**The behavioural-economics and clinical literatures on counter-conditioning, extinction, and relapse** (Bouton; Marlatt; Volkow) supply the mechanism for Q.53 a.1 (contrary acts as the specific destabilizer). Extinction does not erase the original disposition; it builds a competing one. Counter-conditioning is more reliable than mere disuse for habit suppression. The medieval claim that contrary acts diminish a habit recovers as the formal claim about competing-learner dynamics in the agent's policy space.

**Wright on perseverance and the formed disciple** anchors the theological dynamic of Q.53 a.3 (infused virtue's special dynamics). Wright's reading of Pauline ethics emphasises that the *life-in-the-Spirit* is sustained by ongoing relational participation rather than by accumulated act-merit. The Pauline imagery of *abiding* in Christ (John 15; Romans 8) gives the Christological shape: the disposition is sustained by ongoing connection to its source, and lost specifically by *severance* from that source rather than by ordinary disuse. This matches Aquinas's claim that infused virtue is not diminished by partial acts but by acts directly contrary to it.

**Rohr on contemplative practice and the rhythm of formation and fall** completes the theological frame. *Falling Upward* and *Immortal Diamond* treat the spiritual journey as marked by alternating consolidation and rupture; the contemplative habit deepens through ongoing practice and is interrupted not by ordinary disuse but by the agent's withdrawal from the contemplative attention itself. The dynamics Rohr describes line up with Q.53's "virtue's diminution is specifically a moral matter" claim.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

**(a) Shared with Aquinas.** Habit-growth is *intensive*: it is the increasing deep patterning of the agent by the disposition, not the addition of new components. Only acts performed with full intention (attentional engagement, affective stake, predictive-error-rich engagement with the act's object) produce growth. Habits decline by contrary act (the most direct mechanism), by omission of congruent act (slower decline), and by environmental disruption of the conditions of exercise. Infused (gratuitous) virtues have distinctive dynamics — they cannot be diminished merely by lapse but specifically by acts directly contrary to them.

**(b) Where it reframes.** The medieval *quality/quantity* distinction recovers as the architectural distinction between *parameter values* (which sharpen/decay) and *parameter counts* (which would be quantitative growth — the wrong category). The "full-intention" requirement recovers as precision-weighted attentional engagement during the act. The "contrary act" mechanism recovers as competing-learner dynamics in policy space — counter-conditioning, not mere disuse. The special dynamics of infused virtue recover as: dispositions whose sustaining substrate is participation in a wider field (mind-at-large, life-in-the-Spirit, the cosmic Christ) rather than accumulated agent-act-merit; such dispositions decay specifically when the participation is severed, not when isolated acts are merely omitted.

**(c) Where evidence is firm vs. speculative.** *Firm*: dose-response of practice-induced affective stabilization (Fredrickson; Davidson). *Firm*: extinction-as-competing-learner rather than erasure (Bouton); counter-conditioning durability literature. *Firm*: attentional gating of consolidation (Treisman, Posner; Friston precision-weighting). *Empirically supported*: reference-frame consolidation under attention (Hawkins). *Empirically supported at growing scales*: bioelectric attractor dynamics (Levin). *Philosophically rigorous*: Stump on virtue-maintenance. *Theological, well-attested*: Wright on abiding-in-Christ as substrate of sustained virtue; Rohr on contemplative-practice dynamics. *Speculative-metaphysical*: the conscious-realist-monist reading of infused-virtue's special dynamics as participation in mind-at-large.

## The central-theme thread

Q.52-53 specifies the *dynamics* of the central theme's working-out. Loving unity is the computational task, and the agent computes toward it under perspective-limitation. The Q.52-53 dynamics tell us that this computation is *intensive*: the agent does not become a different agent by working out salvation, but is *more deeply patterned* by it. Each full-attention act grows the disposition; each lapse loses ground; each contrary act actively diminishes. The "fear and trembling" of Phil 2:12 makes mechanical sense — the working-out is not safe, not automatic, and not retained against contrary action.

The special dynamics of infused virtue carry the central theme's most distinctive claim. Some dispositions toward loving unity are not built up by the agent's acts alone. They are participations in a wider mind whose perspective exceeds the agent's. Such dispositions are diminished only by the agent's *active turning away* (acts directly contrary), not by ordinary lapse. This is the central theme's "guidance from those who perceive more of the whole" in the dynamic register: the agent that abides in the wider participation retains the disposition; the agent that severs the participation loses it. Wright's *abiding-in-Christ* and Rohr's contemplative-attention give the theological-and-contemplative texture; Kastrup's de-dissociative widening of the alter into mind-at-large gives the metaphysical-monist gloss.

## Bridges

- **Friston PRS-04 + precision-weighted learning** — Q.52 a.1 (intensity not extension): sharpening of priors under attentional precision-weighting; *formal, empirically robust*.
- **Fredrickson PRS-01 / loving-kindness meditation longitudinal data** — Q.52 (growth) and Q.53 (decay): dose-response of practice; return toward baseline on lapse; *empirical, high confidence*.
- **Hawkins PRS-03 / reference-frame consolidation** — Q.52 a.2 (acts cause growth under due intensity): cortical mechanism for attention-gated consolidation; *neuroscientifically grounded*.
- **Bouton on extinction-as-competing-learner; counter-conditioning literature** — Q.53 a.1 (contrary acts as specific destabilizer): formal account of competing-policy dynamics; *empirically robust*.
- **McGilchrist / hemispheric attentional defaults** — Q.53's character-level decline: attentional drift under disuse vs. contrary use; *clinical evidence*.
- **Levin / bioelectric attractor dynamics** — Q.52-53 at the morphogenetic scale: pattern stabilization and destabilization; *empirical at growing scales*.
- **Stump PRS-04 + virtue-maintenance** — analytic-Thomistic keystone on the will's role in sustaining virtuous habit; *philosophically rigorous*.
- **Wright / *After You Believe* and abiding-in-Christ (John 15; Romans 8)** — Q.53 a.3 (infused virtue's special dynamics): life-in-the-Spirit as participatory substrate; *theological, well-attested*.
- **Rohr / *Falling Upward*, *Immortal Diamond*** — contemplative-practice dynamics; consolidation-and-rupture pattern; *contemplative tradition*.
- **Kastrup / de-dissociative widening of the alter** — infused-virtue dynamics under conscious-realist monism: dispositions sustained by participation in mind-at-large; *speculative-metaphysical, internally consistent*.

## Where this leaves us

Q.52-53 supply the dynamics of growth and decay; Q.54-55 (tomorrow) take up the *distinction* of habits — how habits are individuated by their objects, and what specifically constitutes the *virtues* as a distinguished class of habit. The next move is from *general dynamics of habit* to *the specific kind of habit that is virtue*. The central theme will sharpen as the synthesis turns from habit-as-such to habit-ordered-to-loving-unity. The empirical literatures on practice-induced plasticity, attentional consolidation, and counter-conditioning will yield ground to Stump (virtue-as-formal-disposition), Fredrickson (positivity-as-virtue-substrate), McGilchrist (character-as-hemispheric-balance), and Wright + Rohr (virtue-in-Christ as the cosmic-scale formation).
