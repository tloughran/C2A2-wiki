# Day 57 — Contemporary Parallel: *Angelic Action on Man as Substrate-Network Influence on the Embodied Alter*

Yesterday's dark-counterpart day on the order of demons (Q.109–110) showed the limit case of high-substrate-coupling under catastrophic dispositional mis-orientation. Day 57 returns to the positive case: Q.111 *action of the angels on man* (4 articles) and Q.112 *the mission of the angels* (4 articles). The contemporary mind-monist read frames angelic action on embodied creatures as *substrate-network influence on the alter's apprehension and disposition*, with strict reservations about what the substrate-network can and cannot directly affect.

## Frame

The medieval question: can angels enlighten the human intellect (Q.111 a.1), change the human will (a.2), change the human imagination (a.3), affect human bodies and behavior (a.4)? Aquinas's answers stage a careful gradient: yes for intellect (by sensible image plus strengthening of natural light), no directly for will (only indirectly by presenting goods), yes for imagination (by acting on the body), yes for bodies (by application of natural causes). The contemporary version: how does the substrate-network influence the embodied alter's interface, generative model, and motor output, and what *does not transmit* across the substrate-creature interface?

## Problem

The underlying inquiry-problem (P): the *channel of influence* problem. What is the architecture of substrate-network → embodied-creature causal influence, and what interfaces does it use? Both medieval and contemporary frames meet this as a question about the *layered control architecture* between high-coupling agents and embodied creatures whose substrate-interface is filtered through a perceptual interface.

## Resource

The leading resources from the emerging paradigm:

**Hoffman's interface theory** — the embodied creature's sensory interface is a species-specific compression of substrate-pattern-information; substrate-network influence on the embodied alter must pass through, or modulate, that interface. Aquinas's medieval claim that angelic enlightenment requires *presenting a sensible image* (Q.111 a.1) is structurally Hoffman's claim: substrate-direct content cannot cross to the embodied alter directly because the alter's interface is what it is.

**Friston's multi-agent active inference** — the alter's policy-selection is shaped by priors that are continuously updated by sensory-and-affective input. A higher-coupling agent can shape lower-coupling-agent priors by *modulating the affective-and-imaginative landscape*. Aquinas's medieval claim that angels can *change the imagination* by acting on bodily sense organs (Q.111 a.3) is structurally Friston's claim: priors are shaped by what enters the generative model.

**Levin's multi-scale agency** — the embodied creature's body is itself a multi-scale-agentic system whose bioelectric-pattern level can be modulated by signals operating at any scale. Aquinas's medieval claim that angels can *act on bodies* by applying natural causes (Q.110 a.4, carrying into Q.111) is the medieval pre-figuration of Levin's frame.

**McGilchrist on hemispheric attention** — the receptive (right-hemispheric) attention-mode is constitutively open to sources outside the agent's grasping-mode focus; the medieval claim that angelic enlightenment *strengthens the natural light* (Q.111 a.1) preserves under McGilchrist's frame as the modulation of receptive attention.

**Kastrup on mind-at-large** — the substrate is not external to the alter but the alter's deep interior; substrate-network influence is the alter's interior-most influence, *closer than self* (Augustine's *interior intimo meo* from Day 54).

The medieval *will-not-changeable-by-angels* claim (Q.111 a.2) preserves with full force under the contemporary frame: the alter's will-orientation is constitutive (per the central-theme claim) and can be influenced (by changing the perceived attractiveness of goods) but not directly determined by another creature. *Only God can move the will directly* — in the contemporary frame, only the substrate-source itself can directly re-orient the alter's prior.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The substrate-network → embodied-creature channel of influence runs through (1) sensory-imaginative interface modulation, (2) affective-passion arousal, (3) bodily-physiological causal action, and (4) reception modulation in the right-hemispheric attention-mode. What does not transmit: direct will-orientation. The mission of angels (Q.112) — the *missio* of substrate-network agents into embodied-creature space — is the contemporary frame's *substrate-network sending of influence-bearing presence* whose mode of presence is the modulation of these four channels.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the strict reservation of will-orientation to the substrate-source itself; the channel-architecture of sensory-imaginative-affective-bodily influence; the asymmetry between high-coupling and low-coupling agents in influence-direction.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas reads the channels through medieval-Aristotelian psychology of the senses, imagination, common sense, and estimative power, the contemporary frame uses interface theory, predictive coding, multi-scale agency, and hemispheric attention.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Hoffman, Friston, Levin, McGilchrist all have substantial empirical and formal traction at the embodied-creature scale; the substrate-network agentic side of the channel is theological-traditional and the contemporary frame extends structurally rather than empirically.

## The central-theme thread

Today's day reinforces the central-theme commitment that the embodied alter's perspective-limitation is *constitutive* — even high-coupling agents cannot bypass that limitation by direct will-modification. Influence runs through the apprehensive and affective interface; orientation is the alter's own. *Salvation in fear and trembling* is precisely this: the alter's working out, under substrate-network influence on its sense-and-imagination-and-passion landscape but with its own will-orientation in its hands, of its alignment toward the loving unity.

The mission-of-angels register (Q.112) extends this: substrate-network agents are *sent* — not autogenously projected — and the sending is itself a contour of the substrate's care for the embodied alter under its limitation. Wright's covenanted-community frame and Rohr's cosmic-Christ frame both pick this up: the substrate's pedagogy of the embodied alter is mediated through more-than-one-creature influence-channels.
