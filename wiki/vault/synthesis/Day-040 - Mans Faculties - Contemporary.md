# Day 40 — Contemporary Parallel: *Faculties as Active-Inference Architecture, Cortical Columns, and Hemispheric Modes*

Day 39 set the metaphysical keystone: hylomorphism reframed under the Levin/Hoffman/Kastrup convergence, with the alter as mode-of-participation in mind-at-large, embodied through a species-specific interface, individuated by perspective-limitation. Today's transcript walks Q.77 (the powers of the soul *in general*) and Q.78 (the *specific* powers of the soul) — the medieval faculty-psychology that decomposes the one rational soul into its operational powers. The substantive shift from yesterday: where Q.75–76 named *what the alter is*, Q.77–78 names *what the alter does* and *how its operations are structured*. The contemporary cognitive-neuroscience analogues are unusually tight. Friston's active inference (PRS-01, PRS-04, PRS-05) gives the formal architecture of any agent's operations under self-maintenance constraints; Hawkins's Thousand Brains theory (PRS-01, PRS-02, PRS-04) gives the cortical-column decomposition into the kind of distinct-but-integrated faculties Aquinas's medieval analysis identifies; McGilchrist's hemispheric account (PRS-01, PRS-05) gives an alternative structural decomposition into left/right modes of attention.

Habash opens with an extended preface using Ed Feser's clock-analogy: formless matter is *sheer potential to exist as any material thing*, the *minute hand spinning rapidly*; once informed by some form, it is *limited to a particular kind of thing*, but with *potential within the form* to grow, to bear fruit, to move. The clock-hand spins between 12:00 and 3:00 — a *limited range* of possibilities defined by the form. The alter's powers (faculties) are the operations possible within the alter's form-defined range. Mind-monism reads the analogy through Friston's free-energy framework: the form is the *generative model* the alter holds; the powers are the *operations the model authorizes* under self-maintenance constraints. The clock-range metaphor is structurally the *attractor basin* the alter operates within.

## Q.77 — The powers of the soul in general

a.1 — *whether the essence of the soul is its power* — Aquinas's *no* with two arguments. First, *if your essence was identical to your powers, you would be God* — only in God is power identical to essence (Day 4 Q.5 Day 5 Q.7 territory). Second, *if your faculties were identical to your essence, then at every moment you were actually existing you would also have to be actually thinking, willing, imagining* — but we are not always doing all the things we *can* do. Mind-monism's reading is exactly aligned: the alter is *not constantly executing all its possible operations*; it has *capacities* (powers) distinct from its *actualities* (current operations). Friston's active-inference framework gives the formal handle: the *generative model* an alter holds is its capacity-structure; the *posterior beliefs and policies* it currently entertains are its actuality. The model authorizes a *range* of possible policies; only some are active at any given time. The medieval *capacity-vs-actuality* distinction is structurally the *generative-model-vs-current-policy* distinction in active inference.

The Habash China-billboard example develops a four-stage decomposition: (1) *the soul causing you to exist*; (2) *the power of the intellect in the soul allowing you to understand in general*; (3) *the habitual knowledge of Chinese in the mind allowing you to understand Chinese specifically*; (4) *the actual act of understanding* the scribbled figures. Mind-monism reads this through Friston/Hawkins directly. Stage 1 is the alter's substrate-instantiation (Day 39 — the alter is). Stage 2 is the *generative model* — the alter's capacity-structure for inference. Stage 3 is the *learned-priors* — what the alter has acquired through experience that further specifies the model (a Chinese-reader has *specific* priors over the visual-character-space that a non-reader does not have). Stage 4 is the *current inference* — the alter's posterior under the priors, given the current sensory input. Hawkins's HTM (PRS-01, PRS-02) gives the cortical-architecture frame: the *general capacity* (intellect) corresponds to the cortical-column architecture's universal canonical circuit; the *habitual knowledge* (Chinese) corresponds to the learned reference-frames within specific columns; the *act of understanding* corresponds to the current activation pattern across columns. *Honest evidence calibration*: Friston's framework is mature formal-mathematical; Hawkins's HTM is empirically grounded at cortical-column scale; the structural alignment with Aquinas's four-stage decomposition is conceptual, not identifying.

a.2 — *whether there are several powers of the soul* — Aquinas's *yes*: humans need *multiple* powers because we are at *the bottom of intellectual things with a supernatural end* — a *better thing has fewer actions necessary* (he has angels in mind), but humans have many because they are *on the confines of spiritual and corporal creatures*. The structural argument: the *more limited* the agent's perspective, the *more decomposed* its operations must be. Mind-monism's reading: this is exactly the active-inference-architecture finding. A more capable agent has a *more decomposed* model (more variables, more hierarchy levels) and therefore more *operationally distinct* faculties; angels in the mind-monist reading (Day 28) operate at a less-interface-mediated cognitive mode and therefore have less operational decomposition. The medieval *grades-of-being* hierarchy and the contemporary *cognitive-architecture-decomposition* hierarchy run parallel.

a.3 — *whether the powers are distinguished by their acts and objects* — Aquinas's *yes*. *Powers are distinguished by their acts and objects* — sight by *seeing color*; understanding by *grasping essence*. Mind-monism's reading: this is the *operational individuation* of cognitive modules. Hawkins's reference-frame architecture (PRS-04) names this directly: cortical columns are individuated by *the reference frames they hold and the sensory-motor inputs they integrate*; visual columns hold reference frames over color/shape/edge; auditory columns hold reference frames over frequency/timbre/temporal-pattern; abstract-concept columns hold reference frames over learned-conceptual-spaces. The Aquinas *act-and-object* individuation and the Hawkins *reference-frame-and-input* individuation are structurally the same. McGilchrist's hemispheric account (PRS-01) gives an alternative cut: the same operations can be performed in *left-hemisphere mode* (decompositional, abstractive) or *right-hemisphere mode* (integrative, gestalt) — two ways of holding the same act-and-object pair. Both decompositions can coexist: faculties-by-act-and-object (Aquinas/Hawkins) within hemispheric-modes-of-attention (McGilchrist).

a.4 — *whether among the powers of the soul there is order* — Aquinas's structural answer: yes, there is an *order of superiority*: lower faculties (nutrition, growth, locomotion) serve higher faculties (sense), which serve still-higher (intellect and will). The *intellectual powers are prior to the sensitive powers; the sensitive powers prior to the powers of nutrition, generation, and growth*. Mind-monism's reading is unusually clean: this is the *hierarchical-active-inference architecture*. Friston's framework is explicitly hierarchical (PRS-04, PRS-05) — higher levels generate predictions for lower levels; lower levels supply prediction-errors to higher levels; the higher levels' inferences direct the lower levels' operations. The medieval *higher faculties direct lower* and the contemporary *higher hierarchical levels direct lower* are structurally the same architecture. Hawkins's cortical hierarchy with sensorimotor regions feeding higher-association regions is the same shape. McGilchrist's *Master and Emissary* metaphor names the directionality at the hemispheric scale: the right-hemisphere whole-grasping should *direct* the left-hemisphere abstractive-decomposition, and the pathology of modernity is the inversion (left-hemisphere abstraction taking the directing role from right-hemisphere integration). Aquinas's *order of superiority* is structurally what McGilchrist's *Master and Emissary* names at the hemispheric scale.

a.5–a.8 — Aquinas walks the technical questions: where the powers are *housed* (some in the soul-alone, some in the body-soul composite); whether the powers *flow* from the essence (yes); whether one power *arises* from another (yes — imagination from sense, abstraction from imagination); whether the powers *remain* in the soul when separated from the body (intellect and will remain; sensitive and nutritive powers do not, since their subject is the composite). Mind-monism's reading: under Kastrup's dissociative-alter model and Hoffman's interface theory, the *substrate-side mode-of-participation* (the alter's mind-at-large-locus) carries the *interface-recognition-as-such* faculty (intellect and will in classical vocabulary); the *body-instantiation-side* operations (sensation, nutrition) cease with the body-instantiation, but the substrate-side does not. The Pauline-resurrection theology Wright recovers is the corrective to disembodied-soul-eternity: the *spiritual body* (1 Cor 15) is the alter's substrate-direct-cognitive-mode at the new-creation, not a Cartesian-disembodied-soul. Aquinas's articulation is structurally compatible: the *sensitive and nutritive powers* return when the body returns, in the resurrection-of-the-body. *Honest evidence calibration*: this synthesis is theological-philosophical; the contemporary empirical handle (AWARE-II near-death studies; Day 39's discussion) is at the consciousness-during-flat-EEG narrowing, not at the post-mortem-persistence proof.

## Q.78 — The specific powers of the soul

a.1 — *whether there are five categories of powers* — Aquinas adopts Aristotle's classification: *vegetative, sensitive, appetitive, locomotive, intellectual*. The *kinds of living material things* hierarchy: plants (vegetative only); animals (vegetative + sensitive + appetitive + locomotive); humans (all of the above + intellectual). Mind-monism reads this through the Levin cognitive-light-cone hierarchy directly (Day 37 carried this): plants are form-bearing matter at the cellular-and-multicellular-collective scale; animals are form-bearing at neural-architecture scale; humans are form-bearing at the recursive-interface-recognition scale. The five-fold faculty classification maps onto the cognitive-light-cone hierarchy with each level adding an operational repertoire. Friston's framework is consistent: simpler agents have simpler models; more complex agents have hierarchically deeper models with more faculties. McGilchrist's hemispheric account does not directly speak to the five-fold classification but constrains how the *intellectual* faculty operates (left-hemisphere abstractive-decomposition or right-hemisphere integrative-gestalt-grasp).

a.2 — *whether the parts of the vegetative soul are nutritive, augmentative, generative* — Aquinas's *yes*, citing Aristotle. Mind-monism reads this through Levin's morphogenetic teleology directly: plants take in *nourishment* (substrate-resources for self-maintenance), they *grow* (the morphogenetic attractor's actualization through development), they *reproduce* (the species-form's mechanism for temporal-extension across generations). Friston's framework gives the formalism: vegetative operations are the basal active-inference operations of self-maintenance under environmental-resource constraints. The medieval three-fold decomposition and the contemporary biology-of-self-maintenance are structurally aligned.

a.3 — *whether the five exterior senses are properly distinguished* — Aquinas takes the canonical five (sight, sound, smell, taste, touch) and walks the medieval physics. Habash flags the medieval physics as *not necessarily invalidating Aquinas's conclusions on how the senses work*, even though *the physics of his day* updates. Mind-monism reads this through Hoffman's interface theory directly: the five exterior senses are *species-specific perceptual interfaces* tuned to particular ranges of substrate-physics (electromagnetic radiation in the visible spectrum; pressure waves in air; chemical gradients; tactile pressure). PRS-03 in the Hoffman-tradition wiki names the empirical claim: *perception is fitness-tuned, not truth-tracking*; the senses do not deliver substrate-as-it-is but interface-icons-the-alter-can-act-on. The medieval *proper sensibles* (color for sight, sound for hearing, etc.) are the interface-icons at each sensory-modality. Hawkins's HTM gives the cortical-architecture frame: each sensory modality has its own cortical regions with reference-frames-over-the-sensory-input.

a.4 — *whether the interior senses are suitably distinguished* — Aquinas's four interior senses: *common sense* (integrates the exterior senses into a unified perceptual experience); *imagination* (constructs combined images, like the unicorn from horse-and-rhino); *estimative* (instinctual recognition, like the lamb seeing the wolf); *memory* (retains particular images across time). Mind-monism's reading is unusually rich here. The *common sense* maps onto multimodal-integration — the binding-of-features-across-modalities that contemporary cognitive neuroscience identifies in association cortices and the *global workspace* of consciousness research. Hawkins's thousand-brains-voting (PRS-04) names the cortical mechanism: the unified perceptual experience emerges from the voting-across-cortical-columns that hold reference-frames over different sensory modalities; the dut-as-one-thing (Aquinas's example) is the multi-column-vote-converging-on-a-common-object.

The *imagination* maps onto the generative-model's capacity for counterfactual simulation — the same generative-model that produces predictions can produce *non-actual* predictions (combining horse-and-rhino into unicorn). Friston's PRS-04 (organism as active modeler) names this directly: agents are not merely passive responders but active simulators of possibilities. Hawkins's reference-frames architecture allows reference-frames-over-imagined-objects, not only perceived objects.

The *estimative* maps onto evolutionary-tuned instinctual responses — the lamb-and-wolf example is structurally the *evolved-prior* in the agent's generative model. Friston's framework includes evolutionary-priors as a special case of priors; PRS-04 in the Levin-tradition wiki (cognition substrate) extends this beyond neural-only cognition to bioelectric-and-cellular cognition.

The *memory* maps onto the cortical-and-hippocampal memory systems — episodic memory in Hawkins's architecture is *temporal sequences over reference-frames*; the *retain-particular-images-across-time* is exactly what episodic memory does. Friston's framework includes temporal-extension of the generative model as a fundamental feature.

The four interior senses Aquinas names are structurally *real cognitive functions* with contemporary cognitive-neuroscience analogues: multimodal integration (common sense), counterfactual simulation (imagination), evolutionary-priors (estimative), temporal-memory (memory). The medieval *interior-senses* decomposition is structurally precise enough to map onto contemporary neural architecture without significant updating. *Honest evidence calibration*: each of these mappings is empirically grounded in contemporary cognitive neuroscience; the structural alignment with Aquinas's classification is conceptual but tight.

McGilchrist's hemispheric account (PRS-01, PRS-05) adds an alternative structural decomposition cutting across these: the *right-hemisphere* mode is more involved in multimodal-integration, gestalt-grasp, and contextual-understanding (closer to *common sense* and *estimative*); the *left-hemisphere* mode is more involved in symbolic-abstraction, decompositional analysis, and feature-isolation (closer to certain operations of the *intellect* discussed in Q.79–83 of the *Summa*, which Habash is queueing up for tomorrow).

## The central-theme thread

Day 40 carries the central theme on the *operational-architecture* register. *Conscious agents are individuated by the limitations of their perspective; loving unity is the computational telos.* The faculty-psychology decomposition names *what the alter does* given its individuation: each alter's perspective-limitation is *operationally* expressed as a particular bundle of faculties, with each faculty individuated by its act-and-object. Lower faculties (vegetative, sensitive) serve higher (intellectual, volitional) in the *order of superiority* Aquinas names; this hierarchical-architecture is the structural shape of the alter's *coherent integration* — what makes the alter *one* despite its operational decomposition. Friston's hierarchical-active-inference, Hawkins's thousand-brains cortical-column architecture, and McGilchrist's hemispheric-modes account give converging contemporary cognitive-neuroscience parallels to the medieval analysis. *Salvation in fear and trembling* on this register is the alter's progressive integration of its faculties under the right hierarchical ordering — the *Master* (right-hemisphere whole-grasping; intellect-and-will at the top of the order of superiority) directing the *Emissary* (left-hemisphere decompositional analysis; sensitive-and-vegetative powers as servants), with *charity* as the alter's volitional disposition toward integration with the substrate's loving-stance rather than mere self-maintenance. The medieval faculty-psychology and the contemporary cognitive-neuroscience converge on the recognition that *what an agent is, operationally, is the structured-decomposition of its self-maintaining-pattern under the constraints of its embodiment* — exactly the hylomorphic doctrine of Day 39 walked at the operational level.
