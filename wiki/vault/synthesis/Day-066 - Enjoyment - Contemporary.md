# Day 66 — Contemporary Parallel: *Enjoyment as the Substrate-Direct Rest of the Will and the Imperfect Anticipations of It*

Day 65 articulated the multi-source causal architecture of the alter's policy-selection. Day 66 takes Aquinas through Q.10 (the manner in which the will is moved — 4 articles) and Q.11 (enjoyment as an act of the will — 4 articles). The contemporary mind-monist frame engages this as the *necessity-and-freedom structure* of will-movement under perspective-limitation, and the *substrate-direct-rest* phenomenology of enjoyment in its perfect and imperfect modes.

## Frame

Q.10 a.1 establishes that the will is naturally moved toward goods — the universal good, happiness, and the goods proper to other faculties (knowledge for intellect, life for nature). Q.10 a.2 articulates the keystone metaphysical commitment: *that good alone which is perfect and lacking in nothing is such a good that the will cannot not will it* — and this is happiness. *Whereas any other particular good, in so far as they are lacking in some good, can be regarded as non-goods from this point of view; they can be set aside or approved by the will.*

The room-and-light analogy is doing real work: in a fully-lit room my eyes-open necessarily see all colored things; in a partially-lit room my eyes-open do not necessarily see them. The presence of the perfect good is non-optional sight; the presence of partial goods is genuinely under-determined.

Q.10 a.3 — the lower appetite (passion) does not necessitate the will except in the limit case of insanity or rage. Q.10 a.4 — God moves the will *as the kind of thing the will is* — voluntarily, indifferently, contingently — not as mechanical necessity.

Q.11 articulates enjoyment: a.1 it is an act of the appetitive power (perception is in the apprehensive, complacency is in the appetitive, *delight is formally completed in the appetitive*); a.2 it belongs to the rational creature in perfect degree, to irrational animals imperfectly; a.3 enjoyment is most strictly of the last end, but proximate ends can elicit imperfect enjoyment; a.4 perfect enjoyment is of the end-actually-possessed, but imperfect enjoyment is of the end-possessed-only-in-intention (in the mind).

## Problem

The problem (P): the alter's volitional architecture is constitutively *both necessary-and-free*. Necessary toward the universal good (the will's formal directedness); free among partial goods (the will's perspectival latitude). And the alter's *enjoyment* — its rest-in-the-good — has a perfect mode (substrate-direct in the eschatological state) and an imperfect mode (anticipatory-in-this-life). What is the structural account of this dual mode?

## Resource

**Friston on policy-necessity-under-prior-perfectness.** Active inference: when the agent's prior is *fully aligned* with the substrate-source and the substrate-source is *fully present*, expected free energy goes to zero in a single optimal policy — no other policy is selectable. This is the formal correlate of Aquinas's *that good alone which is perfect and lacking in nothing... the will cannot not will*.

**Fredrickson on rest-in-the-good.** Empirically, positivity-resonance with a beloved other generates *cardiac-vagal coherence* — an embodied state of *rest in the good*. The medieval enjoyment-as-resting-of-appetite preserves cleanly under the positivity-resonance frame at its most mature form.

**McGilchrist on right-hemispheric reception in delight.** Delight is right-hemispheric; the receptive openness to the present-good is what allows enjoyment to occur. The medieval *delight follows knowledge* preserves as right-hemispheric reception following the apprehended good.

**Stump on union as enjoyment of God.** The second-personally-known-other is the locus of enjoyment in its full mode; the medieval *beatific vision-and-enjoyment* preserves as second-personal-loving-knowing in its full substrate-direct mode.

**Kastrup on alter-substrate communion.** Enjoyment is, in mind-monist construal, the alter's experiential mode of being-in-substrate-resonance. Imperfect enjoyment is partial communion through prayer, contemplation, or sacramental presence; perfect enjoyment is full alignment with mind-at-large.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.10: the alter's volitional architecture is structurally *necessity-toward-the-perfect-good plus latitude-among-partial-goods*. This is what active inference predicts under hierarchical priors with one fully-determining terminal. The substrate-source's primary causation operates *through* this voluntary architecture, not against it (Day 59's primary/secondary cause structure preserves).

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.11: enjoyment is the alter's substrate-direct-rest in the apprehended-and-attained good. Perfect enjoyment is the substrate-direct-mode resting-in-the-substrate-source. Imperfect enjoyment is partial substrate-resonance through the embodied alter's anticipatory modes — contemplation, prayer, sacrament, positivity-resonance with second-personally-loved others, the Song-of-Songs *I held him and would not let him go*. *Joy in truth* (Augustine) is the alter's empirical access to the substrate-resonance even under perspective-limitation.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the necessity-and-freedom structure; the perfect/imperfect distinction; enjoyment as rest-in-the-good; the substrate-direct character of perfect enjoyment.

What it *reframes*: Aquinas's frame uses faculty-psychology and beatific-vision; the contemporary frame uses active-inference, positivity-resonance, hemispheric attention, and substrate-resonance.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Friston's policy-necessity-under-perfect-good is mature formalism; Fredrickson's positivity-resonance and cardiac-vagal-coherence are empirically strong; the substrate-direct-mode resting reading is speculative-philosophical-theological.

## The central-theme thread

Today's day articulates a load-bearing implication of the central theme: *the work of becoming-aligned with the loving unity is itself accompanied by anticipatory enjoyment*. The alter is not pursuing flourishing as something fully external; the alter has *imperfect enjoyment now* in proportion as it is becoming-aligned. Augustine's *joy in truth* and the Song-of-Songs *I held him and would not let him go* are the alter's contemporaneous-with-perspective-limitation experience of substrate-resonance. The alter's daily-task work is, in part, to receive these anticipatory enjoyments as the *real partial substrate-direct-mode* presence of the loving unity, and to let them shape the priors under which policy-selection proceeds.

*Salvation in fear and trembling* is not joyless; it is shot through with imperfect joy. Wright's resurrection-and-new-creation reading of present joy is load-bearing scripturally; Rohr's contemplative-presence frame is load-bearing devotionally. Fredrickson's empirical work is the contemporary scientific anchor: the alter's flourishing process *now* generates substantive positivity-resonance now, and this is not a distraction from the eschatological completion but its anticipatory partial-presence.
