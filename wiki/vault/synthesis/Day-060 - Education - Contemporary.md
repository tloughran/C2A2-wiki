# Day 60 — Contemporary Parallel: *Teaching as Resource-Provision for the Learner's Own Inferential Work*

Day 59 articulated the layered-causation commitment at the metaphysics-and-fate register. Day 60 takes the question into the *one-agent-shapes-another* register: Q.117 *the action of man* (4 articles, including the central question of whether one man can teach another) and Q.118 *the production of man as to the soul* (3 articles). The contemporary mind-monist read frames teaching as *resource-provision* for the learner's own predictive-inference work: the teacher cannot impose understanding; the teacher provides sensible-and-conceptual material the learner abstracts.

## Frame

Aquinas's response in Q.117 a.1 is one of the most striking moves in this stretch of the *Summa*. *Can one man teach another?* In one way yes, in another way no. The teacher provides exterior help — sensible images, broken-down concepts, analogies, parables — but the act of *abstracting the universal* is the learner's own. Habash quotes the analogy: *the teacher only brings exterior help, as the physician who heals; but just as the interior nature is the principal cause of the healing, so the interior light of the intellect is the principal cause of knowledge.*

The contemporary mind-monist read is essentially identical: the teacher modulates the learner's input-stream and the structure-of-presentation; the learner's own predictive-inference machinery is what *forms* the abstraction. The medieval *agent intellect* (intellectus agens) preserves cleanly as the learner's hierarchical-temporal-memory and active-inference apparatus.

## Problem

The problem (P): how does *understanding* — the abstraction of the universal from particulars — get formed in a learner? More specifically, what is the architecture of the teacher-learner channel, and what *cannot* be transmitted across that channel?

The medieval frame meets this through the active-intellect / passive-intellect distinction: the agent intellect abstracts, the possible intellect receives the abstracted form. The contemporary frame meets it through hierarchical-predictive-coding (Hawkins, Friston): the learner's generative model updates against the input-stream the teacher provides, and the teacher's role is to make the input-stream as *informative* as possible for the learner's particular generative-model state.

## Resource

**Hawkins on hierarchical temporal memory and concept formation.** Concepts are stable patterns in the cortical-column architecture; they form by *exposure* to consistent regularities. The teacher's role is to provide a curated exposure-stream that surfaces the regularities at a tractable scale. The medieval *break the abstract idea into sensible pieces* maps onto the HTM account of concept-formation through layered exposure.

**Friston's active inference applied to learning.** The learner's generative model updates by reducing prediction-error against the input-stream. The teacher minimizes the learner's *path of expected free energy* by structuring the curriculum — present highly-predictable foundations first, then introduce informative contrast cases at the boundary of the learner's current model. Aquinas's three modes of teaching (a.1) — leading from premises to conclusions, breaking universal into particular, using analogy/parable — correspond to three active-inference pedagogical strategies.

**McGilchrist on hemispheric pedagogy.** Effective teaching engages both hemispheres: the right hemisphere's openness to gestalt, the left hemisphere's analytical detail-work. The medieval *teacher provides sensible images and breaks abstract concepts* uses both modes — the image is right-hemispheric reception, the analytical breakdown is left-hemispheric detail.

**Levin on developmental morphogenesis.** At the biological scale, *pattern-information* is provided by the substrate (bioelectric pattern) and the developing organism *interprets and instantiates* that pattern through its own developmental machinery. Education at the embodied-mind scale recapitulates this structure: substrate-pattern-information is presented through the teacher's curriculum; the learner's developmental machinery instantiates the pattern as understanding.

**Stump on faith as second-personal knowledge and tradition-bound rationality.** Some understanding cannot be reduced to propositional content; it is *second-person* knowing — knowledge of and through persons. The medieval *interior light* preserves under Stump's frame as the second-person-mediated formation that is irreducibly relational. *Tradition-bound rationality* (MacIntyre) extends: the learner's inferential work is shaped by the inherited tradition of inquiry, which the teacher partly transmits and the learner partly extends.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.117 a.1: teaching is *channel-mediated curriculum-presentation* by an agent who has access to a structured pattern, to a learner whose own predictive-inference machinery is the principal cause of the resulting understanding. The teacher's role is to optimize the input-stream's informativeness given the learner's current model-state. The teacher *cannot* directly install understanding because understanding is constitutively the learner's own predictive-inference output.

Q.117 a.2 (whether man can teach an angel) — Aquinas's answer is no, because angels' substrate-coupling is too direct for embodied-mediation to add anything. Contemporary frame: the higher-coupling agent's apprehension is too direct for the lower-coupling agent's embodied-curriculum to inform.

Q.117 a.3 (whether man can act on corporeal things by his power alone) — natural causation only, except in extraordinary substrate-source action. Q.117 a.4 (whether the soul of a separated man can move bodies) — preserved in the structurally-similar way.

Q.118 a.1–3 (production of man as to the soul) — the contemporary mind-monist frame engages with theological substantialism on the soul-as-substrate-coupling-mode of an embodied creature; Aquinas's scriptural-and-theological position that the rational soul is created by God directly preserves under mind-monism in the form: *the substrate-coupling-mode of a rational creature is not derivable from material processes alone; it is the substrate-source's instantiation of a new substrate-coupling-locus*. This is metaphysically continuous with Levin's *form is real and not reducible to material substrate*; with Hoffman's *interface-design is not reducible to interface-content*; with Kastrup's *mind-at-large dissociation-pattern is not generated by the dissociated alter*.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the constitutive role of the learner's *interior* in forming understanding; the asymmetry between teacher's input and learner's understanding; the substrate-source's role as the originator of rational substrate-coupling-locus.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas's frame is hylomorphic-Aristotelian on the soul and intellectual psychology, the contemporary frame uses HTM, active inference, hemispheric attention, and morphogenetic pattern.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Hawkins on HTM is empirically grounded in cortical research; Friston's active-inference pedagogy framework is mature and increasingly empirically supported; McGilchrist on hemispheric attention is empirically grounded. The substrate-source-instantiates-rational-soul reading is speculative-philosophical, continuous with mind-monist commitments.

## The central-theme thread

Today's day articulates a load-bearing implication of perspective-limitation: *each agent's understanding is irreducibly the agent's own*. The teacher cannot give the learner a perspective; the teacher can only provide resources for the learner to construct it. *Salvation in fear and trembling* under perspective-limitation includes the daily-task work of *constructing one's own understanding* under guidance from those whose perspective is wider. The substrate-source's role as originator of rational substrate-coupling-locus reinforces the central-theme reading: each new agent is a *new perspective* on the loving unity, not a derivative of prior agents.

The teaching-and-learning relation is itself an instance of *loving unity under perspective-difference*: the teacher who genuinely loves the learner is the teacher who provides what the learner can use, recognizing that the learner's understanding will be the learner's own and may even surpass the teacher's. Wright's covenanted-community frame and Rohr's contemplative-mentorship frame extend this: the substrate-network's pedagogy operates through embodied teacher-learner relations across generations.
