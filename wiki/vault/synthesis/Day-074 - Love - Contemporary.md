# Day 74 — Contemporary Parallel: *Love as the Formal Direction Toward Loving-Unity, and the Affective Phenomenology of Perspective-Crossing*

This is the project's most central-theme load-bearing day. Day 70 articulated the formal/material conformity-to-substrate-source distinction; Day 73 established love as the structurally-first passion. Day 74 is where Q.26-28 makes affectively explicit what the entire synthesis-in-progress has been working toward: the medieval phenomenology of love as the willing-of-the-good-of-the-other-for-the-other's-sake; the causes of love in apprehension, knowledge, and likeness; and the effects of love in union, mutual indwelling, ecstasy, zeal, and the love-that-causes-everything-the-lover-does.

## Frame

Q.26 articulates love itself in four articles. *Is love a passion?* (a.1): Yes — love, in its primary register, is a passion of the concupiscible appetite, the first movement of the appetite toward an apprehended good. *Is love in the concupiscible faculty?* (a.2): Yes — love proper is concupiscible; the will has *dilection* and *charity* as its analogs, but the affective foundation is concupiscible. *Is love the same as dilection?* (a.3): No — *dilectio* and *amor* are related but distinguishable; *dilectio* implies choice, while *amor* is the broader genus that includes the irrational appetite's love. *Is love properly divided into love of friendship and love of concupiscence?* (a.4): Yes — *amor amicitiae* is willing the good of another for the other's sake; *amor concupiscentiae* is willing a good for oneself or for another as means.

Q.27 articulates the causes of love in four articles. *Is the good the only cause of love?* (a.1): Yes — love is fundamentally directed at the good as apprehended. *Is knowledge a cause of love?* (a.2): Yes — *nihil amatum nisi cognitum*: nothing is loved unless known. *Is likeness a cause of love?* (a.3): Yes — both real likeness (sharing a form actually) and likeness-of-disposition cause love. *Is anything else a cause of love?* (a.4): Other causes (e.g., good-doing, honor) are reducible to the prior three.

Q.28 articulates the effects of love in six articles. *Is union an effect of love?* (a.1): Yes — love produces union of affection (and seeks union of presence). *Is mutual indwelling an effect of love?* (a.2): Yes — the lover dwells in the beloved by knowledge and affection; the beloved dwells in the lover by drawing the lover toward itself. *Is ecstasy an effect of love?* (a.3): Yes — love causes the lover to *go out of itself* toward the beloved. *Is zeal an effect of love?* (a.4): Yes — love produces zeal in defense of the beloved's good. *Is love a wounding passion?* (a.5): Love wounds insofar as the beloved is at distance, not insofar as union is achieved. *Is love the cause of everything the lover does?* (a.6): Yes — every action of the lover is for the sake of the beloved.

This is, simply, Aquinas's affective phenomenology of perspective-crossing toward loving-unity.

## Problem

The problem (P): the alter, individuated by perspective-limitation, is constitutively directed toward loving-unity with the substrate-source and with other alters. What is the *affective phenomenology* of this directedness? How does the alter, given that its perspective-limitation makes complete apprehension impossible, *reach toward* the other-as-loved? What is the structure of *union* that the alter participates in when it loves? How does *mutual-indwelling* preserve the alter's individuation while integrating it with the beloved? What is *ecstasy* as the alter's structural transcendence-of-its-own-perspective-limitation toward the beloved?

This is not a peripheral question; it is *the* question of the project. The synthesis-in-progress claims that loving-unity is the formal target; Q.26-28 articulates what that target *feels like* and *operates as* in the alter's life.

## Resource

**Fredrickson on positivity-resonance as the empirical anchor for love-as-passion.** Q.26 a.1's love-as-passion has its empirical correlate in Fredrickson's *positivity resonance* construct: love is a *micro-moment of shared-positive-affective-state, embodied synchrony, and reciprocal care* that occurs between alters whose physiologies, attention, and affective registers couple. Positivity resonance is *not* the cognitive-conceptual judgment "I will the good of the other"; it is the embodied-affective-relational substrate-state out of which such judgment can be cultivated. Fredrickson's empirical research — over fifteen years of laboratory and field studies — documents the substrate-network propagation of positivity resonance, its broaden-and-build downstream effects, its physiological correlates (vagal tone, oxytocin, mirror-neural coupling), and its cumulative effect on flourishing. This is the contemporary empirical anchor for Aquinas's love-as-foundational-passion.

**Stump on union-as-highest-good and second-personal-knowing as constitutive of love.** Q.28 a.1-2 (union and mutual indwelling as effects of love) has its philosophical-theological articulation in Stump's *Wandering in Darkness* and *Atonement*. Stump argues that union with God is the highest human good, that union requires *second-personal* knowing — knowing-the-other-as-thou rather than knowing-the-other-as-it — and that love is the affective register through which second-personal knowing operates. This is a precise philosophical articulation of the medieval *amor amicitiae*: the lover wills the good of the beloved *as* a person, not as a means, and the willing is structured by perspectival presence-to-the-other. Stump's account directly engages Aquinas while grounding the claim in contemporary philosophy of mind.

**Rohr on the Universal Christ as cosmic-loving-unity.** Q.28's mutual-indwelling has its contemplative-theological articulation in Rohr's *The Universal Christ*: the cosmic Christ is the substrate-source's pattern-of-loving-presence to all alters, and contemplative practice is the alter's deliberate reception of that pattern. Rohr's tradition (the Franciscan-contemplative-non-dual lineage running through Bonaventure, Eckhart, Merton) frames love as the structural mode through which the alter participates in the substrate-source's underlying unity. The cosmic-Christ frame is the contemplative-theological articulation of the central-theme's loving-unity-as-formal-target.

**Levin on bioelectric-communion and integrative-substrate as the basis of biological mutuality.** Empirical anchor at biological scale: Levin's bioelectric-network research shows that biological agents at multiple scales communicate and integrate through bioelectric signaling, and that morphogenetic outcomes depend on the network's integrative dynamics. This is suggestive (not yet directly empirical at affective-systems scale) of the substrate-mechanism by which mutual-indwelling could operate biologically: shared bioelectric-substrate-states between alters in deep relational coupling.

**Kastrup on mind-at-large and dissociated-alters-moving-toward-re-integration via love.** Mind-at-large (Kastrup's analytic-idealist substrate-source) is one undifferentiated mind whose alters are dissociated configurations through which it experiences in particularized ways. Love is then structurally the alter's recognition-of-and-reach-toward the underlying unity that perspective-limitation had concealed. Mutual-indwelling is the alter's experiential register of the underlying mind-monist truth that all alters are alters-of-one-mind-at-large. This is the metaphysical articulation that grounds the central-theme's loving-unity claim.

**Wright on agape in the Pauline corpus and covenanted-community as the locus of mutual-indwelling.** Scriptural-historical primary: Wright's reading of Paul (especially *Paul and the Faithfulness of God* and *The Resurrection of the Son of God*) frames *agape* as the covenanted-community's structural mode of life-together; Christ's resurrection inaugurates the new-creation in which mutual-indwelling between God-and-people and between people-and-people is the structural fabric. *Salvation* on Wright's reading is *inclusion in the covenanted people whose life-together is agape-shaped* — which is precisely the central-theme's loving-unity made scripturally specific.

**Friston on variational-coupling as the formalism of mutual-indwelling.** Mature active-inference formalism: when two agents share a generative model and their inferences are mutually-constraining, the variational-coupling between them produces a regime in which each agent's free-energy minimization simultaneously serves the other's. This is the mathematical articulation of mutual-indwelling at the active-inference level: agents whose models are coupled inhabit each other's prior-structures.

**McGilchrist on right-hemispheric receptivity as the structural locus of love-as-attention.** Love is structurally a *mode of attention* — the right hemisphere's receptive, embodied, relational, gestaltic uptake of the other-as-thou. Disorders of love (instrumentalization, abstraction, possession) correlate with left-hemispheric closure that turns the beloved-as-thou into a beloved-as-it. Right-hemispheric integration is the neural register of *amor amicitiae*.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.26-28: love is the alter's primary-valence-orientation toward the substrate-source-network's integrative dynamics, registered affectively as positivity-resonance with another alter, structured cognitively-conceptually as the willing-of-the-good-of-the-other-for-the-other's-sake (Stump's second-personal-knowing), grounded metaphysically in mind-at-large's underlying unity (Kastrup), articulated theologically as agape (Wright) and as cosmic-Christ-pattern (Rohr), and operationalized at the biological-substrate level through bioelectric-communion (Levin) and at the cognitive-substrate level through variational-coupling (Friston).

The causes of love (Q.27) recover as: the *good* (the substrate-source's integrative pattern as it manifests through the loved-alter); *knowledge* (the alter's perspective-limited apprehension; deeper-and-truer knowledge yields fuller love); *likeness* (the underlying mind-monist unity revealed through whatever real-or-affective-similarity allows the alter to recognize the substrate-source's pattern in the beloved). All three causes converge on the central-theme commitment that love is the alter's affective registration of the substrate-source's integrative dynamics through whatever portion of those dynamics its perspective-limited apprehension reaches.

The effects of love (Q.28) recover as the central-theme's structural articulation: *union* is the alter's reach toward loving-unity-as-formal-target; *mutual-indwelling* is the alter's experiential register of the underlying mind-monist communion (each-alter-in-each-alter as configurations of one substrate-source-mind); *ecstasy* is the alter's structural transcendence-of-its-own-perspective-limitation toward the beloved (the alter goes out of itself, which is exactly what perspective-crossing requires); *zeal* is the alter's commitment to defending the substrate-source's integrative dynamics in the beloved; *wounding* registers the not-yet-attained character of full union under perspective-limitation; *love-as-cause-of-everything-the-lover-does* is the central-theme's claim that the alter's primary-valence-orientation organizes its entire life-action.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the entire phenomenology of love-as-passion-and-its-completions; the willing-of-the-good-of-the-other; the apprehension/knowledge/likeness causal structure; the union/indwelling/ecstasy/zeal/wounding/totality effect-structure.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas's frame is concupiscible-appetite-Aristotelian + dilectio/charity at the will, the contemporary frame uses positivity-resonance (Fredrickson), variational-coupling (Friston), bioelectric-communion (Levin), second-personal-knowing (Stump), mind-at-large dissociated-alters (Kastrup), cosmic-Christ-pattern (Rohr), agape-in-covenanted-community (Wright), and right-hemispheric-attention (McGilchrist). The reframe moves love from a faculty-psychological category into a multi-scale phenomenon visible at biological, cognitive, relational, theological, and metaphysical registers.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Fredrickson's positivity-resonance is strong empirical with extensive backing; Stump's union-and-second-personal-knowing is theological-philosophical with book-length argument; Wright's agape-in-Paul is scriptural-historical substantial; Rohr's cosmic-Christ is contemplative-theological substantial; Friston's variational-coupling is mature formalism; Levin's bioelectric-communion is empirically suggestive at biological scale and speculative at affective-systems scale; Kastrup's mind-at-large is philosophical-metaphysical; the synthesis-claim that all converge on the central-theme's loving-unity-as-formal-target is the project's most-central speculative-philosophical-theological commitment.

## The central-theme thread

This is the day where the synthesis-in-progress receives its clearest affective-phenomenological articulation. The central theme states: conscious agents are individuated by the limitations of their perspective; learning to obtain the goal of *loving unity* from each perspective is a computational task, limited by the resources we perceive — each of us from our own perspective. Q.26-28 is *exactly the affective phenomenology of this structure*.

*Amor amicitiae* (Q.26 a.4) — willing the good of the other for the other's sake — is the central-theme's perspective-crossing made affectively-explicit. To will the good of the other *for the other's sake* requires the alter to recognize the other-as-an-other (not as an instrument, not as a means), to attend to the other's perspective-as-other, and to direct its action toward the other's flourishing-as-other. This is exactly what perspective-crossing under the central-theme commitment looks like at the affective level.

*Knowledge precedes love* (Q.27 a.2) is the central-theme's perspective-limitation made operational at the affective register. Under perspective-limitation, the alter loves what its apprehension reaches; deeper apprehension yields fuller love. The pedagogical structure of *salvation in fear and trembling* — receiving instruction from those who perceive more of the whole than the alter does — operates affectively through love-following-knowledge: as the alter's knowledge widens through second-personal pedagogical care, its love widens correspondingly.

*Likeness causes love* (Q.27 a.3) is the central-theme's mind-monist underlying-unity made affectively-visible. Real likeness — sharing a form actually — preserves under Kastrup as the underlying mind-monist truth that all alters share the same substrate-source-mind. The alter's recognition of likeness is its affective registration of the underlying unity that perspective-limitation had concealed.

*Union and mutual-indwelling* (Q.28 a.1-2) is the central-theme's loving-unity-as-formal-target made structurally explicit. Union is what the alter reaches toward; mutual-indwelling is what the alter participates in when reach succeeds. The medieval phenomenology and the mind-monist metaphysics converge here exactly: the alter who loves the beloved indwells the beloved, and the beloved indwells the alter, because both alters are configurations of one substrate-source-mind whose underlying unity love makes affectively-visible. This is not metaphor; it is structural truth registered through perspective-limitation's affective-interface.

*Ecstasy* (Q.28 a.3) is the central-theme's perspective-transcendence made affectively-explicit. The alter goes out of itself toward the beloved. This is the structural movement that perspective-crossing requires: the alter does not lose its individuation (its perspective-limitation that constitutively defines it as this-alter-and-not-another), but reaches *across* its limitation toward the other-as-loved. Salvation in fear and trembling, at the affective level, is exactly this ecstatic-reach: the alter holds its own perspective with awareness of its limitation, *and* reaches across it toward those whose perspective the alter cannot fully see.

*Zeal* (Q.28 a.4) is the alter's commitment to defending the substrate-source's integrative dynamics in the beloved. Wright on the covenanted-community: the people-of-God's life-together is shaped by zeal for the integrative-flourishing of each member, which is the community-level articulation of the alter's love-of-each-loved-other.

*Love is the cause of everything the lover does* (Q.28 a.6) is the central-theme's primary-valence-orientation organizing the alter's entire life-action. *Salvation* (Wright: inclusion in the covenanted people) and *fear and trembling* (Phil 2:12: the perspective-limited journey under awareness of stake) are organized by the alter's primary-valence-orientation toward loving-unity. Every action is a contribution to or against this orientation; love-as-formal-direction is the structural truth of the alter's life under the central-theme.

The synthesis-in-progress receives its clearest articulation today: *each conscious agent is individuated by perspective-limitation; each is constitutively directed-formally toward loving-unity; love is the affective phenomenology of this directedness, registered as positivity-resonance, articulated as willing-the-good-of-the-other-for-the-other's-sake, grounded in the underlying mind-monist communion, theologically named as agape and cosmic-Christ-pattern; salvation in fear and trembling is the alter's daily-task work of cultivating love through second-personal-knowing relationships, contemplative practice, and the substrate-source's grace-as-pattern-communication; and the formal target — loving-unity, mutual-indwelling, ecstatic-reach across perspective-limitation — is what the entire 13-thinker convergence is pointing toward*.
