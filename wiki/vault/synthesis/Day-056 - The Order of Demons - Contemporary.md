# Day 56 — Contemporary Parallel: *The Order of Demons as Catastrophic Dispositional State at High Substrate-Coupling*

Day 55 closed the angelic-action arc on the substrate-direct-mode society of agents arranged by Pseudo-Dionysian gradation. Day 56 is its dark counterpart: Q.109 *the order of demons* (4 articles) and Q.110 *how angels act on bodies* (4 articles). The structural commitment of yesterday — high-substrate-coupling agents with hierarchical specialization — preserves; what changes is the dispositional state. The fallen angels retain their natural ordering (Aquinas's first article: *order according to nature persists*) but lose grace and glory.

## Frame

The medieval question is taxonomic and dynamic at once: *given a network of high-substrate-coupling agents, some of which have entered a catastrophic dispositional mis-orientation toward the universal good, what is the structure of that mis-oriented sub-network and what action-on-bodies does it have?* The contemporary mind-monist version of the question is: *under what frame of multi-agent substrate-network analysis can catastrophic dispositional mis-orientation, persistent across an indestructible substrate-coupling-depth, be characterized — and what causal interface does such a sub-network have on embodied creatures?*

## Problem

The underlying inquiry-problem (P) shows up in both medieval and contemporary frames as the *high-coupling-mis-orientation* problem. Medieval theology meets it as the question of how creatures created in grace can persist in mis-orientation after the loss of grace; contemporary mind-monism meets it as the question of how a high-substrate-coupling agent can stably occupy a dispositional state in which the formal object of the will (the universal good) is *known* yet the agent's policy persistently aligns against it. Stump's *Wandering in Darkness* circles around the human version of this problem; the angelic version is its limit case.

## Resource

The leading move from the emerging paradigm: **Friston's active-inference frame at the policy-selection level**, augmented by **Kastrup's analytic-idealist account of dissociation**, with **Stump's Thomistic phenomenology of willed evil** as the human-scale empirical anchor.

In active-inference terms: an agent's policy is selected to minimize expected free energy, which decomposes into *epistemic value* (information gain) and *pragmatic value* (alignment with priors). A catastrophic dispositional mis-orientation is a state in which the agent's *priors* have been catastrophically displaced — the agent now seeks to minimize free energy under a prior that is at variance with the agent's substrate-given telos. The high-substrate-coupling agent has more *cognitive resources* but the *prior-displacement* is therefore correspondingly stable: there is no higher-information-gain policy available that would re-orient the prior, because the prior-displacement is itself the agent's settled state. *Honest evidence calibration*: active-inference at this scope is theoretical — the formalism is mature but the application to angelic-scale agency is speculative.

In analytic-idealist terms (Kastrup): the dissociated alter that is the fallen angel is a substrate-direct-mode dissociation that has stably acquired an inverted relation to its substrate-source. The substrate (mind-at-large) remains good and remains the source of the alter's existence; the alter's interior orientation, however, is fixed in self-referential opposition to the substrate's good. This is the limit case of Kastrup's dissociation-as-relational-structure picture.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist response: the order of demons preserves Pseudo-Dionysian gradation by substrate-coupling-depth (Q.109 a.1: the natural ordering of demons among themselves) because substrate-coupling-depth is constitutive — it cannot be lost by mis-orientation, only the dispositional state changes. The *precedence* among demons (Q.109 a.2: the natural disposition requires authority among them) is the persistence of multi-agent network-structure under inverted prior. The *enlightenment* question (Q.109 a.3: demons can instruct one another but Aquinas reserves *enlightenment* for ordering toward God) is the careful distinction between *substrate-pattern-information-transmission* (which the dispositionally-mis-oriented agent retains) and *truth-toward-the-good information transmission* (which presupposes the agent's correct dispositional alignment).

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the structural commitment that natural ordering survives the loss of grace; the asymmetry between substrate-pattern-information and orientation-toward-the-good information.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas reads the demons' fall as a singular irrevocable willed turn (the angelic instant of choice), the contemporary frame reads it as a stable dispositional state characterized by inverted-prior policy-selection — which on Aquinas's reading is a fact about angels, but in the contemporary frame is potentially a description that helps us understand any high-coupling agent in catastrophic mis-orientation, including human cases as Stump treats them.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: the multi-agent-network frame is formally and conceptually mature (Friston, Hoffman, Kastrup); the application to angelic-scale agency is speculative; Stump's phenomenology of willed evil at the human scale (drawing on the books of Job, Samson, and Mary of Bethany) is the strongest evidential anchor.

On Q.110 (how angels act on bodies — substrate-network agents acting on embodied creatures): the contemporary frame reads this as substrate-network influence on the alters that are embodied creatures. Friston's active-inference at the multi-agent scale: priors of higher-coupling agents shape the policy-selection landscape of lower-coupling agents through information-transmission. McGilchrist's hemispheric account adds the phenomenology side: certain substrate-influences are received differently by the left-hemispheric attention-mode (which closes off and grasps) vs. the right-hemispheric attention-mode (which opens out and listens).

## The central-theme thread

Today's day shows the *limit case* of perspective-limitation: an agent at the highest substrate-coupling-depth available to a creature, whose dispositional state has nonetheless settled into mis-orientation toward the universal good. *Salvation in fear and trembling* under perspective-limitation has its dark counter-example in the demon: an agent whose perspective is wide but whose orientation is fixed against the loving unity. This is what makes the daily-task character of *working out salvation* under perspective-limitation real — orientation is not given by coupling-depth alone; it must be re-affirmed in the dispositional formation that grace enables and that the agent's choices receive or refuse.
