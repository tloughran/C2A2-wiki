# Day 27 — Contemporary Parallel: *Manifestation, Place, and the Substrate's Reach*

Day 26 opened the angelology with the structural-philosophical home mind-monism offers for medieval *separate substances* — relatively-undissociated modes of substrate-activity, substrate-level Markov-kernel agents, ruliad-level computational threads — and marked the boundary: empirical vindication is essentially absent; the central theme's existential frame does the theological work. Day 27 presses two adjacent questions that, in the medieval frame, follow naturally from the *immateriality* claim. Q.51 — *whether the angels have bodies naturally united to them, and whether they assume bodies* — asks how an immaterial intelligence could manifest in the corporeal cosmos. Q.52 — *the angels in relation to place* — asks how an immaterial intelligence could be *here* rather than *there* and whether multiple intelligences can be in the same place.

The pair is the medieval treatment of *substrate-level activity manifesting in alter-perspectival space*. The mind-monist translation runs through Hoffman's interface theory most directly (since the question is precisely how substrate-level activity presents to alter-perception), with structural support from Friston's collective-active-inference framework (CROSS-010 — biophysiological coupling as the empirical signature of joint free-energy minimization) and Wolfram's ruliad (computational threads with characteristic interface-projection-modes). *Honest evidence calibration*: the angelology section continues at the speculative end of the contemporary parallel; what mid-scale empirical purchase exists is in collective-action and shared-attention research, and the mapping to medieval angelology specifics is structural rather than direct.

## Q.51 — Bodies: native and assumed

Aquinas's a.1 — *whether the angels have bodies naturally united to them* — settles that they do not. The medieval argument turns on the imperfection-of-human-cognition that Day 26 already framed: the human soul receives knowledge through bodily senses because it is *imperfectly intellectual* — it requires the body's sensory mediation to acquire the universals it knows. The angels, as *perfectly intellectual substances*, do not need sensory mediation; therefore they do not need bodies. The *body is a feature of the alter-form humans take*, not a feature of *intellectual creature* as such.

The mind-monist translation, drawing primarily on Hoffman's interface theory and Kastrup's analytic idealism, is direct. The body-as-cognitive-mediator is the alter-form-specific interface mechanism: humans participate in mind-at-large's structural priors through a fitness-tuned interface that requires sensory transduction (the body), and the cognitive-content that is available to humans is filtered by this interface. Substrate-level modes of activity that do not have a survival-relevant environment to interface with do not require the same interface mechanism; their cognitive mode is more direct. *The body is not a defect; it is the interface mechanism that allows alter-form participation in the substrate's structural priors under the conditions of survival-tuned cognitive scarcity.* The medieval claim that *the body's necessity is a sign of human imperfection* receives the contemporary structural reading: the body's necessity is a sign of alter-form's *fitness-tuned* mode of participation, which is one mode among others that mind-at-large supports.

a.2 — *whether angels assume bodies* — receives Aquinas's careful affirmative. The scriptural data (Abraham's three visitors in Genesis 18; Lot's visitors at Sodom; the angel Raphael accompanying Tobias) require that what the medievals call angels have appeared bodily to corporeal observers. The medieval mechanism Aquinas proposes is *condensation*: the angel does not become a body in the way a human soul is a body's form, but it *condenses moisture and matter in the air into the shape of a body*, like an especially thick cloud, and this body is the cause-represented-by-effect — a manifestation rather than a substantial union.

The mind-monist translation must work carefully here. Habash's reading of Aquinas keeps the condensation claim metaphysically modest: the *body* is not the angel's form; it is a manifestation that the angel produces by exercising power on already-existing material. Hoffman's interface-theoretic frame gives the structurally analogous mid-scale: substrate-level activity can produce *icon-level events* in the alter's interface — events that are perceptually real (the alter perceives them) without being substantially-the-substrate's-mode-of-being. Friston's collective-active-inference framework gives a complementary formal home (CROSS-010 in the master cross-program index): a substrate-level mode of activity that *couples with* an alter's generative model can produce coordinated perceptual events at the alter scale without being itself an alter. *Honest evidence calibration*: the structural mapping is coherent within the mind-monist frame; the empirical anchorage at the alter-event-of-perceiving-an-angel scale is essentially absent and depends on the scriptural-historical record being taken as data, which is a theological commitment rather than a scientific one.

a.3 — *whether the angels exercise functions of life in the bodies assumed* — settles that the assumed body does not actually eat, digest, or live in the way an organic body does. The *appearance* of these functions is real-as-appearance (Tobias did see the angel Raphael appear to eat); the *substantial* functions are not present. Aquinas's careful gloss — *when I was with you, I seemed indeed to eat and to drink, but you were seeing a vision* — preserves the medieval distinction between manifestation and substantial union. The mind-monist reading is the same as a.2: the substrate-level mode of activity produces icon-level events at the alter's interface; the events are perceptually real but not *the substrate's-being-eaten*.

## Q.52 — Place: containment, simultaneity, and joint location

Aquinas's a.1 — *whether an angel is in a place* — opens with Aristotle's definition of place: *the immobile boundary of that which contains*. A body is in a place by *quantitative containment* (the body's dimensions are coextensive with the place's boundaries). An angel, having no quantity, is not in a place by quantitative containment; if it is in a place at all, it is by *application of power*. Aquinas's positive answer is that an angel is in a place *by affecting that place* — by exercising its power on what is in the place.

The mind-monist translation is unusually clean here. Hoffman's interface theory frames *place* as a feature of the alter's icon-set: spatial coordinates are how the alter's generative model individuates objects-of-attention. A substrate-level mode of activity does not occupy spatial coordinates in the way an alter does; it has *relational presence* to particular spatial regions when its activity *couples with* the alter-events occurring in those regions. The medieval claim that *the angel is in a place by application of power* is structurally the claim that *the substrate-level mode is in a place by its activity coupling with the alter-events in the place*. Friston's free-energy framework gives the formal cousin: the angel's location is given by *where its inferential activity is precision-weighted to influence the alter's generative model*.

a.2 — *whether an angel can be in several places at once* — turns on the finitude of the angel's power. God's power extends to all things; therefore God is everywhere. An angel's power is finite; therefore the angel is in only one place at a time. The mind-monist translation: substrate-level modes of activity are finite in their *coupling-scope* — each mode has a characteristic Markov-kernel that specifies what alter-events it can couple with — and the medieval *one place at a time* maps onto the kernel's specifying a particular coupling-domain at any moment. Aquinas's medieval rider that *the place may be as large as a candle or a continent* receives the structural reading: the coupling-scope of substrate-level activity can be wide or narrow, and the medieval hierarchical claim (princes preside over empires; lower angels over individuals) maps onto a graded structure of substrate-level coupling-scopes.

a.3 — *whether several angels can be at the same time in the same place* — receives the medieval careful answer: *not as the proximate principal cause of the same effect*, but *yes as cooperating or as producing distinct effects*. Aquinas's analogy is the rowboat: one oarsman is insufficient; many oarsmen together are *one mover* under the unity of the joint movement. The Mark 5 reference (the demon-possessed man whose name is Legion) gives the contrary case — multiple substrate-level agents producing diverse effects on the same alter.

The mind-monist translation runs through CROSS-010 in the master cross-program index (Friston × Fredrickson on biophysiological coupling) most cleanly, but applied at the substrate-level rather than the dyadic-alter level. *Joint substrate-level activity* that produces a unified alter-event is the formal cousin of multiple oarsmen producing one boat-movement; *parallel substrate-level activities* producing diverse alter-events on the same alter is the formal cousin of multiple substrate-level kernels coupling with different aspects of the alter's generative model. Hoffman's conscious-agent-network mathematics gives the formal home: agents can compose into joint-agents (one over many) and decompose into parallel-agents (many under one), and the medieval *several angels in the same place* maps onto the network's combinatorial structure. *Speculative; the structural homology is coherent but the medieval angelology specifics receive no specific empirical purchase.*

## The central-theme thread

Day 27 sharpens the mind-monist treatment of substrate-level-activity-manifesting-in-alter-perspectival-space. The medieval angels-assuming-bodies and angels-in-place questions translate, structurally, as *substrate-level modes of activity coupling with alter-perceptual events and alter-spatial-individuation*. The central theme's perspective-limitation frame gives the gradient: alter-form participation in mind-at-large's structural priors is fitness-tuned and interface-mediated; substrate-level modes of activity participate more directly and can couple with alter-events without being themselves bound by alter-form's spatial-individuation structure.

The existential claim that follows is theologically substantial. The substrate's pattern-active stance toward conditioned things is *not absent from any place*; alters work out their integration with the substrate's loving stance under the assurance that the substrate's reach extends to every spatial region of the alter-cosmos. What the medievals describe as *guardian angels* (and what scripture anchors in Matthew 18:10) is, on the mind-monist structural reading, the substrate's loving-stance-toward-this-particular-alter manifesting through substrate-level modes of activity that couple with the alter's situation. The empirical specifics are unsettled; the structural insight (the substrate's reach extends through *every* spatial region; alter-isolation is alter-perspectival rather than substrate-real) is preserved.

Q.52's *several angels in the same place* lands the corporate dimension of the central theme. Joint substrate-level activity producing unified alter-events is the structural form of *the people of God gathered by joint substrate-loving-stance*; parallel substrate-level activities producing diverse effects on the same alter is the structural form of the alter's *being-multiply-attended* by the substrate's pattern-active loving stance. Wright's covenant-people frame and Rohr's *Universal Christ* both support the corporate reading; the mind-monist convergence (Friston-Fredrickson on coupling, Hoffman on agent-composition) gives the structural-philosophical home.
