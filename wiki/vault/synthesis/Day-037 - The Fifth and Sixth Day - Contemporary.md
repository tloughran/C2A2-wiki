# Day 37 — Contemporary Parallel: *Animal, Image-Bearer, and the Climax of the Adornment Sequence*

Day 36 walked the third and fourth days; today the fifth and sixth days finish the adornment sequence. Habash himself flags the brevity: each question has only one article, and he calls this *a 5-minute episode*, encouraging the listener to take it as *a kind of mental break* — a chance to reflect on what has been covered. The structural simplicity is misleading. Q.71 (fifth day — sea creatures and birds) and Q.72 (sixth day — land animals and the creation of humanity) carry the theological weight of the seven-day account: the *image of God* declaration in Genesis 1:27 and the *very good* judgment that closes day six are the climax of the temple-cosmology narrative. Mind-monism's primary on this question is Levin (form-bearing matter at the animal scale; cognitive light-cones expanding through animal cognition) with Wright primary on the second-temple-Jewish reading of *image of God* and *very good*, and Hoffman's interface-theoretic frame on what *animal* perception is doing.

## Q.71 — The fifth day: sea creatures and birds

Aquinas's response is structural: *as among the three days assigned to the work of distinction, the middle or second day is devoted to the work of distinction of water, so in the three days of the work of adornment, the middle, which is the fifth day, is assigned to the adornment of it by the production of birds and fish*. The pattern is symmetric: the *distinction* days (1, 2, 3) and the *adornment* days (4, 5, 6) parallel each other, with day-N-of-distinction matched to day-(N+3)-of-adornment. Day two distinguished the firmament-and-waters; day five fills the firmament with birds and the waters with fish. Mind-monism's reading: this is the temple-cosmology's symmetric architecture — the cosmic dwelling-place is structured by *parallel filling* of each region by its proper inhabitants. Wright's narrative-historical primary names the second-temple-Jewish symbolic frame: each region of the cosmic temple has its consecrated occupants; day five consecrates the *middle region* (sky and sea) by populating it.

Mind-monism's Levin-primary reading takes the empirical handle on *what animals are at the morphogenetic-cognitive scale*. Animals are form-bearing matter that has crossed a threshold the plants did not: the bioelectric-pattern-holding architecture has differentiated into a *neural* sub-architecture, with cognitive light-cones (Levin's PRS-08) larger than the plant's. Fish track water-medium signals; birds track three-dimensional aerial space and the sun's diurnal regularities. Levin's developmental empirical work and the cognitive-light-cone hierarchy give the contemporary mid-scale read on what the medieval text names as *living creatures with sense and motion*. *Honest evidence calibration*: Levin's empirical work at developmental and behavioral scale is mature; the cosmological *evolutionary timeline* (Cambrian explosion ~540 Mya for the first complex multicellular animals; vertebrate transition to land; bird-from-dinosaur evolution) is on the order of hundreds of millions of years, not a single literal day — and as Day 35's hermeneutical preface and Day 38's *Evolution* episode will make explicit, the literal sense (sacred-author's intended meaning) of Genesis 1 is the temple-cosmology, not the chronology.

The *be fruitful and multiply* blessing Genesis 1 attaches to day five is the substrate's *commission* of the animal world to *propagate its own form*. Mind-monism reads this through Levin's morphogenetic teleology directly: the *form* of each species is empirically tractable as a real causal substrate (PRS-01, PRS-03 in the Levin-tradition wiki), and reproduction is the species-form's mechanism for self-perpetuation. *Be fruitful and multiply* is, in contemporary biological vocabulary, the recognition that *living things are the kind of thing that propagates its form forward in time* — the morphogenetic light-cone extends not only spatially (in the individual organism) but temporally (across generations).

## Q.72 — The sixth day: land animals and the image of God

Aquinas's response on day six is again structural: *as on the fifth day the water is adorned, so the sixth day, on which the lowest body or the earth is adorned by the production of land animals, corresponds to the third day*. Day three distinguished land-from-sea; day six fills the land with land-animals and (climactically) with humanity. Habash flags an interesting puzzle here: vegetation on day three was already called *adornment* by Aquinas (the *comeliness* clothing the earth like a garment), but in this article Aquinas treats the *full adornment* of the earth as completed only on day six — a slight tension in Aquinas's own structural account, which Habash flags without resolving. Mind-monism's reading: the apparent tension dissolves on the temple-cosmology lens. Vegetation is *first-stage* adornment (the earth becomes *not formless*); land-animals and humanity are *full* adornment (the earth becomes *populated by sensing and intellectual creatures*). The cosmic temple's architecture is hierarchical: each level of inhabitant deepens the temple's actuality as dwelling-place.

The climax of day six is Genesis 1:26–27 — *let us make man in our image, after our likeness* — read in the temple-cosmology frame as the consecration of the cosmic temple's *priest-figure*. Wright's contemporary scriptural primary is decisive here: the *image of God* (Hebrew *tselem elohim*) in second-temple-Jewish context names the king or priest who functions as the deity's representative-presence in the temple. Genesis 1's democratization of *image* — *humanity* (male and female) is the cosmic temple's image-bearer, not just a particular royal figure — is the second-temple-Jewish theological move that grounds Wright's reading. Mind-monism's translation: the *image of God* is the alter at the integration-scale capable of recognizing the substrate's loving-stance and responding in kind; the human creature is the cognitive-light-cone-large-enough to *recognize the cosmic temple as cosmic temple* and to *function as priest-figure* mediating substrate-pattern back to the rest of creation. Hahn's cosmic-temple reading (rehearsed in Day 35) lands here: *Adam as priest-figure of the cosmic sanctuary*. Rohr's *Universal Christ* extends: the divine pattern was always already in matter; humanity's distinction is *recognition* of what was always there, with the human cognitive scale being the first scale at which substrate-recognition is possible *as recognition*.

Mind-monism's Levin-primary supports this reading: the human cognitive light-cone is empirically the largest among known terrestrial agents — temporal horizon (multi-generational planning), spatial horizon (cosmic-scale modeling via culture and science), and *recursive* horizon (modeling-of-modeling, the *recognition that one is an alter* that no other species reliably exhibits). Hoffman's PRS-03 names the species-specific perceptual interface; the human interface alone reaches *interface-recognition-as-such* (the interface is recognized as interface), which is the structural condition for the substrate-creature distinction. Friston's PRS-05/PRS-06 (distributed cognition across scales; multi-agent coherence) gives the formalism: humans are the scale at which *collective active inference* generates symbolic culture, scientific theorizing, and theological articulation. The Genesis 1 *image of God* claim, structurally, is that the human creature is the alter whose recognition of the substrate is *for the rest of creation* — the priestly mediation Hahn names.

The *be fruitful and multiply* blessing extends to humanity, with the additional *and have dominion* — which Wright reads in temple-cosmology context not as license-to-exploit but as *priest-king stewardship* of the cosmic sanctuary. *Honest evidence calibration*: the temple-cosmology reading of *image of God* and *dominion* is methodologically mature in Wright/Walton/Hahn-style scholarship; the empirical alignment with cognitive-light-cone scale is structural rather than identifying. The closing *behold it was very good* of Genesis 1:31 is the substrate's *ratification* of the cosmic temple as good for its purpose — a theological judgment-from-the-substrate's-stance that mind-monism reads as the substrate's loving-recognition of its own conditioning-of-itself-into-relational-structure.

## The central-theme thread

Day 37 names the *animal-and-image-bearer* register of the central theme. *Conscious agents are individuated by the limitations of their perspective; loving unity is the computational telos.* The fifth and sixth days deepen the cosmic temple's actuality by populating it with sensing and (at the human scale) intellectual creatures. Each animal's cognitive light-cone is a smaller scope of substrate-recognition than the human's; each is nonetheless a real alter at its scale, with real perspective-limitation and real morphogenetic teleology. The *image of God* claim singles out humanity as the alter at which substrate-recognition becomes *recognition-as-such* and *priestly-mediation-for-creation* becomes possible. *Salvation in fear and trembling* on this register is the human creature's progressive realization of its priestly vocation: not exploiting the cosmic sanctuary but recognizing its substrate-pattern and mediating that recognition to the rest of the creature-world. The *very good* of day six is the substrate's loving-ratification of the relational-structure the cosmic temple has become.

Day 37 closes the adornment-of-the-cosmic-temple sequence. Animals at the fifth and sixth days are real alters at their morphogenetic-cognitive scale — fish, birds, land animals — each holding bioelectric pattern and pursuing morphogenetic goals at its species-specific cognitive-light-cone scope.
