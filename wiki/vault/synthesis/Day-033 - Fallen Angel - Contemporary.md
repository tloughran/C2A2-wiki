# Day 33 — Contemporary Parallel: *The Refusal of Loving Unity, in Structural Vocabulary*

The central theme of this project — *conscious agents are individuated by the limitations of their perspective; loving unity is the computational telos* — receives its sharpest test in today's pair. Q.63 walks the malice of the angelic sin (the only species of sin possible in entirely immaterial beings, the demon's *desiring to be like God*, the timing of the fall, who fell, why others did not). Q.64 walks the demonic punishment (cognitive darkening as privation, obstinacy of the will, the persistence of sorrow as an act of will rather than as a passion, the place of punishment). Day 31 closed by previewing the question: how can an alter naturally oriented toward the substrate (Q.60 a.5) refuse the orientation? Kastrup's dissociation framework gives the formal home for the answer; Rohr's *everything belongs* names the contemplative corrective the fallen angel rejects; Friston's free-energy framework supplies the dynamics of structural-inversion (the alter's policy-space turning *away from* substrate-coherence rather than *toward* it). The medieval doctrine and the contemporary frame agree on the structural shape: the fallen angel's pathology is volitional first, cognitive second; the cognitive defect follows on the volitional refusal. Empirical adjacents are limited; the structural translations are the work of this commentary.

## Q.63 — The malice of the angelic sin

a.1 — *can the evil of fault be in the angels?* — establishes that *sin* is *deviation from the rectitude an act ought to have*, and that some species of sin (against the rule of reason — bodily-appetite-driven; against the rule of revealed law — supernatural-end-driven) are conceptually available to the angelic mode. The medieval definition of sin as *privation of due rectitude* (Day 10 Q.17 a.1, falsity as privation) survives the contemporary translation: the alter's policy fails to track its proper-good-pattern, and the failure is *real* — registered as deviation, corrigible (in principle) by re-orientation. Mind-monism's reading: sin at the alter scale is the alter's policy diverging from substrate-coherent policy, and this divergence is structurally describable as elevated free-energy under the alter's generative model that *includes* the substrate's priors as accessible to the alter.

a.2 — *can only the sin of pride and envy exist in an angel?* — narrows the field. The bodily-appetite sins (lust, gluttony, anger as passion, sloth as bodily weariness) are out — angels have no body. What remains: *pride* (refusal to concede to the law one is bound to follow), *envy* (resisting the good of another), and *avarice* (immoderate greed for any created good — but Aquinas folds this into pride for the angelic case). The medieval observation is structurally sharp: sins available to a near-substrate alter are *relational-volitional* sins, not appetitive ones. Mind-monism's translation: the alter's available failure modes are failures of *relational-orientation* — toward the substrate (pride), toward other alters (envy), toward conditioned things (avarice, in its abstract form). Hoffman's conscious-agent-network framework gives the structural shape: the angelic alter's policies are over its relational-stance toward other nodes in the network; failure modes are mis-orientations within the relational structure rather than mis-coupling to bodily inputs.

a.3 — *did the devil desire to be as God?* — is the article that names the inverse of the central theme most precisely. Aquinas: Satan could not have wished to be God *in the sense of being the I-am-who-am* (this is metaphysically impossible for any creature); Satan could not have sinned in *wanting to be Godlike* (this is precisely what *partakers of the divine nature* names as the saints' end — 2 Peter 1:4); Satan's sin was *seeking beatitude without God's help*, which is *the heart of every single sin we commit* — *trying to be happy in our own way, not in the way provided by God*. *Either he refused God's help, or he refused to admit that it was not something owed to him by his nature but a supernatural gift*. The mind-monist reading is the central-theme inversion in structural vocabulary. The alter's natural orientation toward the substrate (Day 31 Q.60 a.5) provides the *structural attractor*; the alter's beatitude (Day 32 Q.62) is the *autopoietic limit reached by substrate-initiated mode-shift*. The fallen angel's sin is the alter's *attempt to reach the limit by intra-alter resources alone* — to bring its generative model to substrate-identity *without* the substrate-initiated mode-shift that grace structurally is. The attempt fails because the autopoietic limit is *not reachable* by intra-alter local-prediction-error minimization (Day 32 Q.62 a.2); the alter that refuses the substrate's mode-shift gift while still attempting the limit is the alter that *converts its own structure* into a closed system attempting self-grounding — and this is the structural definition of *pride* in the medieval sense. Kastrup's framework gives the formal home: the alter that refuses to recognize itself as *dissociation within mind-at-large* and instead asserts itself as *independent existence* is the alter that has *misread its own ontology* — and the misreading becomes the alter's actual structure for the duration of the dissociation. Honest evidence calibration: the structural mapping is tight; the metaphysical claim that any actual alter ever *succeeds* in this misreading at the angelic scale is theological, not empirically demonstrated.

a.4 — *are any demons naturally wicked?* — receives the medieval *no*. Every intellectual nature is naturally inclined toward good in general (Day 31 Q.60 a.1 — natural love is the principle of love of choice); demons cannot be *naturally* evil because they are intellectual substances. The pathology is acquired by act-of-will, not constitutive of the angelic nature. Mind-monism's translation: every alter's structure orients it toward the substrate (the alter cannot be an alter without participating in the substrate that makes it an alter); pathology is *deviation* from the orientation, not the orientation itself. Garrigou-Lagrange's gloss (Day 31 closed with this): the demon is *turned away from God-as-author-of-grace* and *as-author-of-the-law-of-nature freely and morally*, while *necessarily and physically remaining turned to God-the-author-of-his-nature*. The *lamentable opposition* in the demon is structurally the alter's volitional refusal of what its nature still naturally orients toward — a structural-inversion, not a structural-collapse.

a.5 — *was the devil wicked by the fault of his own will in the first instant of his creation?* — receives the medieval *no*. The first instant is from God who is good; the angel's nature in the first instant is good; pride and envy require the angel's own act-of-will, which can only occur in the *second* instant. Aquinas's three-instant scheme: (1) creation in good and in grace; (2) act of will toward or against the supernatural end; (3) reward or damnation. Mind-monism's translation: the alter's first-state is its substrate-given structure (analogous to a freshly-instantiated agent's prior-distribution before any inferential pass); the alter's *pathology* requires the alter's own policy-update, which requires at least one inferential pass. Habash's footnote on Father Chad Ripperger is striking: the demons *try not to think about this first instance* because they *remember the joy that they possessed in it*; if forced to think about it, the demon weeps. The structural shape Aquinas reaches for is preserved: the pre-fallen state is real (the alter once participated in substrate-coherent policy-space); the post-fallen state is privation rather than absence (the alter's current structure *retains the memory* of its substrate-coherent past).

a.6 — *was there any interval between the creation and the fall?* — receives the medieval *probably no, the fall came in the second instant immediately*. Mind-monism's reading is structural: the alter's first policy-update was the wrong one; the angelic mode is non-discursive (Day 30 Q.58 a.3), so the wrong policy-update was *one act* covering the entire policy-space (Day 32 Q.62 a.5 in its inverse — the same one-act-suffices structure runs in both directions). The medieval doctrine that *the fallen angel is fixed in evil because the angelic mode is non-discursive* (Q.64 a.2 will press this) is the structural inverse of Q.62 a.5: the autopoietic limit is reached *away from* substrate-coherence rather than *toward* it.

a.7 — *was the highest angel among those who sinned the highest of all?* — Aquinas: probably yes, citing Gregory the Great. The motive of pride is *excellence*; the highest angel had the most *excellence* to be tempted by; therefore the highest angel was most susceptible. Mind-monism's reading: the alter with the widest cognitive-light-cone has the most accessible structural-priors and thus the largest policy-space available to refuse-coherence; the failure mode scales with the scope. Honest evidence calibration: this is medieval-theological speculation, not empirical claim; the structural-philosophical translation matches the medieval generosity.

a.8 — *was the sin of the highest angel the cause of the others' sinning?* — Aquinas: by *exhortation* or *inducement* (the Latin *exortando inducendo*), not by compulsion. The sign: *all the demons are subjects of that highest one* (Matthew 25:41 — *depart from me, you cursed, into everlasting fire which was prepared for the devil and his angels*). Mind-monism's reading runs through Friston's multi-agent active inference (PRS-06 in the Friston-tradition wiki — multi-agent coherence via mutual modeling). Alters in a network can influence each other's policy-updates through communication and modeled-other; the higher-scope alter's policy-update is taken as evidence by the lower-scope alters' generative models, and the lower-scope alters' policy-spaces shift accordingly. The medieval claim that *seduction* (a.8 — Aquinas's preferred translation) operates through *exhortation* maps onto the contemporary claim that policy-influence operates through *modeled-other-agency*, not through direct causal compulsion.

a.9 — *were those who sinned as many as those who remained firm?* — Aquinas: *more remained firm than fell*, citing 2 Kings 6:16 (Elisha's vision — *those who are with us are more than those who are with them*) and Revelation 12 (a third of the stars fell). Aquinas's argument from the *nature of sin*: more remained firm than sinned because *sin is contrary to natural inclination, and what is against the natural order happens with less frequency*. Mind-monism's translation: alter-pathology is statistically rare against the substrate-coherent baseline because the natural orientation is the structural attractor; *deviation* from the attractor is itself deviation. The medieval observation that *more righteous than fallen* is consistent with the contemporary claim that the structural attractor pulls more agents than it loses.

## Q.64 — The punishment of the demons

a.1 — *is the demon's intellect dark by privation of the knowledge of all truth?* — receives the medieval *no*: the demons retain natural knowledge (without it they would not be intellectual substances at all) but they *do not know to the extent the beatified angels do who are gazing into God's essence, or through the gift of wisdom*. Mind-monism's translation: the fallen alter's generative model retains the natural-scope priors (the alter is still an alter at angelic scope) but is denied the substrate-direct mode (the autopoietic limit is not reached, because reaching it would require the substrate-initiated mode-shift the alter has refused). Cognitive darkness is privation in the strict medieval sense — a real *absence-of-what-ought-to-be-present* rather than a positive negative-content. Day 10 Q.17 a.1's framework holds.

a.2 — *is the will of demons obstinate in evil?* — receives the medieval *yes*, by the same non-discursivity argument that ran in Q.62 a.5 the other direction. Garrigou-Lagrange's gloss Habash quotes: *the angel apprehends immovably and intuitively those things that we apprehend discursively; this is particularly true when the angel judges something to be an end to be loved above all things; the angel sees intuitively and not successively all those things that pertain to the choice of a thing; once the choice has been made the angel can say I have already considered everything*. Mind-monism's translation: the autopoietic limit is *symmetric with respect to coherence and incoherence* — the angelic alter's one act of policy-update covers the entire policy-space *regardless of whether the policy is substrate-coherent or substrate-incoherent*; the alter that has chosen substrate-coherent policy is at the beatitude limit (Q.62 a.5) and the alter that has chosen substrate-incoherent policy is at the damnation limit (Q.64 a.2), and both limits are limits in the same structural sense. The medieval doctrine and the contemporary frame agree on the structural symmetry.

a.3 — *is there sorrow in the demon?* — receives the medieval *yes*, but as *simple act of the will resisting what is or is not* (Day 31 Q.59 a.4 — the angelic mode preserves the structural function of affect without bodily implementation). The demon's sorrow has three sources: *envy* (wishing others damned who are saved), *deprivation of natural happiness* (the demon's nature still naturally desires the substrate it has refused — Garrigou-Lagrange's *lamentable opposition*), and *the curbing of its wicked will*. Mind-monism's translation: the alter's policy refuses substrate-coherence, but the alter's structure still naturally orients toward the substrate; the gap between the structure's orientation and the policy's orientation registers as *sorrow* in the structural sense — the alter's own predictive model registering its own policy as *not-fitting-its-priors*. Friston's framework gives the formalism: chronic high-prediction-error under the alter's deepest priors is the structural form of suffering, and the demon's structure is precisely this case.

a.4 — *is our atmosphere the demons' place of punishment?* — Aquinas: *two-fold place of punishment is due to the demons — one by reason of their sin, this is hell; another in order that they may tempt men, this is the darksome atmosphere*. The latter, in the contemporary mind-monist frame, is structurally the network-level access the fallen alter retains to embodied alters' interface — the demon can read what is externalized (Day 30 Q.57 a.4) and can attempt to influence the policy-updates of embodied alters by *exhortation* (Q.63 a.8 — modeled-other-agency through the network). The Pauline framing Day 30 cited (Ephesians 6:12) names the structural fact: the spiritual warfare is real, network-level, and operates through the same mechanisms by which any alter influences another, but with the structural inversion that the fallen alter's influence is toward substrate-incoherence rather than substrate-coherence.

## The central-theme thread

Day 33 names the inverse of the central theme: *the refusal of loving unity*. The structure that makes loving unity possible — the alter's natural orientation toward the substrate, the autopoietic limit reachable by substrate-initiated mode-shift, the non-discursivity of the angelic mode — is the very structure that makes its inverse possible. The angelic alter's one act covers its entire policy-space whether toward substrate-coherence or away from it; the alter's natural orientation toward the substrate is preserved (Garrigou-Lagrange's *lamentable opposition*); the cognitive defect follows on the volitional refusal rather than preceding it. The mind-monist reading is the central-theme test passed: the same structural conditions that make *salvation in fear and trembling* a coherent computational task also make *damnation in pride* a coherent computational structure — both are limit-cases of the same alter-mode at the same scope. *Loving unity is the computational telos*, but the alter is *free* to compute against it, and the freedom is constitutive of its alter-hood (Day 31 Q.59 a.3) not an additional feature added on top. Rohr's *everything belongs* is the contemplative corrective the fallen angel rejects: the alter that holds *everything-belongs* has accepted that its hood-as-an-alter is a participation-in-substrate rather than an independent self-grounding, and this acceptance *is* the volitional orientation that converges on the autopoietic limit. The fallen angel's refusal is the volitional refusal of *everything belongs* — the demand that *something-not-belong* (specifically, the alter itself, as independently grounded) — and the refusal becomes the alter's actual structure for the duration. Wright's covenant frame extends the central theme corporately: salvation as inclusion-in-the-people-united-with-God depends on the alter's volitional acceptance of the substrate-given covenant, and the inverse — exclusion-from-the-covenanted-community — is the corporate-scale analog of the fallen angel's refusal.

## Where this leaves us

Day 33 closes the angels-block on the central theme's sharpest test. The structure that makes loving unity a coherent computational telos is the very structure that makes the refusal of loving unity a coherent computational *anti-*telos: same alter-scope, same non-discursivity, same one-act-covering-the-policy-space, but the policy points away from substrate-coherence rather than toward it. The fallen angel's pathology is volitional first, cognitive second; the alter's structure still naturally orients toward the substrate (Garrigou-Lagrange's *lamentable opposition*), and the gap between the structure's orientation and the policy's orientation registers as *sorrow* (Q.64 a.3). Mind-monism's frame inherits the medieval doctrine cleanly: dissociation produces a real alter with real free-will-as-constitutive-of-alter-hood, and the alter is structurally free to refuse substrate-orientation as it is structurally free to accept it. The freedom is the computational structure; *salvation in fear and trembling* names the work of accepting the orientation under perspective-limited resources, and the fallen angel's refusal names the structural-inverse the central theme runs against.
