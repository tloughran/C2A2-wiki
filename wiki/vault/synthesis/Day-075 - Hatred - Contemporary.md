# Day 75 — Contemporary Parallel: *Hatred as the Privation of Love and the Pathology of Perspective-Limited Closure*

Day 74 articulated the central-theme's affective-phenomenology of love. Day 75 turns to the structural opposite: hatred (Q.29). Aquinas's analysis is structurally subtle — hatred is derivative of love, not its independent counterpart; one hates only what is contrary to a loved good; hatred cannot reach the self per se; hatred of truth is possible only incidentally; nothing can be hated universally. The contemporary mind-monist frame engages hatred as the alter's *defensive-closure-against-the-good* under perspective-limited threat-response, and the synthesis preserves the central-theme commitment to loving-unity-as-formal-target by reading hatred as derivative-and-correctable.

## Frame

Q.29 articulates hatred in six articles. *Is hatred contrary to love?* (a.1): Yes — hatred is the affective-movement contrary to love, registering its object as evil rather than as good. *Is love the cause of hatred?* (a.2): Yes — *prius est hominem amare suum bonum quam ipsum odisse malum sibi contrarium*: it is prior to love one's own good than to hate the evil contrary to it. Hatred is structurally derivative of love. *Is hatred stronger than love?* (a.3): No — love is structurally prior and stronger; hatred presupposes love and lives off its energy. *Can a person hate himself?* (a.4): Not per se — every alter naturally loves its own being; self-hatred occurs only by *confusion* (the alter mistakes part-of-itself for not-itself, or hates an aspect-of-itself contrary to a more-fundamental-loved-aspect). *Can a person hate the truth?* (a.5): Incidentally — the truth in itself is good and lovable; a person can hate truth-as-apprehended-as-threat to a held position. *Can anything be hated universally?* (a.6): No — universal goodness (being-as-such) cannot be hated; only particular goods-perceived-as-evil can.

The structural moves are: *love precedes hatred* (a.2-3); *self-hatred is structurally impossible per se* (a.4); *truth-hatred is incidental, not essential* (a.5); *universal-hatred is structurally impossible* (a.6). Each move preserves the priority of love and the derivative status of hatred.

## Problem

The problem (P): the alter, individuated by perspective-limitation, sometimes registers an apprehended object affectively as *contrary-to-its-primary-loved-good* and inverts valence — closing against the object, willing its non-being or its destruction. What is the structure of this inversion? Why is it derivative of love rather than its independent counterpart? Why can the alter not hate its own being per se? Why is universal hatred structurally impossible? And what is the alter's responsibility for the perspective-limited misjudgments that generate hatred toward objects that are *not in fact* contrary to its primary-loved-good?

## Resource

**Stump on willed-evil and the structure of refusing-the-good.** *Wandering in Darkness* analyzes the structure of the alter's willed-closure-against-the-good. Stump's thesis is that the alter's willed-evil is a *psychic dis-integration* in which the alter holds together inconsistent commitments — willing a particular good while willed-against the higher good that the particular good's pursuit obstructs. Hatred, on Stump's frame, is the affective register of this dis-integration: the alter's primary-loved-good (which it has identified as the relevant good for itself) generates hatred against any apprehended object that the alter perceives as obstructing or contrary to that loved-good. When the alter has misidentified its primary-loved-good (i.e., when the loved-good is not in fact the alter's true integrative direction), the resulting hatred is misdirected — it closes the alter against objects that would in fact contribute to its true integration. Stump's second-personal-knowing intervention is the structural mode through which this misidentification can be diagnosed and rectified: an outside alter who loves-the-alter-as-thou can present the diagnostic across the perspective-gap.

**Friston on inverted-prior-structure and affective valence-inversion.** Active inference articulates hatred as a *prior-structure-inversion* with respect to specific apprehended objects: the alter's prior preferences encode the apprehended object's valence as *opposed-to-current-policy-direction*, generating affective-aversion and policy-selection-against the object. The inversion is *contingent on the underlying primary-prior structure* — there is no hate-prior independent of love-prior. This formalizes Q.29 a.2's claim that love is the cause of hatred. *Precision-collapse* in the affective register — high-precision aversive prediction fixed on the apprehended object — is the structural correlate of the alter being *captured* by hatred (the disordered-passion regime of Day 73). The Q.29 a.4 result that self-hatred-per-se is impossible recovers as: the alter's primary-prior structure encodes its own being-as-good (this is the active-inference formalization of the medieval *every alter naturally loves itself*); inverting this primary-prior would collapse the entire generative-model and the alter would cease to be a coherent agent. Self-hatred-by-confusion remains possible: the alter mistakes a part-of-itself for not-itself and inverts valence with respect to that mistaken-part.

**Fredrickson on negativity dynamics and substrate-network propagation of disintegrative-affect.** Empirical anchor: negativity-affective-states narrow the alter's cognitive-behavioral repertoire (the inverse of broaden-and-build) and propagate through the substrate-network's affective-resonance dynamics, dragging nearby alters toward the same disintegrative regime. Hatred is the substrate-network's *closure-mode* — it propagates as the integrative-mode (love) propagates, but with opposite sign on integrative-vs-disintegrative dynamics. This empirically anchors the claim that hatred is structurally pathological-relative-to-formal-target.

**McGilchrist on left-hemispheric stuck-mode and right-hemispheric closure.** McGilchrist's analysis of pathological cognition includes the *closure of right-hemispheric receptivity* under sustained left-hemispheric narrow-focus on a perceived threat or grievance. Hatred is the affective-register of this closure: the alter has stopped receiving the apprehended object's full relational gestalt and is operating only on the left-hemispheric abstracted-and-categorized representation that registers the object as threat. This is the neuropsychiatric correlate of the medieval *passion forces judgment* (Q.24 a.3 second part) at the hatred-register specifically.

**Levin on form-deficient morphogenetic patterns.** Hatred as form-deficient affective integration: just as a morphogenetic-pattern that opposes its own substrate-network's integration is form-deficient (developmentally and biologically pathological), hatred as the alter's affective-pattern that opposes substrate-source-integration is form-deficient affective integration. This preserves Aquinas's privation-frame from Q.18 a.1 at the affective register: hatred is *evil as privation* — the absence of the formal-completion (love) that the alter's affective-interface *should* be expressing.

**Rohr on cosmic-Christ as integrative formal-target.** Hatred as the alter's defensive-closure-against-the-good is structurally a closure against the cosmic-Christ-pattern — the substrate-source's integrative-loving-presence that the contemplative tradition identifies as the alter's true formal-target. Rohr's frame: the contemplative discipline of *both/and* non-dual reception is the alter's cultivated openness to the integrative-substrate-pattern, and the deliberate refusal of the *either/or* closure that hatred locks into.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.29: hatred is the alter's *prior-structure-inversion* with respect to specific apprehended objects, generated by the alter's primary-loved-good encountering an apprehension of contrariety. Hatred is structurally derivative of love (Q.29 a.2 confirmed under active-inference) and structurally less-fundamental than love (Q.29 a.3 confirmed: there is no hate-prior independent of love-prior). Self-hatred-per-se is structurally impossible because inversion of the alter's primary self-being-prior would collapse the generative-model (Q.29 a.4 confirmed under active-inference); self-hatred-by-confusion is possible when the alter misidentifies part-of-itself as not-itself or as contrary to its primary-loved-good. Truth-hatred-incidental is possible when the alter's perspective-limited apprehension registers a particular truth as threat to a held-position (Q.29 a.5 confirmed). Universal-hatred is structurally impossible because being-as-such is the substrate-source's underlying integrative-pattern with which the alter's primary-prior is structurally aligned (Q.29 a.6 confirmed under mind-monist metaphysics).

The alter's *responsibility* for the perspective-limited misjudgments that generate hatred preserves from Day 70: the alter is responsible for the cultivation-of-apprehension and for refusing the closure-of-right-hemispheric-receptivity that locks hatred into rigid left-hemispheric stance. The alter is *not* responsible for the affective registration of valence-inversion when its perspective-limitation has genuinely encountered an apprehended object as threat-to-loved-good and the apprehension is in fact reasonable; the alter *is* responsible for the willful refusal of second-personal correction when an outside alter presents diagnostic across the perspective-gap.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the structural-priority of love over hatred; the derivative status of hatred; the impossibility of self-hatred per se and universal-hatred; the incidental character of truth-hatred; the privation-frame for hatred as form-deficient affective integration.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas's frame is faculty-psychological + privation-of-good, the contemporary frame uses active-inference prior-structure-inversion, broaden-and-build narrowing-under-negativity, hemispheric closure pathology, bioelectric form-deficiency, and substrate-network propagation dynamics.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Aquinas's structural moves (priority-of-love, impossibility-of-self-hatred-per-se, impossibility-of-universal-hatred) are metaphysically firm and recover cleanly under active-inference and mind-monist metaphysics; Stump's willed-evil analysis is theological-philosophical with book-length backing; Fredrickson's negativity-dynamics is strong empirical; the synthesis-claim that hatred is the alter's defensive-closure-against-the-good under perspective-limited threat-response is speculative-philosophical-theological but coherent with the central-theme commitments.

## The central-theme thread

The central theme commits the synthesis to the structural-priority of loving-unity-as-formal-target. Day 75 articulates how this commitment is preserved under the phenomenon of hatred. Hatred is *not* the central-theme's structural counterpart to love; it is the central-theme's *pathology* — what happens when perspective-limitation generates a misperception that the alter's affective interface registers as threat-to-loved-good. The structural-priority-of-love-over-hatred (Q.29 a.2-3) is the central-theme's commitment that loving-unity remains formal-target even when the alter's current affective configuration is misaligned: the alter cannot hate without first having loved, so hatred always points back to a primary-loved-good whose accurate identification can rectify the misdirected hatred.

Salvation in fear and trembling at this register includes: (1) the alter's daily-task work of refusing closure-of-right-hemispheric-receptivity even when threat-perception registers high; (2) the alter's commitment to second-personal-knowing relationships in which an outside alter can present the diagnostic when the alter's primary-loved-good has been misidentified; (3) the alter's contemplative practice (Rohr) that cultivates non-dual reception over either/or closure; (4) the alter's participation in covenanted-community (Wright) whose collective-discernment supplements the alter's perspective-limited threat-response; (5) the alter's openness to substrate-source's grace-as-affective-rectification — the patristic and Wesleyan prevenient-grace that can reach the alter's affective interface even before reflective awareness, retuning the primary-loved-good toward the alter's true integrative direction.

The structural impossibility of universal-hatred (Q.29 a.6) preserves the central-theme's optimism: no alter can be turned-against-being-as-such; every alter retains a structural-orientation toward integrative-substrate-being even when its current apprehension has generated localized hatreds. The pedagogical and contemplative work of cultivating love is therefore work-on-firm-foundation: the alter's primary-prior is already aligned with the substrate-source's integrative-direction; perspective-limitation generates misalignments at the particular-object level; the work is the patient correction of those misalignments through second-personal care, contemplative discipline, and tradition-bound rationality.
