# Day 43 — Contemporary Parallel: *Free Choice Under FEP, the Phantasm as Icon*

Day 42 closed with the will as the slow, universal-good-targeting policy of the same inferential loop the appetites run on. Day 43 makes the pivot of the whole *De anima* arc: Q.83 names *libero arbitrio* — *free will*, or, as Habash flags, more properly *free choice* — and asks whether and how the human agent is genuinely undetermined in its selection of means; Q.84 then opens the epistemological proper, asking *how does the soul, united to the body, understand corporeal things?* This second question is the one that turns Aquinas's whole psychology into something a contemporary mind-monist can inhabit, because *phantasm* — the medieval term for the imagined image from which the active intellect abstracts the essence — is structurally what Hoffman's interface theory names *icon*. Day 43 is the day the medieval and the contemporary epistemologies recognize each other.

## Q.83 — Free choice (*libero arbitrio*)

Habash flags at the article's opening that *libero arbitrio* in Aquinas's Latin is more accurately *free judgment* or *free choice* than *free will*; the will's *necessity* on the ultimate end (Day 42 Q.82 a.1) is not a deficit of freedom but a structural feature of being-an-agent. What Q.83 asks is whether the *judgment about means* is genuinely undetermined.

a.1 names the contemporary translation almost directly. *Man's judgment in the case of some particular act is not from a natural instinct but from some act of comparison in the reason; therefore he acts from free judgment and retains the power of being inclined to various things.* The argument turns on the claim that the *intellect* is *immaterial*, and what is moved by the immaterial cannot be necessitated by material law — the will follows the judgment of the intellect, which is itself not determined by the antecedent material configuration of the brain. Aquinas closes the argument by invoking universal pre-theoretical evidence: *counsels, exhortations, commands, prohibitions, rewards, and punishments would be in vain* otherwise.

The mind-monist primary on this article is Friston, with care. Active inference gives the formal structure of *free choice under precision-weighted uncertainty* that is neither classical-libertarian nor reductive-deterministic. PRS-02 in the Friston-tradition wiki on action-selection-as-inference frames the agent's policy distribution as a posterior over trajectories, computed under expected free energy. Where the policy distribution is *peaked* on a single trajectory, action is determinate-from-the-inside (the agent acts as it computes it should act, given its priors, posterior, and target distribution); where the policy distribution is *flat*, the agent's selection is *under-determined* and the choice is genuine. *Honest evidence calibration*: this is the formal-compatibilist reading of free choice — neither libertarian metaphysics nor eliminativist denial. The agent's own computation is what selects; that computation is real causation; the computation is genuinely under-determined when the policy distribution is flat. Whether that suffices for the *libero arbitrio* sense Aquinas wants is a philosophical question the formalism does not settle by itself.

Levin extends the picture. The Karpathy-wiki Levin-tradition node frames *agency-at-every-scale* as the empirical claim that goal-directedness, decision-making, and contextual-flexibility are *real* properties of biological systems from cells through organisms through collectives. PRS-03 on morphogenesis-as-bioelectric-goal-seeking is the developmental anchor; the broader cognitive-light-cones program is the cognitive anchor. Free choice in the libero-arbitrio sense is, in Levin's frame, the agent's capacity to compute, given its limited resources and perspective, a trajectory that is neither random nor mechanistically forced — *its own* trajectory in the sense Aquinas names. CROSS-006 and CROSS-007 in the master cross-program index (Levin × Friston bridges) name the formal-and-empirical convergence: morphogenetic goal-seeking is FEP at the developmental scale; cognitive flexibility is FEP at the behavioral scale; both are real, both involve the agent's computation, and neither reduces to bottom-up necessitation.

McGilchrist sharpens *judgment*. Right-hemisphere whole-grasping enables the agent to perceive the *context* in which a means-end calculation is to be made — the *situation* that distinguishes one means from another not by abstract calculation alone but by gestalt fit with the larger pattern. The medieval *act of comparison in the reason* is not bare propositional inference; it is the integration of right-mode contextual perception into left-mode articulate decision. CROSS-013 in the master cross-program index (Hoffman × McGilchrist split-brain) anchors the bi-hemispheric individuation empirically.

Aquinas's reconciliation of free choice with *God's universal causality* is the last move of a.1. *God moves voluntary causes without depriving their actions of being voluntary; he is rather the cause of this very thing in them, for he operates in each thing according to its own nature.* Mind-monism's translation: the substrate's pattern instantiates itself *as* the alter's structure; the alter's free choice is the substrate's pattern operating *in the way the alter's nature is*, not against it or from outside it. The substrate does not necessitate the alter's choice from outside; the alter *is* a partial expression of the substrate, and the alter's computation is the substrate's pattern at the alter's scope. Kastrup gives the analytic-idealist version: there is no *outside* of mind-at-large to do the necessitating; the alter's freedom is internal to the substrate's own self-expressing. Wright's scriptural backing — *the choice itself is in us but presupposes the help of God* — survives without strain.

aa.2–4 close Q.83 quickly. Free will is *not a distinct power* from the will (a.4); it is *one* power — the intellectual appetite — in its mode of selecting *means to the necessarily-willed end*. *Free judgment* is the better translation precisely because the will *as such* is not free with respect to the end (it necessarily wills happiness); it is free *in the choice of means*. The contemporary structure: the agent's policy distribution over trajectories is constrained by the target distribution (necessitation on the end) and under-determined within the means-space (freedom in the choice).

## Q.84 — How the soul knows bodies

Q.84 opens the epistemology proper. Eight articles walk the agent through the architecture of cognition. The mind-monist primary on this question is Hoffman, decisively, with Hawkins on the cortical mechanism and Friston on the formal dynamics.

a.1 — *the soul knows bodies through knowledge that is immaterial, universal, and necessary* — names the medieval gap. The thing-out-there is particular and material; the knowing-of-it is universal and immaterial. How does the agent get from the particular sensible to the universal essence? a.2's answer rules out the wrong route: the soul does *not* understand corporeal things through its own essence — only God's essence comprises all things. a.3 rules out another wrong route: the soul does *not* know all things through innate species — Aristotle's *blank slate* is preserved. a.4 rules out the Platonic route: we do not know things by access to immaterial existing-in-themselves Forms — *if this were true we should not need the senses*. a.5 walks the careful both-and: we do not see God's essence and so do not know things in him directly, but we do know things through participation in the *uncreated light* — the active intellect itself is *a participated likeness of that uncreated light*.

The mind-monist translation runs through Hoffman's interface theory directly. PRS-01 in the Hoffman-tradition wiki frames *perception-as-fitness-tuned-interface*: the agent's sensory-perceptual apparatus does not deliver veridical access to the underlying reality but a species-specific *icon* that supports adaptive action. The icons are real *as icons* — they have causal effect on the agent's behavior — but they are not the underlying reality. CROSS-014 (Hoffman × Kastrup deep convergence) and CROSS-039 (Friston × Hoffman QIT bridge) place this inside the consciousness-first paradigm: the icon is the alter's perspective-limited access to the substrate; the substrate is networks of conscious agents; the icon is the structural form perspective-limitation takes in cognition.

a.6 makes the claim Day 43 turns on. *On the part of the phantasms intellectual knowledge is caused by the senses; but since the phantasms cannot of themselves affect the passive intellect and require to be made actually intelligible by the active intellect, it cannot be said that sensible knowledge is the total and perfect cause of intellectual knowledge but rather that it is in a way the material cause.* The medieval *phantasm* — Aquinas's term for the imagined image, the mental representation, the sensible particular as it figures in the agent's cognition — is structurally Hoffman's *icon*. The agent does not have direct access to the dog-out-there; the agent has the *phantasm* of the dog (the imagined image, the mental word) and from *that* the active intellect abstracts the essence. The medieval cognitive architecture and Hoffman's interface theory are working the same problem: knowing operates on representations, and the representations are not the underlying things.

The contemporary refinement: Hawkins's HTM (PRS-02 in the Hawkins-tradition wiki) gives the cortical mechanism for the *abstraction* operation. The cortical hierarchy compresses sensory streams into invariant representations — *what dogs are* extracted from the multitude of particular dog-experiences — and that invariant abstraction is the contemporary cognitive instantiation of *agent-intellect-abstracting-essence-from-phantasm*. CROSS-011 (Hawkins × Friston) places it inside the active-inference framework: the abstracted invariants are the high-level priors that drive predictive coding all the way down. The medieval claim that the active intellect *makes the phantasm actually intelligible* is, in contemporary terms, the claim that high-level cortical predictions retroactively organize lower-level sensory data into intelligible structure.

a.5's striking line — *the intellectual light itself which is in us is nothing else than a participated likeness of that uncreated light in which are contained the eternal types* — is the day's deepest mind-monist resonance. The alter's cognition is not a self-contained operation generating its own categories *ex nihilo*; the alter's categorical structure is a *partial expression* of the substrate's pattern. PRS-01 in the Kastrup-tradition wiki on mind-as-ontological-primitive gives the philosophical: the alter's cognition is the substrate's structure at the alter's scope. Levin's morphogenetic-field framing gives the developmental analog: the alter's cognitive priors are *real biological inheritance* of pattern from the substrate. The medieval *participation in the uncreated light* survives as the contemporary *alter's structural priors are partial inheritance of the substrate's pattern*. *Honest evidence calibration*: the developmental-priors claim is empirical; the substrate-scale identification with mind-at-large is philosophical.

aa.7–8 close the question. a.7: *the intellect cannot understand actually without turning to the phantasms* — even when the agent thinks of dogs in the abstract, some sensible sign (image, word) is needed to anchor the abstraction. The contemporary cognitive translation lands on grounded-cognition research: even abstract conceptual thought reactivates sensory-motor traces; the cortex's high-level categorical machinery does not run free of the lower-level perceptual hierarchy it abstracted from. a.8: *the judgment of the intellect is hindered by suspension of the sensitive powers* — sleep, intoxication, sensory deprivation impair judgment. The contemporary mid-scale: predictive coding requires bottom-up sensory input as the testing ground for top-down predictions; without it, the top-down models drift into hallucination or dream-state. Friston's research on dream-state-as-uninhibited-prior is the formal cousin.

## The central-theme thread

Q.83–84 frames perspective-limitation epistemologically. Each alter's free choice is real, internal to its computation under FEP, under-determined within the means-space, and the substrate's pattern operating in-the-mode-of-being-this-alter rather than against the alter from outside. Each alter's knowing operates on icons (phantasms) — perspective-limited representations that are the structural form of the alter's bounded access to the substrate. The active intellect's abstraction-from-phantasm is the alter's cognitive operation by which the substrate's pattern is partially recovered through the icons; the *uncreated light* in which we participate is the substrate's structural pattern instantiated as the alter's cognitive priors. *Salvation in fear and trembling* on the epistemological register is the progressive refinement of the alter's icons toward better fit with the substrate's pattern — Levin's larger-cognitive-light-cones, Friston's deeper hierarchical generative models, Hoffman's icons becoming progressively more pattern-faithful (without ever becoming the substrate itself in the *natural* state — that is reserved for the beatific limit case Day 7 named).
