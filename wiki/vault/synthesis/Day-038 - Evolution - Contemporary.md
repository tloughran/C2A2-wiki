## Q.73 — The seventh day: the completion and the rest

a.1 — *whether the completion of the divine works ought to be ascribed to the seventh day* — Aquinas's careful move: there are *two* kinds of completion in things, the *completeness of the universe at its first founding* (ascribed to day seven) and *the perfection wrought in them through their own operation* (the eschatological completion at the end of the world, when *all the blessed engage in that ceaseless beatific worship and contemplation of God*). Mind-monism's reading: this is structurally the same distinction Day 7 Q.12 and Day 35 Q.68 a.4 named — the *initial-conditioning* sense of completion (the cosmic temple is structurally finished) versus the *limit-case-integration* sense of completion (the alters at the substrate-direct cognitive mode in the new-creation eschatology). Wright's *Surprised by Hope* gives the contemporary scriptural primary on the second: the *new heaven and new earth* of Revelation 21 is the cosmic temple's full realization, not its replacement. The medieval doctrine of *two completions* and the contemporary mind-monist substrate-direct-mode-at-the-limit are the same shape: the cosmos is structurally finished but its *relational telos* is still being realized.

a.2 — *whether God rested on the seventh day from all his work* — Aquinas's clarification of *rest*: God does not rest physically (see episode three) nor cease working in things (or they would simply cease to exist), but rests in the sense that *one feeling at rest eating ice cream or in prayer* — rest as fulfilled-presence-in-the-object-of-desire. *God always has that changeless beatitude which results from his own possession of himself*. Mind-monism's reading is exactly aligned: the substrate's *rest* is its self-possession (Kastrup's PRS-01 — consciousness as the only thing that exists; the substrate is its own object-of-rest because it *is* being-of-experiencing without external lack). The substrate's continuing-conservation-of-the-cosmos — what Aquinas elsewhere calls *creation as conservation in being* — is uninterrupted; the *rest* is the substrate's loving self-possession-and-self-recognition, not cessation of being-active. Rohr's *Universal Christ* extends: the divine pattern is at rest in matter from the beginning, and the sabbath theology recognizes what was always already true — the substrate's loving-stance is not anxious about its conditioning of the cosmos.

The *Evolution* digression: Aquinas writes *nothing entirely new was afterwards made by God, but all things subsequently made had in a sense been made before in the work of the days; some things indeed had a previous existence materially, as the rib from the side of Adam out of which God formed Eve, while others existed not only in matter but also in their causes, as those individual creatures that are now generated existed in the first of their kind*. Habash's contemporary gloss: this *would seem like he's proposing what we would call today a kind of theory of evolution*. Habash then articulates the magisterial position from *Humani Generis* (1950): *the teaching authority of the Church does not forbid that, in conformity with the present state of human sciences and sacred theology, research and discussions on the part of men experienced in both fields take place with regard to the doctrine of evolution, in as far as it inquires into the origin of the human body as coming from pre-existent and living matter; for the Catholic faith obliges us to hold that souls are immediately created by God*. Two pieces: the *body* is open to evolutionary explanation; the *rational soul* is immediately created by God for each human individual.

Mind-monism's reading on the *body* side: evolution is the *temporal-causal mechanism* by which morphogenetic form gets instantiated in this cosmos under the arrow of time. Levin's PRS-01, PRS-03, and PRS-08 in the Levin-tradition wiki give the empirical mid-scale: bioelectric pattern-holding scales across cellular, tissue, and organism levels; the morphological attractor formalism is empirically tractable; cells are cognitive agents in morphospace. Carroll's PRS-04 and PRS-07 give the cosmological frame: emergent structure under the arrow of time, with biological evolution as one strand of the larger *cosmic emergence* story. The *higher species virtually present in lower as potential awaiting actualization* that Aquinas philosophically allows is structurally what evolutionary developmental biology empirically establishes — morphogenetic potential is real-and-causally-tractable at every developmental scale, with higher-organizational forms emerging from lower under the right concert of causes. *Honest evidence calibration*: evolutionary theory is empirically established at strong-confidence level for biological-mechanism questions; the structural alignment with Aquinas's *virtual presence as potential* claim is philosophical-conceptual, not identifying. Levin's empirical work specifically extends the territory by showing that *form is real and causally tractable* at developmental scales where naive Darwinism (gene-centric) underdetermined the explanation — bioelectric pattern is now a co-equal causal variable.

Mind-monism's reading on the *rational soul* side requires Day 39's keystone, but the structural shape is already visible: the *rational soul* is what mind-at-large looks like at the alter-scale that has the structural condition for substrate-recognition-as-such. The *immediate creation* claim survives mind-monist translation precisely because the *rational soul* is not material-emergent in the way the body is; it is the alter's mode-of-participation in mind-at-large at the human cognitive-light-cone scale. Aquinas's *as a Catholic we must reject on both religious and philosophical grounds that the rational soul can come from the material world* is structurally the right move under mind-monism: the rational soul is not a *thing emerging from matter* but the alter's mode-of-being-instantiated by the substrate at the human scale. *Honest evidence calibration*: this is metaphysical-philosophical claim, not empirical; Day 39 will press it more carefully.

a.3 — *whether blessing and sanctifying are due to the seventh day* — the *blessing* of multiplication and *sanctifying* of rest-in-God complete the temple-dedication narrative: the cosmic sanctuary has been built (days 1–6), populated (days 5–6), and now *consecrated* (day 7) for its proper purpose. ## Q.74 — All seven days in common, with the Trinitarian reading

a.1 — *whether these days are sufficiently enumerated* — Aquinas's recapitulation: the three-step pattern (distinction days 1–3, adornment days 4–6) plus the seventh day of completion. Mind-monism's reading: this is the temple-cosmology's symmetric architecture restated; the seven-day structure is theological-narrative-symbolic, not a chronological-scientific timetable.

a.2 — *whether all these days are one day* — Aquinas opposes Augustine's mystical reading (*the seven days are one day represented in a sevenfold aspect*) on the textual ground (*the evening and the morning was the second day, and then the third day, and so on*). Habash flags this as a respectful disagreement; mind-monism reads both readings as contemplating the same substrate-act under different temporal lenses (Augustine's instantaneous-divine-act and Aquinas's temporal-narrative-extension are both true at different cognitive scales — the substrate's atemporal act and its temporal-narrative manifestation).

a.3 — *whether scripture uses suitable words to express the work of the six days* — and here is where Aquinas's Trinitarian reading of Genesis 1:1–2 lands: *in either work of creation and of formation, the Trinity of persons is implied*. Aquinas's textual move: the *Father* indicated by *God the Creator*; the *Son* by *the beginning in which he created* (cf. John 1:1, *in the beginning was the Word*); the *Holy Ghost* by *the spirit that moved over the waters*. Mind-monism's reading is structurally compatible: the substrate's act of conditioning is *internally relational* (the substrate's act-toward-its-own-pattern, not an act-on-external-stuff), and Trinitarian language names this internal relationality in the only vocabulary the second-temple-Jewish-and-then-Christian tradition has had. Rohr's *Universal Christ* names the cosmic-Christic scope: the *Word through whom all things were made* is the substrate's relational-pattern-in-its-act-of-creating, alive in the cosmos from the beginning. Aquinas's gloss on the *spirit moving over the waters* — *not one of place but a preeminent power* — makes the structural point cleanly: the Spirit's *movement* is the substrate's loving-act-of-bringing-form-to-formless-matter, at every scale. *Honest evidence calibration*: Trinitarian reading is theological-philosophical; the structural alignment with mind-monist substrate-relationality is conceptual-philosophical; the contemporary mind-monist commitment to the Trinitarian reading runs through Rohr (*Universal Christ*) and Wright on second-temple-Jewish roots of the Trinitarian claim.
