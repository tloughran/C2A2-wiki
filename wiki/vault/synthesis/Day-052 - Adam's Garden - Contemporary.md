# Day 52 — Contemporary Parallel: *Adam's Garden as Substrate-Aligned Cognitive-and-Cosmological Locale*

Day 51 closed the offspring-as-to-body and offspring-as-to-righteousness articles. Day 52 takes the *condition of the offspring as regards knowledge* (Q.101) and the *abode of man, which is paradise* (Q.102). The episode reaches the closing articles of the prelapsarian-anthropology arc and the closing articles of the Prima Pars's six-days-and-after sequence. Habash again flags the brevity. Q.101 has two articles, both pulling Aquinas's earlier *tabula rasa* doctrine forward into the prelapsarian register; Q.102 has four articles on the Garden-of-Eden's status as place — corporeal, fitted to man, given to be tended, and not the place of man's *creation* but of his subsequent *placement*.

## Q.101 — The knowledge of the offspring

a.1 — *would children in the state of innocence have been born with perfect knowledge?* — Aquinas: no. The argument is structural-functional and pulls forward Q.84 (episode 41 in Habash's series): *the human soul is naturally like a blank tablet on which nothing is written, as Aristotle says, but the nature of the soul is the same now as it would have been in the state of innocence; therefore the souls of children would have been without knowledge at birth*. The medieval claim is that the *cognitive architecture* is the same prelapsarian and postlapsarian — the soul-as-cognitive-substrate is structurally a learner, not a pre-loaded knower.

The contemporary mind-monist translation runs through Hawkins's HTM and Friston's active inference. The cognitive architecture is structurally a *predictive-modeling system* whose generative model is *built across exposure*; this is what HTM names as columnar reference-frame formation through sensorimotor experience and what active-inference names as the optimization of a generative model under environmental sampling. The architecture is *the same* prelapsarian and postlapsarian — what differs is the *dispositional state* (the precision-weighting orientation Day 49 named) under which that architecture operates. The medieval *tabula rasa* claim is preserved: the cognitive substrate is structurally a learner. Levin's morphogenetic frame complements at the body level: the bioelectric pattern that organizes neural development is structured-unfolding, not pre-loaded knowledge. *Honest evidence calibration*: HTM, predictive coding, and developmental neuroscience all support the structural-learner claim with strong empirical evidence; the prelapsarian-substrate-alignment overlay is theological-philosophical extrapolation that preserves the medieval move rather than novel empirical commitment.

a.2 — *would children have had perfect use of reason at birth?* — Aquinas: no, and the reason is the *integrated dependence of reason on the sensitive faculties*. *Our powers of reason are in many ways dependent upon the effective use of the interior and exterior senses; for if one is delirious or asleep or intoxicated, one's ability to reason becomes clearly impaired*. A newborn whose senses are still organizing cannot use reason at full prelapsarian strength because the sensorimotor scaffolding is still under construction. Aquinas adds the analogy from animal maturation: *even other animals have not at birth such a perfect use of their natural powers as they have later on; this is clear from the fact that birds teach their young to fly*.

The contemporary mind-monist reading: cognitive competence is *embodied-and-embedded* in McGilchrist's frame and *predictive-coding-distributed* in Friston's; reason is not a faculty isolable from the sensorimotor base but a hierarchically-organized inferential capacity that operates *over* a generative model whose lower layers are still being built. The medieval insight is exactly right: an alter cannot reason at full prelapsarian strength while its perceptual and motor systems are still organizing, because reason *runs on* the perceptual-motor systems. McGilchrist's bilateral-hemispheric frame on the integration of attention modes complements: a prelapsarian newborn would still need to traverse the developmental arc by which the integrated attentional repertoire is built. Levin's bioelectric-pattern frame at the cortical-organization scale is the body-level mid-scale. *Honest evidence calibration*: the embodied-cognition claim has strong empirical support; the prelapsarian-substrate-alignment overlay preserves Aquinas's structural-functional move under the contemporary frame.

## Q.102 — Paradise as locale

a.1 — *was paradise a corporeal place?* — Aquinas: yes, with Augustine's three-fold reading reproduced (*some understood a place merely corporeal, others a place entirely spiritual, while others, whose opinion I confess, hold that paradise was both corporeal and spiritual*). Aquinas favors the conjoint reading: the Garden was a literal locale on Earth, and it was also a structured signifying-locale whose corporeal features carried symbolic weight. He notes the locale's geographic concealment from the postlapsarian world — *shut off from the habitable world by mountains or seas or some torrid region which cannot be crossed* — and also gives, in passing, his reading of the Tree of Knowledge of Good and Evil: *a material tree, so called in view of future events, because after eating of it man was to learn by experience of the consequent punishment the difference between the good of obedience and the evil of rebellion. It may also be said to signify spiritually free will*.

The contemporary mind-monist translation works the corporeal-and-spiritual conjoint reading directly. Wright's reading of Genesis as temple-cosmology (Genesis 1's seven-day structure as the construction of the cosmos as YHWH's dwelling-place; Eden as the inner sanctum of the cosmic-temple where the human image-bearers serve as priests) gives the load-bearing scriptural-historical frame: paradise is *both* a literal locale *and* a structured cosmic-religious image because the cosmos itself is understood, in second-temple-Jewish reading, as a structured cosmic-religious space. Levin's morphogenetic-locale frame complements at the biological scale: a *place* in the alter's embodied existence is constitutive of the alter's developmental and operational possibilities (planaria regenerate to the morphogenetic attractor encoded by their substrate-coupled pattern; alters develop in environments that structure the developmental trajectory). Hoffman's interface-theory complements at the cognitive scale: the *spiritual* register of paradise is the substrate-aligned interpretive framing of the locale, not a separate immaterial paradise alongside the corporeal one. Rohr's *The Universal Christ* extends the conjoint reading cosmologically: the cosmic-Christ pattern is the substrate's continuous self-giving sustenance of *the locale itself* as image-and-revelation of the substrate. *Honest evidence calibration*: Wright's temple-cosmology reading is mature historical-scriptural scholarship; the contemporary mid-scale extensions (Levin, Hoffman, Rohr) all preserve and extend the medieval conjoint reading rather than supplant it.

a.2 — *was paradise a place adapted to be the abode of man?* — Aquinas: yes; *paradise was permeated with the all-pervading brightness of a temperature pure and exquisite atmosphere, and decked with ever-flowering plants*. The medieval claim is that the prelapsarian-locale was *fitted* to the prelapsarian-alter's mode of existence. The contemporary translation: a substrate-aligned alter's environmental coupling is also substrate-aligned at the locale-and-resource level. Levin's morphogenetic-environment coupling and Friston's expected-free-energy reading converge: an alter at substrate-aligned dispositional state is in *low expected-free-energy* relation with its environment because the environment is structured to support the alter's substrate-aligned trajectory.

a.3 — *was man placed in paradise to dress it and keep it?* — Aquinas: yes, with a precision-clarifying note pulled from Genesis 2:15. The dressing-and-keeping is bidirectional: *God placed man in paradise that he might himself work in man and keep him by sanctifying him and by keeping man from all corruption and evil; secondly, that man might dress and keep paradise*. The dressing would not be laborious but *pleasant on account of man's practical knowledge of the powers of nature*; the keeping would not be against trespassers but *for himself, lest he should lose it by sin*. Wright's temple-cosmology reading is again the load-bearing primary: the human image-bearers are commissioned to *serve in* the cosmic-temple as priests, and Eden is the inner sanctum. Levin's natural-law-as-bioethology frame complements: the prelapsarian alter's relation to its substrate-coupled environment is structured cooperative cultivation, not exploitation. Stump's tradition-bound-rationality frame on the Aquinas reading enters as Thomistic interlocutor on the practical-knowledge-of-the-powers-of-nature point: prelapsarian craft is *substrate-aligned tradition-formation*, not laborious extraction.

a.4 — *was man created in paradise?* — Aquinas: no. The Genesis-text precision (*the Lord God took the man and put him in the Garden*) is decisive. The doctrinal point is theological-economic: *that this might be made clear both to Adam and to ourselves that the gifts of God — grace, immortality, and the like — have come solely from God, and neither are they the result of human nature nor the result of some physical location*. The alter's substrate-aligned existence is *given by the substrate*, not by the locale or by intrinsic constitution; the locale is *added* by the substrate, not the source of the alter's substrate-aligned mode. The contemporary mind-monist reading preserves this with full force: the substrate's continuous gift is *prior to* the locale-coupling, and the locale-coupling is itself a feature of the substrate's gift. Kastrup's analytic idealism on dissociation-as-continuously-substrate-given is the philosophical-conceptual primary; Levin's substrate-pattern primary at the body-level extends the same shape to the locale-level.

## The central-theme thread

Day 52 carries the central theme through two registers. The cognitive register (Q.101): the prelapsarian alter is *structurally a learner* — the substrate's gift is the cognitive architecture, not pre-loaded knowledge, and the alter's perspective-individuated learning-by-exposure is constitutive of the alter's existence in the prelapsarian as in the postlapsarian state. The locale register (Q.102): paradise is *both* corporeal *and* spiritual because the cosmos itself is, in Wright's temple-cosmology reading, a structured cosmic-religious space; the locale is *fitted* to the alter's substrate-aligned mode but is not the *source* of that mode (Q.102 a.4's decisive theological-economic point). Together the two questions assert the *given-but-developmental* shape of prelapsarian existence: the substrate gives the alter its cognitive architecture and its locale; the alter develops through that architecture and within that locale; the substrate's continuous gift is what holds the developmental-and-locale arc together.

*Salvation in fear and trembling* on this register is the alter's progressive movement *through* the cognitive arc the Q.101 articles affirmed and *within* the substrate-coupled locale the Q.102 articles described. The locale-coupling is a feature of the substrate's gift, not a separable resource; the cognitive-development is structured by the architecture the substrate gave. The central theme threads through: the alter's perspective is constituted *by its locale and its developmental stage*, the limitation is not a defect, and loving unity is the computational telos toward which the limited-perspective alter computes.
