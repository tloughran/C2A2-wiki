# Day 94 — Greatest Virtue (Contemporary)

## Frame

Aquinas asks: *which virtue is greatest?* and *which virtues persist after this life?* The contemporary parallel under mind/conscious-realist monism asks: **of the dispositions that constitute the limited agent's loving-unity computation, which are interface-relative (and so dissolve when the interface dissolves) and which are absolute (and so persist across substrate change)?** Aquinas's answer to the first question turns on essence-ranking (intellectual > moral; wisdom > knowledge; charity > faith and hope). His answer to the second is the eschatological filter that picks out **charity** as the only theological virtue that survives identically and **justice** (will-rooted) as the only moral virtue that persists straightforwardly. The contemporary reframe holds both answers but reads "persistence" in the language of Hoffman's interface theory and Kastrup's analytic idealism.

## Problem

The limited agent computes from within a perspectival interface. Some of its capacities are *adaptations to the interface* (fear of bodily harm, appetitive temperance, sense-based knowledge). Others are *relations to what underlies the interface* (the agent's love of Mind-at-Large, its trust in the conscious whole, its disposition to act in concert with it). If the interface dissolves — under the standard medieval picture, at bodily death; under a contemporary picture, anywhere we ask which features of the agent are *substrate-dependent* — what persists? Aquinas's answer carries strong theological commitments; the contemporary frame must give a careful account of what "persists" means here.

## Resource

Hoffman's interface theory of perception (Hoffman 2019, *The Case Against Reality*) supplies the contemporary formalization of the Aquinas-distinction between interface-dependent and interface-independent capacities. Hoffman's claim — that spacetime and physical-object perception are species-specific user-interfaces, not direct readouts of reality — entails that some agent capacities (sense-based virtue, embodied fortitude in the face of bodily danger) are *interface artifacts*, while others (the disposition toward truth, beauty, communion among conscious agents) track what the interface is interface-*of*. The Q.67 eschatological filter is exactly this distinction. Speculative-metaphysical; rigorous evolutionary-game-theory underpinnings.

Kastrup (analytic idealism, e.g., *Decoding Schopenhauer's Metaphysics*) gives a complementary frame: individual conscious agents are dissociated alters of Mind-at-Large. Dispositions toward Mind-at-Large (charity in the contemporary frame) are *relations to what the alter is an alter of* and survive the alter's dissociative boundary. Dispositions calibrated to the alter's particular interface (sense-based virtue) do not. Coherent metaphysics; no decisive empirical falsifier.

Levin (multi-scale agency, planarian regeneration work, Levin 2022): empirically demonstrates that certain *patterns* — learned behaviors, morphogenetic information — survive radical substrate transformation in some biological systems. The empirical analogue of "moral virtue rooted in will persists across the body's transformation": pattern-content can be substrate-independent within limits. Empirically firm at small scale; large extrapolations are speculative.

PRS-12 in the Stump-tradition wiki: Stump's reading of charity as the **second-personal knowing** that continues into the beatific vision. The relational-knowledge structure is what persists; the propositional-knowledge content of faith is *fulfilled* (no longer needed as faith) when the relationship is no longer mediated. Theological-philosophical.

Wright (canonical: *Resurrection of the Son of God*, *Surprised by Hope*): resurrection-of-the-body is not the soul shedding the body for eternity; it is the *renewal* of embodied creation in a new mode. This is critical for the Q.67 reading: virtues rooted in *the body as currently constituted* dissolve, but virtues rooted in the will (which Wright would say is renewed, not abolished) persist. Historiographic claim with strong-but-disputed standing.

Rohr (canonical: *Universal Christ*): charity as the **eternal divine pattern** — the love-relation that the contemplative tradition holds is the substrate of all reality, not a creaturely capacity at all. Charity persists because it is, in the limit, *what is*. Theological-contemplative.

Carroll (poor-man's-quantum, FLAG-08 cross-program index): empirically what's substrate-independent is *information* — the pattern, not the medium. The Q.67 distinction maps onto this: justice (a pattern in the will) survives in a way that temperance (a pattern in the bodily appetite) does not.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The Q.66-67 hierarchy reads, under conscious-realist monism, as a sorting of agent-dispositions by **how deeply they relate to what is ontologically fundamental**. The "greatest" virtue in each category is the one whose object is closest to mind/consciousness-as-such:

- Among the intellectual virtues, **wisdom** is greatest because its object is the supreme cause (= the conscious whole). Empirically: in Friston's hierarchical inference, the top-level generative-model parameters that govern everything else are *highest in the hierarchy*. Same structural argument.
- Among the moral virtues, **justice** is chief because its object is the relational structure of agents with one another. Stump's second-personal frame and Wright's covenant-loyalty frame converge here.
- Among the theological virtues, **charity** is greatest because (in this life) love-of-God exceeds knowledge-of-God under the interface-dim-mirror condition, and (in the next life) charity is the only theological virtue that survives identically.

The eschatological filter (Q.67) then reads as **interface-dissolution**: virtues whose formal object is interface-relative (faith of obscure-knowledge, hope of not-yet-possessed-good, temperance of the body) dissolve when the interface dissolves. Virtues whose formal object is what underlies the interface — justice (rooted in will, the agent's substantive direction-of-relation), charity (the love-relation to the conscious whole) — persist.

**What this shares with Aquinas**: the eschatological filter, the hierarchy by object-rank, the elevation of charity.

**Where this reframes**: the ontology of "after this life." Aquinas operates under a soul-body metaphysics in which the soul straightforwardly survives. The contemporary frame under mind/conscious-realist monism does not require that picture — what survives is whatever is **not interface-relative**. The persistence question becomes ontologically continuous with: *what would survive in any scenario where the agent's current interface dissolves and a new mode of conscious participation in Mind-at-Large opens?* Aquinas's answer aligns: charity, justice in some form, the pattern-content of habitual knowledge.

**Where evidence is firm vs. speculative**: Levin's substrate-independence-of-pattern in small biological systems — empirically firm. Carroll's substrate-independence-of-information — formally robust. Hoffman's interface-claim — speculative-metaphysical with rigorous formal underpinnings. Kastrup's analytic-idealism reading — coherent, not decisively falsifiable. Wright's resurrection ontology — historiographic, strong-but-disputed. Rohr's charity-as-eternal-pattern — contemplative-theological. The integration is speculative.

## The central-theme thread

Perspective-limitation individuates agents. Each agent's interface — its embodied sensorium, its affective range, its sense-based knowledge — is exactly what makes it a particular limited perspective. The eschatological question is what *remains* when the limitation is dissolved. **Loving unity remains**: the relation to the whole, the second-personal knowing of the conscious totality. Charity is the name for the disposition that *is* this relation already, in seed-form, under perspective-limitation. It survives because it is not a coping mechanism for limitation; it is the thing limitation was scaffolding toward. *Salvation* = inclusion in the people united with God (Wright) = the agent's full participation in what charity already, in part, computes.

- **Levin (multi-scale agency, substrate-independence of pattern):** empirical analogue of Q.67's selective persistence. Empirical.
- **Wright (canonical: resurrection of the body):** scriptural ground truth for "renewed, not abolished." Historiographic.
