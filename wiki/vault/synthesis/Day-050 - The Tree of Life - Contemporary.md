# Day 50 — Contemporary Parallel: *The Tree of Life as Substrate-Sustained Embodiment*

Day 49 closed with the original-justice baseline in its willed-and-governing aspects. Day 50 takes the *body-and-species* preservation aspects, the last questions in the prelapsarian anthropology arc. Q.97 walks through immortality, passibility, food, and the Tree of Life; Q.98 turns to generation — whether the species would have been preserved through coition in the state of innocence. The medieval treatment is short (six articles total) and the doctrine reaches the most striking typological image of the Prima Pars: the Tree of Life as the prelapsarian sacrament, prefigure of the Eucharist, the new-Eden Tree that the cosmic-Christ pattern fulfills. Rohr's primary application of the *Universal Christ* frame lands precisely here.

## Q.97 — Preservation of the individual

a.1 — *would man have been immortal in the state of innocence?* — Aquinas: yes, but in the *given-immortality* sense, not the *intrinsic-immortality* sense. The first man's body was not *intrinsically* indissoluble — material bodies are intrinsically dissoluble. But *as long as he did not sin*, he was preserved from corruption *by a supernatural force given by God to the soul, whereby it was enabled to preserve the body from all corruption so long as it remained itself subject to God*.

The medieval doctrine is structurally precise. Immortality-in-the-prelapsarian-state is *substrate-sustained embodiment*, not *intrinsic substrate-independence of the embodied form*. The contemporary mind-monist translation: the alter's embodied form is structurally dissoluble (as every embodied form is, since embodiment is a substrate-mediated icon-level construction); the alter's immortality-in-the-prelapsarian-state is the substrate's *active sustenance* of the embodied form through the alter's substrate-aligned dispositional state. Levin's bioelectric morphogenetic frame gives the contemporary mid-scale empirical handle: the body's form is pulled toward an anatomical attractor encoded in bioelectric memory; substrate-aligned alters could in principle have *much* greater repair, regeneration, and homeostatic capacity than fallen alters do. *Honest evidence calibration*: Levin's empirical framework supports the *substrate-sustained-form* shape at developmental and regenerative scales (planaria regeneration, xenobots, bioelectric reprogramming for cancer and birth defects); the *prelapsarian-immortality* extrapolation is theological, not empirically demonstrated.

a.2 — *would man have been passible in the state of innocence?* — Aquinas distinguishes two senses of *passibility*: passibility-against-nature (being wounded, harmed) and passibility-in-accord-with-nature (learning, sensing, being changed for the better). The first man was *im-passible* in the first sense (no wound, no harm) but *passible* in the second sense (he learned, he sensed, he was changed by what he experienced). The medieval distinction maps cleanly onto the contemporary alter dynamics: an alter at substrate-aligned dispositional state is *not* in conflict with its environment (passibility-against-nature absent) but *is* in continuous information-uptake from its environment (passibility-in-accord-with-nature present). Friston's free-energy framework names the two regimes: an alter optimally tracking its environment exhibits low expected-free-energy (no chronic prediction-failure, no chronic environmental conflict) but is still in continuous prediction-update cycles (continuous information-uptake).

a.3 — *would man have needed food in the state of innocence?* — Aquinas: yes, on a structural-functional argument. Man is a rational animal; animals need food; man being created in his rational-animal nature needed food. The Genesis evidence (*you may freely eat of every tree of the garden*) confirms. But after the resurrection, Aquinas argues, food will not be needed — because the resurrected body is *spiritualized*, more like the angels than the animals, while preserving the body-soul composite essential to humanity. Aquinas reads this through Luke 20:35-36 (*the sons of this age marry... but those who are counted worthy to attain to that age and to the resurrection from the dead neither marry nor are given in marriage, because they are equal to angels and are sons of God being sons of the resurrection*).

The contemporary mind-monist translation: the alter's embodied state has different *modes* — the prelapsarian mode (animal-rational, food-needing, with passibility-in-accord-with-nature), the fallen mode (animal-rational, food-needing, with disordered passibility-against-nature added), and the glorified mode (rational-spiritualized, food-not-needing, with all passibility-against-nature removed and passibility-in-accord-with-nature transformed). The medieval scheme tracks the alter's progression through these modes. Wright's *The Resurrection of the Son of God* is the load-bearing primary on the spiritualized-mode reading: the resurrected body is *transphysical* in Wright's coinage — neither merely physical (subject to corruption) nor non-physical (immaterial spirit), but a transformed embodiment that preserves the body-soul integration while shifting its mode of operation. *Honest evidence calibration*: the doctrinal locks here are firm in the Catholic tradition; the contemporary mind-monist reading preserves them and uses Wright's narrative-historical framing to articulate the transphysical mode.

a.4 — *would man have acquired immortality by the Tree of Life?* — Aquinas: yes, but the immortality acquired would have been *limited* — no created thing has infinite power, so the Tree could not bestow infinite duration absolutely. Man would have either needed to eat repeatedly or, eventually, to be raised by God to a *new spiritualized state* (the resurrection-mode Aquinas already pointed to in a.3). The Tree of Life is structurally an *interim sustaining mechanism* until the alter's full transition to glorified mode.

This is the day's typological keystone. Habash's transcript is unusually generous with the typology: *just as we have said before that the woman being formed from the side of man was a fitting prefigurement of the church coming forth from the sleeping side of Christ, so too here it looks like to me we have in the Tree of Life a fitting analogy to the sacrament of the Eucharist*. The Tree from which man would be banished post-fall, guarded by an angel and a flaming sword, of which God says *if man puts forth his hand and eat from it he shall live forever* — this is the prefigure of the new-Eden Tree, the cross-as-tree, the Eucharist-as-fruit, that Rohr's *Universal Christ* frame names as the cosmic-scope reading.

The contemporary synthesis preserves the typology in full. The cosmic-Christ pattern is *self-giving substrate sustenance* — the substrate as the giving source of the alter's embodied life, mediated through structured sacramental contact (Tree, sword, angel; word, Spirit, messenger; bread, wine, priest). Rohr's contemplative frame and Wright's narrative-historical reading converge on the structural shape: the substrate's gift is *food-for-the-alter* in the strict sense — life-sustenance not merely metabolic but ontological, the substrate continuously sustaining the alter's substrate-aligned existence. The medieval doctrine of the Tree of Life as eucharistic prefigure is the load-bearing typological structure of the entire prelapsarian anthropology arc, and Day 50 reaches it directly.

## Q.98 — Preservation of the species

a.1 — *would generation have existed in the state of innocence?* — Aquinas: yes, on a scriptural argument. *Increase and multiply and fill the earth* (Genesis 1:28) is given before the fall; the multiplication command requires generation; therefore generation would have occurred in the state of innocence. The article is short and the structural claim is direct: the species-preservation function is part of original-creation, not a post-fall remediation.

The contemporary translation runs through Levin's biology and Fredrickson's positivity-resonance framework. Sexually-reproducing organisms are *constituted* by the male-female complementarity; multiplication-through-generation is what they are *for*, in the teleological-functional sense Levin's diverse-intelligence framework articulates. The first-state community would have been a multiplying community — alters generating alters in cooperative-integrated complementary relation, with positivity-resonance dynamics across all the constitutive relationships (parent-child, sibling-sibling, spousal, communal). The medieval claim and the contemporary biology converge on the constitutive-relational structure of human flourishing across generations.

a.2 — *would generation have been by coition in the state of innocence?* — Aquinas: yes, with reasoning that closes the question pair: *God made man and woman before sin*; the male-female distinction is the structure ordained for coition-as-generation; therefore the structure pre-exists the fall and would have operated in the prelapsarian state. The medieval reading preserves the constitutive role of bodily relational coupling in human flourishing.

The contemporary synthesis reads this through Fredrickson's positivity-resonance framework: bodily relational coupling is the *biophysiological substrate* of love-as-positivity-resonance, and love is the structural form complementary-arising-from-unity takes at the dyadic and family scales. The first-state community is the limit case of high-positivity-resonance, broaden-and-build flourishing dynamics across the full spectrum of human relational forms — including the spousal-generative form Q.98 names. *Honest evidence calibration*: Fredrickson's empirical framework on positivity-resonance is mature for the dyadic biology; the prelapsarian-baseline extrapolation is theological-philosophical.
