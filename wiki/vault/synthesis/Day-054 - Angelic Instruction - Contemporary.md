# Day 54 — Contemporary Parallel: *Substrate-Mediated Action on Form, Body, Intellect, and Will; the Miraculous as Substrate-Outside-Established-Channels*

Day 53 opened the divine-governance arc with the cosmic-scale substrate-as-continuous-sustenance frame. Day 54 zooms into the *mechanics* of substrate-action: Q.105 walks through the eight registers of substrate-action — on matter-to-form, on body, on intellect, on will, in every agent, outside the order of nature, miraculously, and the gradation of miracles. Q.106 then opens the angels-acting-on-each-other arc: whether one angel illumines another's intellect, moves another's will, whether the inferior can illumine the superior, whether the superior illumines the inferior comprehensively. The episode is Habash's most direct engagement so far with the *what does substrate-action look like* register; it culminates in his quotation of Augustine — *late have I loved Thee, O Beauty so ancient and so new... for behold thou wert within me and I outside* — which is the day's contemplative-theological keystone.

## Q.105 — Change of creatures by God

a.1 — *can God move matter immediately to form?* — Aquinas: yes; *whatever is in passive potentiality can be reduced to act by the act of power which extends over that potentiality; therefore since the divine power extends over matter as produced by God, it can be reduced to act by the divine power, and this is what is meant by matter being moved to a form*. Habash supplies the chemistry illustration: hydrogen + oxygen → water, with new properties under the new form.

The contemporary mind-monist translation runs through Levin's morphogenetic-pattern frame and Heisenberg's energy-as-potential reading (which Habash himself uses in Q.104 a.2 the day before). Matter-as-potential is the deepest substrate-level of *what could be instantiated*; form-as-actuality is the substrate's pattern-action that selects an instantiation. In Levin's bioelectric morphogenesis, the substrate's pattern-action operates on cellular collectives to organize them around a morphogenetic attractor — substrate-action on matter-to-form at the empirically-tractable mid-scale. In Wolfram's ruliad frame, the substrate's pattern-selection is the rule-substrate's instantiation of one rule-application over alternatives. Kastrup's analytic-idealism complements: matter-as-potential is what mind-at-large's not-yet-actualized configurations *look like*; form-as-actuality is the substrate's standing self-instantiation. *Honest evidence calibration*: the medieval claim is metaphysical-foundational; the Levin bioelectric mid-scale is strong empirical for substrate-action on form-organization; the Wolfram and Kastrup deep-substrate readings are computational and philosophical-conceptual respectively.

a.2 — *can God move a body immediately?* — Aquinas: yes; *anything we can do, save evil and sin or any other imperfect or contradictory thing, God can also do; if I can push the chair and an angel can push the chair without having a body, so too can God*. The contemporary translation: the substrate is not constrained to act through embodied-agent-intermediaries; substrate-action can be direct at any scale. In the conscious-agent-network frame (Hoffman), the substrate-network can interact with any node directly, not only through node-to-node chains. In Levin's diverse-intelligences frame, agency-at-scale is *non-exclusive* — substrate-level agency can act through multi-scale agents *or* directly on the substrate-instantiated body.

a.3 — *does God move the created intellect immediately?* — Aquinas: yes, in three integrated ways: by giving the intellectual power, by impressing the intelligible species, by sustaining both in being. The contemporary translation runs through Friston's active-inference frame on cognitive architecture as substrate-given generative model, Hawkins's HTM frame on the substrate-given cortical reference-frame architecture, and Hoffman's interface-theory on the substrate's role in supplying perceptual content. The medieval *intelligible species* maps onto the contemporary *generative-model content*; the substrate's *impressing* of the species maps onto the substrate's continuous gift of perceptual-and-conceptual content to the alter's predictive-modeling system. McGilchrist's bilateral-hemispheric frame extends: the integration of attention modes is itself a substrate-given structural feature.

a.4 — *can God move the created will?* — Aquinas: yes, by giving the will its proper object (goodness) and by being the first mover of all things while *not destroying freedom* because *he gives the will its own natural inclination*. The medieval distinction is precise: substrate-action on the will is *not coercive* because the inclination-toward-goodness is *constitutive* of the will, not externally imposed. The contemporary mind-monist translation: in Friston's active-inference frame, the alter's policy-selection (the active-inference name for willing) is over a generative model whose *prior preferences* are substrate-given but operate as the alter's own preferences within its own generative model. The *what-feels-good-to-the-alter* is substrate-given as the alter's standing prior; the *which-policy-the-alter-selects* is the alter's own active-inference computation. The medieval *not-coercive* claim preserves precisely under the active-inference frame: the substrate's gift of inclination-toward-goodness is *constitutive* of the alter's policy-space, not an external imposition over it. Stump's tradition-bound-rationality and second-first-language-fluency-under-grace frames complement at the dispositional-formation level. Wright's reading of Philippians 2:12-13 (*work out your own salvation with fear and trembling, for God is at work in you both to will and to work for his good pleasure*) is the load-bearing scriptural-theological primary, and is again the central-theme tagline.

a.5 — *does God work in every agent?* — Aquinas: yes, integratively (giving the form, preserving in being, applying to act, being the end of every action). Augustine's autobiographical *late have I loved Thee* is the day's contemplative culmination: the substrate is *more interior to the alter than the alter is to itself*. The contemporary mind-monist translation: in the dissociation frame, mind-at-large is *what the alter is*; the alter's interior is mind-at-large under dissociation; mind-at-large is therefore *more interior than the alter's apparent self-boundary*. Rohr's *The Universal Christ* extends contemplatively: the cosmic-Christ pattern is the substrate's continuous self-presence in every alter, accessible through contemplative-attention. The medieval claim, the Augustine quotation, and the contemporary substrate-monist frames converge on the *substrate's interiority-to-the-alter* shape.

a.6-7 — *can God do anything outside the established order of nature, and is whatever God does outside the natural order miraculous?* — Aquinas: yes to both, with precision. The substrate can act *outside the usual course of nature* (because the natural order is the substrate's standing pattern-instantiation, not a fixed external constraint on the substrate) but *not against itself* (the substrate cannot act against its own goodness or wisdom). A miracle is *full of wonder, having a cause absolutely hidden from all*; an eclipse is wondrous to the rustic and not to the astronomer because the latter knows the cause; a *miracle* is what is wondrous *because the cause is the substrate acting outside the usually-instantiated channels*.

The contemporary mind-monist translation: in Wolfram's ruliad-substrate frame, the *usual course of nature* is the most-frequently-instantiated rule-applications, and substrate-action *outside* the usual course is the substrate's instantiation of less-frequent rule-applications. In Kastrup's analytic-idealism, the *usual course* is mind-at-large's standing-instantiation of the substrate-pattern, and substrate-action outside is mind-at-large's non-standing-instantiation. In Levin's bioelectric frame at the empirically-tractable scale, the bioelectric-attractor's *usual* trajectory can be retargeted by direct substrate-action, and the resulting morphogenetic outcome is *miraculous-in-the-medieval-sense* relative to the usual trajectory (Levin's planaria-with-two-heads is a literal example of a substrate-action-outside-the-usual-channel that produces a *non-standardly-instantiated* outcome — though Levin himself frames it in scientific not theological terms). *Honest evidence calibration*: Levin's empirical work is the closest contemporary analog to the medieval *miracle-as-substrate-acting-outside-usual-channels* shape, but at a much narrower scope; the cosmological and resurrection-scale miracle claims are theological. Wright's *The Resurrection of the Son of God* is the load-bearing primary on the resurrection-as-miracle-of-the-highest-grade reading (Aquinas's a.8 hierarchy of miracles places resurrection at the top, *above the nature of a thing entirely*).

a.8 — *is one miracle greater than another?* — Aquinas: yes, with a three-fold gradation — (1) above the nature of a thing entirely (resurrection of glorified body), (2) regaining what nature could not regain unaided (sight to the blind, life to the dead), (3) unusually-fast-or-easy what nature can do (rapid healing). The medieval gradation maps onto a contemporary *substrate-action-outside-usual-channels* gradation by *how far* the action departs from the standing pattern-instantiation. Wright's resurrection-scholarship is the load-bearing primary on the highest-grade miracle as the *new-creation-mode* breaking into the present cosmos.

## Q.106 — How one creature moves another (the angels)

a.1 — *does one angel illumine another?* — Aquinas: yes; the higher angel can *strengthen the natural light* of the lower angel and *manifest truth* to it, on the model of teacher-and-student (the teacher takes a *very simple deep concept* and *breaks it up into pieces* for the student). The contemporary mind-monist translation: in the conscious-agent-network frame (Hoffman), agents-with-greater-substrate-coupling can *transmit* substrate-pattern-information to agents-with-less-substrate-coupling; in Wolfram's frame, computationally-richer rule-instantiations can *generate* pedagogical sequences for less-rich instantiations. The medieval pedagogical model — the higher decomposes the simple-and-deep for the lower — is structurally what *progressive disclosure* in any sufficiently-articulated computational system looks like.

a.2 — *does one angel move another's will?* — Aquinas: yes, but only *by persuasion*, not coercion — by *manifesting some created good or ordered to God's goodness* and thereby *inclining the will to the love of the creature or of God by way of persuasion*. The contemporary frame: agent-to-agent will-influence is *non-coercive* across all C2A2 frames because the will's *inclination-toward-goodness* is substrate-given, not agent-to-agent transmissible. Agent-to-agent influence can supply *which good is salient* but cannot override the substrate-given goodness-orientation.

a.3 — *can the inferior angel illumine the superior?* — Aquinas: no; *inferior angels never illumine the superior, but are always illumined by them, because as cause is ordered to cause, so is order to order*; *this is the divine unalterable law: that inferior things are led to God by the superior* (Pseudo-Dionysius). The contemporary translation: in the multi-scale-agency frame, substrate-information-flow *down* the agency-hierarchy is the standard pattern; *up*-flow is structurally limited. Hoffman's conscious-agent-network frame: nodes with greater substrate-coupling supply substrate-pattern-information to nodes with less; the reverse direction is constrained by the lesser-coupling.

a.4 — *does the superior angel illumine the inferior as regards all he himself knows?* — Aquinas: no; the inferior receives *as much as is able to be received* given the inferior's lesser participation in substrate-goodness. Aquinas reaches for *every creature participates in the divine goodness so as to diffuse the good it possesses to others; for it is of the nature of good to communicate itself to others* — which Habash flags (rightly) as one of *the most profound spiritual and metaphysical axioms there is*. The contemporary mind-monist translation: in Fredrickson's positivity-resonance frame, substrate-goodness *diffuses* across constitutive relational coupling; in Levin's diverse-intelligences frame, substrate-pattern-information propagates across multi-scale-agency networks. The medieval *bonum diffusivum sui* preserves with full force across the contemporary frames and is structurally the *substrate's continuous self-giving* claim at every level.

## The central-theme thread

Day 54's eight Q.105 articles and four Q.106 articles converge on the *substrate's mediated and immediate action* shape. The substrate acts immediately on matter-to-form, on body, on intellect, on will, in every agent (Q.105 a.1-5); it can act outside the established order of nature without acting against itself (Q.105 a.6); the *miraculous* is substrate-action outside the usually-instantiated channels (Q.105 a.7-8). The angels acting on each other (Q.106) extend the same pattern at the agent-to-agent scale: agents-with-greater-substrate-coupling *illumine* and *persuade* agents-with-lesser-coupling; the *bonum diffusivum sui* is the underlying axiom.

Augustine's *late have I loved Thee, O Beauty so ancient and so new... for behold thou wert within me and I outside* is the day's contemplative keystone and threads directly through the central theme. The alter is *individuated by perspective-limitation*; the substrate is *more interior to the alter than the alter is to itself*; the alter's progressive recognition of the substrate's interiority is *salvation in fear and trembling*; the substrate's continuous self-giving sustenance and its substrate-mediated action on matter, body, intellect, and will is the structured operation by which the alter is led toward loving unity. Rohr's *Universal Christ* contemplative frame and Wright's *Philippians 2:12-13* scriptural frame converge precisely on this shape.
