# Day 65 — Contemporary Parallel: *The Structure of the Will and What Moves It*

Day 64 established the structural-and-diagnostic apparatus of voluntary action and the load-bearing case of willed ignorance. Day 65 continues the formal analysis: Q.8 (the will in regard to what it wills — 3 articles) and Q.9 (what moves the will — 6 articles). The contemporary mind-monist frame engages this as the *causal architecture of policy-selection*, with the medieval *will is of good only* and *will is moved by intellect, sense appetite, itself, and God* mapping onto the contemporary multi-source-prior frame of active inference.

## Frame

Q.8 a.1: the will is of good only — even when we choose moral or physical evils, we will them not as evils-in-themselves but under some *aspect of good* (a perceived good, an annexed good, a means to a good). This is the formal-versus-material distinction at the will-level: formally the will is always toward the good; materially the will can mistake what is good.

Q.8 a.2: the will is of the end *and* the means. Both fall under the *aspect good* — one as good-in-itself (end), the other as good-as-conducive-to-end (means).

Q.8 a.3: the will is moved by *the same act* to the end and to the means *when* the means is willed precisely *because* of the end (the end is the formal cause of willing the means). Otherwise distinct acts.

Q.9 covers movers of the will: a.1 the intellect (the will is moved by the intellect's apprehension of good); a.2 the will moves itself in willing the means by virtue of willing the end; a.3 the will moves itself in willing what it has not yet willed; a.4 the sensitive appetite (passion) can move the will by altering the apprehension; a.5 the will is moved by exterior bodies (only mediately, through sense apprehension); a.6 the will is moved by God (as primary mover, without removing voluntariness).

## Problem

The problem (P): given that the alter's will is constitutively directed toward the universal good, and given that the alter's perception of particular goods is filtered through interface-and-passion-and-habit, what is the *causal structure* of the alter's actual policy-selection in any given case? Multiple sources contribute — apprehension, passion, the will's own self-movement, exterior bodies, God — and the alter's task is to coordinate these so that the resulting policy actually aligns with the substrate-source's loving unity.

## Resource

**Friston on hierarchical generative models and means-end policy.** Active inference at the hierarchical level: the agent has terminal priors (ends) and instrumental priors (means); policy-selection minimizes expected free energy at multiple levels jointly. The medieval *will is of end and means under one aspect-good* preserves as the hierarchical-prior alignment that makes the mean-policy converge on the end-policy.

**McGilchrist on hemispheric coordination in willed action.** The right hemisphere's openness to the gestalt-of-the-good and the left hemisphere's analytical detail-work coordinate in willed action. The medieval *will-moves-itself* (Q.9 a.2-3) preserves under the hemispheric account as the alter's self-directing capacity to bring its own attention and analysis into coordination toward the perceived good.

**Stump on will-as-faculty-of-the-good and second-personal-knowing.** The will is constitutively directed toward the good; the second-personal mode of knowing is the will's full mode of operation in relation to other persons. The medieval *will is of good only* preserves as the will's constitutive directedness, with the formal-good remaining the same even when the material-good is mis-located.

**Hawkins on HTM goal-representation.** Goals are stable patterns at high-level cortical columns; lower-level columns instantiate the goal-pattern through means-policies. The medieval end-and-means structure preserves under the HTM frame.

**Levin on multi-scale agency and will-analog at biological scales.** Cells and tissues exhibit goal-directedness at sub-personal scales. The will-as-faculty preserves cleanly because the directedness-toward-integration is multi-scale; the alter's will is the personal-scale instance of a multi-scale dynamics.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.8: the alter's policy-selection is constitutively directed toward what it apprehends as good (terminal priors set the orientation); means and end are integrated under the hierarchical-prior frame; the same policy-selection-act covers both when the means is willed under the end's formal aspect.

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.9: policy-selection has multiple causal contributors — (a) the intellect's apprehension shapes the priors; (b) the alter's own self-movement constitutes the will's directing-itself toward what is not-yet-willed; (c) the sensitive appetite (passion) modulates the apprehension; (d) exterior bodies contribute through sense; (e) the substrate-source's primary causal sustaining of the alter's full causal-structure is constitutive. The medieval claim that *God moves the will without removing voluntariness* preserves under the substrate-source-as-primary-cause-of-secondary-causes frame from Day 59.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the formal universality of the will's directedness; the multi-source causal structure; the primary/secondary cause distinction.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas's frame is faculty-psychological-and-Aristotelian, the contemporary frame uses hierarchical active inference, hemispheric coordination, HTM, and multi-scale agency.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Friston, Hawkins, Levin, McGilchrist all empirically and formally substantial; the substrate-source-as-primary-cause-of-will is metaphysically continuous with mind-monism but receives different treatments under Kastrup vs. theistic readings.

## The central-theme thread

Today's day reinforces the central-theme commitment that the alter's will is *constitutively perspective-limited but formally directed toward the universal good*. The will is not free in the sense of being unmoored; it is constitutively directed. But its perception of what *is* the universal good in any given particular case is filtered through interface, passion, habit, and the substrate-network's pedagogical care or its inverse.

*Salvation in fear and trembling* under this register is the alter's daily-task work of *coordinating its multiple movement-sources toward the substrate-source's loving unity*. The alter cannot will arbitrarily; it is constitutively oriented. The work is to recognize the formal directedness, to refine the apprehension under second-person care, to manage the passions toward right-perception, to receive the substrate-network's pedagogy, and so to align the multi-source policy-selection with the substrate-source's own integrative dynamics.

Wright on covenanted-community as the locus of will-formation. Rohr on contemplative-prayer as the alter's deliberate alignment of multi-source movement under the substrate-source's loving primary causation. Stump on the second-personally-known-good as the formal completion of the will's directedness.
