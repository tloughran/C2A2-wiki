# Day 73 — Contemporary Parallel: *The Moral Status of the Passions and the Order Anchored on Love*

Day 72 established the embodied-affective-interface frame for the eleven passions. Day 73 takes up Q.24 (the moral status of the passions) and Q.25 (the order among the passions). The medieval thesis is that passions, considered in themselves, are morally neutral; that rectified passions add to the moral act rather than subtract; and that there is a structural order among the passions, with love as the first. The contemporary mind-monist frame engages each thesis through active-inference, the broaden-and-build/positivity-ratio research program, and the second-personal-knowing intervention.

## Frame

Q.24 articulates the moral status of the passions in four articles. *Are passions in themselves morally good or evil?* (a.1): No — passions, considered as movements of the sensitive appetite, are morally neutral; they become morally evaluable only insofar as they are subject to the rule of reason and will. *Does every passion of the soul make the act morally evil?* (a.2): No — Stoic asceticism is rejected; not every passion is a perturbation that diminishes moral worth. *Does passion increase or diminish the moral character of an act?* (a.3): When passion *follows* the judgment of reason and is *willed* into participation, it *increases* the goodness or malice of the act (because more of the alter is engaged); when passion *precedes* and *forces* the judgment, it diminishes voluntariness and so diminishes moral character. *Is any passion morally good or evil in its species?* (a.4): Yes — some passions take their species from an object that is intrinsically good or evil (e.g., envy, which is sorrow over another's good, is evil in its species).

Q.25 articulates the *order* among the passions in four articles. *Are the irascible passions before the concupiscible?* (a.1): No — the concupiscible passions are prior in the order of generation. *Is love the first concupiscible passion?* (a.2): Yes — love is the first movement of the appetite; all other passions are reducible to it. *Is hope before love?* (a.3): No — love is prior. *Is anger the last passion?* (a.4): Yes — anger is structurally the most complex and presupposes the prior passions of love and sorrow.

## Problem

The problem (P): the alter's affective register is information-bearing signal — but how should the alter relate to that signal morally? Are passions a perturbation to be suppressed (the Stoic move), or an asset to be cultivated and ordered? And given that they are an asset, what is the *structural order* among them, and what is the implication of *love* being structurally prior to all others?

## Resource

**Aquinas's neutral-passions thesis (Q.24 a.1) under active inference.** The medieval claim that passions are morally neutral *in themselves* preserves under Friston's frame: precision-weighted prediction error is information-bearing signal whose moral status depends on what generative-model and prior-structure are doing the weighting. The signal itself is neither moral nor immoral; the prior structure determines whether the signal-conditioned policy aligns with the alter's formal direction toward loving-unity. Stoic suppression of affect is, in active-inference terms, suppression of the agent's primary information channel — counterproductive precision-collapse rather than virtuous self-regulation.

**Aquinas's rectified-passions-add thesis (Q.24 a.3) under broaden-and-build.** Fredrickson's empirical work shows that positive affect, in appropriate ratio to negative affect, broadens cognitive-behavioral repertoire and supports flourishing; suppression of affect (low overall affective bandwidth) correlates with reduced behavioral flexibility and reduced relational integration. This is the empirical analog of Aquinas's claim that passion-following-judgment-and-willed-into-participation *increases* the moral worth of the act. The alter who acts *with* affective integration acts with more of itself engaged; the alter who acts despite suppressed affect acts with reduced alter-integration.

**Aquinas's irrational-passion-precedes-judgment problem (Q.24 a.3 second part) under Stump.** When passion precedes and forces judgment — the addict's craving, the rage that bypasses reflection, the despair that closes off action — voluntariness diminishes and moral character diminishes with it. Stump's *Wandering in Darkness* analyzes exactly this: the alter under perspective-limitation can be captured by a passion that misaligns with its formal direction; the response is not suppression but *rectification* through second-personal relationships in which an outside alter holds the diagnostic for the captured alter and presents it across the perspective-gap.

**Aquinas's intrinsic-evil-in-species thesis (Q.24 a.4) under Levin and Friston.** Passions like envy (sorrow over another's good) are intrinsically misaligned with the substrate-source's integrative dynamics: envy is structurally directed against the substrate-source-integration of another alter. This preserves under Levin's form-as-pattern-extending: envy is form-deficient at the affective level, structurally analogous to a morphogenetic-pattern that opposes its own substrate-network's integration. Under Friston's frame: envy is a precision-weighted-prior that has internalized an inversion of the substrate-source's integrative direction.

**Aquinas's love-is-first thesis (Q.25 a.2) under active-inference and broaden-and-build.** Active inference confirms structurally that *valence-encoding* (the love/hate axis) is foundational to all subsequent affective registers — there is no policy-selection without prior preferences, and prior preferences encode primary valence. Fredrickson's positivity-resonance research shows empirically that love (positivity-resonance with another alter) is the broadening, generative state out of which other positive affective regimes (joy, hope, daring, integrative-anger-against-injustice) flow. The medieval *love is first* recovers as the active-inference structural finding that valence-orientation is foundational to all downstream affective computation.

**McGilchrist on hemispheric pathology of affect.** Disordered passions correlate with closure of right-hemispheric receptivity and a left-hemispheric stuck-mode. The medieval *passion forces judgment against reason* preserves as the McGilchrist *left-hemispheric hijack* under affective-pressure that closes the right-hemispheric integrative reception.

**Levin on affective-rectification as substrate-realignment.** Bioelectric-substrate work suggests that affective regimes are reflected in whole-organism integrative-state; cultivating rectified passions is therefore not just psychological self-regulation but substrate-realignment work that retunes the alter's formal-pattern-extension into the substrate-network.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.24-25: passions are precision-weighted information-bearing signal under the alter's generative-model; their moral status depends on whether the prior-structure that weights them is aligned with the substrate-source's integrative dynamics (formal direction toward loving-unity). Rectified passions — passions whose prior-structure is formally aligned — *increase* the moral bandwidth of the alter's action because they engage more of the alter in formally-directed action. Disordered passions — passions whose prior-structure is misaligned — *diminish* moral bandwidth because they hijack policy-selection away from the formal direction. The work of moral life under perspective-limitation is therefore not *suppression* of passion (which collapses the information channel) but *rectification* of the prior-structure that weights affective signal.

The order among the passions (Q.25) recovers as the structural-precedence ordering of an active-inference architecture: valence (love/hate) is foundational; desire/aversion follows as future-policy direction; joy/sorrow follows as present-state-evaluation; the irascible regimes (hope/despair, fear/daring, anger) presuppose the concupiscible foundation and add the *difficulty-of-attainment* register. Anger as the structurally-last passion reflects its computational-complexity: it integrates valence + present-state + obstructed-policy + threat-response + targeted action.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the neutral-passions thesis; the rectified-passions-add thesis; the love-is-first ordering thesis; the rejection of Stoic suppression.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas's frame is faculty-psychological-virtue-theoretic, the contemporary frame uses active-inference precision-weighting, broaden-and-build empirical research, second-personal-knowing for affective rectification, and bioelectric-substrate realignment.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: the neutral-passions and rectified-passions theses are theological-philosophical strong with active-inference and Fredrickson empirical support; Stump's willed-affect analysis is philosophical-theological with substantial book-length backing; Levin's bioelectric-substrate-of-affect is empirically suggestive though under-explored at affective-systems scale.

## The central-theme thread

The central theme says that conscious agents are individuated by perspective-limitation while constitutively directed-formally toward loving-unity. Day 73 articulates the *affective-cultivation* register of this structure: the alter's affective interface registers substrate-source-integration-vs-disintegration as feeling, and the alter's *responsibility* is to cultivate the prior-structure that weights this feeling so that affective signal serves rather than hijacks the formal direction. *Love is first* is the central-theme keystone at the affective level: every other affective register in the alter's life is downstream of the alter's primary valence-orientation. Salvation in fear and trembling at this register includes the daily-task work of *retuning the primary valence* — not by introspective effort alone, which under perspective-limitation is unreliable, but through *second-personal relationships in which another alter's love retunes the alter's affective interface* (Stump's second-personal-knowing intervention), through *contemplative practice* (Rohr) that cultivates non-grasping integrative-receptivity, and through the *substrate-source's grace-as-affective-rectification* (the patristic and Wesleyan tradition's *prevenient grace* recovered in the mind-monist register as the substrate-source's pattern-communication that reaches the alter's affective interface even before reflective awareness). The Stoic move toward suppression is structurally *opposed* to the central-theme: it cuts the alter's primary information channel about substrate-source-integration, replacing perspective-limited-but-living signal with no-signal.
