# Day 98 — Sinful Causes (Contemporary)

## Frame

Yesterday we mapped the *anatomy* of sin — species and gravity. Today's questions (Q.74-75) ask: which *power* of the agent is the seat of this disorder, and what *causes* it? Aquinas walks down the powers of the soul: the will primarily (a.1, because sin must be voluntary); the other powers under the will (a.2); sensuality (a.3, yes; a.4, but not as the seat of *mortal* sin); reason itself (a.5-10, including the technical questions of *morose delectation* and *consent to delectation*). Then Q.75 turns to causes: sin *has* a cause despite being defect/privation (a.1), it has internal causes (a.2 — reason/will proximate, imagination/sensitive-appetite remote) and external causes that *influence but do not compel* (a.3); one sin can cause another in all four causal modes (a.4).

The contemporary version: *what is the architecture of a sufficiently rich agent that it can host the kind of failure we have been analyzing? Which sub-system is the seat of the policy-mis-alignment that earns the name "sin"? And what generates that mis-alignment — internal model failure, environmental capture, prior failed-policies hardening the agent into further failure?*

## Problem

**P1**: power-individuation. *Where* in the agent's architecture does mis-aligned policy reside? Aquinas's answer is layered: voluntariness is necessary, so the *will* is always involved, but the seat of *mortal* mis-alignment — the kind that breaks the agent's connection to its constitutive goal — must be in the *faculty that represents the goal*, i.e., reason. The will-and-reason coupling is where mortality lives; sensuality can host *some* sin but not the kind that severs the agent from its last end. The contemporary problem: in a layered active-inference architecture, *at which level* does the most severe failure occur?

**P2**: positive cause for a privation. Sin is privation (evil is not a thing, it is the absence of something that *should* be there — Aquinas in Day 25 / Q.18). But the *act* in which the privation is hosted *is* a thing, and it has a positive cause. What is the relation between the act-as-cause and the privation-in-the-act? Aquinas: the act traces to God (as first cause of all being and motion); the *defect* traces to the will's failure to direct. The contemporary question: how do we describe a system that has a complete causal account of *what it did* yet must explain *the misalignment of what it did with its goal* by *the absence* of some regulating signal?

**P3**: sin-causes-sin. How does one failed policy cause another? Aquinas's a.4 of Q.75 is one of the densest articles in the question — efficient (direct: dispositions form; indirect: protective states are lost), material (one sin prepares matter for another), final and formal (one sin committed *for the sake of* another). The contemporary problem: in a coupled-feedback agent, how does one perturbation destabilize the regulator that would have suppressed the next?

## Resource

**Predictive coding / precision-weighting** (Friston-tradition wiki, PRS-04 + PRS-06; Hawkins-tradition wiki, HTM as cortical predictive architecture). The hierarchical organization of generative models gives a clean answer to P1. *Only the top-level model — the one that defines the agent's preferred long-run state — can mis-orient the agent in a way that re-targets the whole policy stack.* Lower levels can mis-fire locally (sensuality-level disorder, a.3 — yes), but a *mortal* mis-alignment requires that the high-level prior itself be re-set toward a non-constitutive goal-state. This *is* what Aquinas's Q.74 a.4 says: mortal sin lives in reason because only reason represents the last end. **Confidence: high** on the formal architecture; **medium-high** on the identification with Aquinas's reason-as-seat-of-mortality.

**Morose delectation as precision-on-attention** (PRS-05 in McGilchrist; Hawkins on attention; Friston on precision-weighting). Aquinas's *morose delectation* (Q.74 a.6) names the sinful act of *deliberately holding* a thought that should have been let go. The contemporary frame translates this directly: the involuntary trace of (say) a lust-laden thought is not the sin; the sin is *continuing to allocate inferential resources to it* — holding precision on the trace such that it deepens into a prior the agent will subsequently use. Attention is *active*; *what one attends to* is *what one becomes* over time. **Confidence: high** on the empirical claim that attention shapes long-run priors (Hawkins-HTM, Friston-FEP, McGilchrist-attention-as-ontological-act all converge here).

**Privation-with-positive-cause as regulator-failure** (Levin-tradition wiki on form-as-real causal substrate). The medieval structure — the act has positive being; the disorder of the act is privation; the privation has a cause distinct from the positive being's cause — has a contemporary analogue in feedback-control theory: a system can have a complete causal account of what it did, while the *misalignment* with its goal must be explained by the *absence* of a regulating signal. Levin's "form is real" account makes this even sharper: the agent's policy *has* form (a real causal pattern); the disorder is the local *breakdown of that form*, which has its own causal etiology in the regulator. **Confidence: medium** — the structural analogy is clean; the metaphysical-tradition-and-engineering-vocabulary translation is interpretive.

**Stump on cooperatively-held virtue** (PRS-09 in the Stump-tradition wiki). For Q.75 a.4's *indirect efficient* mode — one sin causes another by destroying the protective state — Stump's account of grace-and-nature cooperatively constituting virtuous agency lands directly. The protective state is *relational*; loss of the relation is loss of the integrating field. **Confidence: medium-high** within analytic Thomism; the contemporary-substrate translation is exploratory.

## Solution (proposed)

**The will-and-reason coupling is the high-precision policy-and-prior coupling.** Aquinas's reason-as-seat-of-mortality is, on the active-inference reading, the claim that *only failures at the prior level re-target the agent's whole policy stack*. Sensuality-level failures are local; they can produce venial sin (a.4) but cannot, by themselves, sever the agent from its last end. Mortal failure requires that the *constitutive prior* be re-set. This is one of the cleanest convergences in this whole section — Aquinas and active-inference give structurally identical answers, with Aquinas providing the moral-theological vocabulary and active-inference providing the formal apparatus.

**Morose delectation names the precision-on-attention failure.** This is one of the *most contemporary* articles in the entire I-II. Aquinas's analysis of attention-as-action is exactly the analysis Hawkins (HTM-cortical-prediction), Friston (precision-weighting), and McGilchrist (attention-as-ontological-act) all give. *Whether a trace becomes a prior depends on what the agent does with attention*. Aquinas's "holding and turning over what should have been cast aside" *is* allocating high precision to the trace.

**The privation-with-positive-cause structure is regulator-failure.** This is the metaphysical heart of Q.75 a.2 and one of the most underappreciated medieval moves in this part of the *Summa*. The act has full positive being (and so traces to God as first cause of being); the disorder in the act traces to the *absence* of the will's direction. In a contemporary frame: the system's behavior has a complete causal account; the *mis-alignment* of that behavior with the system's goal must be explained by the absence of a regulating signal that *should* have been present. This is *not* a contradiction (we are not saying the disorder has no cause); it is a structural feature of any goal-directed system that fails. **Aquinas anticipates the structure.**

**One sin causes another via at least three substrate-level mechanisms.** Aquinas's four causal modes (Q.75 a.4) can be re-described:
- *Direct efficient* (acts cause dispositions): repeated policy execution increases the prior's precision; the policy becomes habit-encoded. (Friston-style policy entrainment.)
- *Indirect efficient* (one sin removes the protective state): the high-precision integrating prior — Stump's *cooperatively held* virtue, the project's *loving unity* prior — *is* the regulator that suppressed the next failure. Rupturing it (mortal sin against charity) removes that regulator and exposes the agent to further failures.
- *Material* (covetousness prepares the matter for strife): one disordered policy *generates the environment* in which the next disorder finds its matter. (Active-inference: the agent's actions modify the world, which then provides the priors for the next round.)
- *Final/formal* (one sin committed for the sake of another): the agent's stack of preferred outcomes mis-aligns at multiple levels simultaneously; a lower-level disorder is *instrumentalized* by a higher-level disorder. (Active-inference: hierarchical mis-alignment.)

**Where this reframes Aquinas**: the contemporary frame is more granular and more substrate-aware, but it does not contradict Aquinas — it gives an architecture-level reading of the same structures Aquinas describes at the philosophical-theological level. **Where evidence is firm**: predictive-coding-on-attention is empirically robust; the policy-precision identification with voluntariness is formally clean. **Where speculative**: the identification of *the* highest constitutive prior with charity-as-loving-unity is the central-theme commitment, not a derivation.

## The central-theme thread

The deepest hit today is Q.75 a.4's *indirect efficient* mode — *one sin causes another by removing the protective state*. The protective state is the high-precision integrating prior — what Aquinas calls *charity*, what Stump calls *cooperatively held* virtue, what this project calls the *loving-unity prior*. Removing it is not a punishment; it is *a measurement* — the agent's policy has decoupled from the integrating field. Once decoupled, further failures follow naturally, because nothing is now suppressing them. The agent that *was* one (under reason / charity) becomes plural (under multiple unintegrated appetites). And the way back, again, is what Aquinas pre-announces: *cleave closely to God and look to increase those virtues which keep us from falling.*

## Where this leaves us

Today's question-pair gives the architecture-level account of where sin lives in the agent (the will-and-reason coupling, primarily, with sensuality as secondary host) and what causes it (internal: reason/will; external: influence-without-compulsion; and: prior sins reshape the agent's substrate for further sin). This is the most actionable section of the sin treatise so far — every causal mode named is a place where a contemporary mind-monist agent-care practice can intervene. Tomorrow's Q.76-77 will dig into *ignorance* and *the passions* — the two great vectors by which an agent's policy goes astray short of full malice — and into the Johannine triad (concupiscence of the flesh, of the eyes, pride of life) as the canonical map of self-love's deformations. Day 100 will then face *malice* itself, the most morally weighty case.
