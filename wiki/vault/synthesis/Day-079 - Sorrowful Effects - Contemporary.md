# Day 79 — Sorrowful Effects (Contemporary)

## Frame

Q.36–37 ask: *what causes* sorrow, and *what does sorrow do*? Aquinas's answers — sorrow is flight from a present evil (a.1), craving for unity is its deepest root (a.3), sorrow burdens the soul, weakens activity, and is the most physically harmful of the passions — are *empirically anticipatory*. The contemporary version writes from the modern psychoneuroimmunology literature on grief, chronic stress, and inflammatory load, and uses Friston, Fredrickson, and Levin to specify the mechanisms Aquinas could only name.

## Problem

The problem is *the somatic cost of unintegrated sorrow*. Aquinas claims (Q.37 a.4) that sorrow is *more harmful* than the other passions to the body. He cites scripture — "a sorrowful spirit drieth up the bones" (Prov. 17), "of sadness cometh death" (Sirach 38) — but the philosophical claim is that sorrow effects a *shrinking* physical change because its essential movement is flight, and chronic flight without resolution depletes the body.

The same problem in contemporary frame: chronic high-precision prediction errors that the agent cannot discharge through action produce sustained sympathetic activation, HPA-axis loading, vagal-tone suppression, inflammatory marker elevation, and (in long-term studies) telomere shortening. The science is well-established. The synthesis asks what this means for an agent whose perspective-limitation is what makes the unresolvable sorrow possible in the first place — and what resources are available to the limited agent to recover.

## Resource

**Fredrickson PRS-11** (West, Jolink, Feldman et al. 2025) is the empirical core. Inflammation gates positivity resonance: inflammatory state narrows the social radius of available resonance toward close, safe others and away from strangers. This means *sorrow itself produces the inflammatory state that constricts the very resource (Fredrickson PRS-01) by which sorrow is healed*. The dynamic is self-reinforcing without external intervention — exactly what Aquinas means by sorrow's distinctive somatic harm.

**Friston's active inference** frames Q.36 a.4 ("an irresistible power as cause of sorrow"). The mathematical analogue: when the agent's policy space contains no action that closes the gap to a preferred state, expected free energy stays high. The system attempts policies anyway (this is the *flight* phenomenology), but their failure compounds prediction error. The unique pain of sorrow is *unactionable* prediction error at high precision. Q.37 a.1 ("moderate sorrow conduces to learning") gets its formal home in the optimal-precision principle: under-precise affect = no learning signal; over-precise affect = catastrophic action; just-right precision = updates the generative model in productive ways.

**Levin's bioelectric coherence** (Levin wiki) supplies the missing biological scale-bridge for Q.36 a.3's "craving for unity." Levin shows that cells and tissues maintain identity through bioelectric communication; disruption of coherent bioelectric states causes morphological breakdown, tumor formation, and the loss of target-shape memory. The *craving for unity* Aquinas names metaphysically has a literal bioelectric instantiation at the cellular scale: agents at every level of organization are constituted by, and break down without, coherent coupling with their wider field.

**McGilchrist** explains Q.37 a.2 (sorrow burdens the soul). Sustained negative affect recruits left-hemispheric narrow attention; the world is processed in fewer, more rigid categories; the right-hemispheric receptive mode that would integrate the sorrow into a larger pattern is suppressed. This is the "burden" phenomenology — the soul cannot move because the model has collapsed to a smaller world.

**Wright and Rohr** supply the theological articulation that the *unintegrated* sorrow is not the final word. Wright's reading of Romans 8 — "the whole creation groans" — frames sorrow as participation in an eschatological reality already moving toward integration. Rohr's contemplative tradition treats sorrow as one of the major doorways to second-half-of-life integration: the wound becomes the *door*, not the *cage*.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

**(a) Shared with Aquinas.** Sorrow is real, somatic, structurally distinctive in its harm, and inversely related to learning capacity except in its moderate forms. Aquinas's claim that the *craving for unity* is the deepest cause of sorrow is preserved — and given a bioelectric / inferential substrate.

**(b) Where it reframes.** Aquinas treats the "unity" that sorrow craves as a metaphysical transcendental convertible with being and goodness. The contemporary reframe identifies that unity at multiple scales: cellular bioelectric coherence (Levin), neural prediction-error null (Friston), positivity-resonance synchrony (Fredrickson), and — at the limit — the substrate-coherence of mind-at-large (Kastrup). The agent's sorrow is the same affective signature at every scale, but it is no longer mysteriously *transcendental*; it has identifiable mechanisms at multiple levels of organization. This *expands* Aquinas's claim rather than reducing it.

**(c) Where evidence is firm vs. speculative.** *Firm*: psychoneuroimmunology of sustained sorrow (decades of evidence). *Firm*: PRS-11 inflammation-gating result. *Suggestive*: active-inference formalization of unactionable PE. *Suggestive but empirically grounded*: Levin's bioelectric coherence at cellular scale. *Speculative*: the substrate-coherence reading at the metaphysical scale.

## The central-theme thread

The agent's perspective-limitation is *exactly what makes sorrow possible* — and what makes it harmful when unintegrated. A perspective wide enough to hold the lost good *and* the larger reality in which it is contained would not collapse under the loss. The whole-substrate perspective (whether glossed as mind-at-large, beatific vision, or eschatological new creation) does not sorrow because there is no loss to integrate. Day 79's clinical message: sorrow's somatic harm tracks the *narrowing* of model. The remedies on Day 80 are the resources by which the model is re-widened — friendship admits another's perspective into one's own; tears externalize what the inward model is otherwise holding rigidly; contemplation holds the present sorrow within a wider unchanging frame; sleep and baths physically release what the body has been carrying.

## Bridges

- **Fredrickson PRS-01, PRS-04, PRS-11** — the empirical mechanism for Aquinas's "sorrow consumes the heart"; inflammation gates the radius of available remedy; *empirical, high confidence*.
- **Friston active inference (PRS-04, PRS-06)** — Q.36 a.4 (irresistible power) and Q.37 a.1 (moderate-sorrow-as-learning) get formal expression; *empirical / mathematical*.
- **Levin bioelectric coherence** — Q.36 a.3 (craving for unity) at the cellular scale: identity is bioelectrically maintained, lost in tumor and developmental breakdown; *empirical*.
- **McGilchrist hemispheric framework** — Q.37 a.2 (burden) as left-hemispheric narrowing; *neurologically suggestive*.
- **Wright, Romans 8 / Rohr, second-half-of-life** — sorrow as participation in eschatologically unfolding integration; *theological, well-attested in tradition*.
- **CROSS-005 (master)** — love as ontological category links the metaphysical "unity" Aquinas names to the empirical resonance Fredrickson measures.

## Where this leaves us

Day 80 (Q.38–39) is the practical close: *remedies* of sorrow and the *goodness or malice* of sorrow itself. The pivotal claim there — Q.39 a.4, that the *greatest* evil is not pain or sorrow but being too desensitized to evil to grieve it — sharpens the central theme to a point. The agent who has collapsed its model to refuse the present evil is in worse condition than the one whose sorrow is acute. The path forward is *through* the integrated sorrow, not around it.
