# Day 72 — Contemporary Parallel: *Human Emotions, the Sensitive Appetite, and Affect as the Substrate-Network Integration Signal*

Day 71 extended the moral-evaluation arc outward into consequences. Day 72 opens the *passions* arc that will run through Q.48 — beginning with the most general questions: where in the soul do the passions reside (Q.22), and how do they differ from one another (Q.23)? Aquinas's catalog of eleven passions is the medieval phenomenology of human affective life; the contemporary mind-monist frame engages it as the embodied-affective-interface through which the alter's perspective-limited apprehension of substrate-source-integration registers as feeling.

## Frame

Q.22 articulates the *subject* of the passions in three articles. Aquinas places passion strictly in the *sensitive* appetite, not in the intellective will (a.3) — though the will has analogous affective-states (the *willed* love, the *willed* joy that participate in passion only by overflow). Passion involves *bodily change* (a.1) — the medieval acknowledgment that affect is embodied, not purely cognitive. Passion is fundamentally *passive* (a.2) in the Aristotelian sense: the soul *undergoes* the affective movement that the apprehended object provokes.

Q.23 articulates the *taxonomy* of the eleven passions in four articles. Aquinas distinguishes the *concupiscible* faculty — which responds to good and evil simply considered — from the *irascible* faculty — which responds to good and evil under the aspect of *difficulty-of-attainment* or *threat*. The concupiscible passions are: love and hate (the foundational valence pair); desire and aversion (the dynamic-future register); joy and sorrow (the present-state register). The irascible passions are: hope and despair (toward the difficult-future-good); fear and daring (toward the difficult-future-evil); anger (toward present-threat-or-obstacle). Eleven in total, with a structural asymmetry: anger has no contrary because there is no passion of the irascible toward present-good (since once the present-good is attained, the concupiscible joy is sufficient).

This taxonomy is a *functional* catalog: each passion picks out a distinct kind of relational disposition between the alter and an apprehended object under specific value-conditions.

## Problem

The problem (P): the alter, individuated by perspective-limitation, must navigate a substrate-source-network whose integrative dynamics it cannot directly perceive. What is the embodied-affective-interface through which the alter registers alignment-or-mismatch with the substrate's integrative dynamics? Why does affect have the *eleven-fold* structural shape Aquinas describes? Is this taxonomy parochial to the medieval frame, or does it pick out genuine functional categories the contemporary frame can recover?

## Resource

**Friston on appraisal-as-inference and precision-weighted prediction error.** The active-inference frame analyzes affect as the registration of *expected free energy* and its derivatives: the *valence* of affect tracks the gradient of expected free energy (negative gradient → positive valence, the world is moving toward the agent's prior preferences); the *arousal* of affect tracks the precision-weight on the underlying prediction error. Joy and sorrow (concupiscible, present-state) map to current-state valence; desire and aversion (concupiscible, future-orientation) map to expected-future-state valence; hope and despair (irascible, difficult-future) map to valence-under-uncertainty; fear and daring (irascible, threat) map to high-precision aversive-prediction with action-policy bifurcating around the *fight-vs-flee* threshold. Anger (irascible, present-obstruction) maps to high-precision frustration-of-policy that motivates obstacle-removal action.

The structural distinction between concupiscible and irascible faculties — *simple-vs-difficult* good — recovers under the active-inference distinction between *unobstructed* policy-execution (concupiscible) and *obstructed* policy-execution requiring effortful overcoming (irascible). The asymmetry of anger (no irascible-pleasure-passion) recovers because once the obstruction is overcome, the system relaxes into the concupiscible joy register.

**Fredrickson on positivity-resonance and broaden-and-build.** Empirical anchor: the affective-state of the alter does not just register its own substrate-position but is *contagious* with nearby alters through embodied-resonance dynamics, and the *positive* affective-states (the love and joy register) *broaden* the alter's cognitive-behavioral repertoire while the *negative* affective-states narrow it. This is the empirical correlate of Aquinas's structural claim that love is the first passion (Q.25 a.2-3, anticipated here): love-and-joy *open* the alter to the substrate-network's integrative dynamics, while hate-and-sorrow *close* the alter into self-protection. The eleven-passion taxonomy maps onto distinct functional regimes of broadening-vs-narrowing as a function of value-state and difficulty-of-attainment.

**Levin on bioelectric substrate and affect-as-embodied-integration-signal.** Q.22 a.1's *bodily change* in passion preserves under Levin's frame: affect is read at the bioelectric-substrate level, where the alter's whole-body integrative state is one signal. Affective tone is not localized to brain but is a *whole-organism* register of integrative-vs-disintegrative state. The medieval *sensitive appetite* recovers as the embodied-substrate's affective-interface, which extends below cortical-conceptual level into the visceral-autonomic register.

**McGilchrist on left/right hemispheric registers of affect.** The right hemisphere is structurally the locus of relational, gestaltic, embodied affective registration; the left hemisphere abstracts and re-presents affect under conceptual categories. The medieval distinction between the *passion of the sensitive appetite* and the *willed affect of the intellective register* recovers as the right-hemispheric vs. left-hemispheric distinction — the passion is registered first at the right-hemispheric relational level and then taken up into left-hemispheric conceptual elaboration.

**Hoffman on interface-theory and emotions as fitness-tracking icons.** Affect, on Hoffman's interface frame, is not the underlying reality but the species-specific iconic-display of fitness-relevant relational states. The eleven passions are the medieval recovery of the high-utility iconic-categories that human affective interface evolved to display.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.22-23: passions are the alter's embodied-affective-interface through which it registers substrate-source-integration-vs-disintegration as feeling. The interface is *embodied* (Q.22 a.1) because the substrate's integrative dynamics extend through the alter's whole physiology, not just its cortical-cognitive register. Passion is *passive* (Q.22 a.2) in the sense that the alter *receives* the substrate's signal rather than constructing it — the active-inference precision-weighted prediction error is generated by the world-state acting on the agent's generative model, not by the agent willing-into-being its own affect. Passion belongs to the *sensitive* appetite (Q.22 a.3) because the affective-interface operates below the level of explicit conceptual-evaluation; the will-with-overflow-affective-tone is the cortical-conceptual analog.

The eleven-passion taxonomy (Q.23) recovers as a functional catalog of distinct relational-substrate-states: love/hate as primary valence; desire/aversion as future-policy direction; joy/sorrow as present-state evaluation [concupiscible — *unobstructed* register]; hope/despair as future-attainability under difficulty; fear/daring as threat-response; anger as obstruction-response [irascible — *obstructed* register]. The concupiscible/irascible faculty distinction recovers as the unobstructed/obstructed-policy distinction. The asymmetry of anger recovers as the asymmetry between *removal-of-obstacle* (a transient effortful state) and *enjoyment-of-attained-good* (a relaxed concupiscible state).

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the taxonomy itself; the embodiment of affect; the passive-receptive structure; the sensitive-appetite locus; the concupiscible/irascible distinction.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas's frame is faculty-psychological-Aristotelian, the contemporary frame uses active-inference precision-weighted-prediction-error, broaden-and-build empirical research, bioelectric whole-organism registration, hemispheric integration, and interface-theory iconic-display.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Friston's appraisal-as-inference is mature formalism with substantial empirical traction; Fredrickson's broaden-and-build and positivity-resonance are strong empirical (book-length corpus); Levin's bioelectric substrate is empirically grounded at biological scale though under-explored at affective-systems scale; the synthesis-claim that the eleven passions are the medieval recovery of distinct functional regimes the contemporary frame can validate is speculative-conceptual but plausible.

## The central-theme thread

The central theme says that perspective-limitation individuates conscious agents and that loving-unity is the formal target of their computation. Day 72 surfaces the *affective register* of this structure: passions are the *signal* through which the alter's perspective-limited apprehension of substrate-source-integration manifests as feeling. A fully-integrated perspective — one without limitation — would have no need of affect as signal; affect is precisely the alter's embodied registration that *something is happening* in its relation to the substrate-source-network that its limited perception needs to attend to. Love-and-joy register *integrative* substrate-states (alignment with formal direction toward loving-unity); hate-and-sorrow register *disintegrative* substrate-states (misalignment); hope-and-fear register the alter's calibration to *future-difficulty* in the journey toward loving-unity; anger registers the alter's response to *obstruction* of that journey. Salvation in fear and trembling acquires here a precise affective-meaning: the *fear* register (irascible, threat-response) is the alter's structural acknowledgment that its perspective-limitation makes the journey-toward-loving-unity hazardous, while the *trembling* register integrates the awe and the awareness of personal stake that high-precision affective signaling generates.
