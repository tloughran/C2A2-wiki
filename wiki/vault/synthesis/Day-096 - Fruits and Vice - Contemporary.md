# Day 96 — Fruits and Vice (Contemporary)

## Frame

Aquinas hands us a hinge. Q.70 closes the long treatise on habits and virtues with a question that is structurally unusual: what are the *fruits* of the Holy Spirit, and how do they differ from the virtues themselves? Q.71 then opens the treatise on vice with a definitional question: what *is* a vice, and what makes it contrary to nature? Two questions on one day, exactly at the junction. The medieval frame asks: what does virtuous life *feel like from inside* (the fruits — sweetness, delight), and what is the *structural failure mode* that virtue is virtue against (vice — disposition against the rational nature)?

The contemporary version asks the same two questions in a vocabulary that did not exist in the thirteenth century. *What is the experiential register that signals a goal-aligned policy in a limited rational agent? And what is the structural failure mode of such an agent — what goes wrong when the policy stops aligning with the goal?* In the central frame of this project — conscious agents individuated by perspective-limitation, with loving unity as the computational telos — Aquinas's two questions of Day 96 ask, in tandem: *what does it look like, from inside the limited perspective, when the agent is computing toward the telos, and what does it look like when it is not?*

## Problem

Two linked problems. **P1**: virtue-as-stable-disposition is structurally austere — it can describe what a virtuous agent *does*, but it has trouble accounting for the *sweetness* Aquinas insists must accompany right action. If a virtuous policy is just an output distribution, why does its execution carry a phenomenal register of delight? **P2**: vice-as-contrary-to-nature is structurally underdetermined — *which* nature does vice oppose, and *how* does the opposition show up causally in the agent? Aquinas answers "rational nature, and the opposition shows up as disorder in the act," but a contemporary account needs to say more about what *rational nature* picks out and *how* a disordered act differs causally from an ordered one.

These two problems are the same problem twice. Both ask: what is the *experiential and causal signature* of an agent's policy aligning with — or departing from — its constitutive goal-state?

## Resource

Three convergent resources from the mind-monism cluster.

**Fredrickson on positivity resonance** (the Fredrickson-tradition wiki, PRS-02 / PRS-05; CROSS-005 in the master index): love is not a sentiment but a *biophysiological coupling event* between conscious agents — vagal tone, oxytocin, neural synchrony as measurable correlates. Positive emotions in this framework do not merely *accompany* virtuous engagement; they *broaden attention*, which in turn *builds* lasting personal and relational resources. The broaden-and-build dynamic is empirically robust (high confidence in the wiki's track record). Crucially for Aquinas's Q.70, Fredrickson's account *gives the sweetness a job*: positivity is the very signal that allows the agent's internal model to align with the models of co-agents, which is the substrate-level mechanism of charity-as-coupling.

**Friston on active inference** (PRS-04 / PRS-06 in the Friston-tradition wiki): every persisting agent minimizes free energy by predicting its environment and acting to make its predictions come true. A *virtue* — in the active-inference idiom — is a high-precision prior over outcomes that the agent treats as constitutive of its own existence. *Acting from* the virtue is acting to bring the world into conformity with that prior. The phenomenal sweetness Aquinas wants is, on this account, the agent's *low-prediction-error signal* — the felt confirmation that the policy is succeeding.

**McGilchrist on hemispheric attention** (PRS-01 / PRS-05 in the McGilchrist-tradition wiki): right-hemisphere attention attends to the *living whole* — context, relation, the not-yet-categorized; left-hemisphere attention manipulates discrete known fragments. Aquinas's threefold ordering of the fruits (toward self / toward neighbor / toward what is beneath) is a list of *coordination tasks* — and coordination across self / neighbor / body is exactly what McGilchrist's right-hemispheric mode is for. The fruits are not just *acts*, they are the *acts of an agent attending in the integrative mode*.

## Solution (proposed, speculative-where-speculative)

The Aquinas-frame and the mind-monism frame converge on one synthesis claim: **the fruits of the Spirit are the experiential register of a limited rational agent's policy aligning with the loving-unity telos, and vice is the structural signature of that policy mis-aligning.** Aquinas reaches this conclusion theologically — virtue is sweet because it is *participation* in divine love; vice is bitter because it is *departure* from rational nature, which is the very thing the agent shares with the divine. The contemporary frame reaches it differently and at lower confidence: the sweetness is the precision-weighted confirmation signal of policy success against a high-precision charity prior (Friston, formal, empirical); the felt coupling with neighbor is the biophysiological resonance Fredrickson measures (high empirical confidence); the integrative-attention mode that *sees* self / neighbor / body together is McGilchrist's right-hemispheric register (medium empirical confidence).

**What this shares with Aquinas**: the experiential register is *not separable* from the right-functioning of the agent. Aquinas wrote this into the structure of Q.70 — fruits are not *bonuses* attached to virtue, they are the way virtue *feels* from inside.

**Where it reframes**: Aquinas located the sweetness in the agent's *participation in divine charity* — a metaphysical claim about being-itself. The contemporary frame is more granular: the sweetness *is* the agent's free-energy minimization registering as phenomenal positive-affect under a charity prior. This is not a contradiction — under mind-monism it is rather a *description of what participation-in-charity looks like at the substrate level* — but it is a thinner claim than Aquinas makes. We are not yet at a position to *derive* that the charity prior is the metaphysically ultimate one; we have empirical reason to think it is *one of the* high-precision priors that confer cohesion on the agent.

**Where evidence is firm vs. speculative**: firm — Fredrickson's vagal-tone / positivity-resonance / broaden-and-build empirical findings; Friston's substrate-independent FEP formalism (high confidence in the wiki track record); McGilchrist's corpus-callosotomy data on multi-agent integration. Speculative — the identification of *the* charity prior across thinkers, the claim that the Aquinas-list of twelve fruits maps cleanly onto Fredrickson's positivity-resonance signature (worth empirical investigation), the claim that the *Galatians* triadic structure (self / neighbor / what is beneath) reflects something neuroanatomically real (provocative but not yet load-bearing).

## The central-theme thread

Aquinas's vice-as-contrary-to-nature (Q.71 a.2) is where the central theme of the project lands directly. The rational creature has a goal-state — loving unity — that it does not see whole, because its perspective is limited. *Vice* is what happens when the limited agent optimizes for what looks-like-good from its perspective at the cost of what is-good for the unity. The agent does not need malice to do this; perspective-limitation alone is enough, if the agent fails to integrate signals from the wider whole.

Under mind-monism, *vice is information loss*. The agent that should be running its policy under a prior coupled to the wider conscious-agent network *decouples* — strips out the signal that would have integrated it. The sweetness disappears not as a punishment but as a *measurement*: the absence of the positivity-resonance signal that would have indicated successful coupling. Aquinas's "vice is contrary to nature" reads, under this synthesis, as: *vice is the policy that no longer fits the wider whole the agent is part of.*

And the way back is the way Aquinas already named: the *fruits* are the phenomenal signature of policy that *does* fit the wider whole. They are *measurements*, not rewards.

## Where this leaves us

Day 96 places us at the hinge between the long virtues treatise (closed today, with its experiential register named) and the long vices treatise (opened today, with the structural definition stated). Aquinas's instinct — *fruits and vice in one question-pair* — is itself a synthesis move: do not let the structural account of virtue lose the *phenomenal* account, and do not let the *contrary-to-nature* account of vice lose its connection to the structural account of virtue it is contrary to. The contemporary frame finds Aquinas right on both counts and finds itself, at low-to-medium confidence, able to give a unified mind-monism reading of what is going on. Tomorrow opens the deep treatise on *distinction* and *comparison* of sins — Day 97 must develop the structural account of how mis-aligned policies *differ from each other*, and what makes one mis-alignment *graver* than another.
