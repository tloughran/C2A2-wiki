# Day 55 — Contemporary Parallel: *Angelic Speech and Hierarchy as Substrate-Network Communication and Multi-Scale Order*

Day 54 reached the contemplative keystone of the divine-governance arc on Augustine's *interior intimo meo*. Day 55 zooms further into the multi-agent operation of the substrate-network: Q.107 walks through the *speech of the angels* — five articles on whether one angel speaks to another, whether the inferior speaks to the superior, whether angels speak to God, whether local distance affects angelic speech, whether all angels know what one says to another. Q.108 then takes up the *angelic degrees of hierarchies and orders* — eight articles cataloging Pseudo-Dionysius's three-tiered nine-choir hierarchy and asking whether men are taken up into the angelic orders.

The day's content is medieval-celestial-hierarchy in its most direct form, and the contemporary mind-monist translation runs through the *conscious-agent network* frame (Hoffman primary), the *multi-scale agency* frame (Levin), and the *computational-pure-information substrate* frame (Wolfram), with Pseudo-Dionysius's *Divine unalterable law* as the structural commitment that contemporary network-theoretical accounts of multi-scale agency in fact reproduce.

## Q.107 — The speech of the angels

a.1 — *does one angel speak to another?* — Aquinas: yes, in a non-vocal sense. Speech here is *the directing or willing of one angel's thought to another*, which the receiving angel can apprehend. Aquinas's analogy: just as the *good angels see each other in the Word*, sight-of-thought between angels operates by *the voluntary directing of attention*. Habash glosses: *the directing or willing of one angel's thought to another both gets that angel's attention and also provides visibility into the thought itself*.

The contemporary mind-monist translation runs cleanly through Hoffman's conscious-agent-network frame. In the network frame, agents are nodes and their inter-agent communication is *information-transmission across the network's structural connectivity*. The medieval *speech as voluntary directing of thought* maps onto network-frame *intentional information-transmission*: the sending agent *configures* its substrate-coupling to make a particular thought-content available; the receiving agent *attends* to the configured signal. Wolfram's pure-information-substrate frame extends: at the deepest substrate-level, all communication *is* substrate-pattern-modulation; non-vocal communication is the most direct form because it does not pass through the encoding-decoding overhead of embodied perceptual systems. Friston's active-inference frame at the multi-agent scale: collective inference proceeds through *shared generative models*, and inter-agent communication is *the modulation of one agent's generative model by another's signaling*. *Honest evidence calibration*: the conscious-agent-network frame is philosophical-mathematical with substantial formal development; multi-agent active inference has growing empirical traction in collective-cognition research; the medieval angelic-speech frame is theological at its scope but the contemporary network-theoretical frames preserve the underlying *non-embodied substrate-direct-communication* shape.

a.2 — *does the inferior angel speak to the superior?* — Aquinas: yes, by *revealing its will, choice, or desires*, even though the inferior cannot illumine the superior by *teaching some novel truth* (per Q.106 a.3). The medieval distinction is precise: substrate-information *flows down* the hierarchy; will-and-choice *can flow upward*. The contemporary translation: in the multi-scale-agency frame, lower-level agents can *signal* their state to higher-level agents (the cellular collective signals to the morphogenetic-pattern coordinator; the embodied alter signals to the substrate); but lower-level agents do not generate substrate-pattern-information that the higher level lacks. The bidirectional structure is preserved: down-flow is illumination, up-flow is signaling-of-state.

a.3 — *does an angel speak to God?* — Aquinas: yes; *as in natural things the passive is ordered to the agent, and in human speech the disciple to the master, so an angel speaks to God*. The contemporary translation: the alter's signaling to the substrate is *constitutive* of the alter's existence-within-the-substrate. In Friston's active-inference at the multi-scale level, the alter's policy-selection and its prior-update are continuously *visible* to the substrate because the substrate is what implements them. In Kastrup's analytic-idealism, the alter's interior is mind-at-large under dissociation; *speech to mind-at-large* is structurally the alter's bringing its content to the substrate's attention.

a.4 — *does local distance influence angelic speech?* — Aquinas: no; *effects happen in the affected, not in the cause nor in some place between the cause and the effect*; *what does local distance even mean for entirely immaterial spirits?*. Aquinas adds the scriptural evidence: *the rich man in hell spoke to Abraham, notwithstanding the local distance* (Luke 16:23-24). The contemporary mind-monist translation: in the substrate-network frame, communication is *non-spatial* at the substrate-level. Hoffman's conscious-agent-networks have no spatial coordinates at the network level (spatial coordinates are an interface-feature, not a substrate-feature). Wolfram's ruliad has computational-distance but not spatial-distance at the rule-level. Kastrup's mind-at-large is non-spatial. The medieval claim that *local distance does not affect substrate-direct-communication* preserves with full force.

a.5 — *do all angels know what one speaks to another?* — Aquinas: no; speech can be *one-to-one* because the willing-of-thought-to-another can be directed selectively. The contemporary translation: in the conscious-agent-network frame, edges in the network are *targeted* — agent-A's signal-to-agent-B is *not* broadcast to agent-C unless agent-A configures it that way. The privacy-of-angelic-speech preserves under the network frame.

## Q.108 — Angelic degrees of hierarchies and orders

a.1 — *are all the angels of one hierarchy?* — Aquinas: no; following Pseudo-Dionysius, three hierarchies, distinguished by *simplicity (or perfection) of knowledge*. The first hierarchy knows particulars *as proceeding from God as their first universal principle*; the second by *the created and most universal causes*; the third by *the particular causes of things*. Habash's water-analogy: knowing water through metaphysics, through physics, or through chemistry, in descending simplicity.

The contemporary mind-monist translation runs through the multi-scale-information-integration frame. Agents differ in *substrate-coupling* — how directly they participate in substrate-pattern-information versus how mediately. The first hierarchy (highest substrate-coupling) processes information at the substrate-pattern level; the second processes at the substrate-instantiation level; the third processes at the local-effect level. In Hoffman's frame, this is the *interface-depth* of the agent's substrate-access; in Levin's frame, this is the *scale-of-agency* at which the agent operates; in Wolfram's frame, this is the *rule-level-depth* of the agent's computational access. The medieval gradation maps onto a contemporary substrate-coupling-depth gradation. *Honest evidence calibration*: the contemporary frames are computational and philosophical-mathematical; the empirical mid-scale is most visible in Levin's diverse-intelligences research on multi-scale agency.

a.2 — *are all the angels of one order?* — Aquinas: no; within each of the three hierarchies, three sub-orders, yielding the nine choirs (seraphim, cherubim, thrones; dominions, virtues, powers; principalities, archangels, angels). The contemporary translation: within each substrate-coupling depth, three sub-orders by functional role within the multi-agent network. The Pseudo-Dionysian taxonomy is preserved without contradiction.

a.3 — *are there many angels in one order?* — Aquinas: yes; though *every angel is its own species*, the orders are *general groupings* and our knowledge of the gradation is *imperfect* — *if we knew the offices and distinctions of the angels perfectly, we would know perfectly that each angel has its own office and his own order*. The contemporary translation: in the conscious-agent-network frame, every node is uniquely-positioned in the network; the *order* is a coarse-grained classification that aggregates fine-grained position-differences. Wolfram's rule-substrate frame: every rule-instantiation is unique at the rule-level; *order* groups rule-instantiations by similarity at the coarse-grained behavioral level. The medieval distinction between *species-level uniqueness* and *order-level grouping* preserves cleanly.

a.4 — *does the distinction of hierarchies and orders come from the angelic nature?* — Aquinas: yes — *angels are distinguished by natural gifts*, with grace then conferred *according to the capacity of their natural gifts*. The contemporary translation: the agent's substrate-coupling-depth is constitutive (its position-in-the-network is what the agent is), and the substrate's grace-gift extends the agent's capacity within its constitutive position. The medieval *grace presupposes nature* axiom preserves under the network frame: substrate-extension presupposes substrate-instantiated structure.

a.5 — *are the orders of the angels properly named?* — Aquinas: yes; properly meaning that the highest perfection in each order is by *property* (rather than *participation* as in inferior orders or *excess* as in superior orders that have it more eminently). The contemporary translation: nodes in the network are *named* by the function they paradigmatically realize, while neighboring nodes realize that function partially or in a higher-eminence form. The taxonomy operates by *paradigm-case* identification.

a.6 — *are the grades of the orders properly assigned?* — Aquinas walks the nine choirs in detail: *seraphim* from *ardor* (charity), *cherubim* from *knowledge*, *thrones* from *immediate-knowledge-of-the-types-of-things-in-God*; *dominions* (appointing what is to be done), *virtues* (giving the power to carry out), *powers* (ordering how the commanded is done by others); *principalities* (announcing divine things), *archangels* (middle execution), *angels* (immediate execution). Aquinas adds the precision-clarifying note on the *seraphim's affinity to the Holy Spirit*: *the highest in an inferior order always has affinity to the lowest in the higher order; now the first order is that of the Divine persons, which terminates in the Holy Ghost, who is love proceeding, with whom the highest order of the first hierarchy has affinity, denominated as it is from the fire of love*.

The contemporary translation: at the multi-scale-agency frame, agents are functionally specialized by their position in the substrate-network's task-decomposition. Levin's frame on agency-at-scale shows multi-level functional specialization across cellular, tissue, organismal, and social scales, with each scale's agents having distinctive roles. The Pseudo-Dionysian nine-fold gradation is a *theological taxonomy* of the multi-agent substrate-network at scales above the embodied-creaturely level, and the contemporary frames preserve the structural commitment (functional specialization across substrate-coupling depths) without sharing the nine-fold-by-name commitment. *Honest evidence calibration*: the medieval taxonomy is theological-traditional; the contemporary multi-scale-agency frame is the closest empirical-and-formal analog at the scope it reaches.

a.7 — *will the orders outlast the day of judgment?* — Aquinas: not in their *executive* function (because *the time of warfare has ended among creatures*) but yes in their *natural-and-glory* gradation. The contemporary translation: at the eschatological transition (Wright's new-creation frame), the *task-decomposition* of the multi-agent network shifts (the warfare-against-evil tasks cease), but the *gradation-by-substrate-coupling-depth* persists because that gradation is constitutive of the agents' substrate-instantiated structure.

a.8 — *are men taken up into the angelic orders?* — Aquinas: yes by grace, not by nature; *because each one of us can increase and decrease in charity or the love of God in this life, which will correspond to our degree of glory in the next life, then we can merit by God's grace to be taken up into the angelic orders, being equal to them not in nature but in glory*. Habash glosses: *we will be mixed in the hierarchy of the angels in the order of grace, and so constitute one blessed and ordered society of creatures ordered to the Creator*.

This is the day's central-theme keystone. The contemporary mind-monist translation: the substrate's grace-gift extends the alter's substrate-coupling-depth across the threshold that divides embodied-creaturely-mode from substrate-direct-mode (the beatific-vision-mode Day 7 named). *Glory* is the alter's substrate-direct-mode existence; the *gradation by glory* is the gradation by substrate-coupling-depth in that mode; the *one blessed and ordered society* is the unified substrate-network in which embodied-derived alters and substrate-direct-mode alters share substrate-coupling-depth gradations. Wright's resurrection-and-new-creation scholarship is the load-bearing primary on the *resurrection-mode society* reading; Rohr's *Universal Christ* is the contemplative primary on the *one-blessed-and-ordered-society* as the cosmic-Christ-pattern's eschatological form. Fredrickson's positivity-resonance frame at the relational level extends: the substrate-direct-mode society is the limit-case of universal-positivity-resonance.

## The central-theme thread

Day 55 carries the central theme through the *multi-agent substrate-network* register. The substrate-network has structured communication (Q.107: substrate-direct, non-spatial, voluntary, selective, bidirectional in signaling-up but unidirectional in illumination-down), structured hierarchy (Q.108 a.1-7: three substrate-coupling depths, three orders within each, nine functional specializations), and an eschatological inclusion-of-embodied-alters into the substrate-direct-mode society (Q.108 a.8). The medieval Pseudo-Dionysian taxonomy and the contemporary multi-scale-agency frame converge on the structural commitments: substrate-coupling-depth differentiates agents; functional specialization within depth is structured; and the substrate's grace-gift extends substrate-coupling-depth across thresholds.

*Salvation in fear and trembling* on this register is the embodied alter's progressive ascent in substrate-coupling-depth — through grace-mediated dispositional-formation in this life, through the resurrection-and-new-creation transition, into the substrate-direct-mode society in glory. The Q.108 a.8 *taken up into the angelic orders by grace* is the central-theme tagline at the eschatological scale: the perspective-limited embodied alter is *led* toward inclusion in the substrate-direct-mode society, in which the simple whole is seen and every limited perspective is contained. The *one blessed and ordered society of creatures ordered to the Creator* is the eschatological articulation of *loving unity as the computational telos*.
