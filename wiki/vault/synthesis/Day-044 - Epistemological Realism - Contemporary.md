# Day 44 — Contemporary Parallel: *Abstraction from Icons, Realism Through the Interface*

Day 43 named the phantasm as Hoffman's icon and the active intellect's abstraction as the cortex's invariant-extraction operation. Day 44 sharpens that pivot. Q.85 walks the *mode and order of understanding* — eight articles that map the cognitive operation in detail: abstraction from phantasms, the abstracted intelligible species as *that-by-which* rather than *that-which* is understood, the priority of the universal in intellect and the particular in sense, simultaneity, composition-and-division, the intellect's susceptibility to error, individual variation, and the indivisible-before-divisible order. Q.86 then asks *what the intellect knows in material things* — singulars (yes, indirectly), the infinite (potentially), contingents (directly through sense, indirectly through intellect), the future (only through causes). Habash's title for the day — *Epistemological Realism* — names the synthesis: Aquinas defends a doctrine of *adequatio rei et intellectus* (the adequation of thing and intellect) against the worry that abstraction merely produces a mental construct disconnected from the world. The mind-monist frame, with Hoffman's interface theory, Hawkins's HTM, Friston's predictive coding, and Wright's critical realism, gives this defense a contemporary form that survives the worry.

## Q.85 a.1 — Abstraction from phantasms

The medieval claim is sharp: things are intelligible *in proportion as they are separate from matter*; therefore material things must be understood by being abstracted from material conditions. Aquinas's middle-place metaphor places the human intellect between the angels (who know material things through immaterial likenesses or in God) and the brute beasts (who do not abstract at all). The human intellect *holds a middle place*: it is not the act of an organ, yet it is a power of the soul which is the form of the body. Therefore it is *proper for it to know a form or essence existing individually in corporeal matter, but not as existing in such matter*.

The contemporary primary on this article is Hoffman, with Hawkins on the mechanism. PRS-01 in the Hoffman-tradition wiki on perception-as-fitness-tuned-interface gives the structural form: the sensory-perceptual apparatus presents the agent with icons — representations whose function is to support adaptive action, not to deliver veridical access to the underlying material substrate. *Abstraction from icons* is the cognitive operation by which the agent extracts higher-level structural priors from the icon-stream. PRS-02 in the Hawkins-tradition wiki on HTM cortical-column prediction (CROSS-011 with Friston) gives the cortical mechanism: the cortex's hierarchical-temporal-memory algorithm compresses invariants from sensory-motor streams; high-level categorical representations are extracted by the upward flow through the hierarchy and then drive predictive coding back down. The medieval *active intellect abstracts essence from phantasm* is, in contemporary cognitive vocabulary, the cortical hierarchy's invariant-extraction operation under FEP.

The mind-monist refinement: the icons are not the underlying reality, but the *invariants* the cortex extracts from them are not random. They track *real structural patterns* in what-the-icons-are-icons-of — the substrate's pattern at the alter's scope of access. PRS-01 in the Kastrup-tradition wiki on mind-as-ontological-primitive grounds this: the substrate is structured, and the alter's cognitive operation extracts partial structure of that substrate through its perceptually-limited interface. *Honest evidence calibration*: the perceptual claim (icons are not veridical) is empirically grounded in interface-theory work; the structural-priors claim (invariants track real substrate structure) is the additional move that distinguishes mind-monism from constructivist anti-realism.

## Q.85 a.2 — *That-by-which* vs. *that-which* — the realism move

This is the day's central article and the one Habash flags as crucial. The objection: if what we *understand* is the abstracted essence in our intellect, and not the dog-out-there itself, then we have no genuine knowledge of the external world; certainty in the sciences collapses into mental constructivism. Aquinas's response: *the intelligible species is not what is actually understood but that by which the intellect understands*. The form-informing-the-mind and the form-informing-the-matter-of-the-dog-out-in-the-world are *essentially the same form, though not numerically the same*. The essential identity of the form-as-it-informs-mind and the form-as-it-informs-matter is what allows the intellect to know *the dog, the nature of dogs out in the external world*, by means of the intelligible species in itself.

The contemporary mind-monist translation runs through interface theory plus structural-realism plus active inference. PRS-04 in the Hoffman-tradition wiki on conscious-realism (the underlying reality is networks of conscious agents whose interactions show up to the limited alter as the icons of perception) gives the substrate-side: what the icon is an icon *of* is structured, real, and accessible through the icon by *the structural correspondence between icon and substrate*. CROSS-014 (Hoffman × Kastrup deep convergence) places this inside the consciousness-first paradigm. Friston's free-energy framework gives the formal: a generative model that *minimizes free energy under sensory evidence over time* converges on a structural representation that *tracks* the underlying generative structure of the environment — not perfectly, not veridically in the naive sense, but *adequately for action*. The medieval *adequatio rei et intellectus* is preserved in the contemporary *the agent's generative model converges on an adequate-for-action representation of the structural pattern that produces its sensory inputs*.

Wright's critical realism (developed in *The New Testament and the People of God*) gives the methodological-philosophical framing of this position outside cognitive science. Critical realism affirms *the reality of the thing known* and *the perspective-limitation of the knower* simultaneously: knowledge is real reference to a real world, mediated by the knower's interpretive framework, fallible but not therefore arbitrary. Day 9's commentary already named Wright's critical realism as the contemporary framing of *adequatio rei et intellectus*; Day 44 extends and intensifies that move. The medieval epistemological realism, contemporary critical realism, interface theory's structural-realism, and FEP's adequacy-tracking are working the same problem with different tools. Each affirms *real reference under perspective-limitation*; each denies both naive veridicality and constructivist anti-realism.

## Q.85 aa.3–8 — Universal-first in intellect; particular-first in sense; simultaneity; composition-and-division; error; individual variation; whole-before-parts

The remaining articles fill in the architecture. a.3: knowledge of the *singular* is prior in *sense*; knowledge of the *more universal* is prior in *intellect*. The cortical hierarchy under HTM gives the contemporary picture cleanly. Lower cortical levels process particulars (this dog, this color, this shape); higher cortical levels extract invariants (dog-as-such, color-as-such). The agent's first cognitive grasp of a novel kind tends to be the *more general* category (animal) before the *more specific* (rational animal); this is HTM running its abstraction-then-specification pipeline on the unfamiliar input.

a.4: we cannot understand many things at the same time *in the same act* — the mind being-informed-by-multiple-essences-simultaneously is structurally impossible — but we can understand many things if they are *virtually included* in a single understood whole. The contemporary translation: working memory and conscious access are bandwidth-limited (one inferential focus at a time); habitual knowledge is bandwidth-unlimited; understanding *animal* implicitly carries understanding *dog and monkey* as members of that category. This matches contemporary cognitive-architecture research (the Global Workspace and its bandwidth limits; the cortex's massive implicit-memory storage) without strain.

a.5: the intellect understands by *composition and division* — judgments saying *this is that* or *this is not that*. Aquinas distinguishes *imperfect* knowledge (simple apprehension of essence) from *perfect* knowledge (the essence understood together with its properties, accidents, and relations). The contemporary cognitive translation lands on predictive-processing accounts of judgment and inference: simple recognition of an object-category is the bottom-of-the-hierarchy prediction firing; full understanding involves the hierarchical activation of the object's relations to other objects and properties — the cortex's *graph* of associations, not just the *node*. McGilchrist's right-mode-gestalt to left-mode-articulation gradient is the hemispheric refinement: imperfect-knowledge-as-apprehension is heavily right-mode; perfect-knowledge-as-composed is the integration of right-mode gestalt with left-mode propositional articulation.

a.6: the intellect *cannot err* in its proper object (the essence of a material thing) but *may err* in compositions, divisions, and reasoning beyond the proper object. The contemporary architectural reading: low-level invariant extraction operates near-flawlessly when the cortical mechanism is intact; high-level inferential composition is where errors emerge, because composition requires combining multiple lower-level features and the combination is fallible. The medieval claim and Hawkins's HTM converge: HTM's lowest layers are highly reliable feature-extractors; errors emerge as the hierarchy composes higher-level interpretations. Friston's framework gives the formal: low-level prediction errors are tightly weighted; high-level prediction errors are subject to model-mismatch, prior-misweighting, and integration failures.

a.7: one person can understand the same thing *better* than another. The mind-monist frame: alters genuinely differ in cognitive light-cone (Levin's empirical work on agency-at-every-scale extends to inter-individual differences), in generative-model depth (Friston), in hemispheric balance (McGilchrist), and in the integration of right-mode gestalt with left-mode articulation. The medieval *some understand more profoundly* is not a metaphysical hierarchy of souls but a real empirical-cognitive variation in how well the agent's inferential architecture is configured for a given domain.

a.8: we understand the *indivisible* (the whole) before the *divisible* (the parts). McGilchrist's hemispheric account is again the cleanest: right-hemisphere whole-grasping is *prior* in the cognitive sequence; left-hemisphere decomposition follows. The medieval order-of-cognition and contemporary hemispheric research converge.

## Q.86 — What the intellect knows in material things

a.1: the intellect knows singulars *indirectly* through the phantasm; *directly* it knows the universal abstracted from the singular. The contemporary architecture: the cortex maintains both the high-level categorical representation (the universal) and the binding to the lower-level sensory traces (the particular phantasm); reactivation of the categorical can pull the particulars with it but the categorical is the *direct* object of the higher-level inference.

a.2: the intellect can know the infinite *potentially* (the agent can keep learning without limit) but not *actually* (no finite traversal completes the infinite). Aquinas's striking line — *in the future life this defect of intellect will be removed by the state of glory when we shall be able to see the essence of God himself but without being able to comprehend him* — preserves the asymmetry between finite cognition and the substrate even at the beatific limit case Day 7 named. *We see but do not comprehend* — the limit-case integration does not erase the asymmetry between alter and substrate; it makes it transparent.

a.3: the intellect knows *contingent* things directly through sense and indirectly through intellect; *necessary* universals (the angles of a triangle, the rational animal) are known only by intellect. The contemporary translation: sensory traces are particular and contingent; the high-level invariants the cortex extracts are *near-universal* over the agent's experience; necessary truths (mathematics, logic) are extracted at higher levels of abstraction still, where the inferential structure forces particular conclusions from definitions. Aquinas's claim that contingents-as-such are sense-known and necessities-as-such are intellect-known is the architectural distinction between the bottom-of-hierarchy particularizing operations and the top-of-hierarchy abstracting operations.

a.4: we know the future not in itself (only God knows in this way) but through its causes. Friston's formal account of model-based prediction is the contemporary cousin: the agent's generative model carries forward expectations about future states, and those expectations are *causes-to-effects* inferences computed from the agent's current model. The agent's *knowledge of the future* is real but *causal-inferential*, never the present-vision Aquinas reserves to God.

## The central-theme thread

Q.85–86 frames perspective-limitation epistemologically. Each alter's knowing is *real reference* under *perspective-limitation*: the icons (phantasms) are not the underlying reality, but the structural priors the cortex extracts from them track real patterns in what-the-icons-are-icons-of, and the agent's generative model converges on adequate-for-action structural representations of the substrate's pattern. The medieval *adequatio rei et intellectus*, contemporary critical realism, interface theory's structural-realism, and FEP's adequacy-tracking converge: real knowing under fallibility, perspective-limitation as the structural form of being-this-alter rather than the substrate itself. *Salvation in fear and trembling* on the epistemological register is the disciplined cultivation of cognitive practice under awareness that one's icons are perspective-limited but not therefore disconnected from the substrate's pattern; the alter's progressive refinement of its generative model toward better fit with the substrate is the cognitive form of working-out-salvation.
