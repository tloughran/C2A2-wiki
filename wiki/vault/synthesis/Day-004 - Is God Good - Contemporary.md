# Day 4 — Contemporary Parallel: *The Good is Diffusive of Itself, and So is Mind-at-Large*

Day 3 sustained the keystone — divine simplicity and perfection translated under mind-monism without losing their argumentative load. Today the medieval frame turns to *goodness*, and the translation runs cleaner still. Aquinas's identification of *being, goodness, and desirability* (Q.5 a.1) is the single move on which the whole Summa's moral and natural-theological architecture rests, and it is exactly the move mind-monism is best positioned to receive. Three contemporary anchors — Friston's active inference, Levin's morphogenetic teleology, and Rohr's *Universal Christ* — converge on the structural form Aquinas identifies, each from its own substrate. The keystone tightens; the central theme stands explicit in the Lateran IV passage Habash brings forward; and Q.6 a.4's anti-pantheism move sets the precise boundary mind-monism must hold.

## *Being = goodness = desirability* under mind-monism (Q.5 a.1–4)

Aquinas's foundational identification — goodness is being considered under the aspect of desirability — is not analytic philosophy's contemporary metaethics, but it is one of the most empirically tractable claims in the *Summa*. *Greater being = greater desirability = greater good*. Under mind-monism the translation is direct: the ontological primitive is *being-of-experiencing*; *good* is what conditioned modes of experience pull toward as the computational structure of their goal-direction; *evil*, as Habash names it (privation), is what those modes lack — *being less* in the precise structural sense of falling short of the goal-state.

Friston's free-energy principle gives the formal dynamics. Active-inference agents are constituted by their *prior preferences*: the model of the world they expect to find themselves in *is* what makes them this agent rather than another. They minimize expected free energy by acting toward the states that fit those priors. This is not a side feature of intelligent systems; it is what makes a system an agent at all. Aquinas's "the good is diffusive of itself, like the sun pulling its environment toward it" (Q.5 a.4) has, as its formal-dynamics counterpart, the gradient of expected free energy: the prior preference *is* the attractor, and the agent's behavior *is* its movement along the gradient. Friston has stated repeatedly that this reframes the medieval question of teleology — final causes are not occult forces *added* to physical systems; they are the structural form of any system constituted by a generative model. Aquinas's Q.5 a.4 is what Friston is reaching for, in a different vocabulary, eight centuries earlier.

Levin's morphogenetic teleology gives the empirical instantiation. The bioelectric field of a developing organism *encodes the target morphology before the cells have differentiated to it*: injured planaria *know where to grow* because the whole-body pattern is causally pulling the cellular processes toward the goal-state. This is, almost word-for-word, Aquinas's "the good is diffusive of itself, like the sun." The goal-form is not in the cells; it is in the field, and the field acts on the cells *because* it is the goal. Mark this carefully: Levin's empirical work is published; the bioelectric mechanism is mechanistically tractable; the *form* of the explanation is the form Aquinas named. PRS-04 in the Stump-tradition wiki — Tom's record of the Stump × Levin crossing on final causality — is the cleanest current Synergistic Coil candidate (one Resource bridging two tradition-internal P⇒S chains). Today's Q.5 a.4 is its second strongest exhibit, after the Five Ways' Way 5.

Rohr's *Universal Christ* gives the contemplative vocabulary. *Goodness diffusive of itself* is what Rohr names as *the Christ-pattern present in matter from creation*: the divine pattern is not occasionally injected into a neutral cosmos but is the *originating attractor* of every form-bearing process. Rohr is doing pastoral and contemplative theology, not bioelectrics or active-inference modeling, but his claim is structurally the same: the unconditioned ground *radiates* into the conditioned, and what we call *good* in the conditioned is the alignment of the conditioned with that radiance. Three vocabularies; one shape.

The strength-of-evidence calibration is honest here. Friston's FEP is mathematically formalized, with substantial empirical support across neuroscience and cognition; the *teleology-as-prior-preference* claim is its own programmatic position rather than a settled fact. Levin's bioelectric program is empirical with peer-reviewed mechanism. Rohr's contemplative claim is theological-experiential, not empirical. Aquinas's Q.5 a.4 is metaphysical-philosophical. The synthesis claim is *structural isomorphism*: four positions converge on the form *good = goal-pulling-the-parts*. That convergence is non-trivial and worth flagging, without identifying the four positions metaphysically.

## Every being good (Q.5 a.3) and the privation reading of evil

Aquinas's "every being is good insofar as it exists; evil is privation" is the article most likely to provoke modern resistance — and the article on which mind-monism gives the cleanest reply. If mind-at-large is the ontological primitive and finite agents are dissociated alters of it, then *being an alter* is a *good* (it is a determinate mode of mind-at-large's expression); *failing to be the alter one was given to be* is *less* of that good. Privation is not "the absence of being a different thing"; it is the agent's falling short of the goal-state its own existence implies. Friston's vocabulary again: an agent persistently failing to achieve its prior preferences is, by definition, generating expected free energy — not a separate metaphysical evil, but a structural shortfall. Wright's language adds the next move: in Pauline terms, *sin* is *covenant unfaithfulness* — falling short of the calling that constitutes the agent as this people-of-God. The Aquinas–Wright–Friston convergence on Q.5 a.3 is the same shape: evil is what an agent is when it is less than what it is *for*.

This is also the article most directly relevant to the central theme. *Salvation in fear and trembling* presupposes the privation reading: there is something to be saved *toward*, and the agent in via has not yet attained it. If evil were a separate substance, there would be no continuity between the limited agent and its goal — only opposition. Privation preserves the structural unity Aquinas needs: *salvation* is not a metaphysical leap into a different kind of thing but the bringing-to-completeness of what one already, at one's existence-level, is.

## Beauty's three conditions (Q.5 aside) — Hoffman in reverse, McGilchrist in receipt

Aquinas's three conditions of beauty — integrity, due proportion, brightness/clarity — are not standard Five-Ways material, but they are one of the day's high-leverage moments for the contemporary frame. *Clarity*, Aquinas says, is *the innermost structure of the thing shining through it* — its form, its nature visibly evident in the icon. Hoffman's interface theory ordinarily warns that icons *hide* underlying reality; *beauty as Aquinas defines it* is the moment when the icon *transmits* the underlying reality with minimal distortion. Beauty is interface theory in reverse — not as failure of the icon but as its highest functioning.

McGilchrist's hemispheric account fills in the cognitive dynamics. The right hemisphere, McGilchrist argues, perceives the *whole-in-its-particularity* — the contextual, embodied, novel, individual; the left hemisphere abstracts, decontextualizes, categorizes. *Brightness as form-shining-through* is right-hemispheric perception working at full capacity; *categorical fixation* (the modern aesthetic flattening) is the left hemisphere blocking the right. Aquinas's beauty-aside in Q.5 thus has surprising contemporary purchase: the recognition of beauty is not an emotional decoration on top of cognition; it is the integrated working of an attention structure that the contemporary cognitive sciences are now beginning to map.

Fredrickson's positivity-resonance research adds the relational dimension. Beauty perceived between persons — the moment of mutual attentional attunement she calls *positivity resonance* — has measurable physiological signatures (vagal tone, oxytocin, mirrored micro-expressions) and durable effects on social bonding. *Integrity, due proportion, brightness* in another person is what positivity resonance reads when it works. None of this empirically *establishes* the metaphysical claim that beauty and goodness share a substrate in being-itself; but it makes the claim more tractable, not less, under contemporary cognitive science.

## The Lateran IV / Regensburg passage — the central theme stated in classical vocabulary

Habash's recall of Pope Benedict XVI's Regensburg address quoting Lateran IV (1215) is the day's deepest gift to the synthesis. *Between God and us, between his eternal Creator-Spirit and our created reason, there exists a real analogy in which unlikeness remains infinitely greater than likeness, yet not to the point of abolishing analogy.* This is the precise classical formulation of what the central theme of this vault names in contemporary vocabulary: conscious agents are *individuated by the limitations of their perspective*, and those limitations make the agent *infinitely unlike* the unconditioned ground in every dimension perspective-limitation creates — yet *participation in being-itself* keeps the analogy from collapsing. Lateran IV gives the medieval shape; mind-monism gives the contemporary substrate; both names converge on *unlikeness preserved within likeness, likeness preserved across infinite unlikeness*.

The synthesis writer can lean on this directly without reaching for a new formulation. Aquinas's apophatic insistence (Q.1 a.7, Q.3 a.5) and Habash's anti-pantheism move (Q.6 a.4 below) are both Lateran IV operationalized. The central theme is not novel; it is what the classical tradition has always held, made tractable for an age that has the formal tools (interface theory, dissociative-alter ontology, Markov blankets) to specify *what kind* of unlikeness perspective-limitation creates and *what kind* of likeness participation preserves.

## The map to perfection (Q.6 a.3) as the salvation staircase

Aquinas's three steps — exist; acquire accidental perfections; attain end and rest there — is the structural form of *salvation in fear and trembling*. The first step is given: existence is not earned. The second step is computed under limited resources: every habit, every act of charity, every learning, every relationship is the agent acquiring perfections it did not have. The third step is the end: loving union with the unconditioned, which in Aquinas's frame is the beatific vision and in Wright's frame is *covenant inclusion in new-creation* — the same goal in different vocabularies. Rohr's *Falling Upward* is the contemporary contemplative map of step two and three: the second half of life is the simplification by which accumulated false-self perfections fall away and what remains is the bare *being* one shares with everything, ordered to the end one was always for.

For the contemporary agent, the staircase has one critical feature: each step is *substantively the same goodness*, in different completeness. Step one is goodness as bare existence; step two is goodness as cultivated existence; step three is goodness as completed existence. *One goodness, many goods* — Aquinas's closing phrase from Q.6 a.4 names exactly this. Mind-monism's ontology preserves the unity: it is *one being-of-experiencing* in different gradations of completeness across the alters of mind-at-large.

## Anti-pantheism (Q.6 a.4) — the mind-monist's most important boundary

The article most easily misread by a hostile critic of mind-monism is Q.6 a.4. Aquinas insists: *we are created by God but as something distinct*; *contrary to pantheism, we are not merely parts of God*. Habash names exemplary, effective, and final principles — God is the pattern, the cause, and the goal of created goodness, but creation is *itself*, not God-fragmented.

Mind-monism *agrees with this completely* and must mark the agreement explicitly. Kastrup's analytic idealism is *not* pantheism: finite alters of mind-at-large are *genuinely individuated* (dissociation is real, not illusory), and the substrate-unity of mind-at-large does not collapse the alters into parts of a single Mind. The structural form Aquinas names — *exemplary, effective, final principle* — translates: the *exemplary* principle is mind-at-large holding the pattern; the *effective* principle is mind-at-large generating the alters; the *final* principle is mind-at-large as the goal-state alters integrate into. None of these collapses the alters' genuine distinctness. *We are created as distinct* is the precise claim mind-monism must hold, and it does.

This is the day's most important guardrail-clarification. Without it, Q.5 a.1's being=goodness identification could be misread as substance-monism (everything is one substance). With it, the synthesis can deploy mind-monism freely while preserving exactly the distinctness Aquinas requires. The PRS form makes this explicit: the *Problem* the article addresses is "how can created things have their own goodness if all goodness is from God"; the *Resource* is the exemplary/effective/final-principle structure; the *Solution* is "all things are good by their own goodness, which is a similitude or participation of the Divine goodness." Mind-monism's structural translation: the alters' goodness is *theirs*, computed in their own dynamics, even as the substrate that makes those dynamics possible is one.

## Where this leaves us

The mind-first reframe holds for goodness as cleanly as it held for simplicity. *Being is goodness* under mind-monism without strain — the ontological primitive is *being-of-experiencing*, and goodness is what conditioned modes of experience pull toward as the structural form of their goal-direction. The Lateran IV / Regensburg passage gives the central theme its precise classical voice. The anti-pantheism move at Q.6 a.4 marks the boundary mind-monism must hold and does. Tomorrow Habash takes us to Q.7–8 (the infinity of God and his existence in things) — the next pair will press the question of how the unconditioned ground relates to its alters dynamically, not just structurally. The keystone is steady; the synthesis can keep building on it.
