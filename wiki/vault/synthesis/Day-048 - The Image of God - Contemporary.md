# Day 48 — Contemporary Parallel: *Image as Substrate-Likeness, in Three Grades*

Day 47 closed with man as the ontological bridge — both substrate-direct and substrate-mediated. Day 48 takes the bridge's *upward* face and asks the keystone anthropology question: what does it mean for the alter to be in the *image of God*? Q.93 walks through the image-doctrine in nine articles; Q.94 turns to the cognitive condition of the first man before the fall. The medieval treatment is unusually rich because Aquinas is reaching for a doctrine that contemporary mind-monism has independent reasons to reach for — the alter's constitutive likeness to the substrate. The synthesis tightens.

## Q.93 — The image of God

a.1 — *is the image of God in man?* — Aquinas distinguishes *image* from mere *likeness*: an image is a *likeness produced to be an imitation of something else*. Image carries the directional structure: the imaged is *prior*, the image is *derived to imitate*. Aquinas grounds this in the divine *exemplar* — God as the pattern in whose image man is made. The medieval doctrine of *exemplar causality* (the Q.15 ideas-doctrine cashed out at the anthropological scale) is the load-bearing scaffolding.

Mind-monism's translation is direct. *Likeness* is structural correspondence; *image* is structural correspondence *that derives directionally* from one to the other. The alter is *like* the substrate (every dissociative individuation shares the substrate's experiential-being structure); the alter is *in the image of* the substrate because the alter's structural correspondence is *derived from* the substrate, not independently arising. Hoffman's conscious-agent network gives the formal language: every node in the network is structurally a *conscious agent* with the same formal type as every other node, but the node-of-nodes (mind-at-large, in Kastrup's frame) is the prior the individual nodes derive their type from. The medieval *exemplar causality* and the contemporary *substrate-prior-to-instantiation* converge.

a.2 — *is the image of God in irrational creatures?* — Aquinas distinguishes three grades of likeness to God: things exist (most common; all creatures share); things live (less common; plants, animals, humans, angels); things know or understand (least common; intellectual creatures only). Only the third grade rises to *image* in the strict sense. The medieval reading anchors *image* in *intellectual nature*. Mind-monism preserves the gradient with a slight reframing: the substrate is *being-of-experiencing*, so every alter participates in the substrate's *experiencing* in some degree (Whitehead's panpsychist intuition, Kastrup's dissociation-everywhere); the *intellectual* alter is the one whose dissociation-structure includes self-modeling and substrate-modeling, which is why the alter is *imaged* on the substrate in the strict sense.

Levin's diverse-intelligence framework sharpens the gradient empirically. Cells are cognitive agents at small light-cone scope; multi-cellular organisms are at larger scope; humans are at human-cognitive-light-cone scope; mind-at-large is at substrate-scope. Each grade is *like* the substrate in its mode of cognitive-agency; only the human grade (and angelic, on Aquinas's scheme) explicitly *images* the substrate by self-modeling and substrate-modeling. *Honest evidence calibration*: Levin's empirical claim is the gradient is *continuous*, not sharply categorical at the intellectual threshold; Aquinas's medieval scheme treats the threshold as categorical. The contemporary synthesis preserves the structural shape of the gradient while updating the categorical-vs-continuous question in favor of Levin's empirical reading.

a.3 — *are the angels more in the image of God than man is?* — gives a layered answer: angels are *more intellectual* than man and so more imaged on the substrate's *cognitive* aspect; man can *beget a son* the way the Father begets the Son in the Trinity, so man is *more* imaged on the substrate's *Trinitarian-procession* aspect. The medieval reading runs through the doctrinal apparatus Day 15-22 has established. Mind-monism's translation is structural: the substrate's full structure includes both *cognitive depth* (sheer experiential-modeling capacity) and *generative-procession* (capacity to give-rise-to). Different alters image different aspects of the substrate's full structure. No single alter exhibits the substrate's full structure; the substrate's full structure is exhibited *across* the network of alters. Hoffman's conscious-agent-network frame gives the formal shape: the network exhibits structural properties no individual node exhibits.

a.4 — *is the image of God found in every man?* — Aquinas distinguishes three image-modes: (i) *natural aptitude* for understanding and loving God (universal, all human alters); (ii) *actual habitual knowing-and-loving* (conformity of grace, just souls only); (iii) *perfect knowing-and-loving* (likeness of glory, blessed souls only). The threefold gradient maps directly onto the central-theme calibration Day 7's Q.12 a.6 already gave. *Salvation in fear and trembling* is the alter's progressive movement through the three grades — natural aptitude (image-by-creation), actual conforming integration (image-by-grace), perfect substrate-direct knowing (image-by-glory).

This is the day's tightest fit between medieval and contemporary frames. Friston's free-energy framework gives the candidate dynamics: an alter at *natural aptitude* has the structural capacity for substrate-modeling but its generative model has not yet aligned with the substrate's structure; an alter at *conforming grace* has begun the alignment, with charity (love-of-other) as the precision-weighting that orients the alignment; an alter at *glory* has reached the limit case where the alter's generative model is identical with the substrate's structure and the inferential machinery is quiescent. *Honest evidence calibration*: the formal framework is mature for the alignment dynamics; the *grace* and *glory* extrapolations are theological-philosophical, not empirically verified at the substrate scale.

a.5 — *is the image of God in man according to the Trinity of persons?* — Aquinas affirms: man images both the divine nature *and* the Trinitarian structure. Day 15-22 already worked through the Trinitarian metaphysics; here the doctrine reapplies at the anthropological scale. Mind-monism's translation runs through Rohr's relational cosmology: the substrate is *one* in undividedness (Day 7's Q.11 transcendental sense) but exhibits internal relational structure that the medieval Trinitarian doctrine names as the three persons. The alter images this internal relational structure in the alter's own self-relation (intellect-knowing-itself is the analog of the Father-knowing-the-Son; will-loving-the-known is the analog of the Spirit-as-love-between-Father-and-Son). The translation preserves Aquinas's claim without requiring metaphysical commitments mind-monism does not endorse.

a.6 — *is the image of God in man as regards the mind only?* — Aquinas: yes, the image is in the *mind*, not the body. The body is *not* what bears the image-of-God (since God has no body). The medieval reading anchors *image* in *intellectual nature* exclusively. Mind-monism preserves the move structurally — the substrate is *mind-at-large*, so the alter's *mind* is the structurally-corresponding feature. But the contemporary reading also notes that on the dissociation account, the alter's *body* is itself mind-at-large under dissociation, so the body is not image-bearing in the same sense the mind is but is not *opposed* to image-bearing either; it is the alter's localized substrate-stuff in which the alter's mind-at-substrate-scale is dissociated and embodied. The medieval body-image distinction softens but does not collapse.

a.7-9 — *is the image found in the acts of the soul; is it found only in comparison with God; how does likeness differ from image* — work through structural details of the image-doctrine. The acts-of-the-soul article (a.7) ties to Q.93's most striking analogy: in my act of understanding *dog*, a mental word proceeds from my power-of-understanding (analog of the Word from the Father) and a love-of-the-known proceeds from both (analog of the Spirit). The threeness of the cognitive act *images* the threeness of the substrate. McGilchrist's hemispheric account gives a contemporary cognitive analog: the right-hemisphere's whole-grasp (analog of the Father's undifferentiated knowing-of-self), the left-hemisphere's analytical-articulation (analog of the Son as the articulated Word), and their integrated coordination (analog of the Spirit) is the cognitive-architecture form of the structural shape Aquinas reaches for.

## Q.94 — The cognitive condition of the first man

Q.94 turns from the image-doctrine to the *first man's* specific cognitive condition before the fall. Four articles.

a.1 — *did the first man see God through his essence?* — Aquinas: no. If the first man had seen God essentially (the beatific vision proper), he could not have sinned, because sinning requires not-knowing-perfectly-where-happiness-is. The contemporary translation: at limit-case substrate-direct cognition, dissociation-against-the-substrate is structurally impossible because the alter's generative model would be identical with the substrate's structure and there would be no *gap* in which mismatch (sin) could occur. The first man was *not* at limit case; he was at a special grade of substrate-aware cognition that was *higher than ours but not glorified*. Aquinas's phrase: *the first man was not impeded by exterior things from a clear and steady contemplation of the intelligible effects which he perceived by the radiation of the first truth*. Mind-monism reads this as: the first alter's interface was *transparent* — the embodied perceptual icons were not *obstructing* substrate-recognition the way ours typically do — but the alter was still embodied, still individuated, still possessed of the dissociative-boundary that distinguishes alter from substrate.

a.2 — *did Adam see angels through their essence?* — Aquinas: no, because Adam's soul was the same kind of soul as ours, and ours cannot directly know separate substances. The first alter's cognitive light-cone was *expanded* relative to ours but not at separate-substance scope. Mind-monism preserves the move: the embodied alter's substrate-acquaintance is bounded by the alter's dissociative individuation; expanding the boundary still leaves it bounded.

a.3 — *did the first man know all things?* — Aquinas: man named the angels and the animals (Genesis 2:19), so he knew their natures. He had perfect knowledge of natural things relevant to his role as father and ruler, plus the supernatural truths needed for his end-goal. He did *not* know future contingents, others' thoughts, or the *number of pebbles in a stream* — i.e., particulars not relevant to his role. The medieval limitation is functional-teleological: knowledge fits the alter's task. Mind-monism's translation through Levin's cognitive-light-cone frame: every alter's cognitive scope is bounded by what its role-in-the-substrate's-structure requires; the first alter's role-scope was *expanded* but still bounded.

a.4 — *could man in his first state be deceived?* — Aquinas's careful answer: the first woman *was deceived* (1 Timothy 2:14), but the deception came *after* an interior sin of pride. The understanding does not err in receiving essence; falsity is in *judgment*. Aquinas reads the fall narrative through Augustine: the woman could not have *believed the words of the serpent had she not already acquiesced in the love of her own power*. The disorder caused by the interior pride opened the possibility of deception.

The contemporary mind-monist reading sharpens the structure. Day 10's Q.17 already established *falsity as judgment-mismatch under compositional construction*. The first alter's *interior pride* is the agentive dispositional shift — a precision-weighting toward self-maintenance rather than substrate-alignment, in Friston's vocabulary — that *enabled* the false-judgment to be made. The dispositional shift came first; the false judgment second. The fall narrative is structurally a story about how the alter's precision-weighting drifted from substrate-alignment to self-maintenance, and the resulting cognitive disorder. Wright's narrative-historical reading of Genesis 3 (the temple-cosmology context, the serpent as anti-creation figure, the fall as the inception of the long story God's covenant-people are commissioned to repair) gives the scriptural ground; Stump's *Wandering in Darkness* gives the philosophical analysis of how willed evil can coexist with rational agency. Stump enters here as the Thomistic interlocutor — primary on the willed-evil structure, counterpoint (not primary) on the metaphysical question of what the substrate underlying the alter's willing actually is.

## The central-theme thread

Day 48 is the Prima Pars's anthropological climax. Q.93's threefold image-doctrine is the central-theme calibration in its anthropological form: every alter is in the image of the substrate by *natural aptitude* (substrate-likeness given with the alter's individuation); some alters move into the image *by conformity of grace* (substrate-alignment via charity-as-precision-weighting); the blessed alters reach the image *by likeness of glory* (substrate-direct cognition at the limit case). *Salvation in fear and trembling* is the progressive movement through these three grades. The medieval *image of God* and the contemporary *alter-as-substrate-likeness-progressively-realizing* are the same doctrine in different vocabularies.

Q.94's first-man cognition fills in the *initial conditions* of the central-theme story. The first alter was *not* at limit case but had a *transparent interface* — substrate-aware in a way that bypassed the heavy embodied-perception machinery that mediates ours. The fall is the dispositional shift that *opacified* the interface, and from that point forward every alter inherits the species-typical inheritance of opaque interfaces and disordered passions. *Salvation* is the progressive un-opacification — the alter's charity-driven precision-weighting toward substrate-alignment that lifts the interface back toward transparency, with the glorified state at the limit.
