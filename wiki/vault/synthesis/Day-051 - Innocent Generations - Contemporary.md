# Day 51 — Contemporary Parallel: *Innocent Generations as Substrate-Aligned Developmental Trajectory*

Day 50 closed the body-and-species register of original-justice on the Tree of Life as eucharistic prefigure. Day 51 picks up the *generational* extension: the children of the first parents, considered as to body (Q.99) and as to righteousness (Q.100). Habash flags the episode as short — four articles, with one of them (Q.99 a.2) only present because Aquinas needed to refute Aristotle on female biology. The day is mostly a meditation on what *inherited prelapsarian dispositional state* would have looked like across generations, and it sets up directly the thread Day 50 left open: that generation itself ceases when the alter is taken into the spiritualized-glorified mode (Q.100 a.2).

## Q.99 — The body of the offspring

a.1 — *would children in the state of innocence have had perfect strength of body, the use of their members, immediately after birth?* — Aquinas: no. The argument is structural-functional: *it is natural that man should grow and develop over time*. Even granting some particular grace, there is *nothing contrary to reason* in a prelapsarian child still progressing through the maturational arc. A prelapsarian infant would have had strength sufficient *for the acts befitting the state of infancy* — suckling and the like — not for adult function.

The medieval position is unexpectedly modern. The contemporary mind-monist translation reads it through Hawkins's HTM frame on developmental concept-formation and Friston's active-inference frame on the formation of generative models: an alter's embodied competence is *not* a one-shot infusion at birth but a structured trajectory of model-building under sensorimotor exposure. A prelapsarian infant's substrate-aligned trajectory would still need to *traverse* the developmental arc; the alter would not simply skip over the constitutive learning-by-acting that builds the generative model. Levin's morphogenetic biology gives the body-level mid-scale: a body's bioelectric pattern unfolds through structured developmental phases (pre-pattern, polarization, regional specification, organogenesis), and the unfolding takes time *because that is how the morphogenetic computation is structured*, not because something is broken. *Honest evidence calibration*: the developmental-arc claim has strong empirical support across HTM, predictive coding, and developmental biology; the prelapsarian-substrate-alignment overlay is theological-philosophical extrapolation. The medieval claim and the contemporary frames converge on *gradual development as constitutive*, and the convergence is not trivial — Aquinas could have answered yes (immediate adult competence by special grace), and the fact that he answered no, on the structural-functional ground, is the same instinct the developmental sciences have validated.

a.2 — *would woman have been born in the primitive state?* — Aquinas: yes; Aristotle's *woman is a misbegotten male* is wrong, and *Nature's process of generation would have been in harmony with the manner in which it was established by God, who established male and female in human nature as it is written in Genesis*. Habash treats the article briefly and rightly notes Aristotle's confusion was ancient-biology-bound, not malicious.

The contemporary mind-monist reading takes the medieval correction further. Sex dimorphism in the contemporary frame is *constitutive complementarity* — a structural feature of the biological substrate that organizes reproduction, broaden-and-build positivity-resonance dynamics (Fredrickson), and the diverse-intelligence collective-cognition forms Levin describes at the multi-organism scale. Both forms are full alters in their own right; *misbegotten* is precisely the wrong category. The substrate-aligned generative pattern does not produce one normative form and one defective form; it produces complementary forms whose constitutive relation is the *positivity-resonance dyad* and the *generational-multiplication community* that Q.98 (yesterday) named as integral to original creation. Wright's reading of Genesis 1:27 (*male and female he created them*) and Genesis 2:18-23 (the *helper-corresponding* relation) are the scriptural ground; the medieval correction of Aristotle is the doorway through which the contemporary frame walks more freely.

## Q.100 — The righteousness of the offspring

a.1 — *would men have been born in a state of righteousness?* — Aquinas: yes. The argument cycles back to Day 49's original-justice claim: if Adam was created in grace, with the supernatural subjection of his sensitive faculties to his intellect and of his intellect to God, *and* if the children would have been born in a similar state of harmony (since otherwise the prelapsarian generation would already be carrying a privation of the dispositional gift), then the children would have inherited the original-justice dispositional state. Aquinas adds the precision-clarifying note: *this Grace, however, would not have been natural but would have been conferred on man immediately on his receiving a rational soul, in the same way the rational soul, which is not transmitted by the parent, is infused by God as soon as the human body is apt to receive it*.

The contemporary mind-monist translation runs through the *substrate-aligned dispositional state* the original-justice synthesis (Day 49) named. Inheritance of righteousness in the prelapsarian state is not the *transmission* of a substantial form from parent to child — Aquinas himself denies that — but the *substrate's continuous gift* of substrate-aligned dispositional state to each new alter as it comes into the embodied node. The alter's substrate-aligned precision-weighting (Friston frame: charity-as-orientation toward integration with the substrate's larger structure rather than purely local self-maintenance) is a *standing condition* of the prelapsarian community, given anew to each new alter as a function of the substrate's continuous self-giving sustenance. This preserves the medieval doctrine — grace is supernatural gift, not naturally transmitted — and gives it a contemporary mid-scale shape: the substrate's giving is *constitutive* of each alter's existence, and the dispositional orientation that giving confers is *inherited* in the relevant sense without being *transmitted*. *Honest evidence calibration*: the active-inference frame on dispositional-state-as-precision-weighting is mature; the substrate's-continuous-gift extrapolation is theological-philosophical. Stump enters here as the Thomistic interlocutor: her work on grace as enabling the alter's *second-first-language fluency* in the love-of-God grammar maps onto the prelapsarian baseline as the limit case where the first-language is the love-of-God grammar from the outset.

a.2 — *would children in the state of innocence have been born confirmed in righteousness?* — Aquinas: no. The argument is direct: Adam himself was not confirmed in righteousness — he was free to fall, and did fall — and *it is clear that at their birth they would not have had greater perfection than their parents at the time of begetting*. The confirmation in righteousness Aquinas names is the *no-longer-able-to-fall* state that obtains only after the beatific vision. Aquinas adds the striking note: once the alter is taken into the beatific vision, generation ceases — *as soon as Adam had attained to that happy state of seeing God in his Essence, he would have become spiritual in soul and body, and his animal life would have ceased, wherein alone there is generation*.

This is the day's keystone move and threads back to Day 50's Q.97 a.3 directly. The contemporary mind-monist reading: *confirmation in righteousness* is the alter's transition into the substrate-direct mode that Day 7 named for the beatific vision — the dispositional state in which the alter sees the simple whole (the central theme: *when all limitations are removed, the simple whole is seen*) and therefore cannot *will* otherwise than toward loving unity, because the perspective-limitation that makes alternative-willing structurally possible has been removed. Generation ceases in this mode because generation is the multiplication-by-perspective-individuation function, and the alter at the substrate-direct mode is no longer in the perspective-individuated-by-limitation regime that generation operates within. Wright's transphysical-resurrection framing (*The Resurrection of the Son of God*; *Surprised by Hope*) is the load-bearing primary on this transition — the resurrected body is *transphysical*, neither merely physical nor immaterial spirit, in a transformed mode that preserves embodiment while shifting its operation. Luke 20:35-36 (*neither marry nor are given in marriage, because they are equal to angels and are sons of the resurrection*) is the scriptural anchor Aquinas reaches for and Wright unpacks.

## The central-theme thread

Day 51's two-question pair lands on the central theme through both registers it operates in. The body register (Q.99): the prelapsarian alter develops *through* the natural maturational arc, not around it — substrate-alignment does not collapse the structured trajectory by which the alter's generative model and embodied competence are built. The righteousness register (Q.100): inheritance of substrate-aligned dispositional state is *given by the substrate*, not transmitted by parents, and the perspective-limitation that makes alternative-willing structurally possible is *constitutive of generation itself*. When the alter transitions into the substrate-direct mode, generation ceases — not as an external prohibition but because the perspective-individuation function that generation operates is structurally absent in the substrate-direct mode.

*Salvation in fear and trembling* on this register is the alter's progressive movement *toward* the substrate-direct mode, *through* the developmental arc the Q.99 articles affirmed. Generation, child-rearing, learning, sensorimotor maturation, dispositional formation under grace — these are the structured ways the perspective-limited alter is led toward loving unity. The Q.100 a.2 article makes the eschatological shape explicit: the multiplication-mode of human existence is *for* the substrate-direct-mode, and gives way to it.
