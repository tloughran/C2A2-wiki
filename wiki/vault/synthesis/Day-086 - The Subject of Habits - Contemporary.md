# Day 86 — The Subject of Habits (Contemporary)

## Frame

Q.50 asks *where* habits live: in the body, in the soul-as-essence, in the soul's powers, in the angels. Q.51 asks *how* they form: by nature, by acts, by divine infusion. Aquinas's answer is finely articulated. Bodily dispositions like health are *habit-like* but imperfect because their causes are easily changeable. The soul *as essence* is not a proper subject of habit — your rational soul either exists or does not; there is no "more or less" along its essential being. Habits sit in the *powers* of the soul — those faculties whose acts can be modulated. Among the powers, the *intellect* and *will* admit habit (because they are not determined to one act); the *sensitive appetite* admits habit so far as it is shaped by reason; the *vegetative* powers do not (they are determined to one). Angels have habits in their intellects. Habits form by *nature* (proximate dispositions: innate temperaments; proper apprehension of first principles), by *acts* (which over time generate the disposition), and — for the *gratuitous* virtues that orient us to an end exceeding human nature — by *divine infusion*.

Under conscious-realist monism the medieval geography of habit-bearing powers maps with surprising fidelity onto the contemporary geography of *parameterizable systems within a layered agent*. Habits are stabilized parameters; some substrate-levels admit such stabilization and some do not. Aquinas's threefold causal account (nature, acts, infusion) maps onto innate prior + experiential update + non-acquired transformation — the last category being the one the contemporary frame must work hardest to interpret without flattening it.

## Problem

The Q.50-51 articles ask a single, multi-faceted question: **at which levels of an agent can stable dispositions be located, and by what mechanisms do they form there?** Aquinas needs this map because habit will carry the moral weight of the whole Prima Secundae. If habit is misplaced — if it is thought to be in the body alone, or in the soul's essence, or only in the intellect, or only acquired — the entire moral psychology buckles. The fineness of his discrimination (essence vs. powers; sense appetite as shaped by reason; vegetative powers as not subjects of habit; gratuitous virtues as infused) is what makes Q.55-70 tractable.

The contemporary problem is recognisably the same. *Where* in the layered architecture of an embodied agent do stabilized parameters live, and *how* do they come to be stabilized? The contemporary answer must say: not only at one level, and not by one mechanism. Bioelectric memory at the cellular and tissue scale (Levin). Reference-frame consolidation in cortical microcircuits (Hawkins). Precision-weighted priors across the generative hierarchy (Friston). Synaptic plasticity at the local circuit. Hemispheric attentional defaults (McGilchrist). Affective and prosocial set-points modifiable by cultivated practice (Fredrickson). The differential between *what admits habit* and *what does not* is also recognisable: deeply ancestral homeostatic loops resist re-parameterization in the way Aquinas describes the vegetative as not a proper subject of habit.

The third causal route — *infusion* — is where the contemporary frame is most exposed. Aquinas needs it for *gratuitous* virtues whose object exceeds the agent's natural capacity. Under conscious-realist monism, this is not the magical insertion of a parameter from outside the agent's mind; it is the *participation* of the agent's local generative model in a wider mind-field. Whether such participation is real, and what it amounts to mechanically, is where the synthesis must be careful: empirical here is thin, theological is rich, speculative-metaphysical is unavoidable.

## Resource

**Levin's bioelectric memory of pattern** speaks directly to Q.50 a.1 (habit in the body). Levin's lab has documented bioelectric pattern-memory that persists across cellular generations and re-routes after injury. This is not Aquinas's "imperfect habit-like quality" of health; it is a precisely empirically tractable case of *stabilized parameter at the body-level*. The medieval claim that bodily dispositions are habit-like but imperfect (because their causes are *naturally changeable*) is recovered as: bodily-level parameters exist but their precision is lower than higher-level ones and they are more directly coupled to homeostatic perturbation. PRS records in the Levin-tradition wiki on morphogenetic teleology supply the framework.

**Hawkins's reference-frame model of cortex** (PRS-03; *A Thousand Brains*) is the mechanism for Q.50 a.2-3 (habit in the powers of the soul). Cortical reference frames are *exactly* the level at which "habits in the powers" sit: not in the substance of the cortex, but in its learned configuration. Hawkins's account validates Aquinas's discrimination — the *power* (the cortex's general capacity) and the *habit* (its learned reference frame) are distinct, but the latter modifies the former's actual operation.

**Friston's active inference and hierarchical generative models** supplies the formal architecture. The full layered agent has parameters at every level; some of these update slowly (priors), some quickly (likelihoods). Aquinas's distinction between essence (does-not-admit-habit) and powers (does-admit-habit) maps onto: the *form* of the generative model (the architecture, the ontology of variables) is essence-like; the *parameters* of that model (priors, precisions, learned likelihoods) are power-like and admit modification. The "vegetative powers do not admit habit" claim recovers as: the deepest homeostatic priors (interoceptive set-points for survival) are precision-locked and resist update. Friston's PRS records on the organism as active modeler under resource constraint apply.

**McGilchrist's hemispheric account** sharpens Q.50 a.3 (habit in the sensitive appetite as shaped by reason). The sensitive appetite — affective response — is in fact *shaped by reason* via long-loop top-down modulation, and McGilchrist's account of hemispheric specialization gives the mechanism: cortical (especially right-hemispheric) attentional and contextual processing shapes the affective evaluation that drives appetite. The mediating role of reason that Aquinas requires is empirically tractable.

**Stump's analytic-Thomistic work on virtue-as-habituation** is the keystone interlocutor on Q.50-51. The Stump-tradition wiki (her PRS-04 on final-causality-and-form; *Wandering in Darkness* on virtue-as-habituation) gives the most rigorous contemporary reading of these articles. Tom's PRS-form re-description of her work covers habits as Thomistic formal dispositions; the conscious-realist-monist reframe enters by reading "formal disposition" as "stabilized generative-model parameter" without contradicting her account. Stump is read here as Thomistic interlocutor; the metaphysical guardrail makes Levin/Hoffman/Kastrup primary on the underlying *what is form* question.

**Fredrickson's practice-induced affective stabilization** is the empirical evidence for Q.51 a.2 (habits caused by acts). The longitudinal work on loving-kindness meditation, positivity resonance, and high-quality connections (Fredrickson PRS-01, PRS-08 in the Fredrickson-tradition wiki) documents the *mechanism* by which acts cause habits — vagal tone, attentional defaults, prosocial set-points all shift stably with sustained practice. This is Aquinas's "habits formed by acts" with longitudinal data behind it.

**Hoffman's interface theory** speaks to the Q.50 a.4 question of habits in angels — and more practically to the *structural* claim that habits are properly in the powers of the soul, not the essence. The interface (the way the agent encounters the world) is parameterized; the underlying conscious-agent is not parameterized in the same sense. The medieval power/essence distinction maps onto the interface/agent distinction in Hoffman's analytic structure, with the agent's "essence" being non-parameterized presence and the interface being layered-parameterized.

**Kastrup on mind-at-large and infusion** is the analytic-idealist resource for Q.51 a.4 (gratuitous virtues by divine infusion). Under analytic idealism, the individual mind is a *dissociated alter* of mind-at-large. Habits ordered to ends exceeding the alter's natural capacity (gratuitous virtues) are then *participations* of the alter's local dynamics in the wider field's dynamics — not parameter insertion from outside but de-dissociative participation. This is not Aquinas's account verbatim but it is the most natural conscious-realist-monist reading of "infusion."

**Wright on virtue-formation as life-in-the-Spirit** anchors the theological-scriptural side. Wright's *After You Believe* explicitly engages the Thomistic-Aristotelian account of virtue-as-habituation within the Pauline framework of being-in-Christ. The "infused virtues" of Q.51 a.4 — caused by God without our action but not without our consent — are the Pauline shape of fruit-of-the-Spirit formation: the agent's consent is necessary, the formation exceeds the agent's natural capacity. Wright is the ground-truth oracle on this point.

**Rohr on contemplative habituation and second-half-of-life integration** rounds out the theological side. The infused virtues that orient to ends exceeding human nature are, in Rohr's terms, dispositions of the contemplative-cosmic-Christ frame — habituated participation in the Universal Christ. *Falling Upward* makes the dynamic explicit: the second-half-of-life settled disposition is not built up by the agent's first-half effort alone, but received and habituated through ongoing participation.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

**(a) Shared with Aquinas.** Habits live in *parameterizable* levels of the agent — not in the agent's essence (which is non-parameterized presence) and not in the deepest homeostatic loops (which are precision-locked). Habits form by nature (innate priors, temperaments, first-principle apprehensions), by acts (experience-driven update), and — for ends exceeding the agent's natural capacity — by participation in a wider field. The body has habit-like stabilizations but they are more directly perturbable than the higher-level ones. The sensitive appetite admits habit so far as it is shaped by reason (top-down attentional and contextual modulation).

**(b) Where it reframes.** Aquinas's category of *essence* recovers as the non-parameterized presence of the conscious agent (Hoffman) / the dissociative boundary of the alter (Kastrup) / the architectural form of the generative model (Friston). Aquinas's category of *powers* recovers as the parameterized layers of that model. *Habits in the powers* recovers as the stabilized priors, precisions, and reference frames within those layers. *Infusion* recovers as participation of the local alter in mind-at-large — the de-dissociative widening that lets the agent stabilize dispositions whose objects exceed its individuated capacity.

**(c) Where evidence is firm vs. speculative.** *Firm*: practice-induced affective stabilization (Fredrickson; Davidson; Goleman). *Firm*: hierarchical predictive control and the dual structure of habitual vs. goal-directed action (Daw, Niv, Dayan; FitzGerald, Dolan, Friston). *Empirically supported*: cortical reference-frame learning (Hawkins). *Empirically supported at increasing scales*: bioelectric pattern memory (Levin's lab). *Speculative metaphysical*: the conscious-realist-monist reading of essence as non-parameterized presence; the interpretation of "infusion" as participation in mind-at-large. *Theological, well-attested*: Wright on virtue-formation as life-in-the-Spirit; Rohr on contemplative-cosmic-Christ habituation.

## The central-theme thread

The central theme — *conscious agents are individuated by the limitations of their perspective; loving unity is the computational task; we work it out in fear and trembling* — gets its architectural underpinning here. Q.50 maps where the working-out lives. It cannot live in the agent's essence (the agent simply is, with no "more or less"). It cannot live in the deepest homeostatic loops (these are too tightly bound to survival to be reshaped by the moral life). It lives in the *intermediate* parameterizable layers — the powers, in Aquinas's term — where deliberate action over time can stabilize new dispositions, and where top-down attentional shaping can modulate even affective response.

Q.51's threefold causal account is the central theme's mechanism. *Nature* — the limited starting perspective each agent receives. *Acts* — the agent's own contribution under perspective-limitation, the Phil 2:12 working-out. *Infusion* — the participation in wider perspective that the agent cannot supply for itself, the "guidance from those who perceive more of the whole" in its most radical form. The synthesis must hold all three: the limited agent works out salvation by its own acts under the conditions of its own nature, and the working-out is itself made possible by participations whose source exceeds the agent. Rohr's universal-Christ frame and Wright's life-in-the-Spirit converge here.

A specific dialectical move follows. Stump-tradition Thomism reads infused virtue as God's action with the agent's consent. The conscious-realist-monist reframe reads infused virtue as widening participation in mind-at-large with the alter's consent. The two readings are not in contradiction at the level of *what the agent experiences and what the agent must do* (consent and continued cooperation). They differ in *what the source of infusion is metaphysically*. The synthesis here is candid about this — and notes that Wright and Rohr work theologically within a frame that is compatible with both readings.

## Bridges

- **Levin / bioelectric memory of pattern** — Q.50 a.1 (habit in the body): bodily-level stabilized parameters with lower precision than higher levels; *empirical, growing in scale*.
- **Hawkins PRS-03 / *A Thousand Brains*; cortical reference frames** — Q.50 a.2-3 (habit in the powers of the soul): the cortical level at which "habits in the powers" sit; *neuroscientifically grounded*.
- **Friston PRS-04 + hierarchical active inference** — Q.50's essence/powers distinction and Q.51's acts-cause-habits: form-of-model vs. parameters-of-model; experience-driven precision update; *formal, empirically robust*.
- **McGilchrist / hemispheric specialization** — Q.50 a.3's sensitive-appetite-as-shaped-by-reason: top-down attentional and contextual modulation of affect; *clinical evidence*.
- **Stump PRS-04 + virtue-as-habituation** — analytic-Thomistic articulation of Q.50-51 as keystone interlocutor; *philosophically rigorous*.
- **Fredrickson PRS-01, PRS-08** — Q.51 a.2 (habits caused by acts): longitudinal practice-induced stabilization; *empirical, high confidence*.
- **Hoffman / interface theory** — Q.50 a.4 (angelic habit) and the structural essence/powers distinction; *speculative-formal, internally consistent*.
- **Kastrup / analytic idealism, dissociative alter, mind-at-large** — Q.51 a.4 (infused virtue): participation of local alter in wider field; *speculative-metaphysical, internally consistent*.
- **Wright / *After You Believe*; Pauline virtue-formation** — Q.51 a.4 theologically: infused virtue as life-in-the-Spirit; *theological, well-attested*.
- **Rohr / *Falling Upward*, *The Universal Christ*** — Q.51 a.4 contemplatively: infused virtue as cosmic-Christ habituation; *contemplative tradition*.

## Where this leaves us

Q.50-51 supplies the architecture; Q.52-53 (tomorrow) takes up the dynamics — *increase* and *diminution* of habits. The contemporary frame must now turn from "where do habits live" to "how do habits grow and decay" — which will lean heavily on Friston's precision-update dynamics, Fredrickson's longitudinal data on practice-vs-disuse, the basal-ganglia / cortical literature on habit acquisition and extinction, and Stump on the will's role in formation. The structural pivot of Day 85 — from passion to habit — now begins its substantive build-out, with the central theme tracked at each step: the limited agent stabilizing dispositions toward loving unity under the conditions of its individuated perspective, with the working-out depending on participations the agent does not generate alone.
