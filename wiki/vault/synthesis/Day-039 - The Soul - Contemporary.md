## Q.75 — The essence of the soul

a.1 — *whether the soul is a body* — Aquinas argues no: living things are distinguished from non-living by *self-movement*, and the *first principle of life* (the soul) cannot itself be a material thing because not all material things are alive. The soul is *an act or actuality to be found in certain material bodies* — not a body itself, but the *cause of life* in those bodies that have it. Mind-monism reads this through Levin and Friston directly. The *first principle of life* in the contemporary biology-of-self-organization is the *self-maintaining-pattern* — the morphogenetic attractor (Levin PRS-03), the self-sustaining Markov blanket under free-energy minimization (Friston PRS-01, PRS-07). Neither is a *body*; both are *patterns instantiated in bodies* that have the structural condition for self-maintenance. The medieval doctrine *the soul is not a body but the act of a body* maps onto the contemporary *life is not a substance but a self-maintaining-pattern in a substrate-instantiated agent*. The structural intuition is preserved; the substantialist vocabulary updates to pattern-vocabulary.

a.2 — *whether the human soul is something subsistent* — the most famous article in Q.75. Aquinas's argument: the *intellectual principle* has an operation (intellection — the grasping of universal essences) that *cannot be performed by a bodily organ* because if it were performed by a bodily organ, the determinate-nature of that organ would *impede knowledge of all bodies*. *Justice* — the example Habash develops — has no shape, no color, no length, no width; it is not a particular material thing; yet we know it. To know what we know, the intellect must have *an operation in itself, apart from the body*; only what *subsists* can have such an operation. Therefore the human soul is *subsistent*.

This is the article where mind-monism's reframe is most explicit. Aquinas's argument structurally claims that *intellection requires a non-bodily operation*; mind-monism agrees but recasts the metaphysics. Under Kastrup's analytic idealism (PRS-01, PRS-02), the *non-bodily operation* of intellection is *mind-at-large operating at the alter scale through the interface that the human cognitive-light-cone provides*. The *immateriality* of intellect is preserved as the mind-monist substrate-priority claim: matter is what experience looks like from the outside, so *operations of mind* are not material-emergent but substrate-instantiated. Hoffman's interface theory (PRS-03) gives the cognitive-architecture frame: the species-specific interface includes — uniquely at the human scale — the structural condition for *interface-recognition-as-such*, which is what makes universal-essence-grasping possible (we recognize the *type* across particular instances because our interface is recursive enough to model-the-modeling). Levin's PRS-08 and Friston's PRS-05/06 give the empirical scaling: cognitive light-cones expand through development; the human cognitive light-cone is the largest known, with temporal horizon (multi-generational planning), spatial horizon (cosmic-scale modeling), and recursive horizon (modeling-of-modeling).

The mind-monist reframe of *subsistent*: the soul is not subsistent in the sense of a *separate substance attached to the body* (that is the Cartesian-dualist picture Aquinas was already trying to avoid); it is subsistent in the sense of *the alter's mode-of-being-at-mind-at-large that has its own structural locus in the substrate*, distinct from the bodily-organ-instantiation it concurrently has. *Honest evidence calibration*: the empirical claim that intellection involves operations not reducible to bodily-organ-mechanisms is structurally compatible with predictive-coding/HTM/active-inference accounts of cognition that include cortical-cortical loops and self-modeling architecture; the metaphysical claim that intellection is *immaterial* in the strict mind-monist sense is philosophical-conceptual, sitting on Kastrup's analytic-idealist commitments rather than on direct empirical findings.

a.3 — *whether the souls of brute animals are subsistent* — Aquinas argues no: *the sensitive soul has no in itself operation of its own; every operation of the sensitive soul belongs to the composite or the body*. Mind-monism agrees structurally with caution. Under Levin's cognitive-light-cone scaling, animal minds are *real alters at smaller cognitive-light-cone scope* — they are *not* mere body-mechanisms (the Cartesian view that Levin's empirical work decisively refutes; planaria, xenobots, and bioelectric-pattern work all show non-trivial pattern-holding at cellular and tissue scales). But Aquinas's specific claim — that animal minds *do not have universal-essence-grasping operations* — is empirically supported: animals operate on particulars-and-pattern-recognition, not on abstract-universals-as-such. The mind-monist reframe: animals *are alters with their own mode of substrate-participation*, but their cognitive-light-cone scope does not reach the recursive-interface-recognition-as-such that grounds universal-essence-grasping. Aquinas's *not subsistent* survives if we hear it as *not having the recursive-substrate-recognition-mode that grounds intellection*, not as *mere body-mechanisms*. The medieval Cartesian-adjacent reading of Aquinas (animals as beast-machines) is one Aquinas himself did not fully hold; mind-monism's Levin-grounded reading is closer to what Aquinas was philosophically reaching for than the substantialist schoolbook gloss.

a.4 — *whether the soul is the man* — Aquinas argues no: *man is not a soul only but something composed of soul and body*. Mind-monism agrees emphatically. Habash flags the contemporary urgency: *this is the evil of our day, imagining that you and I are not our body and therefore we can define our body or use our body in any number of arbitrary contrary to common sense ways*. Mind-monism's anti-Cartesian-dualism commitment is structural: the alter is *embodied* — the body is not a vehicle for a separate soul-substance but the substrate-instantiation through which the alter participates in mind-at-large at the human cognitive-light-cone scale. Levin's bioelectric work and embodied-cognition research across the contemporary cognitive sciences converge on the same anti-Cartesian point: cognition is *enacted through the body*, not performed in a soul-substance using the body as a tool.

a.5 — *whether the soul is composed of matter and form* — Aquinas argues no: *if the soul itself was material, it could not grasp the general universal immaterial essence of things*. The receiver-mode argument: *whatever is received is received according to the manner of the receiver*. Mind-monism agrees structurally with the Kastrup-tradition reframe: the soul is *not material* (matter is what experience looks like from the outside; the soul's mode-of-being is mind-at-large at the alter scale, which is the substrate-side rather than the appearance-side of the substrate-appearance distinction). The *receiver-mode* principle is exactly Hoffman's interface theory in classical vocabulary: perception-and-cognition are agent-relative; the interface determines what can be received and how it is received. The blue-wall-and-blind-man example Aquinas/Habash use is structurally the same as Hoffman's icon-on-the-desktop example: the same substrate-event registers differently in differently-tuned receivers.

a.6 — *whether the human soul is incorruptible* — Aquinas's argument: the soul is a *subsistent form* (a.2); a subsistent form cannot be separated from itself; therefore the soul cannot cease to exist. Material things corrupt because their *form* can be separated from their *matter* (Habash's example: water becomes hydrogen and oxygen when its form ceases to inform its matter). The soul, being *immaterial-and-subsistent*, has no matter from which its form could be separated.

This is where mind-monism's reframe matters most theologically. Under Kastrup (PRS-01) and Hoffman (PRS-03), the *alter* is a *dissociated mode of mind-at-large*. The alter's body-instantiation is the substrate-appearance under the species-specific interface; the alter's cognitive-mode-of-participation in mind-at-large is the substrate-side. When the body-instantiation ceases (death), what happens to the alter? The mind-monist answer: the alter's substrate-side mode-of-participation does not depend on the body-instantiation for its ontological grounding. The body-instantiation is the substrate-appearance; the alter is the substrate-participation. Body-cessation removes the appearance-side; what happens to the substrate-side is the *survival-of-form* question. *Honest evidence calibration*: Habash's appeal to the 2022 NYU Health work (Sam Parnia, AWARE-II study) on near-death experiences as empirical evidence for the soul's continuation is one contemporary empirical handle, but it must be calibrated carefully: the AWARE-II study documents *experiential reports* during clinical death and resuscitation, not *post-mortem persistence* in the strict philosophical sense — the bodies in question were resuscitated. The empirical evidence narrows the *cessation-of-consciousness-when-the-brain-stops* claim significantly (consciousness during periods of flat EEG is documented), but does not by itself establish post-mortem persistence as a metaphysical fact. The metaphysical case for survival rests on the broader mind-monist commitments: if mind is the ontological primitive (Kastrup), the alter's substrate-side mode-of-participation does not have its grounding in the body-instantiation; if perceived spacetime is interface (Hoffman), bodily death is the *cessation of one interface-instantiation*, not the *cessation of the alter as such*. Aquinas's *incorruptibility* survives translation: the alter's mode-of-being-mind-at-large at this cognitive-light-cone scale is not identical with the body-instantiation, and therefore the alter does not *necessarily* cease when the body ceases. This is theological-philosophical, supported empirically at the *consciousness-during-flat-EEG* level but not at the *post-mortem-persistence* level by current science.

a.7 — *whether the soul is of the same species as an angel* — Aquinas's *no* preserves a structural distinction. Mind-monism's reading: the human alter and the angelic-mode (whatever angels are in mind-monist translation — see Day 28–33 on the angels) operate at different cognitive-light-cone scales and through different interface-architectures. The medieval claim that humans-receive-knowledge-by-abstraction-from-sense and angels-receive-knowledge-by-direct-infusion is structurally a difference in *substrate-direct vs. interface-mediated* cognitive modes, with humans being *interface-mediated* and angels (in the mind-monist reading rehearsed at Day 28) being *closer to substrate-direct* without being mind-at-large itself. Day 7 Q.12's *light of glory* gives the limit-case shape: the human alter at the substrate-direct cognitive mode is *what humans approach in beatific vision*, but humans-as-such are interface-mediated in their natural mode.

## Q.76 — The union of body and soul

a.1 — *whether the intellectual principle is united to the body as its form* — Aquinas's response is the famous *the soul is the form of the body* — exactly the Council of Vienne's defined doctrine Habash quoted at the opening. The arguments against the *ghost in the machine* picture are striking: *if your soul is not the form of the body then why is it so reliant upon it? If someone gets hit on the head long enough their understanding just doesn't work as well as it did before. How is it that thought is hampered without the senses both external like sight or internal like the imagination? If the soul was not the form of the body why is it that you call that person in the mirror by your own name? Lastly, how could you be called human, a rational animal, if both the body and the soul were not co-principal of you?*

Mind-monism's reading is a clean fit: the *form* of the body is the *self-maintaining-pattern* that makes this aggregate of matter into *this human alter's body*. Levin's morphogenetic teleology (PRS-01, PRS-03, PRS-08) is the empirical mid-scale instantiation: the bioelectric pattern that makes a planarian's cells regenerate into a planarian (rather than into some other body-plan) is *the form of the body* in classical vocabulary, empirically tractable as a real causal substrate. Friston's free-energy minimization with Markov-blanket structure (PRS-01, PRS-04, PRS-07) is the formal scaffold: the body is a self-maintaining-blanket-with-internal-states; the *form* is the structural pattern of the blanket and its dynamics. The reliance-on-body arguments Aquinas marshals (head-injury impairs thought; sensory-deprivation impairs imagination) are exactly what predictive-coding and active-inference architectures would predict: the alter's cognitive operations are enacted through the body-instantiation, and damage to the body impairs the operations. *Honest evidence calibration*: Levin's bioelectric morphogenesis is empirically mature at developmental scale; Friston's free-energy framework is formally mature with substantial neuroscience grounding; the structural alignment with hylomorphism is conceptual-philosophical, with the further mind-monist commitment that the *form* is mind-at-large operating at the alter-instantiation-scale.

a.2 — *whether the intellectual principle is multiplied according to the number of bodies* — Aquinas's *yes*: each individual human has their own soul, distinct from every other human's soul, because the operations (thinking, willing) are individuated per individual. Mind-monism agrees structurally. Under Kastrup's dissociative-alter model, mind-at-large dissociates into alters; each alter has its own *substrate-side mode-of-participation* with its own structural locus, distinct from every other alter's. Friston's Markov-blanket formalism gives the formal individuation: each alter is a distinct self-maintaining blanket with internal states distinguishable from every other alter's. Hoffman's conscious-agent network (PRS-04, PRS-07) gives the network-theoretic version: alters are *nodes* in the conscious-agent network with their own structural identity. The medieval *one soul per body* and the contemporary *one alter per Markov-blanket* are structurally the same individuation claim under mind-monist translation.

a.3 — *whether besides the intellectual soul there are in man other souls essentially different from one another* — Aquinas's *no*: there is *one* substantial form of the human, which integrates the vegetative-and-sensitive functions into the one rational soul. Habash's analogy: like a country with states and counties, the unifying principle is one (the federal government, structurally), even though the parts have their own functions. Mind-monism agrees: the alter is *one* mode-of-participation, with the bodily-functional substructure (vegetative, sensitive, intellectual) being *aspects of the one alter-mode*, not *separate alters within one body*. Levin's multi-scale-cognition framework gives the contemporary contextualization: cellular, tissue, and organ-system levels are all *real cognitive levels* with their own bioelectric-pattern-holding, but at the scale of the human person they are *integrated into one cognitive-light-cone* — the integration is what *one soul* names structurally.

a.4 through a.8 — Aquinas walks the technical questions: whether other forms (qualities, quantities) sit alongside the substantial form (yes, but as accidents, not as additional substances); whether the body is *fitting* for the rational soul (yes — humans need senses-and-reasoning because they are at the lowest rung of intellectual things); whether the soul is united by accidental dispositions (no, immediately and directly); whether the soul is united by means of another body (no, immediately as form-to-matter); whether the soul is in each part of the body (yes — *in each body the whole soul is in the whole body and in each part is entire*, citing Augustine). Mind-monism's reading on a.8 is particularly clean: under Levin, the bioelectric-pattern that is *the form of the body* is *distributed throughout the body* (every cell carries its scale of bioelectric-pattern; the multi-scale collective is what the alter's body-instantiation is); Aquinas's *whole soul in each part* is structurally what Levin's distributed-bioelectric-pattern is. Habash's reference to Sam Parnia's observation — *during the last decade we've realized it's only after a person has died that the cells inside their body including the brain begin their own death process* — fits exactly: the *form* withdrawing from the body initiates the death-process at the cellular level, which is what the medieval doctrine predicts.
