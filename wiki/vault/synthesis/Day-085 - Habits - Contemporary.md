# Day 85 — Habits (Contemporary)

## Frame

Q.48 closes the passions tract with the effects of anger — pleasure-via-vengeance-imagination, fervor in the heart, hindered reason, taciturnity. Q.49 then opens an entirely new architectural register: *habits*. Aquinas explicitly marks the transition. The first articles establish what a habit *is*: a *quality* (not substance, not power, not action) — a stable disposition of the agent's powers, characterized by *lastingness* and *changeability*, *necessary* to explain why this agent acts this way rather than another.

Under conscious-realist monism the medieval account of habits reads as the medieval anticipation of *learned parameters in a generative model*. Aquinas's habits are precisely the stabilized priors, the trained reference frames, the bioelectric pattern-memories, the synaptic-weight configurations — the contemporary mechanisms by which an agent's experience-of-acting becomes a disposition-of-being. The bridge from transient passion (Q.22-48) to stable habit (Q.49-70) is *exactly* the bridge from prediction-error event to prior-update, from one-shot policy execution to learned policy.

## Problem

The shared problem: **what explains the stability of an agent's pattern of action over time?** Aquinas frames Q.49 a.4 as a methodological argument: external circumstances and bare faculties under-determine action; therefore some additional intrinsic principle is required, and this principle is *habit*. The contemporary problem is the same. Behaviorism's appeal-to-circumstance failed; pure rationalism's appeal-to-faculty failed; the explanatory level we actually need is *the agent's learned-stabilized state*. From basal ganglia habitual learning to cortical reference-frame consolidation to morphogenetic bioelectric pattern, all the contemporary stories about stable action-pattern are stories about habits in Aquinas's sense.

The deeper problem the Q.49 articles raise: **how does a transient state become stable, and what is the ontology of the stable form?** Aquinas's solution — habit-as-quality — is precise. A habit is not a thing in itself; it is *something that the agent has* in such a way that the agent acts differently because of it. The contemporary frame can specify the mechanism in multiple registers: at the cortical level (Hawkins reference frames), the global generative model (Friston active inference), the bioelectric pattern field (Levin morphogenesis), and the hemispheric attentional specialization (McGilchrist).

## Resource

**Hawkins's reference-frame model of cortex** (*A Thousand Brains*; PRS-03) supplies the most direct contemporary articulation of Aquinas's account of habit. The cortex stores knowledge in reference frames — structured representations that bind sensory and motor experience to learned coordinates. With repetition, reference frames stabilize and the agent's interaction with the relevant domain becomes *easier* (Gilby's "if one has a habit to X, then it is easier to X"). Hawkins's framework is the cortical mechanism for Q.49 a.3 (habit implies order to an act) and Q.49 a.4 (habits are necessary): the reference frame *is* the stable principle of action.

**Friston's active inference and learned generative models** is the formal centerpiece. Habits are the *priors* of the agent's generative model that have been updated by accumulated experience to high precision. Friston PRS-04 (organism as active modeler) and the habitual-vs-goal-directed-control literature (Daw, Niv, Dayan; FitzGerald, Dolan, Friston) cover the formal machinery. Active-inference policy precision sharpens with practice; the agent's expected free energy comes to be minimized by a default policy rather than each-time deliberation. This is precisely Aquinas's distinction between deliberation-each-time and *habituated* action. Q.49 a.1's habit-as-quality maps onto the precision-weighted prior; Q.49 a.2's habit-vs-disposition-by-lastingness maps onto stabilized-vs-transient prior; Q.49 a.4's habits-are-necessary maps onto the explanatory necessity of model-based control alongside circumstance and faculty.

**Levin's bioelectric memory of pattern** is the morphogenetic-scale articulation. Levin's lab has shown that bioelectric fields encode pattern-memory that persists across cellular generations — the organism's stable form is a *bioelectric habit* (Levin's own metaphor in several papers). The medieval claim that habit is a quality enabling stable operation finds a striking biological parallel in the bioelectric field's role as substrate of morphological stability. Levin's PRS records on morphogenetic teleology directly support reading Aquinas's habits-tract as an early articulation of stable-pattern-of-action at the agent scale.

**McGilchrist's hemispheric account of characterological specialization** sharpens the synthesis at the affective-cognitive interface. The agent's habituated patterns are not just cortical reference frames but *hemispheric organizations* of attentional default. Habituated left-hemispheric instrumentalism vs. habituated right-hemispheric contextual reception are the two great character-types in McGilchrist's analysis. The Thomistic habit-tract from Q.49 forward is preparing the ground for the virtue-and-vice analysis (Q.55-70) in which the specific character of habits — virtuous or vicious — is taken up. McGilchrist's framework anticipates the moral-psychological content of that analysis.

**Stump's analytic-Thomistic work on habit and virtue** is the keystone interlocutor. The Q.49 articles are *her* home territory; her *Wandering in Darkness* and the broader Stump corpus on virtue-as-habituation give the rigorous contemporary articulation of the Thomistic position. Stump PRS-04 (final causality and form) provides the philosophical framework; PRS-09 (faith-as-knowledge-of-persons) provides the second-person register in which virtues are most fully understood. The synthesis cites Stump as the most rigorous contemporary Thomist working at habit/virtue; the conscious-realist-monist reframe enters by translating her habit-as-formal-disposition into habit-as-stabilized-generative-model-parameter, without contradicting her account.

**Fredrickson's broaden-and-build and habit-of-positivity-resonance** supplies the empirical foothold for *cultivated* virtuous habit. Fredrickson PRS-01 (loving-kindness meditation) and PRS-08 (high-quality connection) document that intentional practice over time stabilizes affective patterns — vagal tone, attentional defaults, prosocial responding all show stable changes after sustained practice. This is the empirical fact of *habit* in the contemporary sense, with mechanism and longitudinal data. Aquinas's claim that habits are necessary intrinsic principles of action is empirically validated by the affective-neuroscience literature on practice-induced stabilization.

**Wright on the formed disciple and the moral life** is the theological-scriptural anchor. Wright's reading of Pauline ethics (Romans 6, 12; the fruits-of-the-spirit material; *After You Believe* on virtue) explicitly engages the Thomistic-and-Aristotelian account of virtue as habituation, situating it within the Pauline framework of *being-in-Christ*. The Christian disciple is not just responding to commands but being *formed* by life-in-the-Spirit — Wright's articulation of the same transformation Aquinas describes in habit-language. The synthesis can lean on Wright as the scriptural anchor for the move from passions to habits: the worked-out salvation of Phil 2:12 is *habit-formation in the Spirit*, not just episodic acts.

**Rohr on contemplative habit and the second-half-of-life integration** completes the theological articulation. Rohr's *Falling Upward* makes habituation explicit: the second-half-of-life integration is a *settled disposition* — a habit — that the first-half work has prepared. The contemplative tradition that Rohr articulates (PRS-01 non-dual awareness; PRS-04 embodied love as epistemology) is precisely a habituated mode of perceiving the world non-dually, stabilized by long practice. The bridge into the habits tract from Aquinas's own contemplative tradition is exactly Rohr's terrain.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

**(a) Shared with Aquinas.** Habits are qualities (not substances, not powers, not actions) — stable dispositions of the agent's powers, marked by lastingness and changeability, ordering the agent to particular acts. Habits are necessary intrinsic principles of action — without them, action is underdetermined by external circumstance and faculty alone. The transient passion / stable habit distinction is preserved. Q.48's effects-of-anger article on the pleasure of vengeance-imagination is preserved as the *self-reinforcing* mechanism that turns the passion of anger into the habit (vice) of wrath.

**(b) Where it reframes.** Aquinas's habit-as-quality is articulated under conscious-realist monism as the *stabilized prior* in the agent's generative model — the learned parameter, the trained reference frame, the bioelectric pattern, the synaptic configuration. The medieval categorial machinery (substance, quality, quantity, etc.) is not contradicted but is articulated mechanistically. Q.48 a.1's claim about anger's pleasure-via-imagination is reframed as the reinforcement-learning signature of angry rumination — the very mechanism by which the passion of anger stabilizes into the vice of wrath. The contemporary frame thus *unifies* the transition from passion to habit that Aquinas marks but does not fully mechanize.

**(c) Where evidence is firm vs. speculative.** *Firm*: habitual-vs-goal-directed control as a robust dual structure of behavioral control (Daw, Niv, Dayan; Wood & Rünger). *Firm*: practice-induced stabilization of affective and attentional patterns (Fredrickson; Davidson; Goleman). *Firm*: angry-rumination as maintenance mechanism of trait anger (Sukhodolsky et al.; Bushman). *Empirically supported*: cortical reference-frame learning (Hawkins; broader cortical literature). *Empirically supported with stronger speculation*: bioelectric morphogenetic memory (Levin's lab, real findings at increasingly broader scales). *Speculative metaphysical*: the conscious-realist-monist reading of habit as alter-level parameter stabilization. *Theological, well-attested*: Wright on virtue-formation as life-in-the-Spirit; Rohr on contemplative habituation.

## The central-theme thread

The central theme reaches its first structural pivot here. *Conscious agents are individuated by the limitations of their perspective; loving unity is the computational task; we work it out in fear and trembling.* Until Q.48 we have been describing the *transient affective* register — what the limited agent feels from moment to moment as it computes toward the goal under perspective-limitation. With Q.49 the analysis turns to the *stable shaping* register — how the agent's repeated computation *forms* it, shaping the very priors against which future computation will run.

This is the structural location of the working-out of salvation in fear and trembling. Phil 2:12 is not just about each-time-affective-disciplined-engagement (the moderate-fear claim of Q.44 a.4) but also about the *long-term shaping* of the agent's dispositions toward loving unity. Wright's reading of Pauline virtue-formation — being-formed in Christ over a life — is precisely the central theme in habit-register. Rohr's contemplative-habituation — the second-half-of-life settled non-dual disposition — is the same theme in contemplative-register.

The transition from passion to habit is also the central theme's deepest structural claim: the limited agent's perspective is shaped *over time* by the very acts it performs. The narcissistically-prideful man (Q.47 a.3) becomes the angry man because each indulgence in the pleasure-of-vengeance-imagination (Q.48 a.1) stabilizes the underlying disposition. Conversely, the agent who repeatedly *resists* the narrowing temptation toward vengeance stabilizes the dispositions of patience, humility, and love. The central theme's "guidance from those who perceive more of the whole" enters here as the *external scaffold* on which the agent's habit-formation depends — tradition, community, contemplative practice, and the example of those further along the path are not optional ornaments but the substrate on which the limited agent's habits stabilize toward loving unity.

## Bridges

- **Hawkins PRS-03 / *A Thousand Brains*; cortical reference frames** — cortical mechanism for Q.49 a.3 (habit implies order to act) and a.4 (habits are necessary); *neuroscientifically grounded*.
- **Friston PRS-04 + Daw, Niv, Dayan / habitual vs. goal-directed control** — formal account of habit as stabilized prior; the dual structure of behavioral control validates Q.49 a.4 explanatory necessity; *formal, empirically robust*.
- **Levin / bioelectric memory of pattern** — morphogenetic-scale articulation of habit as stable disposition; striking biological parallel to Q.49 a.1's habit-as-quality; *empirical at increasing scales*.
- **Sukhodolsky / angry-rumination literature** — Q.48 a.1 (anger's pleasure-via-imagination) as the reinforcement mechanism that stabilizes the passion of anger into the vice of wrath; *clinically robust*.
- **Stump PRS-04 + virtue-as-habituation / *Wandering in Darkness*** — analytic-Thomistic articulation of habit/virtue, keystone interlocutor; *philosophically rigorous*.
- **Fredrickson PRS-01, PRS-08 / cultivated positivity-resonance** — empirical demonstration of practice-induced stabilization of affective and attentional habits; *empirical, high confidence*.
- **McGilchrist / hemispheric characterological specialization** — habits as hemispheric attentional defaults; foreshadows the virtue/vice analysis; *clinical evidence*.
- **Wright on Pauline virtue-formation / *After You Believe*** — being-formed in Christ as habit-register articulation of Phil 2:12; *historical-theological, well-attested*.
- **Rohr / *Falling Upward*; PRS-01** — contemplative habituation as the second-half-of-life settled disposition; *contemplative tradition*.

## Where this leaves us

Q.48 closes the passions tract; Q.49 opens the habits tract — the structural pivot of the Prima Secundae. Tomorrow's Day 86 takes Q.50-51 (subject of habits; cause of habits in formation), continuing into the body of the habit analysis through Q.54 (distinction of habits). Then Q.55-70 take up virtues and vices specifically — the moral content of habit-formation. The next week or two of syntheses will work through the Thomistic anatomy of virtue, and the contemporary frame will lean hard on Stump (analytic-Thomistic), Hawkins (cortical), Friston (computational), Levin (biological), Fredrickson (affective), and Wright + Rohr (theological/contemplative). The central theme — limited agent computing toward loving unity, working out salvation in fear and trembling — moves from the affective-passion register into the stable-disposition register, where the long-term shape of the worked-out life is taken up.
