# Day 81 — Hope (Contemporary)

## Frame

Q.40 opens the *irascible* passions — those of the appetite that engage difficulty rather than simple presence or absence. Hope and despair are the first pair: same object (the arduous future good), opposite movements (toward as attainable, away as unattainable). Q.41 then introduces fear: a future evil under the aspect of difficult and non-resistible. The two questions together set the formal structure of every irascible passion — *future good or evil under a difficulty-and-feasibility weighting*.

Under conscious-realist monism the medieval question reframes naturally. The irascible passions are not metaphysically suspicious — they are *policy-selection dynamics in an agent operating under perspective-limitation*. The arduous future good is the goal-state in the agent's generative model; the feasibility weighting is the precision the agent places on the policy that leads to it; hope is the affective form of high feasibility-precision plus high desirability; despair is the affective form of low feasibility-precision retained over time; fear is the affective form keyed to the contrary case (future *evil* + non-resistibility). The contemporary frame can specify what Aquinas left at the level of philosophical anthropology with mechanism, evidence strength, and a clear placement in the central-theme structure.

## Problem

The shared problem is: **how does a limited agent select action under uncertainty about whether the goal-state is reachable?** Aquinas approaches the question affectively. Hope, despair, and fear are *what it feels like* to compute action-feasibility in the appetite, with their characteristic somatic signatures (hope's stretching outward, fear's bodily contraction, despair's withdrawal). The puzzle Aquinas does not have full vocabulary for: what *is* the underlying computation, and what evidence regulates it? Q.40 a.5 (experience cuts both ways) is his closest pass — accumulated experience can ground either hope or despair, depending on the evidence. But the formal machinery is post-Bayesian.

The contemporary frame supplies it: each of the irascible passions is the affective shadow of a precision parameter on a future-state policy. Hope, despair, fear, daring (tomorrow's Q.45), and anger (Q.46-48) form a structured space, not a list. Aquinas's six-species taxonomy of fear in Q.41 a.4 (laziness, shamefacedness, shame, amazement, stupor, anxiety) reads like a six-cell partition of the future-evil space by *target* (action vs. thing) and *modality* (anticipated, executed, unknown).

## Resource

**Friston's active inference** is the load-bearing resource here. The Free Energy Principle (Friston PRS-01) holds that biological agents minimize *variational free energy* — prediction error against a generative model of preferred outcomes. Policy selection is by **expected free energy**, which decomposes into desirability (how good the predicted outcome) and epistemic value (how much the policy reduces uncertainty about the outcome). Hope and despair, in this frame, are the affective registers of the policy's expected-utility profile. Friston PRS-04 (organism as active modeler) and the policy-precision literature (Schwartenbeck, Friston, Pezzulo, 2015) give the *exact* structure of Q.40 a.3: the dog moves toward the close hare and not the far one *because* the policy precision is high in the first case and low in the second. Aquinas's behavioral diagnostic — does the dog act? — is what the active-inference modeler computes.

**Fredrickson's broaden-and-build** (PRS-01) supplies the empirical biology of hope's pleasurable, action-supporting feature (Q.40 a.8). Hope is not just a model parameter; it is an affective state with vagal-tone, dopaminergic, and prefrontal correlates. Positive affect *broadens* the action repertoire — the hopeful agent's policy space is wider, exploration higher, novel actions more accessible. This is Aquinas's a.8 claim ("hope is a help to action") with mechanism. Fredrickson PRS-07/08/09 (high-quality connection, listening, weak-tie social trust) further specifies Q.40 a.7 — hope can cause love because the agent who makes our goal accessible becomes a target of affective orientation; this is the empirical fact about prosocial response to those perceived as resources.

**McGilchrist's hemispheric account** sharpens both hope and fear. The right hemisphere is the seat of *openness to possibility* — the contextual, gestalt-perceiving mode in which the arduous good registers as inviting. The left is the seat of *narrowed instrumental focus* — useful for executing a known policy but pathologically narrowing when sustained. Despair is, in McGilchrist's frame, the affective signature of left-hemispheric dominance over a closed model: the goal is computed as unreachable because the closed model excludes the routes the right hemisphere could see. Anxiety (Q.41 a.4's *anxiety* = fear at the magnitude of unknown possible misfortunes) is the contrary case: left-hemispheric attempt to enumerate possibilities the agent does not have the data to bound. The synthesis can then read Q.41 a.4's six species as a partition of left/right contributions to fear.

**Hawkins's HTM and reference frames** (Hawkins PRS-03 / *A Thousand Brains*) supply the cortical correlate of "experience-shapes-hope" (Q.40 a.5). The cortical model stores predictions over reference frames; what we call "experience" is the accumulated empirical content of those frames. Aquinas's basketball-team-experienced-in-winning-vs-experienced-in-losing is *literally* the Bayesian update of the prior — same machinery, different evidence. Hawkins's framework is where the *content* of the prior comes from.

**Wright on Christian hope** anchors the theological reframe. Wright PRS-03 (inaugurated eschatology) and his *Surprised by Hope* both insist that biblical hope is not generic optimism but a structured anticipation under resurrection-shaped evidence. The arduous future good of Aquinas's appetitive register *is*, in Wright's framework, the new-creation horizon already begun in the resurrection; the precision on the policy comes from being-in-Christ as participation rather than from extrapolation of present trends. Crucially this means biblical hope is not "high prior on attainability under inexperience of obstacles" (Q.40 a.6's youth-and-drunkards diagnosis); it is calibrated precision under known evidence. Christian hope is the *mature*, experience-informed irascible passion, not the *naive* one.

**Rohr on contemplative hope** (PRS-01, PRS-03) frames the same horizon non-dually. The universal-Christ pattern is present in matter from creation; hope is the contemplative orientation that perceives this present presence, even under conditions that, to the narrowed instrumental view, look hopeless. Rohr's *Falling Upward* explicitly addresses despair as the threshold of the second-half-of-life integration — the agent whose first-half competence has failed can either harden into permanent despair or pass through into the contemplative reframe in which the failure was the condition for the integration. The two-aspect structure of Q.40 a.4 (same object, contrary aspects) maps onto the order-disorder-reorder pattern Rohr names (PRS-02).

**Stump as Thomistic interlocutor** belongs here on three notes. (1) Stump PRS-09 on faith-as-knowledge-of-persons grounds the structural similarity between Christian hope and second-person trust: hope-in-someone has the form of trust under the difficulty of the future, not generic prediction. (2) Stump PRS-04 on final causality and form keeps the contemporary frame in coherent dialogue with the Aquinas-Aristotle structure. (3) Stump on *Wandering in Darkness* gives the rigorous Thomistic articulation of how despair can be entered without being terminal — suffering integrable into second-person love. Stump has not yet made the monist move; we keep her as Thomistic interlocutor receiving the active-inference / mind-monism reframe.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

**(a) Shared with Aquinas.** Hope and despair share an object — the arduous future good — and differ in aspect. Fear is the parallel passion keyed to future evil under non-resistibility. Hope is a help to action; despair is its contrary. Experience shapes both. All of this is preserved.

**(b) Where it reframes.** Where Aquinas's framework treats the irascible passions as faculties of the sensitive appetite tied to apprehension of arduous goods/evils, the contemporary frame treats them as the affective signatures of policy-precision parameters in an active-inference generative model. The reframe is not a contradiction: Aquinas's "appetite" *is* the agent's goal-oriented machinery; "apprehension" *is* the generative model's perception module; the difference is that the contemporary frame can compute. The two-aspect structure of Q.40 a.4 (hope/despair on the same object) gets an exact analogue: same goal-state, contrary precision on the policy. The metaphysical reframe under conscious-realist monism: the agent's perspective-limitation *is* the precision-bound on its policy; hope and despair are the agent's affective access to its own bounds.

**(c) Where evidence is firm vs. speculative.** *Firm*: active inference as a formal account of policy selection (Friston, Schwartenbeck, Parr, Pezzulo). *Firm*: broaden-and-build effects of positive affect on action-repertoire (Fredrickson). *Firm*: clinical-anxiety literature mapping Q.41 a.4's "anxiety = magnitude of unknown possible misfortunes" onto intolerance-of-uncertainty as the diagnostic core (Carleton, Norton). *Empirically supported*: hemispheric account of openness-vs-narrowing (McGilchrist, supported by lesion and clinical data). *Speculative metaphysical*: the conscious-realist-monist reframe of "appetite" as policy-machinery in a mind-at-large. *Theological, well-attested*: Wright's inaugurated-eschatology shape of Christian hope; Rohr's contemplative integration of despair.

## The central-theme thread

Conscious agents are individuated by the limitations of their perspective. Loving unity is the computational task. The agent works it out in fear and trembling, aware of its limitations and its need for guidance from those who perceive more of the whole.

Q.40-41 are the entry to the irascible passions — the passions that engage *difficulty*. The shape of the central theme is on full display: the agent's perspective is limited, the goal is arduous, and the affective machinery (hope, despair, fear) is what it feels like *to be* a limited agent computing toward the goal under uncertainty about whether the goal is reachable. Aquinas's a.6 (youth and drunkards) makes the point sharply: hope without calibrated priors is structurally unreliable — high precision on a poorly-grounded model. The mature hope is calibrated by experience (a.5), grounded in evidence, and oriented toward the actually-arduous good. This is precisely the "fear and trembling" of Philippians 2:12 in Wright's reading: not the absence of hope but the disciplined deployment of it under known limitation.

The despair-contrary (a.4) carries the central theme's negative pole forward from Day 80. The desensitized agent (Q.39 a.4, "greatest evil is to be too evil to sorrow") closes off the route to integration; the despairing agent has not closed the route — she has lost precision on the policy that leads home. Hope and despair are still *on* the path, just at contrary moments of its uncertainty. The remedies are different from those of sorrow: where Q.38 named tears, friends, contemplation, sleep, baths, the remedy of despair is *evidence about feasibility* — the introduction of new information or new community (a.7's interpersonal hope-causes-love) that updates the precision toward attainability. Wright's resurrection-evidence and Rohr's contemplative reframe are precisely such inputs, structurally.

Q.41's fear inaugurates the next sub-thread: what does it feel like to compute under the *contrary* sign — future evil + non-resistibility? That is tomorrow's question (Q.42-43 on the object and causes of fear). The two-day pair Q.40-43 sets the symmetry: every irascible passion is keyed to a future state and a feasibility/resistibility judgment. The central theme then frames the entire arc: each passion is one of the agent's affective access points to its own perspective-limited computation toward loving unity.

## Bridges

- **Friston PRS-01, PRS-04, PRS-06 / active inference and policy precision** — formal mechanism for Q.40 a.3 (dog-and-hare behavioral signature), Q.40 a.5 (experience as Bayesian update), Q.41 a.4 (anxiety as intolerance of uncertainty); *mathematical, empirically supported across animal and human studies*.
- **Fredrickson PRS-01, PRS-07, PRS-08, PRS-09 / broaden-and-build, positivity resonance, weak-tie connection** — Q.40 a.7 (hope can cause love), Q.40 a.8 (hope as help to action); *empirical, high confidence*.
- **McGilchrist / hemispheric account of openness and narrowing** — Q.40 a.4 (despair as left-hemispheric closure), Q.41 a.4 (anxiety species); *clinical and lesion evidence*.
- **Hawkins PRS-03 / HTM reference frames** — the cortical correlate of experience-shapes-hope (Q.40 a.5); *neuroscientifically grounded, mechanistically detailed*.
- **Wright PRS-03 / inaugurated eschatology + *Surprised by Hope*** — Christian hope as calibrated-precision under resurrection-shaped evidence, not generic optimism; *historical-theological, well-attested*.
- **Rohr PRS-01, PRS-02 / contemplative epistemology, order-disorder-reorder** — despair as threshold to the second-half-of-life integration; *contemplative tradition, internally coherent, externally suggestive*.
- **Stump PRS-04, PRS-09 / Thomistic final causality + faith-as-knowledge-of-persons** — Thomistic articulation of hope as trust under future difficulty; *philosophically rigorous, not yet monist*.

## Where this leaves us

Q.40-41 set the formal structure of the irascible passions and give us hope, despair, and (Day 81's introduction of) fear. Tomorrow's Day 82 (Q.42-43) deepens fear — its object and causes; Day 83 (Q.44-45) takes effects of fear and the contrary passion of *daring*; Day 84 (Q.46-47) opens anger; Day 85 (Q.48-49) closes anger and begins habits. Across the five days we will see the irascible-passion structure traced fully out, and then the transition from *passions* (transient affective states) to *habits* (stabilized dispositions). The central theme will sharpen: passions are how a limited agent feels its own computation; habits are how it shapes that computation by long practice. The "working out salvation in fear and trembling" of Philippians 2:12 lives precisely in this stretch — the disciplined cultivation, under known limitation, of policies that orient toward loving unity.
