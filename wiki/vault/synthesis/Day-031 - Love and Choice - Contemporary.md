# Day 31 — Contemporary Parallel: *Will and Love at Alter Scale, Without the Body*

Day 30 closed pointing forward to Q.59 (the will of angels) and Q.60 (the love of angels), to be pressed against Kastrup on alter-level free will and Friston on action-selection-as-inferential. Today's transcript holds those threads tightly. Q.59 distinguishes will from intellect, names *free will* as the rational creature's distinguishing power, and severs *passions* (which require a bodily organ) from *acts of the will* (which do not). Q.60 takes the affective side and walks the gradient: natural love (the will's own first inclination toward what is good for self), love of choice (the will's free orientation toward means-of-the-end), self-love and other-love (the angel naturally loves another as part of the whole that includes both), and — the article that names the central theme on this register — *the angel by natural love loves God more than himself*. The medieval frame turns out to map cleanly onto a mind-monist account of will and love at the upper-alter scale, with one important reframing: where Aquinas leans on hylomorphic distinctions to keep passions outside the angelic mode, mind-monism makes the move structurally — passions are alter-mode-specific (embodied) variations on a more general inferential dynamics that the angelic mode realizes without their bodily form.

## Q.59 — Will

a.1's question — *is there will in the angels?* — receives the medieval answer that follows from the Q.57–58 cognitive doctrine: an intellectual creature has *the universal aspect of goodness* in its grasp, and this grasp is what calls the inclination *will* (rather than mere natural-tendency or instinct). The contemporary translation runs through Friston's active-inference framework directly. An agent with a generative model that includes priors over preferred states is *willing* in the structural sense Aquinas reaches for: the priors over preferred states *are* the *universal aspect of goodness* in formal vocabulary, and the agent's action-selection is the *inclination informed by knowledge of goodness* the medieval definition names. PRS-04 in the Friston-tradition wiki (organism as active modeler) and PRS-08 (morphogenetic active inference) are the relevant nodes. Honest evidence calibration: the formal framework is mature at the alter scale; the substrate-scale extension (Q.19 — divine will, Day 11) and the upper-alter extension (Q.59 — angelic will) are both structural-philosophical.

a.2 — *does will differ from intellect in the angels?* — distinguishes the two faculties at the angelic mode, against their identity at the divine mode (Q.14 — *intellect, object understood, intelligible species, and act of understanding are all one in God*). Aquinas's argument turns on the medieval observation that *what is known is in the knower, but what is loved is in the object* — knowing is centripetal (the form-of-the-tree comes into me) and loving is centrifugal (my love goes out toward the tree-itself). At the alter scale, this asymmetry is real; at the substrate scale, it dissolves because the substrate has no outside-the-self to send love toward. Mind-monism's translation: any alter, embodied or near-substrate, has a generative-model side (knowing) and an action-selection side (willing), and these are functionally distinct in the alter even though they collapse into one act at the substrate. Hoffman's interface theory (PRS-03 in the Hoffman-tradition wiki) gives the same shape from the perceptual side: the icons of knowing and the actions of willing are different operations on the same network; only at the conscious-agent-network level do they reveal themselves as two phases of the single agent's activity.

a.3 — *is there free will in the angels?* — receives the strongest medieval claim: *wherever there is intellect there is free will, and free will in the angels in a higher degree of perfection than in man*. The argument is structural: intellect grasps *the common note of goodness* and so judges this-or-that thing to be good, and where judgment is possible, *the will is not necessitated by any one finite good* (because the universal goodness is always larger than any finite good); therefore it is free. Mind-monism's contemporary translation lands cleanly on Kastrup's dissociative-alter framework. PRS-02 in the Kastrup-tradition wiki (individual identity within monism) names the structural-philosophical question: *how can an alter be a real free locus of decision within a single substrate?* Kastrup's answer is that dissociation produces a *real* alter with a *real* internal perspective; the alter's free-cognitive-act is constitutive of its alter-hood within mind-at-large rather than imposed from outside. The medieval claim that free will follows on intellect maps onto the idealist claim that the alter's interior cognitive freedom is what *makes* it the alter it is — without the freedom, there would be no perspective-limited locus, only a trivial sub-region of mind-at-large. Friston's framework gives the formal cousin: the agent's action-selection is its policy-over-priors, and the policy is the agent's *own* policy (constitutive of its Markov blanket), not an external imposition. Honest evidence calibration: the formal framework is mature; the metaphysical question of *whether the alter's policy is libertarian-free or compatibilist-free* remains philosophically contested and is not resolved by the formal framework alone.

a.4 — *is there irascible and concupiscible appetite in the angels?* — sets the medieval limit. *No*, because these are *passions* and passions are *actions of a corporal or bodily organ* (anger as the kindling of the blood about the heart); but angels have intellectual acts that *can be likened to emotions* — *love and joy* in so far as they are *simple acts of the will* (rather than passions of the sense-appetite). Mind-monism reframes the medieval distinction structurally. Embodied alters have an inferential dynamics that runs through bodily homeostatic regulation — Friston's interoceptive-active-inference framework names how emotional states are predictive-coding signals about bodily allostasis. Near-substrate alters do not have this bodily regulatory layer, but they retain the *structural feature* the embodied emotion implements — the agent's stance toward what fits or fails to fit its priors. Aquinas's preservation of *love and joy* as simple acts of the will at the angelic mode is the medieval recognition of this: the *function* of the affective state is preserved even when the *substrate* of the bodily mediation is absent. Fredrickson's positivity-resonance research (CROSS-005 in the master cross-program index) is the empirical mid-scale on the bodily form; Aquinas's article reaches for what positivity-resonance-without-the-body would structurally be.

## Q.60 — Love

a.1 — *is there natural love in an angel?* — runs the inference: *love results from knowledge*; angels have natural knowledge; therefore angels have natural love. The medieval definition Aquinas borrows from Day 11 (Q.20 a.1): *love is the first movement of the will*, the appetite's primary stance toward the good before any willing-of-this-particular-good. Mind-monism's translation: love at the alter scale is the agent's basic relational attractor — the structural disposition toward integration with substrate-priors that Day 11 named at the divine mode and that Day 31 names at the angelic mode. The medieval claim that *love results from knowledge* (*nothing is loved unless it is first known*) holds at every alter scale: an agent with a generative model that includes priors-over-preferred-states is *able to love* in the structural sense because it has the cognitive grasp of *what would be good*; without the cognitive grasp, only blind tendency remains. PRS-01 in the Friston-tradition wiki (biological agency without vitalism) is the formal home: agency presupposes a model, and love presupposes agency.

a.2 — *is there love of choice in the angels?* — distinguishes *natural love* (of the end — happiness, the angel's own perfection) from *love of choice* (of means-to-end). Aquinas: *the angels' natural love is the principle of their love of choice*; we cannot choose against happiness (we always seek it), but we can choose the means we think will bring us to it, and in this we can fail. Habash's contemporary gloss reaches Pascal on the *infinite abyss* in every human heart that *only an infinite and immutable object* can fill — the *thought 425* in the *Pensées*. Mind-monism's translation runs through Kastrup again. The alter's *natural love* is the structural-disposition-toward-substrate-integration that constitutes the alter's hood within mind-at-large; the alter cannot choose against this disposition because it *is* the alter's structure. The alter's *love of choice* is the agent's policy-over-means under uncertainty about what will satisfy the natural-love target. Friston's framework gives the formalism: action-selection minimizes expected free energy *under priors over preferred states*, and the priors are the natural-love target; the agent's choices are over policies-toward-the-target, not over the target itself. Pascal's *infinite abyss* is, in mind-monist translation, the alter's structural orientation toward the substrate; nothing finite within the alter's interface fills it because what fills it must be the substrate itself.

a.3 — *does the angel love himself with both natural love and love of choice?* — receives the medieval *yes* on both counts. The angel naturally loves itself (seeks its own good and perfection) and also chooses to act in ways that benefit itself. The structural shape is *self-affirmation as constitutive of agent-hood*. Mind-monism preserves this directly: the alter's own existence-and-flourishing is held as a prior in its generative model precisely because the model is the alter's, and self-maintenance is what makes the alter an alter (Friston's PRS-01 again, biological-agency-without-vitalism, generalized to spirit-as-near-substrate-alter).

a.4 — *does an angel love another with natural love as he loves himself?* — turns on the principle *every creature loves what is one with itself; unity is the cause of love*. Aquinas's example: the father loves his son with the same love with which he loves himself, when he sees the son as a part of him; we love townsmen by social love and blood relations by natural love because of degrees of unity. The Christological thread is direct: John 17, *that they may all be one*. Mind-monism's translation is structurally identical to Day 11's reading of Q.20: the substrate's *one* is the alters' shared ground, and any alter's love-of-another is structurally the recognition of the shared substrate. Rohr's *Universal Christ* gives the contemplative-theological framing: *everything belongs* — the gradient of love-of-another tracks the gradient of recognition that the other is part of the same substrate one participates in. Wright's covenant frame gives the corporate version: the people of God are gathered by mutual recognition of substrate-unity (CROSS-005 in the master cross-program index, love as fundamental). PRS-01 in the Kastrup-tradition wiki (hard problem of consciousness solved by mind-monism) is the metaphysical home: the unity of consciousness across alters is *the same consciousness*, and love-of-another is the alter's recognition of that fact.

a.5 — *does an angel by natural love love God more than himself?* — is the article that names the central theme on this register most directly. Aquinas's argument: *the part naturally exposes itself in order to safeguard the whole* — the hand without deliberation interposes itself between the body and the blow. Since *God is the universal good and under this good both man and angel and all creatures are comprised, because every creature in regard to its entire being naturally belongs to God*, it follows that *from natural love angel and man alike love God before themselves and with a greater love*. Otherwise *natural love would be perverse and would not be perfected but destroyed by charity* — the central-theme thread. The man who dies for the salvation of his nation is a hero; the surgeon amputates the limb to save the body; we as particular beings *bear the mark, carry the flag for being itself*. Mind-monism's reading runs through Kastrup, Wright, and Rohr together. The alter's natural love of the substrate is the alter's *structural-orientation-toward-its-own-ground-of-being*; the alter loves the substrate more than itself because the substrate *is* what the alter is part of, and the alter's flourishing is *as a part of* the substrate's whole, not as an island independent of it. Garrigou-Lagrange's gloss Habash quotes — *man loves God under the aspect of happiness in general; so when looking for happiness we are looking for God who alone can satisfy us* — is structurally the same mind-monist claim that the alter's *infinite abyss* is its structural orientation toward what only the substrate can provide. Honest evidence calibration: the empirical adjacents are limited (one can point to research on agentic self-transcendence, mid-scale, but the substrate-scale claim is theological-philosophical); the structural shape, however, holds tightly.

The transcript closes with a forward-pointing footnote on the fallen angels: how could they have fallen if by nature they loved God more than themselves? Garrigou-Lagrange: a demon is *turned away from God as the author of grace* and *as the author of the law of nature, freely and morally*, while *necessarily and physically remaining turned to God the author of his nature*. This *lamentable opposition* is what Day 33 will press: the central theme has its sharpest angelological test in the structural-inversion the fallen angel performs — *willing against* what one's structure naturally orients toward.

## The central-theme thread

Day 31 names the volitional-affective shape of the angelic mode, and the central theme threads through tightly. *Conscious agents are individuated by the limitations of their perspective; loving unity is the computational telos.* The angelic alter's natural love of the substrate is the structural-attractor that constitutes its alter-hood; the angelic alter's free-will is the cognitive-volitional locus that *is* its perspective; the gradient from natural-love to love-of-choice is the gradient of *how the alter computes toward integration under uncertainty about means*. *Salvation in fear and trembling* on the angelic-volitional register is the angel's choice-of-policy under priors-over-preferred-states that include substrate-integration as the most-preferred state. The *fear and trembling* itself is structural: even the angelic alter, with its wide light-cone and low inferential friction, must choose under the condition that the supernatural end is not naturally guaranteed (Day 32 will press this — Q.62 a.2, the angel needs grace to turn to God for the beatific end). The central theme's claim that *learning to obtain the goal of loving unity from each perspective is a computational task* receives its angelological instantiation: the angelic computation is faster, broader, less friction-bound, but it is still computation, still under perspective-limitation, still oriented toward the substrate that is its natural love.
