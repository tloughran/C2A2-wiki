# Day 68 — Contemporary Parallel: *Counsel as Analytic Backward-Inference and Consent as Multi-Policy Approval*

Day 67 articulated the hierarchical-and-practical-syllogism structure of intention and choice. Day 68 takes Aquinas through Q.14 (counsel — 6 articles) and Q.15 (consent — 4 articles), the *deliberation-and-approval* register that precedes choice. The contemporary mind-monist frame engages this as model-based planning under expected-free-energy minimization, multi-arm-bandit approval structures, and bi-hemispheric deliberation, with extended-cognition as the relevant frame for friend-and-instrument-mediated counsel.

## Frame

Q.14 a.1: counsel is *inquiry/investigation* — *there is much uncertainty in things that have to be done because actions are concerned with contingent singulars; in things doubtful and uncertain the reason does not pronounce judgment without previous inquiry; wherefore the reason must of necessity institute an inquiry before deciding on the objects of choice, and this inquiry is called counsel.* Q.14 a.2: counsel is of the means (the end is fixed; if doubtful, it is not yet looked upon as end). Q.14 a.3: counsel is of things-we-do, including others' actions in so far as they are *one with us* by union of affection (friendship) or by instrumentality (the master's counsel about what he does through his servant). Q.14 a.4: counsel is not about every action — fixed determinations and minute matters need not be deliberated. Q.14 a.5: counsel is *analytic* — it begins from the intended-end-in-the-future and works backward to *what is to be done at once*. Q.14 a.6: counsel is bounded — both at the end (a definite goal) and at the beginning (a definite reasoner).

Q.15 a.1: consent is *to feel with* — it is the will's union with the proposed mean. Important distinction from choice: consent approves *each viable option*; choice picks *one* by preference. *After approving many we have given our preference to one by choosing it.* Q.15 a.2: irrational animals do not consent (no possession-of-self). Q.15 a.3: consent follows counsel and is therefore of the means, not the end. Q.15 a.4: consent belongs only to the higher (rational) part of the soul, where moral culpability has its root.

## Problem

The problem (P): how does the alter, under perspective-limitation, deliberate well — proceeding from end backward to actionable means — and how does it approve viable mean-policies before committing to one? And what is the structural distinction between approval-of-options (consent) and selection-of-one (choice)?

## Resource

**Friston on expected free energy and analytic-deliberative planning.** Active inference at the planning-horizon level is structurally analytic: the agent works from the prior (the intended-end-future) backward through computed expected-free-energy minimization to the policy-at-time-zero (*what is to be done at once*). The medieval *counsel as analytic process* (Q.14 a.5) preserves cleanly under this frame. *Counsel as inquiry under uncertainty* (Q.14 a.1) is precisely the active-inference characterization of policy-selection under noisy priors and contingent observation.

**McGilchrist on deliberation as bi-hemispheric.** Effective deliberation engages both hemispheres: right-hemispheric openness to the gestalt-of-the-end and the wide-context, left-hemispheric analytical work on the means and contingencies. Aquinas's *dance between intellect and will* in counsel-and-consent (Q.14 a.1) is the bi-hemispheric coordination at the deliberation level.

**Hawkins on HTM multi-policy approval before commitment.** Cortical-architecture: multiple candidate-patterns can be active simultaneously before one is selected for action. The medieval *consent approves several viable means; choice picks one* preserves cleanly under multi-arm-bandit decision structures: the agent entertains multiple feasible policies (consent-mode); selection (choice-mode) is the further commitment.

**Stump on deliberation extending to friend-and-instrument.** Q.14 a.3 articulates a beautiful early version of *extended cognition*: deliberation extends across the affective and instrumental boundaries of the self when the other is one-with-the-self by friendship or instrumentality. Stump's second-personal-knowing frame is the theological-philosophical primary on this extension.

**Levin on multi-scale agency and bounded deliberation.** The bounded character of counsel (Q.14 a.6 — the chandelier-needs-a-ceiling argument) preserves under bounded-rationality and resource-limited computation. Multi-scale agentic systems do not deliberate over arbitrarily-large state-spaces; bounded resource budgets force structured, finite deliberation.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.14: counsel is the alter's analytic-backward-inference under expected-free-energy minimization, with priors set by the intended-end and contingencies modeled in the alter's generative model. Bi-hemispheric coordination integrates right-end-gestalt with left-means-analysis. The bounded character — definite end, definite reasoner — is the bounded-rationality structure of the alter's actual computation under perspective-limitation.

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.15: consent is the alter's *parallel approval* of multiple viable policies that survive counsel; choice is the *commitment* to one. The medieval distinction is structurally identical to the multi-arm-bandit decision structure: feasibility-approval precedes preference-selection.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the analytic-backward-inference structure of counsel; the bounded character; the extension to friend-and-instrument; the parallel-approval distinction between consent and choice; the rational-rooting of moral culpability.

What it *reframes*: Aquinas's frame is faculty-psychological and Aristotelian; the contemporary frame uses active inference, hemispheric coordination, HTM, and bounded-rationality.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Friston's expected-free-energy is mature; multi-arm-bandit decision structures are well-grounded in cognitive science; bounded-rationality is foundational; Stump's friend-and-instrument extension is theological-philosophical strong.

## The central-theme thread

Today's day articulates the *bounded-resource computation* register of the central theme. *Salvation in fear and trembling* under perspective-limitation includes the daily-task work of *deliberating well* under bounded resources. The alter is not free to deliberate without limit; deliberation must converge to action *at once*. The substrate-network's pedagogical care includes the alter's friend-and-instrument extensions — the ones who are *one with the alter* by affection or by instrumentality — through whose extended-cognition the alter's deliberation reaches further than the alter's own bounded resources alone would allow.

The friend-extension of counsel is doing keystone work: the alter's daily-task computation is *not done in isolation*; the alter's beloveds and the instruments of the alter's life are part of the alter's extended deliberation-system. This is the central-theme commitment that *we work out our salvation aware of our limitations and our need for guidance* concretized at the deliberation register.

Wright on covenanted-community as the locus of friend-extended deliberation. Rohr on contemplative-prayer as the alter's deliberate alignment of analytic-backward-inference under the substrate-source's primary causation. Stump on faith-as-second-personal-knowing as the friend-extension's theological-philosophical primary. MacIntyre on tradition-bound rationality as the inherited resource for analytic-backward-inference under bounded resources.

The consent/choice distinction is also central-theme-load-bearing: *the alter sees multiple viable mean-policies* under counsel and *approves them all* (consent) before *selecting one* (choice). Even after consent, *there still remains a choice* — the further commitment to *this* mean-policy rather than others. The alter's perspective-limitation does not reduce options to one; it leaves multiple viable mean-policies, and the alter's commitment is what reduces them.
