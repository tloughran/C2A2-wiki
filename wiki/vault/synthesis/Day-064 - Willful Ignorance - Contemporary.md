# Day 64 — Contemporary Parallel: *Voluntariness, Willed Ignorance, and the Architecture of Human Acts*

Day 63 articulated the constitutively relational and grace-enabled structure of flourishing. Day 64 begins the *Summa*'s formal treatment of human action: Q.6 (the voluntary and the involuntary — 8 articles) and Q.7 (the circumstances of human acts — 4 articles). The contemporary mind-monist frame engages this as the structural-and-diagnostic apparatus the alter needs to *evaluate* its own action under perspective-limitation, including the load-bearing case of *willed ignorance* — when the alter's not-knowing is itself something the alter has chosen.

## Frame

Q.6 a.1 establishes that human acts are *voluntary* — they have a principle within the agent and they imply *knowledge* of the end. The alter knows what it is moving toward, and moves toward it from within. Aquinas immediately adds the central-theme-relevant qualifier (Q.6 a.1 ad 1): *God moves man to act because every movement either of the will or of nature proceeds from God as from the first mover; and just as it is not incompatible with nature that the natural movement be from God as the first mover, so it is not contrary to the essence of a voluntary act that it proceed from God in as much as the will is moved by God; nevertheless both natural and voluntary movements have this in common, that it is essential that they should proceed from a principle within the agent.*

Q.6 a.2: irrational animals have no voluntary acts because they have no rational appetite. Q.6 a.3-5: violence and fear and concupiscence and ignorance can affect voluntariness — sometimes excusing, sometimes not. Q.6 a.7-8: the *willed ignorance* case is the diagnostic moment: when the alter's ignorance is itself something the alter has chosen (avoidably, with culpable refusal-to-know), the resulting act remains voluntary and culpable.

Q.7 covers circumstances: who, what, where, by what aids, why, how, when. The medieval enumeration is structurally identical to the contemporary action-under-uncertainty frame — circumstances are the relevant features the alter must take into account in policy-selection.

## Problem

The problem (P): the alter's perspective-limitation includes limitations of *knowledge*, and the alter's act is evaluated under those limitations — but some not-knowings are themselves the alter's choice, and the structure of voluntariness must be carefully articulated to handle this.

## Resource

**Friston on action under uncertainty.** The active-inference frame is structurally well-suited: the alter selects policies under priors, and *epistemic value* (information-gain) is one component of expected-free-energy minimization. When an alter is *avoiding* information-gain on a particular topic — when expected free energy is dominated by *not-attending-to* a particular signal — that is the formal correlate of willed ignorance. The alter's policy includes *not-looking*, and that not-looking is itself an act.

**McGilchrist on left-hemispheric closure as willed-not-knowing.** The left hemisphere's closure-and-grasp mode resists information that would disrupt its current model. *Willed ignorance* is the left-hemispheric refusal to receive disruptive information; the alter has chosen the closure. The medieval *willed ignorance* preserves cleanly under McGilchrist's frame.

**Stump on willed ignorance and Wandering in Darkness.** The diagnostic structure is in Stump's biblical readings: the agent in willed evil cannot, by introspection alone, see its mis-orientation, but the agent's *not-seeing* is constitutively the agent's own choice in willed evil. *Willed ignorance is internally generated*, even when the alter experiences it as just-not-being-aware. This is the moment where Stump's second-person-knowing becomes critical: only an outside agent who loves the alter can present the alter with the diagnostic that surfaces the alter's own willed not-knowing.

**Kastrup on protective vs. predatory dissociation.** Some not-knowings are *protective* — the alter is not yet developmentally equipped to receive the substrate-content. These are not culpable. Some not-knowings are *predatory* — the alter has settled into closure that resists development. These are culpable. The distinction is constitutive: not all ignorance is equal.

**Fredrickson on broaden-and-build vs. narrow-and-defend.** The empirical contour: agents in narrow-and-defend mode actively avoid information that would broaden the repertoire. The choice to remain in narrow-and-defend mode beyond the situation that initially called for it is the empirical correlate of willed ignorance.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.6: voluntary action requires (1) a principle within the agent (the alter's own policy-selection), (2) knowledge of the end (the alter's terminal-prior is articulated for the alter), and (3) policy-selection that aligns the alter's prior with the chosen end. These conditions preserve cleanly under active inference.

Voluntariness can be diminished by (a) violence — external override of the alter's policy-machinery, (b) fear and concupiscence — disturbance of the alter's prior-structure by passion in ways that didn't engage the alter's deliberation, and (c) ignorance — when the alter's apprehension is genuinely incomplete.

But (c) is the sensitive case. Aquinas distinguishes *concomitant ignorance* (the alter wouldn't have acted differently even if it had known — non-excusing), *consequent ignorance* (the alter chose not to know — non-excusing, this is willed ignorance), and *antecedent ignorance* (the alter could not have known — excusing). The contemporary frame: only the *antecedent* form is exculpatory; the *consequent* form is the alter's own act of closure.

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.7: the circumstances are the relevant features of policy-selection-context. Aquinas's medieval enumeration (who, what, where, by what aids, why, how, when) is structurally identical to the contemporary policy-selection frame — these are the variables the active-inference agent integrates over.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the structure of voluntariness, the willed-vs-antecedent ignorance distinction, the role of circumstances in moral evaluation.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas's frame is faculty-psychological, the contemporary frame uses active inference, hemispheric attention, second-personal knowing, and broaden-and-build.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Friston's action-under-uncertainty is mature; McGilchrist's hemispheric account is strong; Stump's willed-ignorance diagnostic is theological-philosophical strong; Fredrickson's narrow-and-defend is empirically robust.

## The central-theme thread

Today's day articulates the diagnostic side of the central theme. *Salvation in fear and trembling* under perspective-limitation includes the daily-task work of *catching one's own willed ignorance*. The alter cannot, on its own, be sure when its not-knowing is willed and when it is genuine; the substrate-network's pedagogical care presents the alter with diagnostic instances, and the alter's daily task is to receive those instances rather than close against them.

The closure-against-the-diagnostic is itself the alter's own act, and it is the act that compounds the perspective-limitation rather than working with it. The integrity-of-becoming is the alter's openness to the diagnostic-presentation — the willingness to discover that what the alter took to be antecedent ignorance was actually consequent ignorance.

Wright's covenanted-community frame: the community is the relational locus where second-person diagnostic surfacing happens. Rohr's *Falling Upward*: the *fall* into the second half of life often comes through forced surfacing of willed-ignorance the alter had relied on. Stump on Job, Samson, the woman of Bethany: the second-person-loving-knowing is the structural mode by which willed-ignorance becomes seeable.
