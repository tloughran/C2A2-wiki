# Day 76 — Timeless Delight (Contemporary)

## Frame

Aquinas, in Q.30 and Q.31, is asking: what *is* desire (concupiscence) — a passion that draws the appetite toward an absent good — and what *is* delight — the rest of the appetite once the good is possessed? His pivotal move is in Q.31 a.2: delight is "in time" only accidentally; *delight in an altogether unchangeable good is not in time at all*. That single sentence anchors his eventual claim that beatitude — union with God — is not a long satisfaction but a *timeless* one.

The contemporary version of the same question, under mind/conscious-realist monism: if spacetime is a species-specific interface (Hoffman), and mind/consciousness is ontologically prior to that interface (Kastrup), then *temporal* delight is a fact about *limited* perspective, not about the good itself. The question becomes: what is the relation between a *limited agent's* delight and the unchanging field of mind-at-large in which all agents are individuated?

## Problem

The shared problem is *the metaphysics of appetite under finitude*. Aquinas's appetite is a faculty of a soul-body composite that takes-in goods piecemeal: present good causes rest, absent good causes concupiscence, future good causes hope. The phenomenology is unmistakable: we are *moved* toward what we lack. But Aquinas needs a metaphysical anchor for the *cessation* of this movement — somewhere the appetite finally and unchangeably rests — or the entire economy of moral and theological striving has no terminus.

Under contemporary monism the same problem returns in a new register. If conscious agents are individuated by the limitations of their perspective, then *appetite itself* is a diagnostic of perspectival incompleteness. An agent who lacks nothing — who perceives all of mind-at-large — would have no appetite, and equivalently no time-bound delight. The problem is to specify, in non-medieval terms, what "lacking nothing" actually means and whether finite agents can approximate it.

## Resource

Four moves from the C2A2 set carry the load.

**Kastrup's analytic idealism** (Kastrup wiki) holds that mind is the ontological primitive; matter is what experience looks like from the outside; localized minds (alters) are dissociative pockets within mind-at-large. On this view, the "unchangeable good" of Q.31 a.2 is not an external object but the *non-dissociative ground itself*. An alter's delight in that ground is not a state of an object-relation but a recovery of pre-dissociative coherence — which is, by hypothesis, not temporally indexed.

**Hoffman's interface theory** (Hoffman wiki) holds that spacetime is a species-specific user interface, not the underlying reality. Conscious agents perceive icons (sensations) optimized by natural selection for fitness payoffs, not for truth-tracking. This re-reads Q.31 a.6 (touch/sight as natural baselines of pleasure) with surprising sympathy: Aquinas already saw the senses as tuned for utility — Hoffman generalizes the claim to all of phenomenal experience. The implication: time-bound delights are interface states, and "timeless" delight is what one would have in contact with the substrate the interface conceals.

**Friston's active inference** (Friston wiki; FEP + Markov blanket formalism) frames the appetite mathematically: an agent persists by minimizing expected free energy, which decomposes into *pragmatic value* (closing the gap between predicted and preferred states) and *epistemic value* (resolving uncertainty about the generative model). Aquinas's concupiscence-as-movement-toward and delight-as-rest map directly onto policy selection and expected-free-energy minimization. Crucially, the resting state is not a metaphysical ground but a *prediction-error null* — a state in which model and world coincide.

**Fredrickson's broaden-and-build** (Fredrickson wiki; PRS-01, PRS-02, PRS-04, PRS-11) supplies the empirical grain. Positivity resonance — vagally mediated, oxytocin-correlated, neurally synchronized — is the bodily signature of resting in shared good. PRS-04 reframes this as a community biomarker; PRS-11 (West, Jolink, Feldman et al., 2025) documents how inflammation gates the *radius* of available resonance. Aquinas's claim in Q.31 a.5 — that fallen humans turn to bodily pleasure because it is more available and remedies sorrow — receives a startlingly precise mechanistic reading: under inflammatory load, the radius of available higher-order delight contracts, and the lower-order delights become both more accessible and more compulsively sought.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

**(a) Shared with Aquinas.** The contemporary synthesis preserves Aquinas's structural insight that delight is *rest of the appetite in an apprehended good* (Q.31 a.4) — the dynamic of motion-toward and rest-in is intact. It also preserves his hierarchy: intellectual delight is structurally more complete than sensual delight, because the union is more total and the apprehension less piecemeal (Q.31 a.5).

**(b) Where it reframes.** Aquinas's "unchangeable good" is, in his framework, an object *external* to the soul — God as final end, attained in the beatific vision. Under conscious-realist monism the unchangeable is the *substrate of conscious experience itself* — mind-at-large for Kastrup, the unobserved-by-interface reality for Hoffman. This is not a small reframe. Aquinas's ontology distinguishes the soul from God *really* (creature from Creator); on the monist reading, the deepest individuation is dissociative, not real, and the "unchangeable good" is what the alter *already is* beneath its perspective-limitation. **The synthesis here differs from Aquinas precisely in not making God a numerically distinct entity to whom the soul is united, but in making "timeless delight" the recovery of the substrate that *is* the soul's own non-perspective-limited identity.** Wright and Rohr each give versions of this same correction within Christian orthodoxy: Wright's Pauline *en Christō* (being-in-Christ) and Rohr's universal Christ — the cosmic divine pattern in which all creation participates — are theological frames that *do not* preserve the medieval external-object reading.

**(c) Where evidence is firm vs. speculative.** The Friston/Fredrickson layer is empirically substantial: appetite as expected-free-energy minimization is a working mathematical framework with growing empirical purchase; positivity resonance's biomarkers are measurable. The Kastrup/Hoffman layer is speculative metaphysics with strong internal coherence but contested empirical implications — Hoffman's evolutionary-game proof that selection does not favor truth-tracking is formal but disputed; Kastrup's idealism is a philosophical position, not a testable theory. The *theological* claim that timeless delight is recovery of substrate is a synthesis-move, not a finding.

## The central-theme thread

Conscious agents are individuated by the limitations of their perspective. Q.31 a.2's "timeless delight in the unchangeable good" is, in our synthesis, what would be available if a perspective-limited agent encountered the unchangeable substrate that is its own ground. But — and this is the central-theme work — *the agent cannot get there by abolishing its perspective*. The perspective is the agent. What Wright calls "salvation in fear and trembling" (Phil 2:12) names the *computational* character of the task: the agent must learn, from its own resources, to *recognize* the substrate that is already there, and to *love unite-ly* even from its limited vantage. Q.31 a.5's observation — that the broken majority turn to sensual goods because spiritual ones are not yet attainable — is the same insight: the computational task takes time, evidence, formation, and community. The "remedies of sorrow" we will encounter on Day 80 are exactly the resources by which the limited agent computes its way toward less-limited apprehension.

## Bridges

- **Kastrup, analytic idealism** (Kastrup wiki) — "unchangeable good" reads as the non-dissociative ground; *speculative metaphysical*, internally coherent.
- **Hoffman, interface theory** (Hoffman wiki) — Q.31 a.6's touch/sight ordering is fitness-tuned channel ordering; "timeless" = substrate-contact not mediated by interface; *formally established, empirically contested*.
- **Friston, active inference + FEP** (Friston wiki, PRS-04, PRS-06; CROSS-006, CROSS-007) — appetite as policy under expected-free-energy minimization; delight as prediction-null state; *empirically tractable, mathematically robust*.
- **Fredrickson, positivity resonance + PRS-11** — bodily delight has measurable substrate; chronic inflammation contracts the radius of available delight, mechanistically explaining Q.31 a.5's "majority turn to bodily pleasure"; *empirical, high confidence*.
- **Rohr, universal Christ** — cosmic-Christ frame as theological articulation of substrate-recovery rather than external-object union; *theological*.
- **CROSS-005** (master wiki) — love as a fundamental ontological category in Fredrickson, Stump, and Kastrup; positions delight-in-the-unchangeable as the *theologically and biologically congruent* terminus of the appetite.

## Where this leaves us

Tomorrow — Day 77, *Delightful Sorrow* (Q.32–33) — pushes on the same axis from the opposite side: how can sorrow be a cause of pleasure? The contemporary monist answer is already audible: under perspective-limitation, no privation is pure non-being for the apprehending agent, because the agent's generative model includes the lost good as a counterfactual against which the present is read. The open thread we carry into Day 77 is the relation between *inflammation gating* of positivity resonance (PRS-11) and the felt phenomenology of grief — sorrow that *contains* delight is a marker of an intact computational route back toward the substrate.
