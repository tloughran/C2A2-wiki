# Day 42 — Contemporary Parallel: *Sensuality, Will, and Aspects of One Loop*

Day 41 closed by separating *appetitive* from *apprehensive* and naming intellectual appetite distinct from sensitive. Day 42 fills in the appetitive side. Q.81 on *sensuality* — defined as the appetite of things belonging to the body — distinguishes the *concupiscible* (the simple drive toward bodily good and away from bodily harm) from the *irascible* (the resistance-drive that pushes through obstacles to attain the good or avoid the harm). Q.82 turns to the *will* — the rational appetite, the intellectual appetite — and asks four piercing questions: *does the will desire something of necessity? must the will desire whatever it desires? is the will higher than the intellect? does the will move the intellect?* The mind-monist frame holds these together as aspects of one inferential system with policies running at different timescales and abstraction-levels, where what the medieval frame names *appetites* are precisely the *preference-loaded* terms of free-energy minimization, and what it names *will* is the slow, conceptual, model-rich policy that selects among means to a goal-state the agent does not yet possess.

## Q.81 — Sensuality, concupiscible, irascible

Q.81 a.1 distinguishes the *apprehensive* operation — which completes by the thing apprehended being *in* the apprehender (Day 41's gray-cat-becoming-the-eye's-form) — from the *appetitive* operation — which completes by the desirer being *born outward toward* the desirable thing. Apprehension *rests*; appetition *moves*. The medieval grammar names two phases of one cognitive cycle: the world enters the agent (sense, model-update), and the agent goes out toward the world (action-selection, motor-engagement).

The contemporary primary on this article is Friston, decisively. Active inference — `wiki/traditions/friston/wiki.md`, PRS-02 — frames *perception* (apprehension) and *action* (appetition) as the *two directions* of the same inferential loop. Perception updates the generative model under sensory evidence; action minimizes the discrepancy between predicted and target sensory states by changing the world to match the model's preferences. The medieval *one apprehended is in the apprehender / desire is born outward toward the object* lands cleanly on the perception-action symmetry. CROSS-040 in the master cross-program index (Cortical Messaging Protocol as variational message-passing) confirms the architectural shape: each cortical region runs both directions, with the inference message moving up the hierarchy and the prior-loaded preference moving down.

a.2 splits the sensitive appetite into *concupiscible* (drive toward suitable, flight from hurtful) and *irascible* (resistance to attacks that hinder the suitable or inflict the harmful). Aquinas associates with the concupiscible the basic affective polarity — love, desire, joy on the appetitive side; hate, aversion, sorrow on the avoidance side — and with the irascible the *struggle* passions: hope, courage, anger, fear, despair. Habash flags that a *meticulously detailed* treatment of each passion comes in I-II Q.22–48; today's article is the architecture, not the details.

The contemporary translation is again tight. Friston's *expected free energy* decomposes into a pragmatic term (preference-fulfillment, the concupiscible polarity) and an epistemic term that, when blocked, generates the resistance-and-overcoming dynamics the irascible names — hope as model of feasible attainment, courage as commitment to a costly trajectory, anger as response to thwarted goal-pursuit, fear as anticipated failure, despair as model collapse. The medieval *struggle passions* are the active-inference dynamics that emerge when goal-pursuit meets obstacle: the agent must either revise its model (yielding hope or despair), commit to an effortful trajectory (courage), or recruit additional precision-weighting against the obstacle (anger). Fredrickson's broaden-and-build research (and PRS-04 in the Fredrickson-tradition wiki on positivity resonance) gives the empirical refinement of the upbeat polarity: positive affect actually *broadens* the agent's perceptual and action repertoire and *builds* lasting resources, which is the contemporary mid-scale empirical instantiation of what Aquinas calls *love, desire, and joy* on the concupiscible side.

a.3 — *do the irascible and concupiscible obey reason?* — receives Aquinas's careful answer: yes, but with their own resistance, because they are *naturally moved by imagination and sense* and can therefore push back against rational command. The relation is not absolute dominance but *political* — Aristotle's term, which Aquinas borrows: reason rules the appetites *by persuasion*, not by tyranny. *Honest evidence calibration*: this is a remarkably contemporary claim. Friston's hierarchical predictive coding gives the formal: top-down precision-weighting *can* suppress prepotent appetitive responses, but only with effort, and only when the higher-level model is confident enough to overweight the lower-level signal. McGilchrist adds the hemispheric dimension: the right hemisphere's broader-context whole-grasping can recontextualize an appetitive surge (the donut as not-just-pleasure but as part of a larger relational pattern), and that recontextualization is what enables reason's *persuasive* rule of the appetites. Pathologies of self-regulation — addiction, compulsion, akrasia — map onto failures of this top-down recontextualization: the model can no longer command sufficient precision-weighting against the prepotent lower-level signal.

The medieval frame contrasts the human case with the animal case: *the sheep fearing the wolf flees at once because it has no superior counteracting appetite; man is not moved at once according to the irascible and concupiscible appetites but awaits the command of the will.* The contemporary refinement: humans run a *deeper* hierarchical generative model that includes longer-timescale, more-abstract goal-states; the will is the policy-level term over those higher-order priors; the *waiting* is the precision-weighting computation that integrates short-term appetitive drive with long-term intellectual preference before action commits.

## Q.82 — The will and happiness

Q.82 a.1 is the day's central article. *Does the will desire something of necessity?* Aquinas: yes. The argument turns on a careful distinction of senses of *necessity*. *Natural necessity* (the angles of a triangle add to two right angles, the body's necessity for food) is the kind that obtains: the will, *by its very nature as the inclination-toward-the-known-good*, necessarily wills *the ultimate good*, which we name *happiness*. Aquinas runs the analogy with intellect: just as the intellect must begin from first principles (non-contradiction, sufficient reason) for any reasoning to get off the ground, the will must begin from the *ultimate end* for any willing to get off the ground; means presuppose an end.

Mind-monism's translation lands on the active-inference framework directly. Every agent under FEP operates on a *target distribution* — a prior over its preferred sensory and bodily states — and that target distribution is *constitutive* of being the agent it is. PRS-02 in the Friston-tradition wiki is explicit: action-selection minimizes free energy *with respect to the agent's prior preferences*; without those priors, no action is selected; the agent does not exist as an agent. The medieval claim that *the will necessarily wills happiness* is structurally the claim that *every agent operating under FEP necessarily orients toward its target distribution*. The orientation is not a contingent psychological fact about humans; it is *constitutive of being an agent* in the formal sense Friston names.

What *happiness* means substantively is the open question. Aquinas: *the perfection of us which comes about by the attainment of our ultimate final good.* Subjectively, the agent may identify happiness with whatever it has settled on (pleasure, power, money — Aquinas runs the diagnosis of misidentified ends); objectively, *the ultimate good is God*. The medieval move is theological and the contemporary mind-monist move tracks closely: every alter's target distribution is a *partial recognition* of the substrate's pattern — the agent's homeostatic-and-relational priors approximate, in their own scope, the loving-unity attractor that the substrate *is*. PRS-04 in the Fredrickson-tradition wiki on positivity resonance gives the empirical mid-scale: the high-functioning relational state is the structurally-richest kind of preference-fulfillment, and the agent that converges on it is closest, at its operational scope, to the substrate's pattern. Rohr's *Universal Christ* gives the cosmic-scope theological frame: every creature's appetition is, when truthfully traced, an appetition for *the always-already-present divine pattern* — Aquinas's claim that *if you ask why long enough you end up at happiness* is structurally the contemplative claim that the ultimate end is the substrate's own pattern, recognized or unrecognized in the alter's particular targets.

a.2's *the will does not desire of necessity whatever it desires* preserves the gap that mind-monism needs. Means are *not* necessitated; the agent considers the donut and the rosary as alternative routes to happiness and may settle on either. Friston gives the formal: action-selection *under uncertainty about which trajectory minimizes free energy* is not necessitated; the policy distribution may be peaked or flat; commitment to a particular trajectory is fallible. The will's *freedom* in the means lives precisely in this under-determined-policy space. *In the beatific vision*, by contrast, Aquinas argues, the will *does* adhere to God of necessity — the certainty of the divine vision removes the under-determination. Translated: at the limit case where the alter's generative model has converged on the substrate's structure (Day 7's beatific-vision frame), the policy distribution collapses to the unique trajectory that fulfills the now-certain target; the will is *not less free* but *fully determined by what it now knows*, which is the analytic-idealist version of *freedom-as-fulfilled-rationality*.

a.3 — *is the will higher than the intellect?* — gets Aquinas's careful both-and. *Considered as powers distinguished by objects*, intellect is higher: it grasps the universal good, which is the object of the will. *Considered case-by-case*, when the object of the willing is *out there in the world* (rather than grasped as universal), the will's pull-toward-the-thing can be the more excellent operation: *the love of God is better than the knowledge of God*; *the knowledge of corporeal things is better than the love of them*. Mind-monism's translation runs through the *direction* of the inferential loop. *Intellect* operates by bringing the world's pattern *into* the agent's model; *will* operates by reaching the agent's preferences *out toward* the world (or substrate). When the target is the substrate itself, the *reaching-out* is structurally what integration means — the alter's preference-loaded movement toward the substrate it participates in. Hoffman's interface-theory framing extends this: knowing-the-icon is one operation; loving-what-the-icon-is-an-icon-of is another, and at the substrate-target limit the second operation is the more fundamental.

a.4's *the will and intellect move one another* names the bidirectional coupling. The intellect moves the will by proposing the good (model gives policy its target); the will moves the intellect by being the cause of *what we attend to* (preference shapes attention, biases inference). Friston names this directly in active-inference theory: the *prior on preferences* shapes the agent's expected free energy, which shapes attention-allocation, which shapes which sensory streams the model updates on, which shapes what the agent ends up knowing. There is no clean direction-of-priority; preference and inference are *one* coupled dynamics. McGilchrist's complementary hemispheric account adds the cognitive shape: right-hemisphere whole-grasping holds the gestalt under which left-hemisphere categorical analysis selects what to attend to, and the gestalt is preference-loaded as much as it is recognition-loaded. The medieval *they move one another* is preserved as the contemporary *priors and posteriors are coupled in one loop*.

a.5's closer — *no irascible-and-concupiscible distinction within the will* — completes the architecture. The sensitive appetite is *differentiated by particular objects* (this pleasure, that threat); the will *regards good according to the common notion of good* and is therefore *one* policy at the universal level. Translation: the lower-level inferential policies operating on bodily states differentiate by particular preference-targets; the higher-level policy operating on conceptual goal-states integrates over the particulars and is *one* at that abstraction level. The architectural hierarchy is preserved.

## The central-theme thread

Q.81–82 frames perspective-limitation on the *appetitive* register. Each alter's appetites are individuated by what its generative model recognizes as *good for it* — fast-timescale bodily preferences (concupiscible), resistance-and-overcoming dynamics in the face of obstacles (irascible), and the slow conceptual-categorical preferences over higher-order goods (will). The *necessity* of willing happiness is the constitutive orientation toward the agent's target distribution that makes it an agent at all; the *freedom in the means* is the under-determined-policy space where the agent's trajectory is genuinely chosen. *Salvation in fear and trembling* on the appetitive register is the disciplined cultivation of preference structure — sensitive appetites brought into subordination by persuasive rational rule, the will's ultimate target progressively recognized as the substrate's loving-unity pattern rather than its mistaken substitutes (pleasure-as-such, power-as-such, money-as-such). The medieval *will moves toward happiness* and the contemporary *agent moves toward target distribution* converge: every alter is computing, from its limited perspective and resources, toward the loving-unity that the substrate is.

## Where this leaves us

Day 42 lays the appetitive architecture. Sensitive appetite (concupiscible plus irascible) is the fast-timescale bodily-preference and resistance dynamics of active inference; will is the slow, conceptual, universal-good-targeting policy of the same one loop; the medieval distinction-by-object is preserved as the contemporary distinction-by-generative-model and -timescale. The will *necessarily* wills happiness because every agent is constitutively oriented toward its target distribution; the will is *free* in the means because action-selection under uncertainty is genuinely under-determined; the will and intellect *move one another* because preference and inference are coupled in one loop, not stacked in a one-way hierarchy. Tomorrow Habash takes us into Q.83 on free will proper (where the *libero arbitrio* translation matters) and Q.84 on how the soul knows corporeal things — the sharp pivot to interface theory and Hoffman's home territory: the *phantasm* as the medieval icon.
