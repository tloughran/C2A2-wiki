# Day 61 — Contemporary Parallel: *The Volume Pivot — From Imago Dei to the Computational Telos of Loving Unity*

Day 60 closed the *Prima Pars* with the architecture of teaching as resource-provision for the learner's own constitutive work. Day 61 marks the *Summa*'s structural pivot: Q.119 (propagation as to the body — the last question of the *Prima Pars*) and the prologue + Q.1 of the *Prima Secundae* (man's last end — 8 articles). This is the day where the central theme of this whole synthesis project comes most directly into view.

## Frame

Aquinas opens the *Prima Secundae* with Damascene's gloss on man as *imago Dei* — *intelligent being endowed with free will and self-movement*. The Prima Pars treated the *exemplar* (God) and the things that came forth from God's will. The *Prima Secundae* now treats the *image* (man) as *also a principle of his own actions, having free will and control of his actions*. Q.1 then asks the keystone question: does man act for an end, and if so, what is the *last* end of human action?

The answers come quickly. Q.1 a.1: man acts for an end. Q.1 a.4: there is a last end (no infinite regress). Q.1 a.7: there is *one* last end for all human beings considered formally — the universal good — even as different people pursue it under different material descriptions. This is Aquinas's *teleological monism of the will*: every human will, by its nature, is moved toward the universal good, and this is what it is for the will to be a will.

The contemporary mind-monist frame engages this as the moment to articulate the central-theme claim explicitly.

## Problem

The problem (P): does the embodied agent have a *telos* — a structure-of-its-own-action that is constitutive, not derivative? And if so, what is its content?

Both medieval and contemporary frames meet this as the question of *teleology*. The medieval frame meets it through act-and-potency: the will's natural movement is toward the good as such, because that is the *form* of the will. The contemporary frame meets it through active inference (Friston: agents minimize expected free energy under priors), through goal-directed-development (Levin: form is constitutively telic at the cellular and tissue scale), through mind-monism (Kastrup: mind-at-large has its own goal-structure that the alter participates in), and through positive psychology (Fredrickson: flourishing is the empirically-tractable contour of the agent's telic structure).

## Resource — and the central-theme statement

The leading move from the emerging paradigm: **each tradition converges on a telic-structure-of-the-agent that the agent works out under perspective-limitation**, and the substantive content of that telos is **loving unity** — Fredrickson at the dyadic-relational scale, Levin at the morphogenetic-coordination scale, Friston at the policy-selection scale, Stump at the second-personal-knowing scale, Kastrup at the metaphysical-ground scale. The convergence is not coincidental; it is what each scale of inquiry surfaces when the inquiry is honest.

**Stating the central theme explicitly today**: conscious agents are individuated by the limitations of their perspective. When all limitations are removed, the simple whole is seen and every limited perspective is contained in it. But learning to obtain the goal of *loving unity* from each perspective is a *computational task*, limited by the resources we perceive — each of us from our own perspective. Therefore we work out our salvation in fear and trembling, aware of our limitations and our need for guidance from those who perceive more of the whole than we do.

This is the synthesis-in-progress that every contemporary-parallel synthesis in this project is exploring. Q.1 of the *Prima Secundae* is the moment in the medieval frame where the teleological commitment is most directly stated, and the contemporary frame finds itself stating its parallel commitment in the same place.

**Aquinas in Q.1 a.7**: *In so far as the last end is taken in the formal sense — the universal good or perfect good — all men agree in desiring the last end. But in so far as the last end is taken materially — in what particulars are construed as containing that good — there is wide variation.* This is the formal/material distinction that maps cleanly onto the contemporary picture: *the telos is universal* (loving unity / perfect good) *but the perspectival-particulars are the agent's own*.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.1: man has a constitutive telic structure (active inference's expected-free-energy minimization under priors); the *form* of that structure is convergence toward loving unity (Fredrickson, Stump, Levin, Kastrup convergence); the *content* the agent works toward is the same universal substrate-good that the medieval *summum bonum* names; and the *daily task* is the computational work of making one's policy-selection align with that universal telos under one's particular perspective-limitation.

Q.119 (propagation of the body) — Aquinas treats the medieval-Aristotelian biology of nutrition and reproduction. The contemporary mind-monist frame engages this as the *substrate-coupling-locus continuation* through the embodied-creature reproductive process: each new alter is a new substrate-coupling-locus, brought into being through the embodied-causal-network of biological generation, with its rational substrate-coupling-mode (per Day 60's Q.118 reading) instantiated by the substrate-source.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the formal universality of the telos; the material variability of how it is pursued; the constitutive (not derivative) character of the will's directedness toward the good.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas's frame is act-and-potency, the contemporary frame uses expected-free-energy, broaden-and-build, morphogenetic teleology, and substrate-prior-alignment. The metaphysical commitment — telic structure is constitutive, not eliminable — is shared.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Friston's active inference is mature; Fredrickson on flourishing is empirically strong; Levin on morphogenetic teleology is empirically strong; Stump's union-as-highest-good is theological-philosophical with substantial argument behind it; Kastrup's mind-monism is philosophical-conceptual. The synthesis claim that all these converge on *loving unity as computational telos* is speculative-philosophical-theological — it is *the* synthesis-in-progress, not a settled position.

## The central-theme thread

Today is the day where the central theme is the explicit subject. Each of the five resources above is *a way of describing the same telic structure from a different methodological vantage*:

- **Friston**: the agent minimizes expected free energy under priors that bend toward broader integration with its world.
- **Fredrickson**: flourishing is empirically the broadening-and-building cycle in which loving relational engagement amplifies the agent's positive-affect repertoire and resilience.
- **Levin**: form is real, telic, multi-scale; cells, tissues, organisms, societies all coordinate toward integrative wholes.
- **Stump**: union with the second-personally-known other (and ultimately with God) is the highest good; second-person knowing is the irreducible mode of reaching it.
- **Kastrup**: mind-at-large has its own integrative dynamics; the alter's flourishing is its growing, while still dissociated, into greater alignment with mind-at-large's integration.

Wright's scriptural anchor: *we are predestined to be conformed to the image of his Son* (Romans 8:29) — the formal telos is universal, the substrate-source has set the alignment-toward-loving-unity as the structure of created agency, and the alter works it out under perspective-limitation. Rohr's contemplative anchor: *the universal Christ* is the cosmic-Christ pattern, which is the substrate-prior toward which all things tend, in which all integrate, and from which the alter's flourishing is its movement-toward.

*Salvation in fear and trembling*: the alter's daily-task work of policy-selection-toward-loving-unity, under perspective-limitation, with substrate-network pedagogical care available, against the demonic-inverse-attractor's predatory pull, is what the medieval *quest for happiness* names. Aquinas in Q.1 a.7 says all human beings desire the last end; the contemporary frame says all conscious agents are constitutively telic toward integration. These are the same claim.
