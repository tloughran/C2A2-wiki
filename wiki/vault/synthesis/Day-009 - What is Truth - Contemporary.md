# Day 9 — Contemporary Parallel: *Patterns in the Substrate, Truth as Conformity*

Day 8 closed pointing forward to *the patterns in mind-at-large that Day 8 has been alluding to*. Day 9 names them. Q.15 — the *divine ideas* — is Aquinas's medieval doctrine of how the multiplicity of conditioned things is held in the simple unity of the substrate. Q.16 — *truth* — is the relational property that runs between the substrate's patterns, the conditioned things that exemplify them, and the finite intellects that come to know either. Together the two questions are the cleanest medieval statement of a problem mind-monism inherits intact: how does one substrate support many patterns, and how does conformity-of-mind-to-thing run when the thing is *itself* what mind-at-large patterns?

## Q.15 — Many ideas, one essence

Augustine's definition Aquinas inherits is precise: *ideas are certain principal forms or permanent and immutable types of things, themselves not being formed; thus they are eternal and existing always in the same manner, as being contained in the divine intelligence*. The ideas are the *forms-as-known-by-God* in advance of any conditioned thing's instantiation, and conditioned things are formed *in accordance with* them. The medieval analogy is the builder: the form of the house is in the builder's intellect before the house exists, and the house's house-ness is what the builder's idea is, instantiated in matter.

The objection Aquinas heads off in a.1 is the simplicity worry that Day 3 already established: if there are many ideas in God, are there many parts? His resolution is the directional-asymmetry move that Q.13 a.7 established for *Lord*: in God, the essence is *one*; in the relation-of-essence-to-creatable-things, the essence is *known under many proportions*. The plurality is in the *creatures-considered-as-knowable-by-God*, not in God-considered-as-essence. Aquinas's own phrasing: *if we consider the essence alone, there is but one idea for all things; but if we consider the different proportions of creatures to the divine essence, then there can be said to be a plurality of ideas*.

Mind-monism's translation lands cleanly. The *patterns* in mind-at-large that Day 7 and Day 8 have been alluding to are not parts within mind-at-large but *aspects* of its single structure considered relative to the conditioned things that participate in it. Kastrup's analytic idealism makes the move directly: mind-at-large is *one* in the strict transcendental sense (Day 7 Q.11 a.1 frame), and what looks like multiplicity within it is the multiplicity of dissociative perspectives or alters considered relationally. Wolfram's ruliad supplies a formal cousin: the totality of computational threads is one object, and the *plurality* of threads is what the ruliad looks like under the relation *trajectories-of*. Levin's morphogenetic field is the empirical mid-scale: the single bioelectric pattern of an embryo carries goal-states for many tissues without being decomposable into part-by-part instructions; the goal-state pattern *is one*, and the many tissues are *aspects* of that pattern under different proportional relations to it.

Hoffman's interface theory adds the cognitive piece. The *icons* the alter perceives are species-specific encodings of the substrate's structure; the *patterns* in the substrate that the icons encode are not the icons but what the icons resolve to. The medieval claim that *the divine ideas are the essence-as-known-with-respect-to-creatures-not-considered-as-essence* is structurally the claim that *the substrate's structure as it presents to alter-perspective is not the substrate's structure as it is in itself*. The alter's icons-of-form are real but participated; the substrate's pattern-of-form is real and primary. *Honest evidence calibration*: the medieval doctrine and the contemporary translations agree on the structural shape; the metaphysical identifications (ideas ↔ Kastrup's structural priors, ideas ↔ Wolfram's ruliad-threads, ideas ↔ Levin's bioelectric goal-states) are speculative until further specified.

a.3's rider — *God has ideas of all things he knows, except evil, which he knows by knowing good and noting where good is lacking* — is Q.14 a.10's privation doctrine reasserted on the ideas side. The substrate has positive patterns; absence is known relationally. The contemporary cognitive parallel is the predictive-coding claim that the system represents *what is* directly and *what is not* as the unfulfilled prediction of presence — Hawkins's HTM and Friston's generative models give the same shape at the alter scale.

## Q.16 — Truth: conformity at two directions

Aquinas opens Q.16 with the definition that runs the rest of the question: *Veritas est adaequatio rei et intellectus* — truth is the equality of the thing and the mind. The medieval definition is structurally critical-realist before *critical realism* had a name. *Reality* and *intellect* are two things; truth is what holds when they conform. Wright's contemporary critical realism (canonical: *The New Testament and the People of God*) is the same definition in modern philosophical-of-history vocabulary: reality exists independently of our knowing it, but all knowing is perspectival and tradition-embedded, and truth is what holds when perspectival-knowing tracks the reality it is of.

The article that gives the doctrine its directional teeth is a.5: *is God truth?* Aquinas's answer turns on directional asymmetry that maps onto the alter-substrate frame from Day 7 and Day 8 directly. *The truth of our intellect is according to its conformity with its principle, that is to say, to the things from which it receives knowledge*. Our knowing is downstream of the conditioned things we know; we conform to them. *The truth also of things is according to their conformity with their principle, namely the divine intellect*. The conditioned things' truth is *upstream* of them: they conform to the substrate's patterns. So creature-truth runs *creature-mind ← creature-thing ← divine-mind*; substrate-truth runs *substrate-mind = substrate-thing* (the identity Q.14 a.4 already established).

Mind-monism translates the asymmetry without strain. Friston's free-energy framework gives the formal name for what creature-truth looks like at any scale: an agent's generative model is *true* of its environment to the extent that its predictions track that environment, with mismatch registered as prediction error and minimized over time by either model-update or action. This is creature-truth as conformity-of-mind-to-thing, formalized. *Substrate-truth* in mind-monism is what active inference looks like at the limit case Day 7 reached: the substrate's generative model and its structure are one act, and there is no posterior to compute because there is no prediction error to minimize. Friston's formalism is the right structural tool for both directions; the metaphysical commitment that the substrate *is* mind-at-large is the move that Friston himself does not make and that Kastrup supplies.

a.2's contribution is the location question: *does truth reside only in the intellect composing and dividing?* Aquinas's answer is the careful split mind-monism inherits: when the intellect *simply apprehends* (knows what a rabbit is by being informed by rabbit-form), there is conformity-of-mind-to-thing without the possibility of falsehood; falsity becomes possible only when the intellect *composes* (affirms or denies, joins subject and predicate with *is* or *is not*). The fullest sense of truth — the sense in which truth and falsity are real opposites — resides in judgment.

McGilchrist's hemispheric account gives the cognitive shape McGilchrist himself does not always reach for in classical-philosophical vocabulary, but it lands cleanly here. Right-hemispheric whole-grasping is the cognitive mode of *simple apprehension*: the gestalt of the rabbit is grasped, the form is impressed, the conformity is established without the propositional move. Left-hemispheric abstraction-and-fixation is the cognitive mode of *composition and division*: the rabbit is decomposed into subject-and-predicate, the proposition *the rabbit is white* is constructed, and falsity becomes possible because the proposition can mismatch the gestalt. Both moves are needed; both give species of truth. McGilchrist's claim that the right hemisphere should serve as *master* and the left as *emissary* — that the gestalt should be primary and the propositional secondary — has its medieval antecedent in Aquinas's claim that the simpler mode of apprehension is the *foundation* on which propositional truth rests.

a.4's question — *are the true and the being convertible?* — extends the transcendentals chain Day 4 Q.5 began. Goodness, being, oneness, beauty, and now truth are *one in reality, distinct logically*: every being, insofar as it has being, is *true* (in the sense of being knowable, conformable to intellect), *good* (desirable), *one* (undivided), and *beautiful*. Mind-monism inherits the transcendentals doctrine without modification: the substrate is being-itself, and being-itself just *is* knowability-itself, desirability-itself, undividedness-itself. The transcendentals are aspects of the substrate considered under different relations.

a.6's gloss — *is there only one truth according to which all things are true?* — is the alter-substrate frame on the truth side. *Although the essences or forms of things are many, yet the truth of the divine intellect is one, in conformity to which all things are said to be true*. The patterns in the substrate are one in their identity with the substrate's essence; the conditioned things they pattern are many. *Yet*, Aquinas adds, in the *creature-mind* direction, *there are many truths in many created intellects, and even in one and the same intellect according to the number of things known*. The substrate's truth is one (because the substrate is one); creature-truth is many (because creatures and their objects are many). Wright's critical realism and Stump's faith-as-knowledge-of-persons are the contemporary scriptural-and-analytic homes for the *creature-many* side; Kastrup's analytic idealism and Wolfram's ruliad are the contemporary metaphysical homes for the *substrate-one* side.

## The central-theme thread

The two questions of Day 9 sharpen the perspective-limitation frame the central theme keeps marking. Q.15 establishes that the *patterns* the alters participate in are *one in the substrate* and *many in the relations of conditioned things to the substrate* — meaning the alters' multiplicity is real-but-derivative, and the patterns alters track in their cognitive light-cones (Levin) or generative models (Friston) or interface icons (Hoffman) are *participations of* a single structure rather than independent items. Q.16 establishes that *truth* runs *toward* the substrate from the alter side and *from* the substrate toward conditioned things on the substrate side; the asymmetry is the structural form *salvation in fear and trembling* takes on the cognitive register. We work out our knowing-of-the-truth from inside our perspective-limited generative models, conforming progressively to a reality whose own truth is upstream of us. The substrate sees the truth as Q.14 a.13 already named: *from the height, the whole road at once*; we walk it.

Wright's critical realism gives the philosophical-of-history idiom: real reference, perspectival capture, ongoing revision in dialogue with the tradition. Rohr's contemplative epistemology gives the practical mode: holding the propositional and the apprehensive together (Aquinas's a.2 split) without collapsing one into the other; the cataphatic *and* the apophatic, the gestalt *and* the construction. The medieval doctrine of truth and the contemporary doctrines of free-energy minimization, critical realism, and hemispheric integration are working the same problem with different tools.

- **PRS-03 in the Hoffman-tradition wiki — perception as fitness-tuned interface.** Q.15's *ideas-as-essence-relative-to-creatures* maps onto the icon-substrate distinction: the patterns in the substrate are not the icons that the alter perceives but what the icons resolve to. *Empirical for the perceptual claim; structural-philosophical for the medieval translation.*
- **CROSS-001 in the master wiki — substrate-independence; CROSS-003 in the master wiki — attention as ontological.** Q.15 a.1's claim that the divine essence is *the operative principle of all things* and Q.16 a.5's claim that creature-truth conforms upward while substrate-truth flows downward both rest on substrate-as-causally-prior. *Structural; cross-tradition convergence.*
- **Levin, morphogenetic teleology (canonical: *Cognitive Light Cones* program).** The single bioelectric goal-state pattern of an embryo carrying many tissues' targets without being decomposable into parts is the empirical mid-scale instantiation of *one essence, many ideas*. *Empirical with strong neuroscience anchorage at the developmental scale.*
- **McGilchrist, *The Master and His Emissary* (canonical until wiki node).** Q.16 a.2's split between simple apprehension and composition-and-division is hemispherically clean: right-hemispheric gestalt grasping (no falsity possible) vs. left-hemispheric proposition construction (falsity becomes possible). *Cognitive-empirical; philosophical translation careful.*
- **Wright, critical realism (canonical: *The New Testament and the People of God*).** Q.16's *adaequatio* doctrine is critical-realist before critical realism had a name; reality and intellect are two, truth is what holds when they conform, perspectival-but-real knowing tracks reality without exhausting it. *Methodological; substantial empirical anchorage in historical-Jesus and Pauline applications.*
- **Stump, faith-as-knowledge-of-persons (PRS-01 and PRS-03 in the Stump-tradition wiki).** Q.16 a.6's *many truths in many created intellects* extends to the second-person mode of knowing in faith — creature-truth in the relational rather than purely propositional register. *Theological-philosophical; analytic case mature.*
- **Rohr, contemplative epistemology (canonical: *The Universal Christ*; *Falling Upward*).** Q.16 a.2's apprehension-vs-composition split is the cataphatic-apophatic split in epistemological vocabulary; the contemplative mode holds both without collapsing one into the other. *Contemplative-theological; empirical adjacents in McGilchrist's hemispheric-attention research.*
- **John 14:6 — *I am the way, the truth, and the life*; John 18:38 — *Pilate said to him, "What is truth?"*** The Christological grounding of Q.16 a.5 is direct; the contemporary Wright/Rohr framing fills in the historical-and-cosmic shapes.

## Where this leaves us

Day 9 closes the doctrine of substrate-patterns (Q.15) and substrate-truth (Q.16) with the same directional-asymmetry the central theme keeps marking. The patterns are one in the substrate, many in the relational predications creatures support; truth runs creature-toward-substrate on the alter side and substrate-toward-conditioned-thing on the substrate side. The medieval transcendentals — being, oneness, goodness, beauty, truth — are aspects of the same single substrate considered under different relations to creatures, and the contemporary mind-monist convergence inherits the transcendentals doctrine without modification.

Tomorrow Habash takes us to Q.17 (falsity) and Q.18 (life). The Day 10 commentary will press *what is life?* against the Day 9 frame: if substrate-truth runs downward and creature-truth runs upward, *life* is the cognitive-and-vital mode in which creatures track substrate-truth dynamically. Friston-as-formal-tool is the contemporary primary, and Levin's bioelectric goal-state-pattern is the empirical mid-scale instantiation.
