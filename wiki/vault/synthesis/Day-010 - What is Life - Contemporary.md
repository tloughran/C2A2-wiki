# Day 10 — Contemporary Parallel: *Life as Self-Movement, Falsity as Mismatch*

Day 9 closed pointing to *life as the cognitive-and-vital mode in which creatures track substrate-truth dynamically*. Day 10 pairs that gesture with a backstop: Q.17 names what *failure* of conformity-of-mind-to-thing looks like (falsity), and Q.18 names what *successful* self-determining tracking looks like as a *kind of being* — the living thing. The two questions read as a single argument when the transcendentals frame from Day 9 is held in place: truth and falsity are properties of judgments and of things-considered-against-their-divine-pattern; life is the substantive mode in which a being holds enough internal pattern to direct its own motion toward its own goals.

## Q.17 — Falsity as privation and as judgment-mismatch

a.1's question — *does falsity exist in things?* — gets the careful medieval answer. *Things* are not in themselves false in the way *judgments* are; but a thing can be called *false* in two derived senses. First: a thing departs from the *pattern in the divine intellect that it ought to instantiate* — Aquinas's example is the moral case (the sinner is called a *lie*; Psalm 4: *how long will you love vain words and seek after lies*). Second: an *artifact* falls short of what its maker had in mind. Both senses are derivative — primary falsity belongs to the *judgment* that affirms or denies what is not the case.

Mind-monism's translation lands cleanly. The *pattern in the divine intellect* the conditioned thing ought to instantiate is, in Day 9's translation, the structural prior in mind-at-large that gives the conditioned thing its identity. A conditioned thing's *falsity-as-privation* is its departure from the pattern it participates in — a real mismatch, marked by the agent's own internal sense that something is missing or off-target. Levin's bioelectric framework gives the mid-scale empirical handle: cells and tissues that *fail to attain* their morphogenetic goal-state — by injury, by genetic perturbation, by environmental disruption — are *false* in this medieval sense, missing the form they ought to instantiate. Friston's free-energy framework gives the formalism: a generative model whose predictions chronically fail to track its environment registers high free energy, and the system is *off* its self-maintaining attractor. Falsity-as-privation in either case is real, relationally specifiable, and (importantly) corrigible by either action or model-update.

a.2's split between *senses* and *intellect* is the same simple-apprehension-vs-judgment split Day 9 Q.16 a.2 gave. The senses do not get *color* wrong (the eye's reception of color is just the eye being informed by what is there); the senses get *shape* or *species* wrong — not because the proper sensible is mis-received but because non-essential properties are inferred from the proper-sensibles. Hoffman's interface theory gives the contemporary cognitive frame: the *icon-level* construction the alter performs above its proper-sensible inputs is fitness-tuned, and fitness-tuning is precisely the kind of operation that can mis-fire as judgment without mis-firing as raw reception. Predictive-coding hierarchies (Hawkins's HTM, Friston's generative models) name the same shape: low-level reception is veridical *enough* for the level it operates at, and falsity emerges as the higher-level inferences are constructed.

a.3's resolution — *falsity is in the intellect composing and dividing* — closes the question on the Day 9 frame. Falsity is structurally the *compositional* failure: the proposition *the rabbit is white* can mismatch the gestalt because *the proposition* is constructed; the gestalt cannot mismatch what informs it because the gestalt is the form-impressed-on-the-eye. McGilchrist's hemispheric account is again the cleanest cognitive translation: right-hemispheric apprehension is pre-propositional and gives the gestalt without the possibility of falsity-as-judgment-error; left-hemispheric proposition-construction is where falsity-as-mismatch becomes possible. Both modes are needed; both are part of how we know.

a.4's contraries claim — *true and false are contraries because both are within the intellect and specifically in the judgment of the intellect* — closes Q.17 by making explicit the *common arena* in which the opposition holds. Football teams are opposed because they share *being football teams*; angels and demons share *being immaterial spirits*; truth and falsity share *being assertions of the intellect-in-judgment*. The contemporary cognitive translation is structural: prediction-error and prediction-confirmation share *being the agent's posterior under the same generative model*; they are intelligible as opposites because they live in the same inferential space.

## Q.18 — Life as self-movement, life in God

Q.18's opening definition — *all things are said to be alive that determine themselves to movement or operation of any kind* — is the medieval doctrine of life as *intrinsic self-direction*. The hierarchy Aquinas walks through is the contemporary biology hierarchy in classical vocabulary: stone (no self-movement); plant (self-movement determined by nature, growth-toward-light); animal (self-movement determined by sense-knowledge plus instinct); human (self-movement determined by intellect-and-will, knowing the essence of things and willing the means to ends); God (self-movement absolute — end, means, and cause-of-movement all identical with himself).

Mind-monism's contemporary primary on this question is Levin, and the alignment is unusually tight. Levin's *Cognitive Light Cones* program is the empirical map of exactly the hierarchy Aquinas names. A cell has a small cognitive light-cone — a limited horizon of states it can perceive and a limited goal-set it can pursue. A tissue (multi-cellular collective) has a larger light-cone. An organism has a larger one still. A community of organisms has a yet larger one. Each level is *alive* in Aquinas's medieval sense — capable of self-determined movement toward its own goals, where the goals are real (bioelectric attractor states, in Levin's empirical formalism) and the movement is real (morphogenetic action, behavioral action, communicative action). The medieval hierarchy of *grades of life* maps onto the contemporary biology hierarchy of *grades of cognitive light-cone*, and the empirical evidence for the latter is substantial (bioelectric work on regeneration, planaria, xenobots).

Friston's free-energy framework is the formal structure Levin's empirical work uses: a *living* thing in Friston's terms is a system that maintains itself by minimizing free energy under a generative model of its environment, where *self-maintenance* is precisely the *self-determining motion* Aquinas names. Friston's PRS-07 (substrate-independence of FEP, formally established 2026) and PRS-08 (morphogenetic active inference) are the active-research nodes; CROSS-006/007 in the master cross-program index is the formal Levin-Friston bridge. *Honest evidence calibration*: the formal framework is mature; the substrate-scale extrapolation (mind-at-large as the *living* substrate) is philosophical rather than empirical, and depends on the additional move that mind-monism makes and Friston himself does not.

a.3 — *life is found supremely in God* — receives the same directional-asymmetry treatment Day 7 and Day 8 have established. Creature-life is graded by *how much* of the motion is self-determined and *how little* is determined by external cause; God's life is the limit case — *no* external determination, end-and-means-and-cause identical with the substrate's own essence. Mind-monism's contemporary statement is that the substrate is *self-maintaining* in the way Friston's autopoietic limit names: the generative model and the structure are one act, and the *living* the substrate exhibits is not an achievement against external constraint (because there is no outside) but the substrate-being-itself-and-thereby-being-active. Wolfram's ruliad gives a formal cousin: the totality of computational threads is, considered as a single object, *active* in the sense that its threads *run* — without being run by anything outside. Kastrup's analytic idealism makes the metaphysical move directly: mind-at-large is *being-of-experiencing*, and experiencing is intrinsically active — not a passive register but a generative process.

a.4's closer — *all things are alive in God* — is the Q.15 ideas-doctrine reasserted on the life side. Habash reads it through John 1: *all things were made through him and without him was not anything made that was made; in him was life*. Aquinas's gloss: things-as-they-are-known-by-God are *alive in God* in the sense that they exist in his life-which-is-his-knowing-which-is-his-essence. Mind-monism's translation: the structural priors in mind-at-large that pattern conditioned things are *active* aspects of mind-at-large, not inert templates; the patterns *live* in the substrate's own activity. Wright's contemporary Christological framing — Christ as the agent through whom all things were made — adds the scriptural ground that Aquinas's own Christological reading rests on; Rohr's *Universal Christ* extends the cosmic-scope reading: the divine pattern is alive in matter from the beginning, not imposed on inert stuff later.

## The central-theme thread

The two questions of Day 10 frame the central theme on the *vital* register. Q.17's falsity-as-privation is the structural form *failure* takes for an alter computing toward loving union: the alter departs from the pattern it ought to instantiate, and the departure is *real* — registered as predictive failure or moral-spiritual mismatch, corrigible by either action or model-update. Q.18's life-as-self-movement is the structural form *success* takes: the alter holds enough of the substrate's pattern internally to *direct its own motion* toward its own goals, and the more of the pattern it holds (the larger its cognitive light-cone), the more *alive* it is in Aquinas's medieval sense. *Salvation in fear and trembling* is, on the vital register, the alter's progressive acquisition of pattern-holding capacity sufficient to direct its own motion toward integration with the substrate — Levin's larger-light-cone trajectory in classical vocabulary.

The asymmetry between creature-life and substrate-life is what the central theme keeps marking. Creatures are alive *graded*; the substrate is alive *unconditionally*. The medieval doctrine of life-as-self-movement and the contemporary biology of cognitive light-cones / free-energy minimization / morphogenetic teleology converge on the recognition that *life is self-pattern-holding-and-pattern-pursuing*, and that the gradient runs from minimal (cell) to maximal (mind-at-large). The scriptural verse Aquinas centers — *I am the way, the truth, and the life* — names the substrate's mode at the limit case Day 7 already reached: the substrate's life is identical with its truth which is identical with its mode-of-being.

## Where this leaves us

Day 10 closes the Day 9 frame by naming what failure of conformity (falsity) and success of self-pattern-holding (life) look like under the mind-monist translation. The medieval transcendentals doctrine — being, oneness, goodness, beauty, truth, plus the substantive mode of life — finds its contemporary mid-scale empirical instantiation in Levin's cognitive-light-cone biology and its formal home in Friston's free-energy framework, with Kastrup and Wolfram supplying the substrate-scale metaphysical commitments that Levin and Friston themselves stop short of.
