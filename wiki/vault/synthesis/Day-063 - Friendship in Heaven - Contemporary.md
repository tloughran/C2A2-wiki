# Day 63 — Contemporary Parallel: *Vision, Delight, Comprehension, and the Friends of Heaven as Constitutive Accompaniments of Loving Union*

Day 62 established that happiness consists in the integrative act of contemplative union with the substrate-source, and that particular candidate-goods fail to be the substantive content of flourishing because they are means or consequences, not the integrative act itself. Day 63 takes Aquinas through Q.4 (those things required for happiness — 8 articles) and Q.5 (the attainment of happiness — 8 articles). The contemporary mind-monist frame engages this as the *accompaniment-conditions* register: what *must accompany* the integrative act for it to be flourishing, and what *enables* the alter's attainment.

## Frame

Q.4 a.1: delight is required for happiness — *because happiness is nothing else but the attainment of the sovereign good, it cannot be without concomitant delight*. Q.4 a.2: vision (knowledge) ranks before delight — we cannot delight in what we do not know. Q.4 a.3: comprehension (the will's grasping-and-resting, in Aquinas's translation of *catching*) is necessary. Q.4 a.4: rectitude of the will is necessary. Q.4 a.5: the body is required for *perfect* happiness (in the resurrected state — Wright's territory). Q.4 a.6: bodily goods are required for *imperfect* happiness in this life. Q.4 a.7: external goods are required for *imperfect* happiness in this life. Q.4 a.8: the *fellowship of friends* is required for happiness *in this life*; the friends of heaven *contribute to the well-being* (bene esse) of beatitude though they are not strictly required for *esse*.

Q.5 covers attainment: whether man can attain happiness (yes, in part), whether one man can be happier than another (yes), whether happiness is attainable in this life (only imperfectly), whether happiness once attained can be lost (not in heaven; yes in this life), whether man can attain happiness by his natural powers (no, perfect happiness requires grace), whether the help of grace is needed (yes), whether every man desires happiness (yes), whether all creatures desire happiness (in the analogous sense — formally yes).

## Problem

The problem (P): given that flourishing is the integrative act of union, what *constitutive accompaniments* must obtain for the integrative act to be the act it is? And given that the alter is constitutively perspective-limited, how does the alter actually *attain* the integrative act — what enables it?

## Resource

**Fredrickson on relational well-being and Love 2.0.** Empirically, flourishing is *relational*: positivity-resonance occurs in dyadic-and-group settings, where shared attention and mutual care generate cardiac-vagal coherence and affective amplification. The medieval *fellowship of friends* preserves cleanly under Fredrickson's frame. *In this life*, friends are required for flourishing because flourishing is constitutively relational. *In heaven*, the relational structure persists — the substrate-source is itself relational (Trinitarian, in the theological frame; mind-at-large with its own integrative dynamics, in Kastrup's frame), and the friends of heaven amplify the well-being-quality of the integrative act.

**Stump on union as highest good.** The second-personal-knowing structure of union *requires* the second-person other; happiness is constitutively relational. The medieval *vision* and *comprehension* preserve as second-personal knowing in its full mode — vision is the seeing-of-the-other-as-other, comprehension is the holding-of-the-other-in-mutual-presence.

**McGilchrist on right-hemispheric receptivity.** Vision-and-delight in the medieval frame depend on the agent's mode-of-attention being open and receptive. The right hemisphere is the locus of receptive attention; it is the receptive mode that allows the integrative act to occur. The medieval *delight follows vision* preserves as right-hemispheric reception generating the affective-amplification side of integration.

**Friston on terminal-prior alignment.** Q.4 a.4's *rectitude of the will* — the will being correctly ordered toward God — preserves under active inference as the agent's terminal-prior being aligned with the substrate-source's integrative dynamics. *Rectitude* is prior-alignment.

**Kastrup on mind-at-large as relational ground.** The accompaniment-conditions register makes sense under analytic idealism because mind-at-large is itself relational — the alter's flourishing is the alter's growing-into the relational structure of mind-at-large. This is the metaphysical anchor for *why* friends of heaven contribute to well-being even if not strictly required for *esse*: the substrate-source's own integrative dynamics are relational.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.4: the integrative-act of union has constitutive accompaniments of (1) cognitive-receptive vision (the alter perceives the substrate-source under right-hemispheric receptive attention), (2) affective delight (positivity-resonance amplification at the maximum-coherence limit), (3) volitional comprehension (the alter's terminal-prior catches and holds the substrate-source in mutual-presence), (4) prior-alignment rectitude (the will's ordering matches the substrate-source's), (5) embodied participation (resurrected body is the full mode; embodied life is the imperfect mode), (6) instrumental-good-sufficiency in this life (the alter has what it needs to pursue the integrative-act), and (7) relational fellowship (friends here required, friends in heaven contribute to bene esse).

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.5: the alter can attain the integrative-act in part by its own resources (natural happiness in this life, in the imperfect form), but the *full* integrative-act with the substrate-source requires the substrate-source's enabling — Stump's *grace-as-enabling-second-first-language-fluency* preserves under the contemporary frame as the substrate-source's communication-of-its-own-integration-pattern to the alter, which the alter could not infer from its own resources because its perspective-limitation precludes the substrate-direct-mode access.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the constitutive structure of accompaniment-conditions; the imperfect/perfect distinction between this-life and heavenly happiness; the necessity of grace for full attainment.

What it *reframes*: Aquinas's frame is hylomorphic-and-virtue-theoretic on the conditions; the contemporary frame uses positivity-resonance, second-personal-knowing, hemispheric-attention, active-inference-prior-alignment, and substrate-source-communication.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Fredrickson's relational-well-being is empirically very strong; Stump's second-personal-knowing is theological-philosophical with substantial backing; McGilchrist's hemispheric account is empirically grounded; Friston's prior-alignment is mature formalism. The substrate-source-communicates-its-integration-pattern reading of grace is speculative-philosophical-theological.

## The central-theme thread

Today's day articulates a load-bearing implication of the central theme: *loving unity is constitutively relational*. The integrative-act of union with the substrate-source is not solitary contemplation; it is *participation* in the substrate-source's own relational structure, *with* others who are also participating, and this with-others-character is not extrinsic to flourishing but constitutive.

This is where Wright and Rohr converge load-bearingly. Wright on covenanted community: salvation is *inclusion in the people united with God*; flourishing is *together*. Rohr on the Universal Christ: the cosmic-Christ pattern is the relational pattern, the *we-in-Christ-in-God* structure that the alter participates in by integration. Fredrickson's empirical work on positivity-resonance is the contemporary scientific anchor: flourishing is *between*, not *within*.

*Salvation in fear and trembling* under perspective-limitation, then, is *not solitary*. The alter works it out *with* the friends with whom it is becoming-aligned to the substrate-source, *under* the substrate-network's pedagogical care, *toward* the eschatological inclusion in the *one blessed and ordered society of creatures ordered to the Creator* (Day 55's Q.108 a.8 keystone).

Q.4 a.4's *rectitude of the will* is doing keystone work in today's day: the will is not just any-orientation; it is the will *correctly ordered* toward the substrate-source. And rectitude is *learnable* — it is the daily-task content of dispositional formation. The substrate-network's pedagogical care, the friends with whom the alter walks the daily task, the diagnostic-of-mis-location from yesterday's exclusion arguments, the substrate-source's grace-as-enabling-pattern-communication: all these converge on the alter's *rectitude-formation* as the central daily task.
