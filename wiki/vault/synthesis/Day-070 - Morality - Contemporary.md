# Day 70 — Contemporary Parallel: *Moral Evaluation, Erring Conscience, and Formal-vs-Material Conformity to the Substrate-Source*

Day 69 articulated the layered-control execution architecture. Day 70 closes the human-action-pipeline with the moral-evaluation arc proper: Q.18 (the good and evil of human acts in general — 11 articles) and Q.19 (the goodness and malice of the interior act of the will — 10 articles). The contemporary mind-monist frame engages this as the *object-circumstance-end* triple of moral specification, the *erring-conscience* problem of action under perspective-limitation, and the central-theme-load-bearing keystone of Q.19 a.10: *the human will is bound to be conformed to the divine will as to that which is willed formally — the divine and universal good — but not as to that which is willed materially*.

## Frame

Q.18 establishes the *moral-specification* architecture: every voluntary act has its primary moral character from its *object* (a.2) — the kind of act it is. The medieval *evil is privation* (a.1) is the metaphysical anchor: a morally evil act is one that lacks the goodness proper to it. *Circumstances* (a.3) sometimes specify the act's species (stealing the chalice from the cathedral *is* sacrilege, a.10) but more often only aggravate or diminish (the magnitude of theft, a.11). The *end* (a.4) is the proper object of the interior-act-of-will (a.6) — when willed as the reason for the means, the end specifies the interior act.

Q.18 a.8-9: no individual deliberate action is morally indifferent. Even Aquinas's example of *picking up a piece of straw*, considered abstractly indifferent in species, becomes morally specified when actually willed — by intention, circumstance, or relation to the rule of right reason.

Q.19 articulates the *interior-act-of-will* register: the will's goodness depends on the object (a.1) and not on circumstances as such (a.2). The will's goodness depends on *reason* (a.3) — the will that *persists in its desires in opposition to reason* is unruly and so not good (Hilary). The will's goodness depends on the *eternal law* (a.4) — *human reason is the rule of the human will, and the eternal law is the rule of human reason*. Q.19 a.5: the will is evil when at variance with erring reason — *every will at variance with reason, whether right or erring, is always evil*. Q.19 a.6: the will abiding by erring reason is good only if the ignorance is *antecedent* (excusable, through-no-fault); willed or culpable ignorance does not excuse. Q.19 a.7: ends added *after* the act do not retroactively determine its goodness. Q.19 a.8: the degree of goodness/malice is not equivalent to the degree of intention's goodness/malice — bad intention can spoil a good act, but cannot make an intrinsically evil act good. Q.19 a.9: the will's goodness depends on conformity to the divine will. Q.19 a.10 — the keystone — *in order that a man will some particular good with a right will, he must will that particular good materially and the divine and universal good formally; therefore the human will is bound to be conformed to the divine will as to that which is willed formally — the divine and universal good — but not as to that which is willed materially*.

## Problem

The problem (P): the alter, under perspective-limitation, must evaluate its acts and act under perspective-limited apprehension. What is the *moral-specification* architecture, what is the structure of action under *erring conscience*, and what is the relation between the universal-good (which the alter is constitutively directed toward) and the particular-goods (which the alter pursues under its particular perspective)?

## Resource

**Stump on willed evil and erring conscience.** Q.19 a.5-6 articulates a structure that Stump's *Wandering in Darkness* makes vivid: *the conscience binds, even when erring*. The alter must act on its best apprehension. But *willed ignorance* (consequent ignorance, in Aquinas's earlier classification at Q.6) does not excuse. The structure preserves: the alter's responsibility is to *its current apprehension*, but also to the *cultivation of its apprehension* over time. Stump's second-personal-knowing intervention is the structural mode through which erring apprehension can be corrected — by an outside agent who loves the alter and presents the diagnostic.

**Friston on prior-alignment.** Q.19 a.9-10's *conformity to the divine will as formal universal good* maps onto active inference's *prior-alignment with the substrate-priors*. The alter's terminal-prior (formal) must be aligned with the substrate-source's integrative dynamics; the *material* particulars are the alter's perspective-specific instantiation of that formal alignment. The Dominican-fasting example (Q.19 a.10) is the clean illustration: *fast for God's glory* is the material; *do God's will* is the formal; obedience to the religious-superior is the formal-alignment when the material is contingently displaced. This is structurally identical to active inference's distinction between terminal-prior alignment (which the agent must hold) and instrumental-prior optimization (which is contingent on circumstance).

**Levin on form-as-bioelectric-pattern and privation as deficient morphogenesis.** Q.18 a.1's *evil is privation* metaphysical commitment preserves under Levin's frame: a morally-deficient act is structurally analogous to a developmentally-deficient morphogenetic pattern — what *should be there* given the formal target is not there. Form is real and telic; privation is the absence of the formal completion the act *should* have.

**McGilchrist on hemispheric moral judgment.** Right-hemispheric reception of the moral-gestalt (the kind-of-act and its relational character); left-hemispheric analysis of circumstance and end. The medieval object-circumstance-end triple maps onto the bi-hemispheric integration of moral judgment.

**Fredrickson on positivity-resonance and relational-as-formal-good.** Empirical anchor: the *formal-good* of conformity-to-divine-will, in its relational character, is the substrate-source's *loving-unity-with-the-alter*. Positivity-resonance research provides empirical traction at the relational level.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.18: moral specification operates through (1) the act's *object* (Levin: form of the act), (2) *circumstances* (sometimes specifying the form, more often modulating its magnitude), and (3) *end* (the alter's terminal-prior at this act). No deliberate act is indifferent because every deliberate act either aligns with or fails to align with the alter's terminal-prior structure, and the terminal-prior is constitutively directed toward the universal good. Privation-theory preserves: morally-deficient acts are *form-deficient* in the way Levin's morphogenetic-deficient patterns are form-deficient.

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.19: the will's goodness has its formal locus in *conformity-to-substrate-source priors* (a.9-10). The alter must will *materially* what is in fact good in its particular circumstance, and *formally* what is the universal good — the substrate-source's integrative dynamics. Erring conscience binds: the alter must act on its current apprehension; willed ignorance is itself an act and is culpable. The alter is responsible to its current apprehension *and* to the cultivation of its apprehension under second-person care.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the object-circumstance-end specification structure; the privation-theory of evil; the binding-but-correctable character of conscience; the formal/material conformity-to-divine-will distinction.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas's frame is hylomorphic-Aristotelian-virtue-theoretic, the contemporary frame uses active-inference-prior-alignment, morphogenetic-form, second-personal-knowing, hemispheric judgment, and positivity-resonance.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Friston's prior-alignment is mature formalism; Stump's erring-conscience and willed-ignorance arguments are theological-philosophical with substantial book-length backing; Levin's form-as-real and privation-as-deficient-morphogenesis is empirically grounded; the synthesis-claim that the medieval *conformity-to-divine-will* is the contemporary mind-monist *prior-alignment-with-substrate-source* is speculative-philosophical-theological.

## The central-theme thread

Today's day is *the* central-theme-load-bearing day in this stretch of synthesis. Q.19 a.10 articulates with full force the formal/material distinction at the volitional level. *Materially*, the alter wills *this particular good in these particular circumstances under this particular perspective*. *Formally*, the alter must will *the universal good* — and this is the central-theme keystone.

The central theme states: conscious agents are individuated by the limitations of their perspective; learning to obtain the goal of *loving unity* from each perspective is a computational task, limited by the resources we perceive — each of us from our own perspective. Q.19 a.10 articulates this with theological precision: each alter, under its perspective-limitation, must will materially what is good in its particular case (which is perspective-specific), but formally must will the universal good (which is the same for all). This is the *individuation-by-perspective-limitation-with-formal-unity* structure that the central theme names.

*Salvation in fear and trembling* under perspective-limitation is, structurally, the alter's daily-task work of (1) cultivating its apprehension to better perceive the material good in its particular case, (2) maintaining its formal alignment with the universal good even when its material apprehension is partial or erring, (3) receiving second-person care that surfaces the diagnostic of willed-ignorance and erring-conscience, and (4) holding the formal-unity of all alters' direction-toward-the-universal-good even when material disagreement persists.

The Dominican-fasting example is doing keystone work today: the alter who *would* fast for God's glory must *not fast* if the religious-superior commands it, *for God's glory* — the formal alignment is preserved precisely by the material reversal. Wright on covenanted-community as the locus of this formal-unity-through-material-obedience: the community is where formal alignment is actually instantiated even when individual material judgments diverge. Rohr on contemplative-practice as the alter's deliberate alignment with the formal-universal even when the material-particular is uncertain. Stump on tradition-bound rationality (MacIntyre) as the inherited resource through which the formal/material distinction can actually be operationalized in real moral judgment.

The erring-conscience structure (Q.19 a.5-6) is the perspective-limitation made operational. The alter cannot, on its own, fully escape erring; the alter must act on its current apprehension; the substrate-network's pedagogical care is the alter's resource for cultivating apprehension over time. The alter's responsibility is *both* to act on its current apprehension *and* to receive correction from those whose perspective is wider. The alter is *not* responsible for what it cannot apprehend through no fault of its own; the alter *is* responsible for the cultivation-of-apprehension and for not closing-against the diagnostic.

The synthesis-in-progress that this entire project is exploring receives its sharpest formulation today: each conscious agent is individuated by the limitations of its perspective; each must will the universal good *formally* even as its material judgment is perspective-specific; the work of *salvation in fear and trembling* is the alter's daily-task computation of formal alignment + material best-apprehension under second-person care + tradition-bound resources + the substrate-source's grace-as-enabling-pattern-communication. The loving unity is the formal target; the perspective-limited journey toward it is the material content of the alter's life.
