# Day 80 — Sorrowful Remedies (Contemporary)

## Frame

Q.38–39 close the affective tract of the Prima Secundae with a remarkable two-part movement. Q.38 catalogues the *remedies* of sorrow: pleasure of any kind, tears, sympathy of friends, contemplation of truth, sleep and baths. Q.39 then asks whether sorrow itself is evil, virtuous, or useful — and at a.4 makes the surprising claim that *the greatest evil is not pain or sorrow but being too evil to sorrow*. The contemporary synthesis recognizes the second part as the more radical: under perspective-limitation, the agent who has collapsed its model so as not to register present evil is in worse condition than the one whose sorrow is acute. The remedies of Q.38 are then read as the *concrete computational resources* by which a limited agent re-opens its model to receive evidence and integration.

## Problem

The shared problem is *how a limited agent recovers from unintegrated sorrow without dissolving the perspective that makes it an agent*. Aquinas catalogs five remedies. Each is a real and ordinary practice — drink something pleasant, weep, sit with a friend, contemplate, sleep, take a warm bath. None requires special grace; all are available across cultures and conditions. The puzzle Aquinas does not fully articulate but the contemporary frame can: *why* does each work, and *what* do they have in common that lets us call them remedies of the same condition?

The contemporary frame supplies the unifying answer: each remedy re-opens the agent's generative model — either by *introducing* new evidence (a friend's perspective, contemplated truth) or by *resetting* the precision-weighting on existing prior models (sleep, somatic comfort, externalization through tears). The remedies share a structural property: they all *re-admit* what the perspective-limited agent had narrowed away.

## Resource

**Fredrickson's program** is the empirical anchor for nearly every remedy on Aquinas's list.

- *Pleasure as remedy* (Q.38 a.1) — broaden-and-build: positive affect broadens attention and rebuilds psychological and physiological resources (PRS-01). Even minor pleasures — warmth, food, music — produce vagal-tone shifts that move the agent out of sympathetic dominance.
- *Tears as remedy* (Q.38 a.2) — emotional disclosure literature (Pennebaker, Frijda) shows that *expressing* affect, including in writing or speech, reduces its physiological load and improves health outcomes over weeks.
- *Sympathy of friends* (Q.38 a.3) — PRS-08 (high-quality listening, sensory connection, perceived safety) and PRS-09/10 (weak-tie connection produces intellectual humility, prosocial belief, civic efficacy). Aquinas's "friends bear the burden with us" is the empirical co-regulation finding: synchronized vagal tone in close emotional contact (Fredrickson Love 2.0).
- *Sleep and baths* (Q.38 a.5) — parasympathetic restoration, HPA-axis return to baseline, glymphatic clearance of stress metabolites. Each is mechanistically substantive.

**Friston's active inference** (Friston PRS-06 multi-agent coherence) frames *contemplation of truth* (Q.38 a.4) as the most powerful remedy because contemplation expands the model itself. Sorrow at a present evil is computed against a model of preferred states; contemplating divine truth (Aquinas's frame), the cosmic-Christ pattern (Rohr), or the resurrection-oriented future (Wright) widens the model so the present evil is no longer the model's terminus. The expected-free-energy calculation changes: what was unactionable in the smaller model becomes integrable in the larger one. This is the formal home of "in the midst of tribulations men rejoice in the contemplation of divine things and future happiness."

**Levin's bioelectric framework** lets the synthesis name what sleep and baths are doing at the somatic-collective scale. The body is itself a community of cells whose coherence is maintained bioelectrically; sustained sorrow disrupts this coherence (Day 79's PNI literature); restorative physical practice (sleep, warmth, vagal stimulation) restores the bioelectric field at the tissue scale, which is the substrate of any further psychological recovery.

**McGilchrist** explains *why* contemplation works as Aquinas says — and why it is the most powerful remedy. Contemplative practice cultivates right-hemispheric receptive attention: world-as-gift rather than world-as-objects-to-manipulate. The narrowed left-hemispheric model under which sorrow has become a cage is interrupted by right-hemispheric openness to the larger context. The Carthusian remark in Aquinas's text — "frostbitten, hungry, tired, alone, and happy" — is precisely the right-hemispheric receptivity that holds bodily privation within a larger unchanging frame.

**Kastrup's analytic idealism** lets the synthesis articulate what Q.39 a.4 means metaphysically. The agent whose model has collapsed to *not register* present evil — the desensitized — is the alter whose dissociative boundary has hardened beyond access to the larger field. The agent in acute sorrow, however painful, is *still in contact* with mind-at-large through the registration of loss. Q.39 a.4's "greatest evil is to be evil" then receives an articulation Aquinas could not have given: the greatest evil is the alter so dissociated from its substrate that it cannot even register the privation. The integrated-sorrow agent has a route home; the desensitized agent does not.

**Wright and Rohr** complete the theological articulation. Wright's lament tradition (Psalms, Lamentations, Romans 8 "the whole creation groans") establishes scripturally that the path to resurrection-hope runs *through* lament — not around it. Rohr's *Falling Upward* makes the same claim in the spiritual-developmental key: the second half of life requires integration of loss into a larger meaning, and the contemplative-non-dual mode (the universal-Christ pattern) is the cultivated stable foundation that lets this integration happen without collapse.

**Stump's *Wandering in Darkness*** belongs here as the Thomistic interlocutor: her contention that suffering is integrable into second-person love of God (and of other persons) coheres with everything above, but does not yet make the move into mind-monism. The synthesis cites her as the most rigorous contemporary Thomist working at this problem, and reads her as compatible with — though not yet making — the deeper monist reframe.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

**(a) Shared with Aquinas.** Each of Aquinas's five remedies is preserved as a real, ordinary, effective resource. The unity Aquinas names but does not fully explicate — that pleasure, tears, friends, contemplation, and sleep all remedy *the same* condition — is preserved and given its mechanism. Q.39 a.4's startling claim that the greatest evil is desensitization to evil is preserved and amplified.

**(b) Where it reframes.** Aquinas's "contemplation of divine things" is, in the medieval Thomistic register, the soul's union with a numerically-distinct God. Under conscious-realist monism the same contemplative practice is the alter's re-opening of its dissociative boundary to mind-at-large. This is not Aquinas's exact metaphysic. Wright's *en Christō* (being-in-Christ as participation, not external union) and Rohr's universal Christ (cosmic pattern in which creation participates) are theological articulations that *do* reframe the medieval external-object reading without abandoning Christian orthodoxy. The contemporary synthesis aligns with these. Q.39 a.4's "greatest evil is to be evil" gets a precise mechanism: the dissociation that has hardened beyond access to substrate is the *most* perspective-limited state, and thus the *least* able to compute its way toward loving unity.

**(c) Where evidence is firm vs. speculative.** *Firm*: each remedy's mechanism (vagal stimulation, emotional disclosure, social co-regulation, contemplative attention, parasympathetic restoration) is empirically grounded. *Firm*: PRS-08/09/10/11 on listening, weak-tie connection, inflammation-gating. *Suggestive but mechanistically robust*: active-inference framing of contemplation as model-widening. *Theological, well-attested*: Wright's lament-to-resurrection tradition; Rohr's contemplative integration. *Speculative metaphysical*: the Kastrup-style reading of Q.39 a.4 as dissociative hardening.

## The central-theme thread

This is the day where the central theme becomes clinical. *Conscious agents are individuated by the limitations of their perspective. Loving unity is the computational task — and the agent works it out in fear and trembling, aware of its limitations.*

Q.38's five remedies are precisely the *cooperative computational resources* by which a limited agent extends its perspective: pleasure restores the body's capacity to receive; tears externalize what the inward model holds rigidly; the sympathy of friends imports another's perspective (PRS-08 listening; PRS-09/10 weak-tie humility); contemplation of truth holds the present sorrow within an unchanging larger frame (Rohr, McGilchrist, Friston); sleep and baths return the somatic substrate to coherence (Fredrickson PRS-11 unwinding; Levin bioelectric restoration).

Q.39 a.4's "greatest evil is to be too evil to sorrow" is the central theme's negative pole. The agent who has *collapsed* its perspective so as not to register present evil has shut off the computational route home. Aquinas's claim — that being-evil is worse than feeling-pain — is, in our frame, the claim that *narrowing* the perspective is worse than *suffering* within it. The grief that contains delight (Day 77), the inward pain that exceeds outward (Day 78), the somatic cost of unintegrated sorrow (Day 79) — all of these remain on the road home. The desensitized agent has stepped off it. The remedies of Q.38 are not luxuries; they are the *means* by which an agent under the central-theme conditions actually does the work of salvation, understood in Wright's covenantal sense as inclusion in the people united with God.

## Bridges

- **Fredrickson PRS-01, PRS-03, PRS-08, PRS-09, PRS-10, PRS-11** — empirical mechanism for nearly every remedy; listening, weak-tie connection, vagal restoration, inflammation gating; *empirical, high confidence*.
- **Friston PRS-06 / collective active inference** — contemplation as model-widening; remedies as introducing new evidence or resetting precision-weighting; *mathematical, empirically supported*.
- **Levin bioelectric coherence** — somatic remedies (sleep, baths) as restoration of cellular/tissue-scale coherence; *empirical*.
- **McGilchrist right-hemispheric receptivity** — contemplative practice as cultivation of receptive mode; "frostbitten and happy"; *neurologically grounded, contested*.
- **Kastrup analytic idealism** — Q.39 a.4 as dissociative hardening; the most-evil agent is the most-dissociated alter; *speculative metaphysical, internally coherent*.
- **Wright lament-tradition + Rohr *Falling Upward* / *Universal Christ*** — theological articulation of integrated-sorrow path; resurrection-hope as model-widening prior; *theological, well-attested*.
- **Stump, *Wandering in Darkness*** — Thomistic interlocutor; suffering integrable through second-person love; *philosophical/theological, rigorous within Thomism*.
- **CROSS-005 (master)** — love as ontological category; the same field that makes the remedies effective makes the central-theme telos possible.

## Where this leaves us

Day 81 turns to Q.40 — hope and despair. We have moved through delight (76), mixed valence (77), sorrow and pain (78), the effects of unintegrated sorrow (79), and now the remedies and the bracing claim that desensitization is worse than suffering (80). The next region is the irascible passions — passions of pursuit and avoidance under difficulty. *Hope* is where the future-oriented appetite re-engages after sorrow's lessons. The open thread we carry into 81: hope is now empirically expressible as *expected free energy with a non-trivial pragmatic-value gradient* — a generative model in which the preferred state remains reachable. The remedies of Day 80 are precisely the resources by which an agent rebuilds that gradient.
