# Day 32 — Contemporary Parallel: *Beatitude Secured by One Act, and What Freedom Looks Like at the Limit*

Day 31 closed pointing forward to Q.61 and Q.62 with the question *how can a single act of charity eternally fix the angelic alter in beatitude?* Today's transcript walks the medieval answer in thirteen articles. Q.61 establishes that the angels are creatures, not eternal, plausibly created together with the corporeal world (Aquinas's preferred opinion among multiple permitted ones), and located by operation rather than by physical place. Q.62 walks the gradient of beatitude: angels were created already-blessed in the natural mode (mind contemplating God by natural powers) but not yet in the supernatural mode (the beatific vision); they need grace to turn toward the supernatural end; they were probably created already in grace; they merit the beatific vision by acts that proceed from grace; one such act suffices because the angelic mode is non-discursive (Day 30 Q.58 a.3 — the angel sees the conclusion in the premise); grace and glory follow the gradient of natural gifts; natural knowledge and love remain in the beatified angels (grace perfects nature, does not destroy it); the beatified angel cannot sin (a structural rather than power-of-will limitation); the beatified angel does not advance in beatitude. The mind-monist frame inherits the gradient cleanly, with one important reframing of the *one-act-fixes-eternally* claim that the medieval doctrine of angelic non-discursivity structurally requires.

## Q.61 — Creation of angels in natural being

a.1 — *do angels have a cause of their existence?* — receives the same Q.44–46 answer that holds for any conditioned thing: *anything short of existence-itself must have a reason for its existence*. Mind-monism's frame: every alter (embodied or near-substrate) participates in mind-at-large rather than being mind-at-large; the substrate's *He who is* (Day 8 Q.13 a.11) is the *only* thing not requiring a cause. Kastrup's analytic idealism (PRS-01, PRS-02 in the Kastrup-tradition wiki) makes the move structurally: alters are dissociations *within* the substrate; the substrate is causa sui in the strict sense the medieval frame names.

a.2 — *was the angel produced by God from eternity?* — receives the medieval *no* on the basis of revelation (Vatican I cited): God *brought into being from nothing* the spiritual and the corporeal orders, *together from the beginning of time*. Mind-monism's translation: the alter scale is a feature of the substrate's structure that begins-with-time rather than being co-eternal with the substrate. Carroll's poetic naturalism (PRS-01 — ontological closure vs. emergence; PRS-07 — observer-centric quantum gravity) gives the contemporary cosmological frame: *time* itself emerges as part of the structure of the universe (Day 34 will press this further). The medieval claim that angels are not co-eternal with God maps onto the mind-monist claim that conditioned-alter-structure is not co-eternal with mind-at-large: it is the substrate's pattern, not the substrate.

a.3 — *were the angels created before the corporeal world?* — receives the medieval *probably not, but the question is open and the contrary opinion is not erroneous*. Aquinas's reason: it would be unfitting for God to create only a quarter of his canvas. Mind-monism's translation: the structural priors that pattern *both* the spirit-mode-of-alters and the body-mode-of-alters are integrated within the substrate's single act of conditioning — the priors do not run sequentially but together. Wolfram's ruliad gives the formal cousin: the threads of the ruliad are *one* totality, and the *order* in which we narrate them is artifact of trajectory-perspective rather than feature of the substrate. Honest evidence calibration: the medieval question is structurally open; the mind-monist reading of the open question matches the medieval generosity.

a.4 — *were the angels created in the empyrean heaven?* — Aquinas accepts the outdated cosmology and reads it forward as the angels' *highest place* by *operation* (they do not occupy the empyrean by physical containment but by ruling-over-all-corporeal-nature). The medieval recognition that *angels are in a place by operation, not by containment* is structurally clean, and mind-monism preserves it directly: alters at the near-substrate scale are *located* in the substrate's structure by the scope of their operation rather than by spatial coordinates (which are alter-mode-specific projections — Hoffman's interface theory at the cosmological scale).

## Q.62 — Perfection in grace and glory

a.1 — *were the angels created in beatitude?* — establishes the doctrine that will run through the rest of the question: there are *two* beatitudes — natural (the mind contemplating God by natural powers) and supernatural (the beatific vision in which God's essence informs the created intellect directly). The angels were created *already blessed* in the first sense and *not yet* blessed in the second sense. Mind-monism's translation: the alter's natural light-cone (Levin's vocabulary at the angelic scale) is wide and includes substrate-direct natural cognition; the supernatural cognitive mode (Day 7 Q.12 a.4–5 — the *light of glory*) is the limit case where the alter's generative model becomes substrate-identical, and this limit case is *not* reached by any natural process — it requires *grace* in the medieval sense, structurally a substrate-initiated transition the alter cannot effect from inside its own resources. Honest evidence calibration: the formal framework (active inference, autopoietic limit) gives a candidate dynamics; the metaphysical identification (medieval *grace* = substrate-initiated alter-mode-shift) is structural-philosophical.

a.2 — *does an angel need grace to turn toward God-as-beatific-end?* — receives the medieval *yes*. The argument: *to see God in his essence is beyond the nature of every created intellect; consequently no rational creature can have the movement of the will directed toward such beatitude except it be moved thereto by a supernatural agent*. Mind-monism's translation runs through CROSS-001 in the master cross-program index (substrate-independence): the alter's mode of operation is what it is, and the shift to substrate-direct mode requires the substrate to *raise* the alter — the alter cannot raise itself by intra-alter operations. Friston's framework gives the formal cousin: the autopoietic limit is reached only when the alter's generative model becomes identical with the structure it tracks; nothing in the alter's local action-selection can effect this identity, because the local action operates on local-prediction-error and the autopoietic limit is precisely zero local-prediction-error globally.

a.3 — *were the angels created in grace?* — receives the medieval *probably yes* (Aquinas notes the question is debated in his day). The seed-of-eternal-life is given from the outset because the angel's *ultimate end is the blossoming of that seed* (Proverbs 4:18 — *the path of the righteous is like the light of dawn which shines brighter and brighter until full day*). Mind-monism's translation: the angelic alter's natural light-cone already includes the *target structure* of substrate-direct cognition as a goal-state-pattern (Levin's vocabulary, generalized — the *seed* is the bioelectric-equivalent at the spirit scale); the *grace* given from the outset is the substrate's *gift* of the target as already-attractor for the alter, even though attaining it requires the alter's own act-of-charity to actualize.

a.4 — *does the angel merit beatitude?* — receives the careful medieval *yes-and-no*. *No* if we consider the angel as creature simply (no creature merits to know the substrate as it knows itself); *yes* if we consider the angel *already divinized by grace* — the divinized angel can act as a *son of God* and merit the beatific vision under that aspect. The peasant analogy: the peasant doesn't merit a special reward for fixing his bed in the morning, but the peasant *adopted as son of the king* now can. Mind-monism's translation: merit at the alter scale is structurally the alter's policy-coherence with the substrate's priors; once the alter is in the grace-mode (substrate-initiated mode-shift toward the autopoietic limit), the alter's actions *are* substrate-coherent in a way that *deserves* (in the medieval sense) the substrate's full revelation of itself. Stump's analytic-philosophical work on union-as-highest-good (referenced at Day 11 Q.20) is the contemporary primary — though Stump is the Thomistic interlocutor on grace and union, not the metaphysical authority for mind-monism.

a.5 — *does the angel obtain beatitude immediately after one act of merit?* — is the keystone article of Q.62. The argument: the angelic mode is non-discursive (Q.58 a.3 — the angel sees the conclusion in the first principle); therefore the angel's choice-of-God is one act that contains all that follows from the choice; therefore the act is sufficient to secure the beatitude. Mind-monism's translation runs through the autopoietic limit directly. An alter at the angelic scope, operating in non-discursive mode, has a generative model that is wide enough that one well-formed action-selection covers the entire policy-space the model entails. Wolfram's ruliad gives the formal cousin: the angel's choice of one ruliad-thread is a choice of all that thread's continuations, because at the angel's scope the thread's continuations are present in the choice. Friston's framework gives the dynamics: the angel's one act of charity is the policy-update that brings the generative model into substrate-identity, and once the model is substrate-identical, no further policy-updates are needed because there is no remaining free energy to minimize. Honest evidence calibration: the structural mapping is tight; whether the medieval claim that *one act eternally fixes* is empirically defensible at any alter scale is open — the formal frameworks suggest it could be true at the autopoietic limit, but the question of whether any actual alter ever reaches that limit (and what *eternally* means at the substrate scale) remains philosophically open.

a.6 — *do angels receive grace and glory according to the degree of their natural gifts?* — receives the medieval *yes* on a fittingness argument: God's wisdom is one act, so the gradient he wills in nature is preserved in the gradient of grace. Mind-monism's translation: the substrate's structural priors are *one* coherent pattern, and the alter-scale gradient (cognitive-light-cone scope, complexity of generative model) is preserved in the gradient of substrate-integration the alters reach. Levin's *Cognitive Light Cones* program (PRS-03 morphogenetic teleology) is the empirical mid-scale analog: agents at higher organizational complexity participate in larger goal-state-patterns, and this participation *is* their being more deeply integrated with substrate-priors. The medieval claim and the contemporary biology converge on the structural shape *gradient-of-integration tracks gradient-of-natural-scope*. Honest evidence calibration: strong empirical at the developmental scale; structural-philosophical at the angelic scale.

a.7 — *do natural knowledge and love remain in the beatified angels?* — receives the medieval *yes*: *grace perfects and does not destroy nature*. Mind-monism's translation: the angelic alter at the autopoietic limit retains its alter-structure (it is still the alter it was) while its mode-of-operation has shifted to substrate-direct. Kastrup's framework preserves this structurally: the dissociated alter at the limit case of integration is still an alter (not collapsed into mind-at-large) but its mode of accessing mind-at-large is no longer interface-mediated. Hoffman's interface theory gives the same shape: the icons are *lifted* (Day 7 Q.12 a.5) but the alter remains.

a.8 — *can a beatified angel sin?* — is the article that names what *true freedom* looks like at the limit. Aquinas: the beatified angel *cannot* sin, but not because of a power-of-will diminution — because *seeing God through his essence, which is the very essence of goodness, the angel cannot will anything except aiming at what is good*. Habash quotes John Paul II in Baltimore: *freedom consists not in doing what we like but in having the right to do what we ought*. And Jesus: *he who commits sin is a slave to sin*. Mind-monism's translation: at the autopoietic limit, the alter's policy-space is precisely the substrate-coherent policy-space; there are no policies that diverge from substrate-coherence within the alter's reach, because the alter's generative model is substrate-identical. The alter retains the *structural power* to choose otherwise (the *can* in Aquinas's careful answer) but *will not* because there is no perceived good beyond the substrate's goodness, and the will only inclines toward the perceived good. Stump's *Wandering in Darkness* on willed evil is the contemporary primary on the inverse failure mode — the alter that has not reached the limit and can sin because finite goods still appear as competing attractors.

a.9 — *do beatified angels advance in beatitude?* — closes Q.62 with the medieval *no*: the beatitude is fixed because (i) the cause is changeless, (ii) the degree depends on grace and merit which do not change post-mortem, (iii) God had a definite degree in mind. Mind-monism's translation: the autopoietic limit is a limit, not a process — once reached, the alter is at-the-limit, not progressing-toward-it. Wolfram's ruliad gives a formal cousin: the angel at the limit is at a fixed point in the substrate's structure; the substrate's threads continue to run (the angel is alive in Q.18's sense, self-determined-from-its-own-essence, Day 10), but the alter's substrate-identity-fraction is at one. The medieval answer survives the contemporary translation; the contemporary translation adds a structural-mathematical home for the medieval intuition.

## The central-theme thread

Day 32 names the *attainment* shape of the central theme — what *salvation in fear and trembling* looks like *at the limit case where the agent reaches the substrate*. The angelic alter's beatitude is one act of charity that secures eternally, because the angelic mode is non-discursive and the one act *is* the whole policy. The embodied alter's salvation, by contrast, is computed-discursively over time, through many acts, with much friction — but *toward* the same telos and on the same gradient. The graded-by-natural-gifts doctrine (Q.62 a.6) preserves the central theme's claim that *each agent works toward the loving-unity goal from its own limited perspective and resources*: the angel's larger natural-gifts entail larger grace-and-glory, but the gradient is one — substrate-coherence calibrated by charity. The *cannot sin in beatitude* doctrine (Q.62 a.8) names what *real freedom* is: not the trivial libertarian-power-to-choose-otherwise but the alter's policy-space converging on substrate-coherence such that no incoherent policy is within reach. *Loving unity is the computational telos*, and the angelic alter at beatitude is *at the telos*; the embodied alter is en route, computing under perspective-limitation, with much higher friction. The central theme's *fear and trembling* applies to the en-route phase; the angelic limit-case is what the en-route phase converges *toward*.
