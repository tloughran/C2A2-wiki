# Day 62 — Contemporary Parallel: *The Negative Argument — What Happiness Is Not, and Why That Matters*

Day 61 stated the central-theme commitment that loving unity is the computational telos of conscious agents under perspective-limitation. Day 62 takes Aquinas through the famous *exclusion arguments* of Q.2 (those things in which happiness consists — 8 articles) and Q.3 (what happiness is — 8 articles). The contemporary mind-monist frame engages each ruling-out as a *diagnostic* of how the agent's perspective-limitation tends to mis-locate the universal good in particular candidate goods, and how the convergence to the right answer requires the right structure-of-ascent.

## Frame

Q.2 systematically rules out: wealth (a.1), honor (a.2), fame/glory (a.3), power (a.4), goods of the body (a.5), pleasure (a.6), goods of the soul understood as the soul's own state (a.7), any created good (a.8). Q.3 then asks what happiness *is*: it is not God himself as the object only (because happiness is the agent's act, not just the act's object), but the agent's *act of contemplation* of God by which the agent attains union (a.4 — the Vision-of-God answer).

The contemporary frame: each excluded candidate is something Fredrickson's empirical work, Stump's theological-philosophical work, Friston's active-inference framework, and Kastrup's mind-monism each in their own way also rule out as the *substantive content* of flourishing.

## Problem

The problem (P): the perspective-limited alter, working out its salvation, has access to many particular candidate goods and a constitutive directedness toward the universal good — but the alter's perception is filtered through interface and shaped by passion and habit, so it can mis-locate the universal good in particular candidates. What is the *structure* of correct location, and what is the *diagnostic* of mis-location?

## Resource

**Fredrickson on hedonic adaptation.** Empirically, the alter's affective state returns to baseline after acquired wealth, honor, pleasure, and even bodily goods. The *broaden-and-build* cycle, by contrast, accumulates: relational positivity-resonance does not adapt out; it builds resilience and capacity. The wealth/honor/fame/pleasure exclusions of Q.2 are *empirically* the candidates that hedonic adaptation reveals not to be the substantive content of flourishing.

**Stump on faith as second-personal knowledge of God.** The *goods of the soul* exclusion (Q.2 a.7) — happiness is not just the soul's own state — preserves under Stump's frame: the second-personal mode of knowing requires an *other* to be known second-personally; happiness as relational-union cannot be reduced to the alter's own state.

**Friston on terminal vs. instrumental goals.** The active-inference distinction between terminal goals (priors that ground policy-selection) and instrumental goals (sub-policies in service of terminal priors) maps onto Aquinas's *means-and-ends* analysis. Wealth, honor, fame, power, bodily goods, pleasure are all *instrumental* under correct prior-structure; only the universal good is *terminal*.

**Kastrup on mind as ground.** The *no created good is happiness* conclusion (Q.2 a.8) preserves under analytic idealism: the alter is mind-at-large under dissociation, and the alter's full flourishing is its alignment with mind-at-large's own integrative dynamics — which no individual created good can reproduce.

**Levin on integration as telos.** Multi-scale agentic systems flourish when they integrate well across scales. The *contemplation* answer (Q.3 a.4) — happiness as the act of contemplating God — preserves as the alter's most-integrated mode: contemplation is the integration-act *par excellence*.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.2: each excluded candidate is *real* (Carroll's poetic naturalism — wealth, honor, fame, power, bodily goods, pleasure, goods of soul are all real at their level), but *none of them constitutes happiness* because happiness is the alter's *integrative act of union with the substrate-source*, and integrative-act-of-union is structurally distinct from any particular candidate-good.

The exclusion arguments are *diagnostic*: each candidate fails for a different reason. Wealth fails because it is means, not end (Aquinas's dollar-as-means argument). Honor and fame fail because they are *in the honoring agent*, not in the alter. Power fails because power is means. Bodily goods fail because they are subordinate to soul. Pleasure fails because pleasure is the *consequence* of attaining the good, not the good itself (Q.2 a.6 — pleasure is the rest of the appetite in the good, not the good). Goods-of-the-soul-as-the-soul's-own-state fail because flourishing is *relational*, not solipsistic.

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.3: happiness is the alter's *integrative-act of union* with the substrate-source. Aquinas's specific answer — *beatific vision*, the contemplative possession of God — preserves under Kastrup as the alter's full-alignment with mind-at-large's integrative dynamics; under Stump as the second-personal-loving-knowing of the substrate-source as person; under Friston as the agent's full free-energy minimization under priors aligned with substrate-priors; under Fredrickson as the limit-case of positivity-resonance with the substrate-source; under Levin as the integration-act at the scale of the alter's full participation in the substrate-network.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: each exclusion preserves; the conclusion that happiness is contemplative union preserves.

What it *reframes*: Aquinas's frame is hylomorphic-Aristotelian on the soul's act of contemplation; the contemporary frame distributes the analysis across multiple methodological vantages, each of which independently arrives at *integrative-act-of-union with substrate-source* as the substantive content of flourishing.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Fredrickson's hedonic-adaptation evidence is strong; Stump's second-personal-knowing argument is theological-philosophical with substantial book-length backing; Friston's terminal/instrumental distinction is mature formalism; Levin's integration-as-telos is empirically grounded at biological scales; the synthesis-claim that all converge on contemplative union with mind-at-large is speculative-philosophical-theological.

## The central-theme thread

Today's day is the *negative-and-positive* statement of the central theme. Negatively: each particular candidate-good fails to be the substantive content of flourishing because flourishing is the alter's *integrative act* of becoming-aligned with the substrate-source's loving unity; particular candidates can serve as means but not as the end. Positively: the integrative act of contemplative union with the substrate-source *is* the loving unity that the central theme names as computational telos.

*Salvation in fear and trembling* under perspective-limitation includes the daily-task work of *recognizing the diagnostic of mis-location*. When the alter notices that a particular pursued good has produced the hedonic-adaptation pattern (Fredrickson) or has narrowed the alter's openness (McGilchrist) or has settled the alter's prior into a dispositional-fixity (Friston), the alter has diagnostic data that this candidate is *not* the universal good. The substrate-network's pedagogical care includes presenting these diagnostic patterns to the alter at tractable scales.

Wright's *Surprised by Hope* is the load-bearing scriptural primary on the *what is wrong with our contemporary candidates* register — Wright is unsparing about consumerism, prestige-capture, fame-seeking, and pleasure-as-end. Rohr's *Falling Upward* extends: the first half of life often pursues these candidates, and the *falling* into the second half is precisely the diagnostic experience of their failure that opens the alter to the contemplative mode.
