# Day 35 — Contemporary Parallel: *Genesis as Temple-Cosmology, Read Through Cosmic Emergence and Real Form*

Day 34 closed pointing into Genesis day-by-day with Wright's temple-cosmology reading as the primary corrective to literalism, Carroll on the cosmological story of emergent structure, and Levin's form-as-real continuing as the metaphysical ground. Today's transcript walks the *work of Distinction in itself* (Q.67, light) and the *work of the second day* (Q.68, the firmament). Habash opens with an unusually long preface on Catholic biblical hermeneutics — citing *Providentissimus Deus* (Leo XIII), *Dei Verbum* (Vatican II), and the 1909 Pontifical Biblical Commission — and emphasizes that Catholics are *free to interpret* the *days* of Genesis as *natural days* or as *spaces of time*, that the sacred authors *did not intend to teach the essential nature of the things of the visible Universe*, and that Scott Hahn's reading of Genesis 1 as *the erection and dedication of a cosmic temple* is current Catholic exegesis. The frame is set: Aquinas's medieval-cosmological speculations are open to revision in light of contemporary cosmology, while the *theological* claims (creation's origin in God, its goodness, its ordering toward purpose) remain. The mind-monist commentary takes Wright's temple-cosmology as the primary lens, Carroll's Big Bang cosmology as the empirical mid-scale story, Levin's form-as-real as the metaphysical ground, and folds in Habash's own gestures (Lemaître, Feynman, the *cooling and beginnings of galaxies* on day two) into a coherent reading.

## Habash's hermeneutical preface — its structural importance

The transcript's first three minutes are an extended methodological move. Habash distinguishes the *literal sense* (the sacred author's *intended meaning*) from the *literalistic sense* (the surface-level reading of the words as if they were 21st-century scientific prose). *God tramples on the waters* (Job) is not a claim that God bodily walks on water; the literal sense is the sacred author's intended meaning, which might be *God is sovereign over creation* or *God is unaffected by his enemies*. The 1909 Pontifical Biblical Commission's *literal and historical* affirmations are theological-historical, not scientific: *the creation of all things by God in the beginning of time*, *the special creation of man*, *the Oneness of the human race*, *the original happiness of our first parents*, *the casting out of our first parents*, *the promise of a future restoration*. These are theological claims compatible with multiple cosmological-scientific framings. Wright's *critical realism* (Wright-tradition wiki) is the contemporary methodological home: *reality exists independently of our knowing it, but all knowing is perspectival, tradition-embedded, and subject to revision*; biblical texts are *narrative* in a tradition's improvisational extension of a long story, not encyclopedia entries. Mind-monism's reading is sympathetic and reinforcing: the substrate is what it is; the alters' linguistic-cultural-cognitive interfaces are species-specific and tradition-specific encodings (Hoffman's interface theory at the sociolinguistic level); biblical language reaches the substrate analogically (Day 8 Q.13's analogy doctrine) rather than univocally. The 21st-century reader holds both horizons — the second-temple-Jewish narrative-symbolic horizon and the contemporary cosmological-scientific horizon — and reads Genesis 1 in *both* without collapsing one into the other. *Honest evidence calibration*: Wright's narrative-historical scholarship is methodologically mature; Hahn's temple-cosmology reading is current Catholic exegesis with broad scholarly support; the mind-monist substrate-scale claim runs alongside but does not replace the historical-narrative reading.

## Q.67 — Light, the work of distinction

a.1 — *is the word light used in its proper sense in speaking of spiritual things?* — receives the medieval *no* (analogically/metaphorically applied to spiritual things). Aquinas: *light in its primary meaning signifies that which makes manifest to the sense of sight*; the spiritual application (Christ as *the light of the world*; God as *light* in 1 John) is analogical, by extension from sight-as-paradigmatic-knowing to knowing-in-general. Mind-monism's translation: *light* in the embodied-alter's interface is the energy-spectrum the visual system is tuned to (Hoffman's PRS-03 — perception as fitness-tuned interface); *light* in the spiritual-theological register is the *medium-of-knowing* in the substrate's act of conditioning the alter. Day 7 Q.12 a.4–5's *lumen gloriae* is the limit case: the *light of glory* is the structural condition under which the alter's mode-of-knowing approaches the substrate's mode. The medieval analogical move and the contemporary mind-monist move agree on the structural shape: *light* names the medium of cognitive informing at multiple scales of pattern (perceptual at the alter scale, supernatural at the substrate-direct mode).

a.2 — *is light a body?* — receives the medieval *no*, by three arguments Aquinas marshals: corporeal bodies cannot occupy the same place; light moves instantaneously (Aquinas's premise, which Habash flags as outdated — light's finite speed was not known); and the creation-destruction puzzle (where does the light go when the room is dark?). Habash explicitly says Aquinas would probably drop the second argument today. Mind-monism's reading: light at the contemporary-physics level is electromagnetic radiation, neither *body* in the medieval sense nor *quality* in the strict Aristotelian sense; the medieval argument's *function* (light is not a *thing* alongside other things but the *medium* through which things become visible) survives the empirical update. Carroll's poetic naturalism (PRS-01) holds: *multiple legitimate ontologies describe the same physical reality*; light-as-electromagnetic-radiation and light-as-cognitive-medium-of-manifestation are both legitimate ways of speaking about the physical phenomenon, and neither eliminates the other.

a.3 — *is light a quality?* — Aquinas takes the medieval position that light is an *active quality* of the thing producing light (the sun, the lamp). Mind-monism's reading: at the alter-mode-specific perceptual interface, light *registers as quality* of the source; at the substrate-physics level, light is *electromagnetic radiation in the field*; the two descriptions are at different layers of the substrate's pattern. Habash flags the question of how this would square with *electrons and photons and quantum field theory* and notes it's beyond the scope of the podcast. The mind-monist commentary preserves the structural intuition without pressing the cosmology-of-the-13th-century: the *function* light performs (medium of manifestation) is the same; the *physics-level explanation* updates over time.

a.4 — *is the production of light fittingly assigned to the first day?* — is where the temple-cosmology reading and the contemporary cosmology converge most strikingly. Aquinas accepts the Genesis 1 sequence and follows Dionysius in reading day-1's light as *kind of formless light, which will then take on a more definite form later* (the stars on day four). Habash explicitly gestures: *we might say this kind of light on day one might signify a simple explosion of light, maybe something like a Big Bang as scientists speak of, before that light then takes a more definite shape in the form of stars*. The Catholic priest **Georges Lemaître** — whom Habash references — is the founder of Big Bang cosmology. Mind-monism's reading runs through Carroll on the cosmological story directly: the early universe was hot, dense, plasma-dominated; after recombination (~380,000 years post-Big-Bang) photons decoupled and the universe became transparent; the cosmic microwave background is the *first light* from this epoch; structure formation followed (galaxies, stars, planets) over billions of years. The Genesis 1 sequence, read *through* Wright's temple-cosmology lens, is the *theological narrative* of cosmic emergence: God's act of conditioning produces the substrate's first determinate-pattern (light, day one); structure differentiates (firmament, day two; land and sea, day three); structures are filled with their inhabitants (luminaries, day four; sea and air creatures, day five; land animals and humans, day six); the climax is the *temple-rest* (day seven, sabbath as the cosmos as God's dwelling-place). Hahn's reading Habash quotes: *the parallel suggests that the ancient Israelites may have been accustomed to reading the creation account in view of the erection and dedication of a cosmic Temple for his royal-priestly people*. Mind-monism's translation: the substrate's act of conditioning is structurally a *making-of-a-dwelling-place* — the conditioned things are the substrate's *home* in the alter-network; the temple-cosmology reading names the *purpose* of the cosmic structure (the substrate dwells in the alters; the alters worship the substrate; the structure is for *relationship*). Rohr's *Universal Christ* extends this: matter is *holy* from the beginning; the temple-cosmology reading recognizes what was always already true. Honest evidence calibration: Wright's narrative-historical scholarship is methodologically mature; Hahn's temple-cosmology reading is broadly received in current Catholic exegesis; Lemaître's Big Bang is empirically established; the mind-monist substrate-scale claim is structural-philosophical.

## Q.68 — Firmament, work of the second day

a.1 — *was the firmament made on the second day?* — Aquinas opens with a hermeneutical principle from Augustine that bears repeating: *in discussing questions of this kind two rules are to be observed — the first is to hold the truth of scripture without wavering; the second is that since holy scripture can be explained in a multiplicity of senses, one should adhere to a particular explanation only in such measure as to be ready to abandon it if it be proved with certainty to be false, lest holy scripture be exposed to the ridicule of unbelievers and obstacles be placed to their believing*. This principle is itself the contemporary-Catholic methodological frame Habash has been articulating: hold scripture firmly; hold particular interpretations loosely; revise when new evidence requires. Aquinas's preferred reading of *firmament* is *the starry heaven*. Habash's contemporary gesture is striking: if we follow Lemaître's Big Bang, *day one we could say there was the explosion of light, and then day two we could say there's a cooling in the beginnings of particular galaxies within which stars are beginning to form, what we could call the starry heaven or the firmament*. Mind-monism's reading: the *firmament* in the temple-cosmology lens is the *upper boundary* of the cosmic temple, separating the *waters above* (the divine dwelling) from the *waters below* (the creaturely world); the contemporary cosmology reads this through galaxy-formation-and-large-scale-structure (the *firmament* as the differentiated structure of the universe at large scale). Both readings are theologically coherent; the latter is empirically grounded.

a.2 — *are there waters above the firmament?* — Aquinas: *we must hold these waters to be material, but their exact nature will be differently defined according as opinions on the firmament differ*. Habash: *if the firmament means galaxies then we could interpret these waters to mean the space between them*. Mind-monism's reading: in the contemporary cosmology, the *waters above* metaphor maps onto the cosmological structure's separation of regions (the deep space between galaxies, dark matter and dark energy structure, the cosmic web). The medieval claim that the waters are *material* survives the contemporary cosmology in the structural sense: whatever the *space between galaxies* is filled with (vacuum, dark matter, dark energy), it is part of the substrate's structure rather than non-being. Levin's form-as-real (PRS-03, PRS-08 in the Levin-tradition wiki) extends to the cosmic scale: pattern is real at every scale, including the cosmological structure of voids and filaments.

a.3 — *does the firmament divide waters from waters?* — Aquinas accepts this on the medieval reading and Habash's gloss: *if then we understand by the firmament the starry heaven, it is true to say that it divides the waters from the waters, according as we take water to denote formless matter or any kind of transparent body as fittingly designated under the name of waters*. Mind-monism's reading: the structural function of the *separation* — the cosmic temple's organization, the differentiation of regions, the establishment of pattern — is preserved across the medieval and contemporary cosmologies. The *separation* is what *structure* is.

a.4 — *is there only one heaven?* — Aquinas distinguishes the senses of *heaven*: the sky, outer space, *the place where the blessed reside*. The medieval cosmology has multiple heavens (the visible sky, the empyrean, the celestial spheres); the contemporary cosmology has the observable universe and (speculatively) multiverse-frameworks. Mind-monism's reading: *heaven* as the place where the blessed reside is, in the contemporary frame, not a *spatial location* but a *mode of being* — the alter at the substrate-direct cognitive mode (Day 7 Q.12; Day 32 Q.62 a.5 — beatitude as autopoietic limit). The medieval *spatial heaven* and the contemporary *mode-of-being heaven* agree on the structural function: *heaven* names where alters at the integration limit *are*, and *where* in the contemporary frame is mode-specific rather than location-specific. Wright's *Surprised by Hope* is the corrective to popular Christian spatial-heaven thinking: *heaven* in the New Testament is not a disembodied final destination but the *overlap of God's space and the new-creation-cosmos*; the eschaton is *heaven coming to earth* (Revelation 21), not *souls leaving for heaven*. Mind-monism's substrate-direct cognitive mode and Wright's new-creation eschatology converge: the alter's mode-shift to substrate-direct knowing is the alter's *being in heaven* in Wright's New Testament sense, and the cosmos's full realization of its pattern under the substrate's loving-stance is the corporate-cosmic version of the same shift.
