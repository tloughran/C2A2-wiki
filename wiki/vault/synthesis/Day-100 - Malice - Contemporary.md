# Day 100 — Malice (Contemporary)

## Frame

Day 100. We are roughly a third of the way through the year. The episode closes the *causes of sin* treatise with its weightiest case — *malice* — and then turns to the *external causes* (God himself, in particular: can God be the cause of sin, of the act of sin, of spiritual blindness?). Two questions, each of them taking the analysis to its limit.

The medieval frame asks: *what is going on in an agent who chooses an evil known as evil, for the sake of some other good?* And then: *how does the metaphysics work — the act of sin must have some cause; can God, the first cause of all being, be cause of sin? If not, how does the disorder enter being at all?* The contemporary frame asks the same two questions in mind-monism vocabulary. The first is harder than ignorance and passion; the second is the hardest theological problem of the *Summa* this far.

## Problem

**P1**: malice as a distinct policy-type. Aquinas distinguishes three modes of sin: through ignorance, through passion, through malice. Yesterday we had the first two. Malice is the case where neither model-error nor precision-capture explains the failure: the agent's *policy itself* selects the disordered outcome, knowingly, *for the sake of* a non-constitutive good. This is the morally weightiest case because the agent's high-level apparatus is intact and the disordered outcome is chosen anyway. The contemporary question: what is the architecture-level signature of *malice as policy*?

**P2**: positive cause of a privation, divine version. Q.79 a.2 — *the act of sin is from God, the defect in the act is not*. This is one of the highest-altitude metaphysical articles in this part of the *Summa*. The act has positive being; God is the first cause of all being; therefore God is the cause of *the act qua act*. The *defect* in the act is privation; privation traces to the will's failure to direct; therefore God is *not* the cause of *the sin*. The structure is the same as Day 98's regulator-failure analysis, raised to its theological maximum. The contemporary problem: how does a mind-monism frame describe an act whose positive being traces to the integrating field while its disorder traces to the agent's voluntary decoupling from that field?

**P3**: hardness of heart and the cosmic ordering of evil. Q.79 a.3-4: God can be cause of *just withdrawal of grace* (a.3); every evil God does or permits is *ordered to some good* (a.4), though not always to the good of the one in whom the evil is. The cosmic-scope move — *the sin of tyrants is directed to the good of the martyrs, the punishment of the lost to the glory of his justice*. The contemporary problem: under mind-monism, what is the structure of cosmic-scope ordering of local evil?

## Resource

**Active inference on policy-level mis-alignment** (Friston-tradition wiki, PRS-04 + PRS-06; hierarchical predictive architectures). The most useful contemporary frame distinguishes three failure modes of a hierarchical agent:
- *Model error* — the generative model is wrong (corresponds to Aquinas's *ignorance*).
- *Precision error* — the model is right but the precision-weighting is locally corrupted (corresponds to *passion*).
- *Policy mis-alignment* — the model is right, precision-weighting is intact, but the *policy* selects the disordered outcome because it dominates on some other dimension (corresponds to *malice*).
The third is by far the hardest to model and *correspondingly* the most morally weighty. **Confidence: medium-high** on the architectural distinction; the identification with Aquinas's trichotomy is interpretive but quite clean.

**Levin's form-as-real account** (Levin-tradition wiki on bioelectric form and agency-at-scale). For Q.79 a.2's act-from-God / defect-from-creature structure, Levin's account gives a useful contemporary handle: *form* is a real causal pattern; an act *has form* (it is a determinate causally efficacious pattern); the *disorder in the act* is the local breakdown of that form, which has its own causal etiology in the regulator. The act-qua-act traces to whatever supports form-generation (the integrating field of the conscious-agent network in mind-monism, *being-itself* in Aquinas's frame); the disorder traces to the regulator's failure (the agent's failure to direct). **Confidence: medium** on the structural analogy.

**Kastrup on analytic idealism and divine permission** (Kastrup-tradition wiki). For Q.79 a.3's *just withdrawal of grace*: under analytic idealism, the agent is a dissociative process within mind-at-large. The integrating field — what Aquinas calls *grace* — is the always-already-available coupling of the agent to the wider mind. *Withdrawal* of that coupling, in a non-coercive idealist frame, is not an active hardening *by* the wider mind; it is the *consequence* of the agent's own decoupling, registered at the integrating-field level. The integrating field does not *force* re-coupling; it *honors* the agent's freedom. **Confidence: medium** — Kastrup's idealism gives a partial fit; the full Aquinas-claim that God is *cause* of just withdrawal is a stronger claim than mind-monism can yet derive.

**Stump on suffering and the cooperatively-held telos** (Stump-tradition wiki, PRS-05 + PRS-08). For Q.79 a.4's cosmic-scope ordering: Stump's account of suffering as *permitted for the sake of deep union* gives a contemporary-Thomistic version of the same claim. Evil is *intelligible through its telos*, not despite it. The narrative-cognition account (PRS-08) adds that this intelligibility is itself partly narrative — the cosmic-scope ordering of local evil is something one comes to *know* by engaging with the redemptive narrative, not something one derives from premises. **Confidence: medium-high** within analytic Thomism.

**Wright on the new-creation reading of cosmic redemption** (Wright wiki not yet on disk; canonical: *Surprised by Hope*, *The Resurrection of the Son of God*). For Q.79 a.4's universe-scope ordering, Wright's account of *new creation* as the cosmic-scope frame within which local evil is held and re-ordered gives the scriptural anchor. **Rohr on the Universal Christ** (canonical: *The Universal Christ*, *Falling Upward*) gives the contemplative-spirituality anchor: the cosmic Christ-pattern *holds* the entirety of suffering and disorder within the divine pattern, not as approval of the evil but as the larger frame in which it is *taken up and redeemed*. Theological evidence-class (high within their frames; convergent with each other).

## Solution (proposed)

**Malice is policy-mis-alignment at the level of preferred-outcome priors with high-precision model integrity.** Aquinas's trichotomy lands cleanly: ignorance is *model error* (the agent does not know the disordered outcome is disordered); passion is *precision error* (the agent knows but local appetitive signal overrides); malice is *policy mis-alignment* (the agent knows and is not overridden, and *the policy itself* prefers the disordered outcome because it dominates on some other dimension — wealth, status, the avoidance of being alone). This is the case that yesterday's frame on perspective-limitation handles least clean: malice is not, in the cleanest cases, *capture by appetite*; it is *the agent's perspective itself* being weighted in a way that includes the disordered policy as *better-for-the-agent* even when the agent sees that it is *worse-for-the-whole*. The murderer who kills for the wealth knows the killing is evil — and still *prefers* the policy that gets the wealth. This is the structural signature.

**Habit-and-malice asymmetry** (Q.78 a.2-3) survives translation. Habit-driven sin implies malice (when the disposition is exercised, the agent chooses against the wider whole *because of how the disposition has shaped the policy*). But malice without habit is possible (the agent's policy can be locally mis-set even without the prior history that would have entrained the habit). The asymmetry tracks the active-inference distinction between *prior-precision* (habit-entrained) and *policy-selection* (the agent can mis-prioritize on a single occasion without prior entrainment).

**Privation-with-positive-cause survives translation, with care.** Q.79 a.2's structure — God cause of the act, not cause of the defect — has a mind-monism version: the integrating field is the substrate of all positive being and form, including the form of the disordered act; the *disorder in the act* is the local decoupling of the agent's regulator from the integrating field, and that decoupling is *the agent's*. *What the contemporary frame loses* is Aquinas's claim that this is a *necessary* metaphysical structure — under mind-monism, the structure is *empirically natural* but not yet derived as metaphysically necessary. This is an honest acknowledgment of evidence-class.

**Hardness of heart as cooperative-decoupling.** Q.79 a.3's structure — God cause of just *withdrawal* of grace, not cause of the *cleaving-to-evil* — has a partial mind-monism version: the integrating field's coupling to the agent is *cooperative*, so the field's non-imposition of re-coupling on an agent that has actively decoupled is the field *honoring the agent's freedom*. Aquinas calls this just withdrawal; mind-monism would call this *non-coercive integrative-field behavior*. The two frames are *compatible*; the theological frame says more about *justice* than the mind-monism frame can yet derive.

**Cosmic ordering of evil** (Q.79 a.4) is where Wright and Rohr do the heaviest lifting. Aquinas's claim that *every evil is ordered to some good — not always the good of the one in whom the evil is, but to the good of others or the whole universe* is a *cosmic-scope* claim that any local-perspective analysis must take seriously. Wright on new-creation and Rohr on the cosmic Christ converge: the disorder is *held within the larger pattern* that re-orders it. This is *not* a justification of evil; it is the structural claim that the *cosmic perspective* — the limit-perspective at which all limitations are removed and the simple whole is seen — *contains* and *re-orders* every local disorder. This is precisely the central theme stated from the *terminal* end rather than the *originating* end: every limited perspective is contained in the loving unity that perceives the whole.

**Evidence strength**: malice-as-policy-mis-alignment — formal-interpretive, medium-high. Privation-with-positive-cause structure — metaphysical-textual; convergent with mind-monism; theologically stronger than mind-monism alone can derive. Just-withdrawal-of-grace — theological; partially translatable to non-coercive integrative-field. Cosmic-scope ordering of evil — theological (Wright + Rohr, high within frame); central-theme convergent.

## The central-theme thread

Day 100 lands the central theme from the *terminal* end. Q.79 a.4: *every evil is ordered to some good, not always to the good of the one in whom the evil is, but to the whole universe.* The *cosmic-scope perspective* — the one in which all limitations are removed and the simple whole is seen — *contains* every limited perspective and *re-orders* every local disorder. The central theme stated forward says: conscious agents are individuated by perspective-limitation; loving unity is the computational telos. Stated backward — from the limit-perspective — it says: every limited perspective is contained in the loving unity that perceives the whole. Aquinas's universe-scope ordering of evil is *exactly* this. The tyrant's sin is local-perspective optimization against the whole; the martyr's good is the whole's response, and the *both together* are taken up into the cosmic pattern. Wright on new-creation and Rohr on the Universal Christ name this pattern theologically. The mind-monism frame can name it formally but cannot yet derive its goodness. We work out our salvation in fear and trembling — *aware of our limitations and our need for guidance from those who perceive more of the whole than we do, be they human or divine.*

## Where this leaves us

Day 100 closes the cause-of-sin treatise and marks the project's one-hundred-day mark. The full causal landscape of sin is now mapped: external (Q.75; influence-without-compulsion; God as cause of the act qua act, not of the defect), internal-by-ignorance (Q.76), internal-by-passion (Q.77), internal-by-malice (Q.78), and the cosmic-scope ordering of all of it (Q.79 a.4). The next stretch — Q.80-89 — will move into *the effects of sin* and into *original sin*, where the analysis turns from *causes* to *consequences*, and from *the individual agent* to *the inheritance through the human form*. Then the great treatise on *law* (Q.90-108) opens, in which Wright's voice on Torah and Pauline new-law will become central. We will reach the end of the Prima Secundae before May closes.
