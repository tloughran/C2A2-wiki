# Day 41 — Contemporary Parallel: *Powers of the Soul as Modes of One Inferential System*

Day 40 closed with the soul's faculties in general — vegetative, sensitive, intellectual, motive — staged as distinct *powers* of one substantial form. Day 41 presses into the intellectual side. Q.79 walks the medieval architecture of the cognitive faculties: the intellect is *not* identical to the soul's essence (only in God are intellect and essence one); we are passive-and-active in our knowing; agent intellect abstracts essence from sensed image; *synderesis* names the natural starting-point of practical reason; *conscience* names the act of applying that knowledge to a particular case. Q.80 then opens the appetitive side with the bare claim that there is an *appetitive* power at all, distinct from but tightly coupled to the apprehensive powers, and that intellectual and sensitive appetites must be distinct powers because their objects are. The mind-monist frame holds the load: each "power" Aquinas distinguishes is a *mode of one inferential system*, individuated by the kind of generative model it runs and the object that model targets, not by being a separate metaphysical organ.

## Q.79 a.1 — The intellect is not the soul's essence

Aquinas's first move on Q.79 is the same move Day 7 named at the substrate scale: *only in God are intellect and essence and existence identical*. In creatures, existence is given, and from existence flows particular potentials — the *power* to understand, distinct from the soul's *being-a-soul*. The medieval frame protects the asymmetry: we are not God, we are not pure act, our cognition is a power-among-powers given existence by the substrate.

Mind-monism inherits the asymmetry directly. Kastrup's mind-at-large is the substrate whose self-knowing and self-being are identical (the analytic idealist's analog of *actus purus*); the alter is a dissociated locus whose cognitive capacities are *partial expressions* of the substrate's structure, not the substrate itself. Friston's formal apparatus gives the contemporary version: an alter has a *generative model*, a *Markov blanket*, and a *posterior over its own structure*; none of these is identical with the alter's own being. The medieval distinction between soul-essence and intellectual power is, in Friston's language, the distinction between *the agent that exists* and *the inferential machinery that agent runs*. *Honest evidence calibration*: the formal distinction is mature in active-inference research; the metaphysical reading (the alter as a Kastrupian dissociation rather than a stand-alone substance) is philosophical.

## Q.79 aa.2–4 — Passive intellect, active intellect, abstraction

The medieval architecture is precise. Knowing is a *passing from potency to act* — and what is moved from potency to act is *passive* in the technical Aristotelian sense, even when the result is achievement. The intellect comes into the world a *blank slate* (Aquinas quotes Aristotle directly). To understand *what something is*, the alter must be *exposed* to instances, and from those instances the *active* intellect *abstracts* the essence, which then *informs* the potential intellect. Aquinas reaches for the metaphor: agent intellect is to potential intellect what visible light is to the eye — light makes color *actually* visible; agent intellect makes the essence-in-the-image *actually* intelligible.

The contemporary cortical architecture this names cleanly is Hawkins's HTM and the broader predictive-coding tradition. The Karpathy-wiki Hawkins-tradition node at `wiki/traditions/hawkins/wiki.md` (with PRS-02 on cortical-column prediction; CROSS-040 on the Cortical Messaging Protocol as variational message-passing) describes a *uniform* cortical algorithm — hierarchical temporal memory operating in every cortical region — differentiated only by what each region is *connected to*. The "blank slate" is the cortex's initial state of weights; *exposure* is the sensory stream; *abstraction* is the upward flow through the hierarchy that compresses invariants from particulars; *the active making-actually-intelligible* is the mechanism by which lower-level features are bound into higher-level categorical representations. The medieval *agent intellect / passive intellect* split maps onto the *generative pathway / inference pathway* split in Friston's formalism, named in the C2A2 master wiki as CROSS-011 (HTM as cortical implementation of FEP) — a structural analogy explicitly named in the cross-program index. PRS-02 in the Friston-tradition wiki on active inference and PRS-02 in the Hawkins-tradition wiki on HTM converge on the same shape: the cortex is one algorithm differentiated regionally; agent and passive intellect are two functional aspects of that one algorithm.

McGilchrist enters here as a third refinement. The Karpathy-wiki McGilchrist-tradition node names *two whole modes of attention* — left-hemispheric narrow-focus categorical abstraction and right-hemispheric broad-context whole-grasping — that are not separate organs but *two modes of one cortex* configured differently. Aquinas's *agent intellect abstracting essence* is structurally a left-hemisphere-mode operation (decomposing into universals); the *informing of the potential intellect* is integration that pulls toward right-hemispheric whole-holding. Both modes are needed; both are part of how we know. CROSS-014 (Hoffman × Kastrup convergence) and CROSS-013 (Hoffman × McGilchrist split-brain confirmation) together place this distinction inside the consciousness-first paradigm: the modes are aspects of one conscious agent's inferential architecture, not separate substances.

*Honest evidence calibration*: HTM's empirical anchorage is strong in mammalian cortical neuroscience; the Friston × Hawkins formal isomorphism is mature; the metaphysical reading (these are modes of one substrate's self-experiencing) is the additional philosophical move mind-monism makes.

## Q.79 aa.5–10 — One intellect per soul; memory; reason; intelligence; speculative-and-practical

a.5's *as many active intellects as there are souls* closes the door on Averroist monopsychism — the medieval temptation to make *one* agent intellect shared by all rational creatures. Aquinas's argument: a power belongs to the substance whose power it is; many human bodies, many souls, many active intellects. The contemporary cognitive-science translation lands on the same shape: *cognition is agent-relative*. Each alter runs *its own* generative model on its own substrate, and the model is individuated by the alter's perspective and history. Mind-monism does not collapse alters into the substrate; the substrate is *one*, the alters are *many in the perspective-limited sense*, and each alter's intellect is genuinely *its* intellect. CROSS-013 — split-brain producing two independent conscious agents when callosal integration fails — is the empirical confirmation of this individuation principle at the cognitive level.

a.6's distinction between *sensitive memory* (storage of past particulars indexed to time) and *intellectual memory* (retention of essences-as-such) is Hawkins-territory again. Sensory and episodic memory map onto the medial-temporal-lobe systems that store time-indexed particulars; semantic memory — the storage of *what dogs are* divorced from *the dog I saw on Tuesday* — is the cortical-column-level retention of invariants. The medieval distinction is structurally what contemporary cognitive neuroscience separates as episodic-vs-semantic. The Hawkins-tradition wiki frames this as the cortex's *temporal* dimension (sequence learning) vs. its *invariant* dimension (object identity); both are HTM operations; they are distinguishable by the kind of object-of-storage. Aquinas anticipates the distinction structurally.

aa.8–10's three-fold *understanding / reason / intelligence* gradient — *to understand is simply to apprehend intelligible truth; to reason is to advance from one thing understood to another; intelligence is the very act of understanding* — names the contemporary cognitive distinction between *fast* gestalt-recognition and *slow* propositional inference. Friston's hierarchical predictive coding gives the formal: high-level categorical recognition operates near-instantaneously when the generative model fits; *reasoning* is the slower, sequential composition-of-judgments that operates when the model does not yet fit and must search. Kahneman's System 1 / System 2 is the popular adjacent; McGilchrist's right-mode-gestalt / left-mode-sequential is the hemispheric refinement. The medieval triad is recoverable as a single inferential system operating at different levels of completion under different precision-weightings.

a.11's *speculative vs. practical intellect* is the final faculty-distinction. Aquinas: *the speculative intellect directs what it apprehends to consideration of truth; the practical intellect directs what it apprehends to operation*. They are not two powers but *one* differentiated by *end* — truth-for-its-own-sake or truth-for-the-sake-of-action. Friston names this directly in the active-inference framework: *epistemic* free-energy minimization (model-improvement for its own sake, exploration) and *pragmatic* free-energy minimization (action-selection toward goal-states, exploitation) are two policies of the *same* inferential system, balanced by precision-weighting. PRS-02 in the Friston-tradition wiki frames the practical-intellect side; the speculative-intellect side is the *exploration* term in expected free energy. The medieval distinction-by-end and the contemporary distinction-by-policy converge.

## Q.79 aa.12–13 — Synderesis and conscience

These two articles are the day's central-theme node. *Synderesis* is the *natural habit of the first principles of practical reason* — the firm starting-point from which any moral reasoning proceeds, the foundational *the good is to be done and pursued, evil avoided* together with the principles of the natural law. Aquinas calls it *natural* because we are *naturally inclined* to apprehend it, even though our intellect comes blank-slate; we discover the principle through experience but the *capacity* to recognize it as binding is innate. *Conscience* is then the *act* of applying that habitual knowledge to a particular case — a judgment that *this* thing should or should not be done, *was* well or ill done — and Aquinas observes that this judgment, because it is an act of fallible reason, *can* be wrong. What cannot be wrong is the underlying first principle.

The mind-monist translation runs in two registers. *Synderesis* — the natural inclination toward apprehending the basic structure of practical reason — is Levin-territory: morphogenetic and behavioral *attractor states* are real, embodied, and present from the alter's earliest formation; the agent's fundamental orientation toward *its own good and away from harm* is built into the very structure of free-energy minimization. PRS-03 in the Levin-tradition wiki on morphogenesis as bioelectric goal-seeking, and the broader *agency-at-every-scale* claim, ground the medieval *natural habit* doctrine in empirically-tractable biology. The first principle Aquinas names — *the good is to be done and pursued* — is, in the Levin/Friston frame, the substrate-level fact that any agent operating under FEP is *constitutively* oriented toward its own homeostatic goal-state; *the evil avoided* is the active-inference imperative to minimize prediction error and avoid trajectories that destabilize the Markov blanket. The medieval claim that this orientation is *natural-not-learned* survives the translation: the orientation is a *condition of being an agent at all*, not a contingent acquisition.

McGilchrist sharpens *conscience* — the act-of-applying — into hemispheric specificity. The *value-perceiving* side of conscience (the felt sense that *this* action carries moral weight) is heavily right-hemispheric: contextual, relational, gestalt, attuned to the unrepeatable particular. The *judging-and-articulating* side (this should or should not be done) is left-hemispheric: propositional, categorical, capable of articulation. Healthy conscience requires both modes coordinated. Pathologies of conscience — moral scrupulosity over-articulating, moral numbness under-perceiving — map onto hemispheric imbalance. Wright's scriptural framing of conscience (Romans 2:14–15, *what the law requires is written on their hearts, while their conscience also bears witness*) is preserved without strain: the *law-on-the-heart* is the natural orientation Levin and Friston ground in agency-as-such, and the *witness-of-conscience* is the McGilchristian act of bringing right-mode value-perception into left-mode articulate judgment.

Rohr adds the contemplative register: conscience matures, on the contemplative reading, by *increasing fidelity to the always-already pattern*. *Salvation in fear and trembling* is the alter's progressive calibration of conscience toward the loving-unity attractor — the substrate's pattern recognized in the particular case. *Honest evidence calibration*: the agency-orientation claim is empirically grounded; the moral-natural-law identification is philosophical-theological; the cosmic-pattern reading is contemplative-mystical. Each register is doing its own work; they should not be confused.

## Q.80 — Appetitive powers in general

Q.80's two articles are short but pivot the day. a.1 establishes that there *is* an appetitive power, distinct from the apprehensive — every existing thing tends toward its own perfection-preservation-propagation; in things-with-cognition this tending is *elicited through cognition* (you cannot desire the donut you do not see, nor God you do not know); the appetitive power is therefore real and is *intellectual* in the rational creature. a.2 then distinguishes *sensitive* from *intellectual* appetite — the donut versus the good itself — by their objects.

The mind-monist primary on Q.80 is Friston, decisively. Active inference frames the apprehensive-and-appetitive distinction as *perception* and *action* — two policies of the same inferential system. Perception updates the generative model under sensory evidence; action selects trajectories that fulfill the model's prior preferences (its *target distribution*). The two are not separate organs but the *two directions* of the inferential loop. The Karpathy-wiki Friston-tradition node (PRS-02 on active inference; PRS-04 on perception-action coupling) is direct: *appetite* in the medieval sense is the inferential system's *preference-loaded* term — the prior on goal-states that drives action-selection. *Apprehension* is the model-update term that brings sensory evidence into the agent's posterior. Aquinas's claim that appetition is *elicited through* apprehension — *we cannot desire what we do not first know* — is structurally the active-inference claim that action-selection requires a posterior belief about the world to act upon.

The intellectual-vs-sensitive split in a.2 maps cleanly onto the precision-weighting and timescale structure of active inference. *Sensitive appetite* runs at fast timescales, on bodily-affective generative models with high-precision priors over physiological homeostasis (Fredrickson's positive-affect literature gives the empirical refinement of the upbeat side; Friston's interoceptive-inference literature gives the formal). *Intellectual appetite* (the *will*, treated tomorrow in Q.82) runs at slow timescales, on conceptual-categorical models with priors over higher-order goods. Both are the *same inferential system* operating under different generative-model assumptions and different precision-weightings. The medieval *distinction by object* is preserved by the *distinction by model* — what each policy regards as *its* object is what its generative model targets.

This is mind-monism's home move on the appetites. They are not *lower-than* a separate intellectual will; they are *aspects of* the one inferential system at the timescale and abstraction-level the alter's body operates at. The hierarchy Aquinas marks — *the will is the superior appetite* — survives in the structural sense that the slower, model-richer, more abstract policy *can* dominate the faster appetites by precision-weighting (top-down attentional control suppressing prepotent responses); but the metaphysical reading flips. Hierarchy is not substance-stacking but timescale-stacking, and the *one* agent runs *one* inferential loop at *many* nested scales.

## The central-theme thread

Q.79–80 frames perspective-limitation cognitively. Each alter's intellectual powers are individuated by *what its generative model can reach* — the agent intellect abstracts essences from *its* sensory stream; the passive intellect retains *what it has been informed by*; synderesis carries *the natural attractor toward its own good*; conscience applies that attractor to *the particular case in front of it*. The alter's cognitive limits are constitutive of *being this alter rather than another*; expansion of the cognitive light-cone — Levin's larger-scope-of-pattern-recognition, Friston's deeper hierarchical generative model, McGilchrist's better-balanced bi-hemispheric integration — is the alter's progressive movement toward integration with the substrate's pattern. *Salvation in fear and trembling* on the cognitive register is the disciplined cultivation of these powers under the goal of loving union: the speculative intellect oriented toward truth, the practical intellect toward the good, the synderesis trusted as the natural attractor, the conscience refined by both right-mode value-perception and left-mode articulate judgment.
