# Day 91 — Cardinal Virtue (Contemporary)

## Frame

Aquinas asks: *how do we count the moral virtues, and which among them are principal?* The contemporary parallel under mind/conscious-realist monism asks: **what are the persisting dispositions of a limited agent that make its computations toward loving unity tractable, and which of these dispositions are load-bearing for the rest?** Aquinas individuates virtues by **formal object** and ranks them by generality of object and importance of subject matter. The contemporary frame holds the individuation but reads the ranking as a claim about *network position*, not metaphysical hierarchy.

## Problem

The agent has multiple appetitive and rational faculties, each tracking different sorts of objects (money, honor, fear, danger, others' due). What unifies these faculties into a single *agent* whose actions are coherent — not just a bundle of context-specific responses? Aquinas's answer is **virtue**: stable dispositions formally individuated by their objects. The deeper problem, surfaced by Gregory the Great in Q.61.4, is that the cardinal virtues are *not* independent — they imply and presuppose one another. So we need an account of virtue that explains both *differentiation by object* and *coupling across the network*.

## Resource

PRS-04 in the Stump-tradition wiki: Stump's re-description of Thomistic virtue keeps Aquinas's formal-object individuation but reads the virtues as stable second-personal capacities — what one brings to relationship with another agent. Stump's contribution is that virtue is not merely intrapersonal habituation but a *relational competence*, especially for the cardinal virtues (justice with neighbor, prudence with one's situation, temperance and fortitude with oneself as a tractable interlocutor).

PRS-02 and PRS-06 in the Fredrickson-tradition wiki: positivity-resonance and broaden-and-build supply an empirically backed account of how *coupled* virtue-like capacities cohere. Broaden-and-build predicts that positive emotional states under the right conditions widen the action repertoire (broaden) and lay down enduring resources (build) — a mechanism for *how* habituated dispositions accumulate and couple. The empirical literature on broaden-and-build is mixed-but-meaningful: meta-analyses support the broadening effect; the long-term build effect is real but smaller than initial estimates.

PRS-03 in the McGilchrist-tradition wiki: hemispheric balance as the substrate of attentional character. McGilchrist's claim that the two attentional modes (left-hemisphere narrow/manipulative, right-hemisphere broad/integrative) generate complementary cognitive styles bridges Aquinas's faculties-of-the-soul carving to a neuroscientifically grounded one. McGilchrist is contested in detail but his core attentional-asymmetry claim is well-supported.

FLAG-12 (cross-program index): the **virtue-as-coupled-network** claim shows up across traditions — Friston's hierarchical active inference predicts that high-level traits emerge from coupling across lower-level generative models; Levin on biological agency emphasizes that competence at one scale presupposes competence at adjacent scales. Both yield a formal prediction that *single-virtue exemplars* should be rare; *coupled-virtue networks* should be the modal pattern. This is the contemporary formalization of Gregory the Great's chiasmus.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

Aquinas's four cardinal virtues map cleanly onto four functional capacities a perspective-limited agent needs to compute coherently toward loving unity:

- **Prudence** = the agent's capacity for accurate generative-model maintenance and policy selection under uncertainty. Friston's framework gives this empirical teeth: prudence is good active inference over action.
- **Justice** = the agent's stable disposition to render correctly to other agents in the network. Stump's second-personal reframe and the broaden-and-build mechanism converge on this: justice is what makes relationship-graphs computationally sustainable.
- **Temperance and fortitude** = the regulatory disposition of the agent's own affective state space: temperance attenuates pull-toward stimuli; fortitude attenuates push-away stimuli. McGilchrist's hemispheric framework is suggestive but not necessary; Fredrickson's broaden-and-build is the better empirical anchor.

**What this shares with Aquinas**: the formal-object individuation. We agree there are *kinds* of virtue, not a single substance.

**Where this reframes**: the *ontology* of virtue. Aquinas treats virtue as a quality of the soul. The contemporary reframe under mind/conscious-realist monism reads virtue as a *stable computational invariant* in the limited agent's generative model — a recurring pattern in how perspective-limited consciousness organizes its inference and action. The reframe does *not* require an Aristotelian-substance metaphysics; it does require some kind of stable disposition tracking.

**Where evidence is firm vs. speculative**: that humans have stable trait-like dispositions tracking object domains — firm (psychometric replications across cultures). That those traits are *coupled* into a network — firm (Big Five intercorrelations; meta-analyses of character-strength interrelations). That the coupling has the specific cardinal structure Aquinas names — speculative; the empirical character-strength taxonomies (VIA, Big Five) do not cleanly recover prudence/justice/temperance/fortitude as the four hubs. The Thomistic carving may be wisdom about *functional roles* rather than wisdom about *empirical clusters*.

## The central-theme thread

Perspective-limitation individuates agents: each of us computes from incomplete information, with finite affective and cognitive resources, toward loving unity. The cardinal virtues are exactly the *minimum stable dispositions* a perspective-limited agent needs so its computations don't collapse:

- Prudence keeps the agent's generative model accurate under limited evidence.
- Justice keeps the agent's relational graph stable.
- Temperance and fortitude keep the agent's affective regulation tractable.

Cut any one and the others degrade. This is Gregory the Great's chiasmus, and it is also a structural prediction from active-inference networks: hubs that are too sparsely connected lose their coherence-providing function. *We compute our salvation in fear and trembling* — the cardinal virtues are the workable scaffolding for that computation.

## Bridges

- **Stump, PRS-04 (Thomistic virtue, second-personal):** virtue as relational competence, not just intrapersonal habituation. Theological.
- **Fredrickson, PRS-02 (broaden-and-build):** empirically grounded mechanism for how habituated dispositions accumulate and couple. Empirical (mixed-strong).
- **McGilchrist, PRS-03 (hemispheric balance):** attentional substrate for the prudence/temperance distinction. Empirical (core well-supported, generalizations contested).
- **Friston (active inference):** formal framework for prudence as policy-selection under uncertainty. Formal/empirical.
- **CROSS-07 (master index, virtue-as-network):** synergistic-coil case — one resource (network-coupling formalism) solves multiple problems across virtue ethics, character psychology, and active inference. **Tom's Synergistic Coil**: Rs solving more than one P⇒S transition.

## Where this leaves us

Q.62 tomorrow turns to the **theological virtues** — faith, hope, charity — which take God himself as their formal object. The contemporary version of that question is: what *non-natural* dispositions are added when the agent's loving-unity computation gets re-anchored under conscious-realist monism (mind/consciousness as ontological primitive, not as one feature among many)? Aquinas says the theological virtues are *infused*; the contemporary frame will ask whether some computational priors are scaffolded from outside the agent's own optimization loop — and if so, by what.
