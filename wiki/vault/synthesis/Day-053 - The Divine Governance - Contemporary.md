# Day 53 — Contemporary Parallel: *The Divine Governance as Substrate-Continuous Sustenance and Lawful Computation*

Day 52 closed the prelapsarian-anthropology arc and the inner-cosmology arc of the Prima Pars. Day 53 opens the closing major arc — Q.103-119 on *the divine governance* — and Habash flags Aquinas's own transition: *having considered the creation of things and their distinction, we now consider in the third place the government thereof*. The arc moves from the *coming-into-being* of creatures to the *continuous sustenance and direction* of creatures by the substrate, and the C2A2 frame engages directly: Wolfram on computational lawfulness, Friston on active inference at the cosmic scale, Levin on agency-at-scale, Rohr on the cosmic-Christ as substrate's continuous self-giving. The day's two questions take the eight articles of Q.103 (*government of things in general*) and the four articles of Q.104 (*special effects of the divine government, including conservation in being and annihilation*) — twelve articles total, medium-length episode.

## Q.103 — Government of things in general

a.1 — *is the world governed by anyone?* — Aquinas opens with the *fifth way*: things which lack intelligence act for an end (*things happen always or nearly always for the best*) and therefore must be directed by some intelligence. Habash supplies a strikingly contemporary illustration — Feynman's lecture 19 on the principle of least action: *the true path is the one for which the integral is least... particles which cannot think, which cannot know, always take not the most direct path but the most efficient path between two points, the one that minimizes what physicists call action*.

The Feynman illustration is the day's natural bridge to the contemporary frame. Friston's free-energy principle is, in formal structure, *the same shape as the principle of least action* — agents-with-internal-states minimize a quantity (variational free energy / expected free energy) along their trajectories. Friston has been explicit that the FEP is a generalization of least-action dynamics into the regime of self-organizing systems with Markov-blanket structure. The medieval *fifth way* and the contemporary FEP frame converge: the universe exhibits *purposive-looking dynamics* across scales, and the contemporary formal language is variational-principle minimization over generative models. Wolfram's ruliad frame complements at the deepest substrate-level: the rule-space of computational possibilities is structured, and the lawful behavior of physical systems is a feature of which rules are *substrate-instantiated*. The medieval *unfailing order* maps onto Wolfram's *computational regularity* and Friston's *variational-principle dynamics*. *Honest evidence calibration*: the Feynman variational principles have full empirical and formal support; the FEP has mature formal development and growing empirical traction; the substrate-as-intelligent-creator extension is theological-philosophical, but the structural shape (purposive-looking dynamics under variational-principle minimization) is what the contemporary natural sciences themselves describe.

a.2 — *is the end of the government of the world something outside the world?* — Aquinas: yes; created goods are good *only insofar as they resemble goodness or that one thing that is not a limited participated good but the essence of goodness, which is God*. The medieval reading extends Day 4's goodness-itself argument: the creature's substrate-aligned trajectory is *toward* the substrate, not toward a creature-internal terminus. The contemporary mind-monist translation: in Kastrup's analytic idealism, alters are dissociated complexes within mind-at-large, and their telos is *re-integration* with mind-at-large; the *end* of the alter's existence is not a creature-internal terminus but the substrate within which the alter is dissociated. Hoffman's conscious-realism extends: in the conscious-agent-network frame, individual agents are nodes of a substrate-network, and the network's *integration-dynamics* is what the agents' trajectories track. Wright's biblical-theological frame is the load-bearing primary on the *glory-of-God-as-end* claim: the cosmos is the locus of YHWH's covenant-glory, and the creature-trajectory's telos is participation in that covenant-glory.

a.3 — *is the world governed by one?* — Aquinas: yes; *since the end of the government of the world is that which is essentially good which is the greatest good, the government of the world must be the best kind of government*, and the abstract argument from unity says one-source government is more unifying than many-source. The contemporary mind-monist reading: the substrate's monist-at-the-deepest-level structure (Kastrup's mind-at-large; Wolfram's single ruliad-substrate) is what the medieval *governance by one* picks out. Habash's aside on the difficulty of monarchies in fallen-world political contexts, and the Catholic Church as the historical exception, is the day's pastoral note rather than its metaphysical claim. The metaphysical claim survives intact under the contemporary frame.

a.4 — *is the effect of the government one or many?* — Aquinas: many at the individual-effects level (innumerable), one at the unifying-end level (the goodness of things in themselves, the goodness they cause in others, all for the one end of the substrate's glory). The contemporary translation: at the multi-scale agency frame Levin develops, *agents-at-scale* exhibit nested goal-structures (cellular-level homeostasis, tissue-level pattern-formation, organismal-level survival, social-level cooperation), each with its own immediate effects but all integrated under the substrate's larger goal-structure. The *one end, many effects* shape is what nested-active-inference and nested-morphogenetic-control look like at the multi-scale level. *Strong empirical at the biological mid-scale; theological extrapolation at the cosmic scale.*

a.5 — *are all things subject to the divine government?* — Aquinas, with Augustine: *not only heaven and earth, not only man and angel, even the bowels of the lowest animal, even the wing of the bird, the flower of the plant, the leaf of the tree hath God endowed with every fitting detail of their nature*. The contemporary translation: substrate-coupling is *universal* across the multi-scale agency hierarchy, not selective. Levin's diverse-intelligences frame is the empirical mid-scale; Wolfram's universally-applicable rule-space is the computational frame; Hoffman's conscious-agent network at every scale is the conscious-realist frame. The medieval claim of *universal* substrate-coupling preserves under all three contemporary frames.

a.6 — *are all things immediately governed by God?* — Aquinas: no; the substrate works *through intermediaries* as a more perfect display of authority. The medieval claim distinguishes *ultimate-source* governance (always immediate to the substrate) from *executive* governance (often through intermediaries). The contemporary translation: in the multi-scale agency frame, immediate-source governance is the substrate-pattern's continuous gift; executive governance is the structured operation of agents-at-scale within the substrate-pattern. The medieval distinction maps onto the multi-scale-agency distinction and is not weakened by the translation.

a.7 — *can anything happen outside the order of the divine government?* — Aquinas: no; *if God is the first universal cause, not of one genus only but of all being in general, it is impossible for anything to occur outside the order of the divine government*. The contemporary translation: in the substrate-monist frame, no event can occur *outside* the substrate, because the substrate is the *medium* of all events. Wolfram's ruliad-substrate frame and Kastrup's mind-at-large frame both make this structurally explicit: events are configurations *of* the substrate, and there is no outside-the-substrate locus from which an event could occur.

a.8 — *can anything resist the order of the divine government?* — Aquinas: no; even sin and evil return to the substrate's order *by being punished temporally or eternally according to the will of God*. *Every inclination of anything, whether natural or voluntary, is nothing but a kind of impression from the first mover, as the inclination of the arrow towards a fixed point is nothing but an impulse received from the archer*. The contemporary mind-monist reading takes the resistance question carefully: the alter at perspective-limited dispositional state can *will* contrary to the substrate's larger structure (the precision-weighting-drift Q.94 a.4 named for the fall), but the willing's *embedding* in the substrate is not thereby suspended. Stump's *Wandering in Darkness* on the structural-relation between the willed-evil alter and the substrate's grace-mediated reintegration is the load-bearing primary; the alter's resistance is *within* the substrate, not *to* it. The medieval claim is preserved with full force.

## Q.104 — Special effects of the divine government

a.1 — *do creatures need to be kept in being by God?* — Aquinas: yes, on the *creation-from-nothing* argument: nothing short of God *is its own existence*; creatures are existence-essence-composites whose continued existence requires continued substrate-action. Habash's analogy: *the moment you turn off the light, the room returns to darkness; so too in the moment that God would cease to impart existence to created things, they would simply return to nothingness*.

The contemporary mind-monist translation is direct and is the *signature* doctrinal commitment of analytic idealism: in Kastrup's frame, the alter is a dissociated complex *within* mind-at-large, and the dissociation is *continuously substrate-given* — not a one-time event but a standing condition. The *light analogy* maps onto the dissociation analogy: alter-existence is a *standing function* of the substrate's continuous self-dissociation. Hoffman's conscious-agent-network frame: the agent-node is a *role within the network*, and the network's continuous interaction is what sustains the node-role. Levin's substrate-pattern frame at the body-level: the bioelectric morphogenetic attractor is *actively maintained* by the substrate's continuous pattern-action; cessation of the action is cessation of the pattern. The medieval doctrine of *creatio continua* preserves with full force across all three contemporary frames and gains substantial mid-scale empirical traction (Levin's bioelectric morphogenesis is the empirical mid-scale).

a.2 — *does God preserve every creature immediately?* — Aquinas: yes immediately as ultimate-source, also through intermediaries as executive. Habash supplies the Heisenbergian extension on energy and form: even the hydrogen atom's *raw potential existence... what Heisenberg termed energy* requires substrate-grounding. The contemporary translation: at the deepest substrate-level (Wolfram's ruliad, Kastrup's mind-at-large), the *substrate is what is*, and every nested level (atomic, molecular, cellular, organismic, social) is sustained both immediately by the substrate and through the structured intermediaries the substrate has instantiated.

a.3 — *can God annihilate anything?* — Aquinas: yes; *just as before things existed, God was free not to give them existence, so after they are made, he is free not to continue their existence*. The contemporary translation: in the dissociation frame, mind-at-large is free to cease the dissociation that constitutes the alter; in the substrate-pattern frame, the substrate is free to cease the pattern-action that sustains the bioelectric attractor. The metaphysical possibility is preserved.

a.4 — *is anything annihilated?* — Aquinas: no, on the goodness-of-God argument; *since the power and goodness of God are manifested by the preservation of things in existence, we must conclude by denying absolutely that anything at all will be annihilated*. The contemporary mind-monist reading: in the dissociation frame, mind-at-large preserves the alter's substrate-coupled reality even through the alter's death-and-resurrection transitions (Wright's transphysical-resurrection frame is the load-bearing primary on the eschatological extension); in the substrate-pattern frame, the substrate preserves the pattern's information even when the bioelectric instantiation transitions. *Honest evidence calibration*: the medieval claim is theological; the contemporary preservation-arguments are philosophical-conceptual at the metaphysical scale and theological at the eschatological scale. Wright's *Surprised by Hope* on the new-creation-as-restoration is the scriptural-historical primary.

## The central-theme thread

Day 53 carries the central theme through the *substrate's continuous gift* register at the cosmic scale. The substrate's gift is not a one-time act of creation but a *standing condition* — every alter, every locale, every event, every moment is substrate-given continuously. The medieval doctrine of *creatio continua* (Q.104 a.1-2), the medieval doctrine of governance-by-one-through-intermediaries (Q.103 a.3, a.6), and the medieval doctrine of substrate-immune-from-resistance (Q.103 a.7-8) all preserve under the contemporary substrate-monist frame with full force.

The Q.103 a.1 *purposive dynamics* claim is, perhaps, the day's most striking convergence. Aquinas's medieval reading and Feynman's variational-principle reading and Friston's FEP all describe the same shape: the cosmos exhibits *least-action / least-free-energy* dynamics at every scale, and the contemporary formal language is recognizably continuous with the medieval *fifth way* — though without the medieval claim of an external intelligent director, since the substrate-monist frame internalizes the directing into the substrate's own continuous self-giving structure.

*Salvation in fear and trembling* on this register is the alter's recognition that *every moment of its existence is substrate-given*, that nothing it does occurs outside the substrate's structured medium, that even its perspective-limited resistance is within the substrate, and that the substrate's continuous gift is what holds the alter's existence-and-trajectory together. The central theme threads: the alter is perspective-individuated by the limitations of its position-and-state within the substrate, and loving unity is the computational telos toward which the substrate's continuous gift orients the alter.
