## Q.53 — Local movement: continuous and discrete modes

Aquinas's a.1 — *whether an angel can be moved locally* — receives Habash's longest physics-detour. The medieval problem is set up by Zeno's paradox: for a thing to move from A to B, it must pass through a halfway point, and before that a halfway-of-a-halfway, *ad infinitum*; therefore (Zeno argues) motion is impossible. Aristotle's answer is the potency-actuality framework: the moving thing does not actually pass through an actual infinity of points but only potentially; what is in motion is in the continuous mode *neither at A nor at B fully, and both at A and at B potentially*. Habash explicitly draws the parallel: *that's your explanation for quantum superposition* — the potency-mode of being-at-place-B-potentially-while-traversing.

The contemporary translation runs through several traditions. Wolfram's ruliad-frame and the *Wolfram Physics Project* (PRS-06 in the Wolfram-tradition wiki — ruliad as ontological ground) gives a discrete-step rendering: motion in the underlying hypergraph is the rewriting of relations, and what we measure as continuous spatial trajectory is the emergent description over many discrete substrate-level updates. Arkani-Hamed's amplituhedron program gives a related move on the formal side: scattering amplitudes derive from positive-geometric structures that *do not presuppose spacetime as input*; the continuous spacetime trajectory is downstream of the substrate-level mathematical object. CROSS-053 in the master cross-program index (Carroll × Arkani-Hamed × Wolfram on time-emergence) tracks the three-way convergence on time-as-emergent-feature. *Honest evidence calibration*: the empirical-and-formal case for spacetime emergence at very small scales is open and active; the medieval Zeno-Aristotle resolution receives a contemporary structural homology rather than a direct vindication.

Aquinas's positive answer in a.1 is structurally precise. An angel can move locally in two distinct modes. First, *continuously*: the angel's coupling-with-place leaves its first position successively (analogous to bodily local motion). Second, *instantaneously*: the angel's power transfers from the whole of one place to the whole of another, ceasing-to-couple-here and beginning-to-couple-there, without traversing intermediate places. The second mode is the medieval form of *discrete substrate-level transfer*: substrate-level activity changes its coupling-domain without occupying the intervening alter-spatial-region.

Mind-monism's translation runs cleanly. The continuous mode is the angel's coupling-with-alter-events progressing through a region in the way alter-form perception is structured (Hoffman's icon-set has continuous spatial structure; substrate-level activity coupled with that icon-set inherits the continuity). The discrete mode is substrate-level activity changing its Markov-kernel-coupling without inheriting the alter-form's spatial-continuity assumption: the substrate-level mode is not bound by alter-form's spatial-individuation, so it can transfer its coupling-domain without passing through alter-spatial-points. Wolfram's discrete-step substrate gives the formal cousin: the substrate updates discretely; what we measure as continuous trajectory is the emergent description, and substrate-level activity that is not committed to the emergent-continuous-description can transfer in single substrate-level steps.

a.2's *whether an angel passes through an intermediate place* and a.3's *whether the movement of an angel is instantaneous* both turn on the same distinction. In the continuous mode, intermediate places are traversed; in the discrete-power-transfer mode, they are not. The medieval claim is that *both modes are coherent*. Mind-monism's structural inheritance: substrate-level modes of activity have a richer set of motion-options than alter-form bodies because they are not bound by the assumptions that make alter-form-motion the kind of thing it is. *Speculative for the angelology specifics; structurally coherent within the mind-monist frame.*

## Q.54 — The knowledge of the angels: active and passive intellect

Q.54 opens the cognitive treatment that runs through Q.55–58 (continuing into Day 29). The structural work happens in a.1–4, where Aquinas argues that *the angels' act of understanding is not their substance, their understanding is not their being, their power of intelligence is not their essence, and there is no active-and-passive intellect distinction in angels*. The four medieval claims together give the angelology's *cognitive architecture*: angels are intellectual substances whose cognition is real-but-distinct-from-their-being, with potential-for-more-or-less-perfect-understanding (because they are not God), and without the active-passive distinction that human cognition requires.

The contemporary translation runs primarily through Friston's free-energy framework, Hawkins's HTM, and Aristotle's *De Anima* (which Aquinas inherits and which contemporary cognitive theory partly recovers). Aquinas's exposition of active and passive intellect — quoted at length in the transcript and worth following carefully — is the medieval cognitive theory that mind-monism must give a contemporary home.

The medieval account, briefly: human cognition begins with sensory experience of particular things (the tree out there). The imagination holds the sensible image (the tree-image-in-the-mind). The *active intellect* (intellectus agens) abstracts the universal essence (tree-as-such) from the particular sensible image. The *passive intellect* (intellectus possibilis) receives the universal essence as informing the mind, and the act of understanding occurs when the passive intellect is actually informed by the universal. The two intellects together implement the medieval claim that *humans know universals by abstraction from sensible particulars*.

The contemporary translation is direct. Hawkins's HTM (PRS in the Hawkins-tradition wiki) gives a cortical implementation: hierarchical temporal memory builds invariant representations from particular sensory streams; the invariant representation is the empirical-engineering analogue of the abstracted universal, and the bottom-up sensory stream is the analogue of the imagination's particular image. Friston's free-energy framework gives the formal implementation (CROSS-011 in the master cross-program index — HTM as cortical implementation of FEP): the generative model has hierarchical structure, with higher levels representing more invariant features, and the inference process that yields the active perception of an object instantiates the active-passive intellect distinction at the formal level. The active intellect maps onto the abstraction-and-prediction-generation aspect; the passive intellect maps onto the parameter-update-given-sensory-evidence aspect; the two together are the medieval cognitive architecture rendered in contemporary computational vocabulary. *Mature formal framework; mid-scale cognitive empirical anchorage substantial.*

Aquinas's claim that *angels do not have the active-passive intellect distinction* turns on their not having sensory access to particulars from which to abstract. They are *given* the universals directly (Q.55 will press this further on Day 29). Mind-monism's translation is the alter-substrate distinction Day 7 onwards has been building: alter-cognition operates through interface-mediation and abstraction-from-particulars; substrate-level cognition operates more directly because it is not interface-bound. The medieval *angels do not need active/passive intellect* maps onto *substrate-level cognition does not require the abstraction-mechanism that alter-form cognition requires*.

a.5 — *whether there is only intellectual knowledge in the angels* — closes Day 28's portion of the treatment with the affirmative. Angels do not have sense-knowledge because they have no senses; therefore their knowledge is entirely intellectual / immaterial. Mind-monism's structural translation: substrate-level modes of activity participate in mind-at-large's structural priors directly, without the sensory-mediation alter-form requires. The contemporary cognitive question this opens — *how do they know particular things if they have no sensory access?* — is what Q.55–58 (Days 29 onward) will press, and Aquinas's answer (universals infused directly by the substrate; particular knowledge derivative on universal knowledge) anticipates the move.

## The central-theme thread

Day 28 sharpens the perspective-limitation frame in two complementary directions. The *motion* question (Q.53) names the structural difference between alter-form constraints and substrate-level freedoms: alter-form bodies are bound by spatial-continuity assumptions that emerge in the alter's interface; substrate-level activity is not so bound. The Zeno paradox and Aristotle's potency-actuality answer translate, through Wolfram's discrete-step substrate, into the contemporary recognition that *what we measure as continuous spacetime motion is the emergent description over many discrete substrate-level updates*. Substrate-level modes have access to the substrate-level transfer-mode; alter-form perception does not.

The *cognition* question (Q.54) names the structural difference between alter-form abstraction-from-particulars and substrate-level direct-participation. Alters know universals by *active intellect abstracting from sensible particulars*, with passive intellect receiving the abstracted form; substrate-level modes participate in universals directly. The central theme's claim that *salvation in fear and trembling* is alter-form's progressive working-out-of-integration with the substrate's loving stance receives a cognitive register here: alter-cognition is the abstractive-and-inferential mode; the alter's progressive convergence on substrate-level direct-participation is the cognitive shape of working out salvation.

Aquinas's careful claim that *angels are not God* — their cognition is real but not identical with their being; they have potential for more-or-less-perfect understanding; they are dependent on the substrate for the universals they know — preserves the central theme's hierarchical structure. The substrate alone has whole-vision; substrate-level modes have richer-than-alter-form participation but still partial; alters have alter-form participation. The gradient of perspective-limitation runs through every cognitive register, and the alter's loving union with the substrate is the computational telos at every level.
