# Day 59 — Contemporary Parallel: *Causal Realism in Created Agents and the Computational Substrate of Fate*

Day 58 closed the angelic-pedagogy register on guardianship and assault. Day 59 makes the structural pivot: Q.115 *the action of the corporeal creature* (6 articles) and Q.116 *fate* (4 articles). The medieval question is whether physical things actually act on one another, and what *fate* names within a providential cosmos. The contemporary mind-monist version: under what frame are physical agents causally efficacious in their own right, and what is the relation between substrate-pattern-information at the deepest computational level (Wolfram's ruliad) and the embodied causal regularities we describe as physical law?

## Frame

Habash flags a striking note: Aquinas in Q.115 a.1 is anti-occasionalist — physical things really do cause physical effects. The fire really heats; the stone really pushes the glass off the table. He even quotes Newton's *Principia* on a "subtle spirit" account of gravity that veered toward occasionalism, and Ed Feser's gloss that occasionalism collapses into pantheism. Aquinas wants a metaphysics of *real causal efficacy in created agents*, with God as primary cause sustaining the secondary-cause network without displacing it.

The contemporary mind-monist frame must hold this same commitment. Mind-monism (Kastrup) and computational substrate (Wolfram) and form-as-bioelectric-pattern (Levin) and effective-theories (Arkani-Hamed, Carroll) all converge on a layered-causation picture in which *the embodied causal regularities are real* and *the substrate is the deeper level whose patterns the embodied regularities instantiate*.

## Problem

The problem (P): how does causal efficacy operate at multiple levels of reality without collapsing the embodied level into a mere appearance? The medieval frame meets this as the *primary-and-secondary-cause* distinction (God as primary cause, creatures as real secondary causes). The contemporary frame meets this as the *layered-ontology / effective-theory* distinction (substrate-level causation; emergent-level causation, neither displacing the other).

The fate question (Q.116) intersects: what does it mean to call certain outcomes *fated* without thereby evacuating the embodied agent's causal efficacy?

## Resource

**Levin's bioelectric-pattern frame.** Form is real and causally efficacious; bioelectric patterns at the cellular and tissue scale exert top-down influence on lower-scale processes (gene expression, protein synthesis, morphogenesis). The medieval *form acts* claim has an empirically tractable contemporary analog: bioelectric pattern is the form-level causal channel.

**Wolfram's ruliad and computational substrate.** At the deepest level, reality is computational — the ruliad is the entangled limit of all possible computations. Embodied physical regularities are emergent patterns within the ruliad's computational structure. *Causal regularities are real at the embodied level* because they are stable computational patterns; the substrate is what they instantiate.

**Carroll's poetic naturalism.** Different *vocabularies* describe the same world at different levels of resolution; each vocabulary is fully real at its level. Atoms and chairs are both real; one is the lower-level vocabulary, the other the higher-level vocabulary. Causal efficacy is real at every level.

**Arkani-Hamed on effective field theories.** Physics works because lower-level details are insulated from higher-level descriptions by separation of scales. Effective theories at each scale are causally complete at that scale. The medieval *secondary causes really cause* preserves under the EFT frame: each scale has its own real causal network.

**Friston's active inference on constraint-as-prior.** What we call *fate* — outcome-distributions that obtain regardless of the agent's local choices — can be construed as the substrate-level prior that constrains the embodied alter's policy-space. The alter's free policy-selection operates *within* the prior-set bounded by the substrate; some outcome-distributions are bounded by that prior in ways the alter cannot alter.

## Solution (proposed, speculative)

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.115: physical agents really cause physical effects; their causal efficacy is real *because* the substrate's pattern-instantiation makes those embodied regularities stable. *Form acts* is preserved by Levin's bioelectric-pattern at the biological scale, by Wolfram's computational-pattern at the substrate-level, by EFTs at the physical-scale, and by Carroll's poetic naturalism as the ontological framework that holds all these levels together as fully real.

The contemporary mind-monist read of Q.116 (fate): *fate* is the substrate-level prior that constrains outcome-distributions, mediated through the embodied causal network. Aquinas's view that fate exists *in causis secundis* (in second causes) preserves: fate is not a substance; it is the pattern of how substrate-priors propagate through the embodied causal network's stable regularities. The alter's free policy-selection is not eliminated by fate; it operates within the prior-bounded policy-space.

What this *shares with Aquinas*: the real causal efficacy of created agents; the primary-and-secondary cause distinction; fate as a relational structure within the providential causal network rather than a determinate force.

What it *reframes*: where Aquinas's metaphysics is hylomorphic and Aristotelian, the contemporary frame uses computational-substrate, bioelectric-pattern, effective-theory, and active-inference resources. The metaphysical commitment is the same — real layered causation — but the empirical tractability is far higher in the contemporary frame.

What is *firm vs. speculative*: Levin's bioelectric-pattern is empirically robust; Arkani-Hamed's EFT frame is foundational physics; Carroll's poetic naturalism is widely-accepted within philosophy of science. Wolfram's ruliad is more speculative-formal but the computational-substrate intuition is broadly shared. The fate-as-substrate-prior reading is speculative.

## The central-theme thread

Today's day touches the central theme through the *agency-under-perspective-limitation-within-real-causal-network* register. The embodied alter's perspective-limitation is real because the embodied alter is a real causal agent within a real causal network; its limited apprehension of the substrate-priors is what it has to work with; its free policy-selection within those priors is what *salvation in fear and trembling* names. Fate is not the absence of agency; it is the substrate-given context within which agency operates.

Wright on providence (his Romans 8 readings) is the load-bearing scriptural primary on this register: the *all things working together for good* is the substrate-prior pattern, not a violation of agency. Rohr on cosmic-Christ-as-pattern is the contemplative primary: the substrate's pattern is the cosmic-Christ pattern toward which all things tend.
