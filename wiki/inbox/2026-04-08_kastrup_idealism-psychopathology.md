## Summary
An essay published January 2, 2026 at the Essentia Foundation (authored or co-authored by Kastrup as executive director) argues that Analytic Idealism could resolve the current conceptual impasse in psychiatry. The essay reinterprets all major psychiatric disorders as disturbances in the dissociative boundary between the individual "alter" and universal consciousness — rather than as brain malfunctions. The resulting framework enables a deconstruction of neuroscientific data from an idealist perspective and suggests a therapeutic shift toward boundary modulation rather than symptom management. The essay is part of Kastrup's broader 2025–2026 project of applying analytic idealism to specific empirical domains (he also published "Analytic Idealism in a Nutshell" and "The Daimon and the Soul of the West" in 2025).

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This essay extends analytic idealism from ontological argument to clinical application — a significant development. The psychopathology framework operationalizes the dissociative model (individuals as alters of universal consciousness) in an empirically engaged domain, making testable predictions about therapeutic outcomes. This is the most recent primary source from Kastrup's program available in 2026 and demonstrates the research productivity of the analytic idealism tradition. It also directly connects to several cross-program items (CROSS-001 on substrate-independence of consciousness, the Friston-Levin bridge).
