## Summary
On *The Great Simplification* (Ep. 217), Nate Hagens hosts McGilchrist for a discussion on how left-hemisphere dominance obscures our perception of value — particularly abstract goods like truth, goodness, and beauty — and how the corrective is to deliberately cultivate "spaciousness": slowness, silence, deep listening, and resistance to the productivity mania of contemporary culture. McGilchrist links this practice directly to right-hemisphere modes of attention and frames it as a civilizational therapeutic, not merely a personal one. The episode also engages crisis dynamics (climate, polarization, meaning-loss) through the lens of *The Matter With Things*.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is McGilchrist applying the divided-brain framework to a *practice* — spaciousness as a remediation for left-hemisphere capture. It's a conversion of his theoretical apparatus into prescriptive form, which is exactly the move that builds research-program track record under MacIntyrean criteria (a tradition advances by showing what *to do* given its diagnosis). It also positions the McGilchrist program in direct conversation with the Great Simplification community (Hagens' polycrisis ecosystem) — broadening the program's interlocutors beyond the usual philosophy / theology axis.
