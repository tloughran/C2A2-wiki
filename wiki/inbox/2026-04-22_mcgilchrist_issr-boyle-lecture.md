## Summary
On April 16, 2026, McGilchrist delivered the prestigious International Society for Science and Religion (ISSR) Digital Boyle Lecture on Science and Religion, organized in partnership with St Mary-le-Bow church. The Boyle Lectures are the oldest continuous science-and-religion lectureship in the English-speaking world (est. 1692). The lecture was followed by the Digital Boyle Discussion chaired by ISSR President Professor Niels Gregersen, with respondents Michael J Reiss, Mari van Emmerik, Chris Oldfield, and Marius Dorobantu — locating McGilchrist's hemispheric theory explicitly inside the academic science-and-religion conversation and subjecting it to peer response from working theologians and scientists.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is McGilchrist's first delivery at the Boyle Lectures — the flagship venue for science/religion dialogue — which represents institutional uptake of hemispheric theory by the professional science-and-religion field. It shifts the thesis's standing from philosophical/cultural commentary toward a recognized research program with respondents in theology, philosophy of biology (Reiss), and AI/theology (Dorobantu). It also directly addresses the question C2A2 has flagged as central: can McGilchrist's framework do normative work within religious-scientific inquiry, or does it remain a diagnostic tool?
