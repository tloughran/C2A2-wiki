## Summary
In this 74-minute unscripted livestream Q&A, Wolfram addresses questions about machine intelligence and consciousness, AI automation and the future of work, AI hype cycles vs. real progress, human senses' effect on science, automated visualization and interfaces, and genetic engineering. The consciousness and AI segments are notable: Wolfram has previously argued that LLMs "put the final nail in the coffin" of the idea that consciousness requires something beyond physics, and that our sense of a single thread of experience is an observer-dependent feature, not an ontological given.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This Q&A provides Wolfram's most current public thinking on consciousness and AI as of March 2026, extending his observer-theory framework. His claim that consciousness does not require anything beyond physics — combined with his Ruliad framework where observers are computational paths — positions him distinctly within the C2A2 Consciousness Cluster: he is the physicalist/computationalist voice against Kastrup's idealism and Hoffman's conscious realism, yet his observer-dependence framework creates unexpected convergences with both.
