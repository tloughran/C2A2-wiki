## Summary
Lyons, Pio-Lopez & Levin (Preprints.org, April 2026) reframe AI alignment in economic terms as the problem of externalities — homeostatically/allostatically relevant events that fall outside an agent's "cognitive light cone" — rather than as a goal-alignment problem. They argue that the same homeostatic principles that prevent cancer (cellular defection from the body's cooperative morphogenetic community) provide a substrate-independent template for keeping AI systems aligned with human flourishing. The paper extends Levin's diverse-intelligence and cognitive-light-cone framework into a formal proposal for AI alignment grounded in biology rather than in classical decision theory.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is the most direct articulation to date of PRS-05 (AI alignment via biology) — Levin's program operationalized as an alignment paradigm. It moves the Levin × C2A2 alignment thesis from suggestive analogy ("biology achieves multi-scale cooperative agency that AI lacks") to a formal program: *measure and design AI systems by how well their cognitive light cones contain the homeostatically-relevant consequences of their actions.* It also tightens Levin's continuity thesis: cancer and AI misalignment are framed as the same failure mode at different scales — collective coherence breaking down because subunits' cognitive light cones no longer overlap with the larger whole's homeostatic horizon.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: AI alignment is typically framed in terms of value or goal alignment, but this framing is brittle — it requires specifying what humans want and verifying AI internal states. It does not naturally scale to multi-agent ecosystems and lacks a measurable homeostatic primitive.
  Resource: Reframing alignment as externality management — defining externalities as homeostatically/allostatically relevant events outside an agent's cognitive light cone, and treating alignment as the convergence of cognitive light cones across agents at different scales.
  Solution: A homeostatic, biology-grounded definition of alignment that admits a measurable structural criterion (do agent cognitive light cones overlap on the relevant homeostatic variables?), generalizes across substrates (cells, organisms, AI), and inherits the empirical track record of cancer biology — where cellular misalignment is well characterized and treatable.
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: The paper's core formal move is to expand the economic concept of externality with the Levin-program concept of cognitive light cone, producing a definition of alignment that is (a) formal, (b) substrate-agnostic, and (c) empirically anchored in cancer biology where the same failure mode is documented.

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: Levin's cancer framework (cellular defection from morphogenetic community) and the AI-alignment problem (AI systems pursuing objectives at odds with human/social welfare) have been treated as separate domains, despite obvious structural parallels. No formal bridge has connected them.
  Resource: Cognitive-light-cone framework as the shared currency between cancer and AI alignment — both are diagnosed as failures of light-cone alignment between subunit and whole, where the subunit's homeostatically-relevant horizon does not include the costs imposed on the larger collective.
  Solution: A unified theoretical framework treating cancer, organizational dysfunction, and AI misalignment as instances of one failure mode — externality generation due to cognitive-light-cone mismatch — opening cross-domain transfer of interventions (e.g., bioelectric coherence-restoration techniques as a model for AI alignment interventions).
  Confidence: Medium-High
  Evidence: The paper explicitly connects bioelectric coherence loss in cancer with cognitive-light-cone mismatch in AI, mobilizing the homeostatic-principles framework as the connective tissue.
