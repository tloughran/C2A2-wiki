## Summary
The 2026 Center for Action and Contemplation (CAC) Daily Meditations program — running for the entire calendar year, contributed by Rohr, CAC faculty (James Finley, Brian McLaren, Mike Petrow, Cassidy Hall), and guest teachers — operates under a single theme: *Good News for a Fractured World*. Stated purpose: "to reclaim the Bible's message of liberation and wholeness for our time" and to ask "how we can reclaim the Bible as truly good news, rather than a weapon that wounds." Methodologically the most significant move: the 2026 program will *not* read the Bible as if it were "a divinely dictated book that descended out of heaven on a parachute," but instead as "a set of precious literary artifacts that emerge in the unfolding story of humanity," taking seriously its historical, social, ecological, economic, and political context. This is a major and explicit hermeneutical shift in CAC's public catechesis — toward exactly the kind of contextual, critical-realist reading that Wright has been arguing for since 1992. The published meditations through the past four months (Jan–early May 2026) instantiate this shift week by week.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is the largest single Rohr-program development of 2026 and arguably the most consequential rohr-wiki update of the year. Three reasons. First: the explicit adoption of a contextual, anti-fundamentalist, literary-artifacts hermeneutic places Rohr's program *much closer to Wright's critical realism* than any prior Rohr articulation, opening a substantial new wright × rohr cross-program convergence on biblical hermeneutics. Second: "good news for a fractured world" is the precise pastoral-theological articulation of the Summa 2026 project's central theme — the project is, in essence, an attempt to make traditions speak good news to a fractured world by interacting them. Third: the year-long structure means the daily syntheses can draw on a steady stream of CAC material that is hermeneutically aligned with the project's other ground-truth oracle (Wright). This is the missing piece that lets the Wright + Rohr pairing function as a *single hermeneutical posture* across both ground-truth oracles, rather than as two separately-anchored voices.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: Rohr's contemplative-mystical program has historically been read as soft on textual specificity — "the Universal Christ" is easily caricatured as a way of dissolving the particularity of Scripture into a vague spiritual gestalt. This caricature has limited Rohr's usability as a partner for biblical-theology programs (Wright, Stump) that take canonical text seriously.
  Resource: The 2026 CAC Daily Meditations theme: an explicit, year-long hermeneutical commitment to reading Scripture as historically situated literary artifact, in context, with attention to social, economic, ecological, and political dimensions — *not* as a dehistoricized symbolic resource and *not* as a divinely-dictated proposition-set.
  Solution: A late-Rohr public hermeneutic that closes the gap between contemplative-mystical engagement with Scripture and critical-realist biblical-theological engagement with Scripture. Lets the Rohr agent cite a hermeneutic that Wright would approve of, opening a productive wright × rohr methodological partnership rather than a tradition-pairing held together only by shared theological commitments.
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: The CAC theme statement; the published 2026 meditations through Jan–early May; corroboration in the Brian McLaren framing PDF posted on CAC's site.

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: The C2A2 master wiki needs a contemplative-mystical articulation of the Summa 2026 central theme — "loving unity is the computational telos; perspective-limitation individuates conscious agents" — that is *operational* rather than purely metaphysical. Rohr's existing wiki has the metaphysical articulation (Universal Christ) but not the practice-level articulation.
  Resource: The 2026 Daily Meditations frame: contemplative practice (sit meditation, lectio divina, action-and-contemplation rhythm) as the operational pathway by which agents move from individuated perspective-limitation toward inhabited loving unity. Pairs with Brian McLaren's framing of the year as "Bible inviting contemplative practice that nurtures ongoing transformation and draws toward acts of love that ripple through lives and communities."
  Solution: The contemplative-practice operational layer of the Summa 2026 central theme — what the Wright/Stump-side metaphysics actually requires of an agent in lived practice. Closes the gap between metaphysical articulation (Universal Christ as cosmic pattern) and lived articulation (contemplative practice as the way the cosmic pattern is inhabited).
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: The Daily Meditations format itself; CAC's "action and contemplation" mission; the year's stated focus on practice that "nurtures ongoing transformation."

PRS-CANDIDATE-03:
  Problem: Rohr's Universal-Christ frame, when applied to Scripture, has historically tended to flatten differences between texts — every text gets pulled toward the cosmic-Christ pattern. This is theologically powerful but exegetically lossy.
  Resource: The 2026 commitment to reading texts in their specific historical, social, and political context preserves textual specificity *while still* situating the texts within Rohr's cosmic-Christ frame. The contextual reading is not in opposition to the cosmic-Christ reading but provides the material on which the cosmic-Christ reading operates.
  Solution: A late-Rohr two-level hermeneutic — context-first reading at the literary-artifact level, cosmic-Christ-pattern reading at the contemplative level, with both held together rather than collapsed. Resolves a long-standing internal tension in Rohr's program and gives the rohr × stump cross-link a more defensible methodological footing.
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: The 2026 theme's explicit dual commitment to context AND liberation/wholeness; the published meditations' demonstrated practice of holding both layers; the broader trajectory of post-*Universal Christ* Rohr to engage critical biblical scholarship more directly.
