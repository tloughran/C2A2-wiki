## Summary
A 2025 theoretical paper in Perspectives on Psychological Science (Vol. 20, pp. 639-668) co-authored by Brian P. Don, Jeffry A. Simpson, Barbara L. Fredrickson, and Sara B. Algoe. The paper introduces Interparental Positivity Spillover Theory (IPST): the claim that positive relational interactions between parents — gratitude, capitalization, shared laughter — spill over to children through three mechanisms: (1) children's experience of positive emotion in the family field, (2) beneficially altered perceptions of parents, and (3) emulation of parents' positive interpersonal behaviors. PMC: PMC12231790; PubMed: 38252555.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is the first formal extension of positivity resonance theory beyond dyadic adult interaction into the family system as a multi-agent positivity field. Existing PRS triplets cover dyadic, weak-tie, and community-scale positivity resonance (PRS-07 through PRS-16), but no triplet yet captures vertical/intergenerational transmission. IPST closes a major gap and adds a developmentally-grounded mechanism for how a tradition's affective signature is reproduced across generations — directly relevant to MacIntyre's question of how living traditions are transmitted.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: Positivity resonance theory has been developed primarily for dyadic adult interactions and weak-tie community-scale interactions, but the developmental pathway by which children acquire the capacity for positivity resonance — and the role of the parent-pair dyad as a developmental positivity field — has been theoretically underspecified
  Resource: Don, Simpson, Fredrickson, & Algoe's Interparental Positivity Spillover Theory (IPST): a three-mechanism account of how interparental positive interactions transmit to children via (a) the child's own positive emotional experience, (b) altered perceptions of the parents, (c) emulated interpersonal behaviors
  Solution: A developmental extension of positivity resonance theory: the parent-pair functions as a positivity field that scaffolds the child's nervous system, perception, and behavior — providing the biographical mechanism by which capacity for positivity resonance is constructed in the first place
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Published peer-reviewed theoretical paper in Perspectives on Psychological Science, 2025; identifies three distinct mechanism pathways with empirical sub-claims under each

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: For the C2A2 project, the question of how a tradition's affective signature (its lived emotional grammar — what gratitude, joy, lament, and reconciliation look like inside that tradition) is reproduced across generations has been treated implicitly but not theoretically modeled
  Resource: IPST's spillover mechanism, when generalized beyond the parent-child dyad, suggests that any high-positivity-resonance dyad inside a tradition functions as a "spillover field" for tradition-naive observers — including children, students, AI agents, and second-tradition learners
  Solution: Treat tradition-internal high-resonance dyads (mentor-student, master-apprentice, rabbi-disciple, lab head-postdoc) as spillover fields whose emotional grammar transmits to observers via the same three mechanisms IPST identifies — providing C2A2 with a developmental theory of tradition acquisition
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: This is a theoretical extension of IPST beyond its original parent-child scope; the three-mechanism structure is general and does not depend on biological parenthood

## Cross-Tradition Signals
- **C2A2 master signal (high relevance):** IPST gives positivity resonance a developmental/intergenerational dimension that maps directly onto MacIntyre's question of how traditions are transmitted. This is the first PRS triplet that addresses tradition-acquisition as a developmental rather than purely cognitive process.
- **Stump connection:** Stump's narrative epistemology (PRS-07, PRS-08) argues that some knowledge is only accessible via second-personal acquaintance with persons in narrative; IPST provides the empirical/developmental mechanism by which such second-personal knowing becomes constitutive — children learn what gratitude is not by definition but by witnessing it between parents.
- **McGilchrist connection:** Spillover via "altered perceptions of parents" (mechanism 2) is structurally a right-hemisphere/contextual-knowing phenomenon — the child sees the parent as a person-in-loving-relation rather than as a function-bearer; this is McGilchrist's RH primacy thesis at a developmental level.
- **Levin connection:** IPST's three-mechanism cascade is structurally similar to Levin's morphogenetic field — the parent-pair generates a field whose properties shape the developing child, not by direct instruction but by the field's gradients. A "morphogenetic" account of moral-emotional development.
- **Friston connection:** Positivity-resonance fields can be modeled as low-level prior distributions that the child's predictive coding system inherits — IPST is a candidate empirical instantiation of a Fristonian account of how priors are transmitted intergenerationally.
- **Hawkins connection:** Mechanism 3 (behavioral emulation) is straightforward HTM: children build cortical-column models of parental positive interaction patterns and replay them in their own future relationships.
