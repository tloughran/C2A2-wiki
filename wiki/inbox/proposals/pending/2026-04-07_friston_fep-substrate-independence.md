## Summary
Friston and colleagues present a comprehensive synthesis of the free energy principle (FEP) as a unified theory of self-organization, applying it from single cells to social systems. The paper formalizes the claim that any system maintaining a Markov blanket — a statistical boundary separating internal from external states — necessarily minimizes free energy. This makes FEP formally substrate-independent: it applies to neurons, cells, organisms, and potentially AI systems.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is the most direct confirmation yet of FINDING-002 in the C2A2 Pattern Detector findings: if any Markov-blanketed system minimizes free energy, then Levin's bioelectric morphogenetic networks ARE active inference systems, not merely analogues. The paper explicitly addresses non-neural substrates.
