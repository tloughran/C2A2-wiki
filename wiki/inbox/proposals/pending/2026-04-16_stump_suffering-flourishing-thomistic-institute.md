## Summary
A long-form conversation between Stump and Fr. Gregory Pine on the Thomistic Institute podcast, exploring the relationship between suffering and human flourishing. Stump discusses how stories illuminate the arcs of human lives and how suffering, understood through a Thomistic lens, can be a condition for — not an obstacle to — flourishing.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is Stump's most recent public-facing articulation of her core program, making the suffering-flourishing connection explicit in a way that directly bridges her philosophical work and the C2A2 project's normative claims. The podcast format also makes it accessible as a pedagogical resource for tradition-learners.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: The relationship between suffering and flourishing is typically framed as oppositional — flourishing is what happens when suffering stops
  Resource: Stump's Thomistic account of suffering as instrumentally ordered to a higher good (personal union / beatitudo), articulated in accessible podcast format
  Solution: Suffering and flourishing are not opposed but teleologically linked — suffering can be a condition for the deepest forms of human flourishing, understood as union with persons and with God
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: Podcast discussion explicitly connects suffering narratives to human flourishing through Thomistic final causality
