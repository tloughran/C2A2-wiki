## Summary
The TRACE Institute is a research-organization-grade moonshot initiative founded and led by Donald Hoffman and Chetan Prakash (with a team of frontier scientists). Its mission is the formal development of trace logic, decorated permutations, and conscious agent networks as the foundational machinery from which spacetime, quantum mechanics, and physical interactions emerge. This is the first formal *institutional* embodiment of the Hoffman–Prakash research program and represents a significant escalation from journal-paper-by-paper publishing into a coordinated long-horizon research operation. The institute appears to host the canonical statements of the trace-logic program and serves as the institutional partner of the Mind-Body Solution dialogue series, the HOMeHOPe symposium track, and the Thoughtforms-Life talks (Levin, host).

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
Up to this point Hoffman's program has been carried by individual papers and talks scattered across venues (PRS-07 through PRS-18 in the Hoffman wiki). The TRACE Institute is the first organizational commitment that signals the program intends to outlive any single paper or career. For the C2A2 wiki, this is significant because: (a) it provides a stable canonical-source URL for tracking the Hoffman program over time; (b) it formalizes Prakash as co-principal rather than co-author, which has implications for who the C2A2 dispatch system should treat as voice-of-the-tradition; (c) it suggests a research program with infrastructure (research staff, sustained funding, long-horizon projects) — placing Hoffman's program closer in institutional-maturity terms to Levin's Allen Discovery Center and Friston's UCL group than to a single-PI lab.
