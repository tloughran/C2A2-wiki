## Summary
A co-authored paper by Stump and Patrick Zoll in AGATHEOS — European Journal for Philosophy of Religion (Vol. 1, No. 2, 2024, pp. 28-45). The article makes the case that Aquinas's metaphysics and ethics together constitute a philosophical "good" — i.e., that Thomism functions as an intellectual resource for contemporary philosophy of religion not because it is historically interesting but because its substantive claims are presently load-bearing for problems analytic philosophy cannot solve on its own. A PDF copy is hosted at hfph.de/files/2025/05/2-stump-and-zoll-for-publishing.pdf.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is a deliberate methodological statement by Stump (with co-author Zoll) about why Thomism deserves a seat at the analytic-philosophy table — a kind of programmatic essay defending the live-tradition status that the C2A2 wiki uses as its operational definition of tradition vitality (PRS-06, the MacIntyre criterion). It is exactly the kind of meta-level paper that should be captured because it articulates Stump's case for what her own program is for.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: Analytic philosophy of religion has tended either to treat Aquinas as a historical curio or to extract isolated arguments from his system without committing to the metaphysical framework that makes those arguments work — leaving Thomism perpetually domesticated and therefore unable to challenge the host paradigm
  Resource: Stump and Zoll's "Good of Thomas Aquinas" essay, which argues that the metaphysical-ethical unity of Aquinas's system is itself the contribution — not particular doctrines but the integrated framework
  Solution: A defense of Thomism as a live, integrative research program whose value is exactly its non-extractability — the framework is the contribution, and partial appropriation always loses what was philosophically distinctive
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: AGATHEOS publication, peer-reviewed, co-authored with Zoll (Hochschule für Philosophie München); part of an ongoing programmatic effort by Stump to defend Thomism's live-tradition status in analytic philosophy
