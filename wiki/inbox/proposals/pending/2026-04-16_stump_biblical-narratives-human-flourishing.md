## Summary
An edited volume (Routledge, co-edited with Judith Wolfe) in the series Routledge Studies in Analytic and Systematic Theology. The volume explores the thesis that biblical narratives transmit a distinctive kind of knowledge relevant to human flourishing — non-propositional, second-person knowledge that cannot be reduced to doctrinal claims.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This volume extends Stump's "Franciscan knowledge" program into a collaborative, multi-author format — showing that her narrative epistemology is generating a research program, not just a single-author thesis. This is direct evidence of tradition vitality by the MacIntyre criterion: Stump's program is producing new questions and attracting new inquirers.
