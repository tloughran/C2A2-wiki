## Summary
A pair of preprints (gluon: arXiv:2602.12176, posted Feb 12, 2026; graviton extension submitted March 4, 2026) shows that single-minus tree amplitudes — long assumed to vanish in massless gauge theories and gravity — in fact survive on a precisely defined "half-collinear" slice of momentum space, and admit clean closed-form expressions. The key formula was first conjectured by GPT-5.2 Pro, then proved and independently verified by the human authors (IAS, OpenAI, Vanderbilt, Cambridge, Harvard). Arkani-Hamed (IAS) endorsed the work as "clearly journal-level research advancing the frontiers of theoretical physics" and noted he had been curious about these specific scattering processes for fifteen years. Although Arkani-Hamed is not a co-author, the result lands directly inside his program — it overturns a fifteen-year-old assumption about a structural property of amplitudes — and it is the first widely-noted theoretical-physics result where an LLM produced a closed-form conjecture that survived peer-level verification.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
Three reasons make this a high-priority capture for the Arkani-Hamed wiki:
1. **Directly inside his amplitude program.** The result concerns the kinematic structure of tree-level scattering amplitudes — exactly the territory the amplituhedron and positive-geometry programs are organized around. A long-standing belief that single-minus amplitudes vanish at tree level has been overturned on a precise kinematic slice; this changes what counts as "the simplest" scattering data and may open new entry points to the geometric reformulations Arkani-Hamed has championed.
2. **Methodological inflection point.** The work is the first prominent case of LLM-conjectured-then-verified theoretical physics. Arkani-Hamed's program has long emphasized "the inevitability of the result" (PRS-04) as an epistemic guide; the new methodology — AI-conjecture + human-proof + consistency-check — is a candidate addition to that toolbox and to the broader discussion of what counts as discovery in pre-empirical fundamental physics. This is a methodological PRS, not just a content PRS.
3. **Carroll's Bayesian standard, satisfied differently.** Where the formal positive-geometry papers can struggle to meet Carroll's "tangible explanatory gain over established physics" criterion (PRS-Carroll-03), this result is unambiguous: a previously-believed-zero quantity is shown to be nonzero, with a closed-form expression. It is the kind of advance Carroll's epistemology is designed to certify, and it sits squarely inside Arkani-Hamed's program.
