## Summary
Sean Carroll hosts MIT theoretical physicist Daniel Harlow (Mindscape Episode #349, March 30, 2026) for a deep discussion on what quantum gravity teaches us about quantum mechanics — inverting the usual framing. Harlow and collaborators propose a framework where the observer is treated as fundamentally classical and special within the theory, with new rules for dealing with observers built into the formalism. This is reminiscent of Copenhagen but with more precise mathematical definitions. The episode explores why decades of effort have not produced convergence on reconciling general relativity with quantum mechanics, and how ideas from black hole radiation, AdS/CFT, and quantum information theory are reshaping the foundations.
