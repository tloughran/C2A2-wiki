## Summary
Wolfram publishes a major essay (February 2026) applying the Ruliad framework directly to classical metaphysical questions — "What ultimately is there?" He argues that the Ruliad is the answer: everything that exists is the entangled limit of all possible computations. He introduces "emes" as the ultimate raw material for existence — entities with no intrinsic structure except distinctness — and shows how relations between emes build up the Ruliad. Crucially, he foregrounds the observer's role: observers embedded within the Ruliad necessarily perceive it in particular ways, and our "inner experiences" must be represented within it. This essay was written partly to launch the Wolfram Institute's Templeton-funded "Computational Metaphysics" project.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is Wolfram's most explicit engagement with metaphysics to date. Previous Wolfram Physics Project work focused on deriving physics from hypergraph rules; this essay asks what the Ruliad implies about the nature of existence itself. The wiki currently has Wolfram PRS triplets focused on computational irreducibility, observer-dependent spacetime, and the Ruliad as a universal structure. This essay adds: (1) a formal metaphysical claim (the Ruliad as the answer to "what exists"), (2) the concept of "emes" as ontological primitives, and (3) explicit engagement with the philosophical tradition — Wolfram is now deliberately positioning the Ruliad in conversation with academic metaphysics.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

  Resource: The Ruliad as a formally defined mathematical object — the entangled limit of all possible computations — proposed as the ultimate ontological ground
  Solution: A computational metaphysics: "what exists" = the Ruliad; "what we perceive" = our observer-slice of it; "why these laws of physics" = because observers like us necessarily sample the Ruliad in ways that produce these regularities
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Wolfram's explicit framing of the essay as an answer to the metaphysical question, supported by the Templeton "Computational Metaphysics" grant

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: The relationship between abstract mathematical structures and concrete physical reality (the "unreasonable effectiveness of mathematics" problem) lacks a satisfying explanation
  Resource: "Emes" — ontological primitives with no intrinsic properties except distinctness, from which all structure emerges through relations
  Solution: Mathematics and physics are not separate domains — both are observer-slices of the same Ruliad; mathematics appears effective because it samples the same underlying structure that physics samples
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: Wolfram's argument that emes are the raw material for both mathematical and physical structure
