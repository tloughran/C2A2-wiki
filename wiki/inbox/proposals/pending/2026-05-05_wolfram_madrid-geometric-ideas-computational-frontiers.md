## Summary
Wolfram delivered the closing colloquium (online) of a joint AQUACELL–Wolfram Institute event held at the Faculty of Mathematical Sciences, Universidad Complutense de Madrid, themed "From Geometric Ideas to Computational Frontiers." The event explored synergies between the geometric perspective and the computational paradigm — connecting geometry, dynamics, modeling, and scientific computation in the study of complex phenomena — and culminated in Wolfram's address situating the Wolfram Physics Project within that synthesis.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is the first significant academic-mathematical engagement of the Wolfram Physics Project with a traditional mathematics-and-geometry research community. It signals that the program is being positioned not as an outsider claim about physics but as a participant in the long arc from differential geometry (general relativity) and gauge theory (the Standard Model) toward the next foundational substrate. Capturing it strengthens PRS-01/PRS-02 (the program's claim to derive GR and QM from hypergraph rewriting) by adding institutional academic uptake as evidence of its viability as a research program.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: The Wolfram Physics Project has been received with skepticism in mainstream physics partly because it appears to bypass the mathematical scaffolding (differential geometry, fiber bundles, gauge symmetry) that makes contemporary physics work. Without an articulation of how the hypergraph picture *contains* or *generalizes* that scaffolding, the program reads as orthogonal rather than continuous with mature physics.
  Resource: The Madrid colloquium framing — "From Geometric Ideas to Computational Frontiers" — explicitly positions geometry and computation as complementary lenses on the same complex phenomena, with the hypergraph framework as the computational counterpart to the geometric one.
  Solution: A research-program move: Wolfram Physics is to be developed not as a replacement for geometric physics but as the substrate from which geometric physics emerges in the limit where rule-induced causal structure is dense enough to coarse-grain into a manifold. This is a programmatic commitment to interoperability with mainstream mathematical physics, not a separatist claim.
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: Joint workshop framing and the colloquium's stated motto "Ideas in Motion" exploring geometry × computation synergies, with Wolfram delivering the closing address (per Wolfram publications page entry for 2026-02-20).
