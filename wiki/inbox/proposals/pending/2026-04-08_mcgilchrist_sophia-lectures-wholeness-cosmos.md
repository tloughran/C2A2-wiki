## Summary
Iain McGilchrist delivered three public lectures at Ralston College (Savannah) on March 13–15, 2026, under the title "Wholeness, Imagination, and Cosmos." The lectures were free, open to the public, and recorded. Drawing on neuropsychology, physics, philosophy, poetry, music, painting, and myth, McGilchrist addressed three principal themes: (1) the creative dance between division and union — separation that must be brought to higher synthesis; (2) the asymmetrical nature of the cooperative relationship between the hemispheres, and the role of asymmetry throughout the cosmos, especially in life and beauty; (3) how to understand paradox — in particular how to make sense of the infinite. McGilchrist is now Chancellor of Ralston College (appointed October 2025).
