## Summary
A new paper by Nima Arkani-Hamed, Carolina Figueiredo, Lawrence J. Hall, and Claudio Andrea Manzari (arXiv:2602.17754, submitted February 19, 2026) proposes a class of theories generating the quark and lepton mass matrices at TeV scale new physics — without inducing the large flavor- and CP-violating processes typically thought to push flavor origins above ~100 TeV. The models introduce vector-like leptons and quarks, with mass mixings and Yukawa couplings to Standard Model fields encoded in "chains" reminiscent of dimensional deconstruction. Locality along these chains both produces the hierarchical Standard Model Yukawa pattern and suppresses CP/flavor-violating effects. A simple extension generates neutrino masses parametrically tied to the square of the electron Yukawa.
