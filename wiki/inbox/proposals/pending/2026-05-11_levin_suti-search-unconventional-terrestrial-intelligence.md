## Summary
Levin introduces SUTI ("Search for Unconventional Terrestrial Intelligence") as a research program complementary to SETI: rather than scanning the sky, scan our own planet for cognitive systems whose substrates and behaviors fall outside conventional definitions of mind — swarms, software AIs, embodied autonomous robotics, basal cognition of non-brainy life forms (cells, organs), synthetic biological entities, and chimeras/hybrids/hybrots fusing evolved and engineered components. SUTI is framed as an antidote to "mind blindness" and an enlargement of moral and scientific concern to the diverse intelligences already among us.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
SUTI is the first explicit, named operational *program* (with epistemic protocols and target classes) that operationalizes Levin's diverse-intelligence framework as an organized research and detection effort, rather than a methodological commentary. It promotes mind blindness (PRS-12) from epistemic obstacle to actionable research target, and aligns directly with the C2A2 tradition-accelerator's stated goal of detecting agency in unconventional substrates. It also creates a public-facing recruitment vehicle for the program.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: We lack a coordinated research program for *detecting* intelligence in non-standard terrestrial substrates — efforts to recognize basal cognition, synthetic life, and emergent AI agency are scattered across disciplines without a shared protocol or target taxonomy
  Resource: SUTI — a named program proposing systematic search protocols for unconventional terrestrial intelligences across categories (swarms, software, embodied robots, basal cognition in non-neural life, synthetic biology, hybrid/chimera systems), built to operationalize the diverse-intelligence framework
  Solution: A shared methodological scaffold, target taxonomy, and rhetorical framing that converts "mind blindness" from an epistemic complaint into a positive detection program — enabling cross-disciplinary inventory of candidate intelligences and standardized protocols for engaging them
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Levin frames SUTI as engagement with "aliens" already among us, lists six distinct candidate categories, and ties the program explicitly to enlarging the "radius of care" (cited in his January 23 2026 evolution talk and in the standalone SUTI essay)
