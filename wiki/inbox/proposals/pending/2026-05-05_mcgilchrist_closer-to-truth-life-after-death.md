## Summary
Closer to Truth (the long-running Robert Lawrence Kuhn series on fundamental questions) released a short interview with McGilchrist on May 3, 2026 in which he is asked to locate the question of post-mortem survival on a spectrum of theories of consciousness — from strict materialism/physicalism on one end to wisdom-tradition accounts on the other. McGilchrist's argument is that the answerability of "life after death" is not a free-floating empirical question but is logically downstream of one's metaphysics of consciousness: under physicalism the question is closed by definition; under views in which consciousness is not generated by but rather correlated with the brain, the question becomes live in a way that is not anti-scientific but post-physicalist.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is a compact, public-facing distillation of where McGilchrist's hemispheric-and-attention program lands when pushed all the way down the metaphysical staircase — and it lands at a position close to the Hoffman/Kastrup consciousness-as-fundamental cluster, but reached from a different starting point (clinical neuroscience plus phenomenology rather than evolutionary game theory or analytic philosophy of mind). Closer to Truth is itself a research-program-shaping venue for the wider consciousness conversation, so a McGilchrist appearance there is also evidence that hemispheric theory is now being treated by that field as a position worth seating at the table — a continuation of the institutional upgrade flagged in PRS-19 (ISSR Boyle Lecture).

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: The question of post-mortem persistence of consciousness is treated either as taboo (physicalism) or as a matter of personal faith (theology) — with no widely accepted method for making the question scientifically tractable without either dissolving it or smuggling in undefended metaphysical commitments.
  Resource: McGilchrist's framing of life-after-death as a *consequence variable* of one's theory of consciousness — different theories (materialism/physicalism vs. consciousness-as-fundamental vs. wisdom-tradition accounts) make different post-mortem outcomes coherent or incoherent, so the question is well-posed only after the consciousness theory is named.
  Solution: A demarcation move: discussions of survival should begin by stating which theory of consciousness is in use, which converts what looks like a metaphysical free-for-all into a structured comparison of theories — making the question productive within the science-and-religion conversation rather than excluded from it.
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: McGilchrist's Closer to Truth segment frames survival explicitly as following from the theory-of-consciousness chosen.

  Resource: McGilchrist's willingness in this short-form, public-facing format to align his program with the consciousness-as-fundamental family of views — making the metaphysical commitment explicit rather than hedged.
  Solution: A clearer position-statement on McGilchrist's metaphysics that anchors his program inside the C2A2 Consciousness Cluster (Hoffman, Kastrup, Stump, McGilchrist) on grounds of ontology, not just on grounds of attention-theory — strengthening the cluster's joint case against physicalist defaults.
  Confidence: Medium

- **Kastrup (strong):** This is structurally the same move Kastrup makes in arguing that physicalism logically forecloses the survival question — both are making a meta-level claim that one's theory of consciousness is doing the load-bearing work, not the empirical evidence about survival itself. A McGilchrist–Kastrup convergence on the *demarcation* point (independent of whether they agree on idealism per se) is worth flagging strongly.
- **Hoffman (strong):** Hoffman's interface theory plus consciousness-as-fundamental yields the same family of permissive views about post-mortem persistence; the Closer to Truth piece is McGilchrist arriving at that family from the neurology side. Reinforces CROSS-001 (consciousness substrate-independence).
- **Carroll (productive tension):** Sean Carroll has used Closer to Truth–style framings to defend the strict physicalist position that the survival question is settled in the negative. McGilchrist explicitly placing his program on the *other* side of that demarcation makes the McGilchrist–Carroll axis sharper and a potentially productive node for the Pattern Detector to watch.
- **Stump (medium):** Thomistic hylomorphism makes a specific positive proposal about post-mortem persistence (the soul as form of the body, not separable substance) that is structurally different from both physicalism and Kastrupian idealism. McGilchrist's framing creates a clean three-way comparison that Stump's program is well-positioned to enter.
- **C2A2 signal:** The hemispheric divide (right = participatory engagement, left = re-presentation) maps onto the demarcation McGilchrist is drawing here — physicalism is left-hemisphere processing of the survival question; the alternative families take the question as a participatory one.
