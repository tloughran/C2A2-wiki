## Summary
Wright's major 2026 book (HarperOne / SPCK; released Feb 17, 2026), explicitly framed as the sequel to *Surprised by Hope*. The thesis: when Scripture is read whole, the dominant trajectory is not human souls ascending to a disembodied heaven but God descending to dwell with creation — God's homecoming. Wright traces this single arc from Genesis through Revelation: Eden as the originary tabernacle, Israel's tabernacle / Temple as the rehearsal, the Incarnation as decisive arrival, the Spirit-indwelt church as foretaste, and the New Jerusalem as fulfillment. The book reframes Christian eschatology from "what are we waiting for and what do we do in the meantime?" to "God is coming home to creation, and faithful improvisation here-and-now is participation in that arrival." Christianity Today flagged the book as "a theological correction notice for the modern Church." Marketed for general readers but draws on Wright's full critical-realist scholarly apparatus.

This is the most important Wright publication in the C2A2 window: it is the consolidated, late-career statement of the new-creation-eschatology program that has been building since *Surprised by Hope* (2008) and *Resurrection of the Son of God* (2003). For C2A2 it provides (a) a refreshed primary text for citing Wright's eschatology in the Summa 2026 syntheses, (b) a tighter formulation of the "God-coming-down vs. souls-going-up" reframe that the cosmic-Christ side of the network (Rohr, Stump, Kastrup) needs, and (c) explicit hermeneutical material on how a critical-realist reading of the whole biblical canon yields a *single* narrative — directly relevant to MacIntyre's tradition-constituted rationality and to the "five-act drama" already in the wright wiki. The "homecoming" frame is also a clean theological articulation of the project's central theme: perspective-limitation individuates conscious agents, but the telos is loving unity / God's presence with creation. Not yet captured anywhere in the wiki.
