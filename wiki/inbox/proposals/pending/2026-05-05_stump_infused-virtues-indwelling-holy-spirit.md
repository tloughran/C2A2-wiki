## Summary
Stump's peer-reviewed article in New Blackfriars (Volume 106, Issue 1, January 2025; published online March 2024 via Cambridge Core, DOI: 10.1017/nbf.2024.18) develops her account of the Thomistic moral life as initiated by a non-meritorious act of will-quiescence (cessation of resistance) which permits God's infusion of grace, the indwelling Holy Spirit, and the simultaneous arrival of all the infused virtues, gifts, and fruits. The paper articulates a precise philosophical psychology in which moral transformation is neither pure self-effort nor passive receipt but a cooperative dynamic between human will and divine causality.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is the published scholarly companion to PRS-09 (the Jan 2026 Thomistic Institute podcast on the cardinal virtues) and goes further by formalizing the distinction between acquired and infused virtues, the role of "will-quiescence" as a non-meritorious threshold condition, and the metaphysics of how grace alters the soul's powers. It substantively extends Stump's analytic Thomism in print form and is essential primary literature for the wiki's account of her philosophical psychology.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: Analytic philosophy lacks a rigorous account of how moral transformation can be both genuinely the agent's own act AND the work of an external causal agent (grace) — without collapsing into either Pelagian self-effort or determinist passivity
  Resource: Stump's reconstruction of Aquinas's "will-quiescence" — a state in which the will ceases to actively resist God's love without yet positively willing union; this is presented as a non-meritorious threshold condition that grace can then convert into the will of faith
  Solution: A two-stage model: (1) quiescence as the only "act" available to the unaided will, (2) infusion as the simultaneous arrival of grace, indwelling Spirit, and all infused virtues — preserving both agency and divine priority by locating them in different causal roles rather than in a competition for the same role
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Stump's published abstract: "in an instant this grace moves the person's will to the will of faith. In that same instant, the Holy Spirit comes to indwell in him and also brings into him all the infused virtues, as well as all the gifts and fruits of the Holy Spirit" (New Blackfriars 106:1)

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: Contemporary virtue ethics typically treats virtues as developmentally accumulated dispositions, but this leaves no room for the phenomenology of moral discontinuity (sudden conversion, instantaneous change of heart) that biblical and clinical literature both report
  Resource: The Thomistic distinction between acquired virtues (built up by practice) and infused virtues (received in an instant with grace), as systematized in Stump's article
  Solution: A dual-track virtue theory in which slow developmental virtue and instantaneous infused virtue are not rivals but operate at different levels of the soul's powers — providing a philosophical framework for moral discontinuity that is neither mystical nor reductive
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: Stump's framework distinguishes the "in an instant" character of infused virtue from the developmental character of acquired virtue (as treated in her parallel cardinal-virtues podcast and book chapters)

## Cross-Tradition Signals
- **MacIntyre connection (high relevance for Tom's ISMI paper):** The infused/acquired distinction echoes MacIntyre's claim that traditions develop both by slow accumulation and by tradition-redefining moments of reception from outside; Stump provides the metaphysical apparatus for the latter.
- **Friston connection:** Will-quiescence as a threshold condition for grace's "informational" reshaping of the soul is structurally analogous to active inference's claim that learning requires lowering precision on prior beliefs. Quiescence ≈ precision-relaxation.
- **Fredrickson connection:** The "indwelling" of the Spirit and the simultaneous arrival of charity-as-virtue maps onto Fredrickson's positivity resonance as the biophysiological substrate of love — could the infused virtue of charity be empirically traceable in Fredrickson's vagal-tone / oxytocin signatures?
- **Levin connection:** The instantaneous arrival of all the infused virtues (a coordinated package, not a sum of parts) is structurally similar to Levin's morphogenetic transitions — whole-organism reorganizations rather than additive change.
- **C2A2 master signal:** Stump's hylomorphic account of moral transformation — where natural powers are perfected (not replaced) by a higher cause — is a paradigm case of the tradition-bridge architecture C2A2 needs: science and theology cooperating rather than competing for the same causal slot.
