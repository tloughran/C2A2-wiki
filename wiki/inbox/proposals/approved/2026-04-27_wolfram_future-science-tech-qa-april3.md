## Summary
A 1h26m unscripted live Q&A in which Wolfram addressed (per published topic list): "What counts as knowledge with AI?", "Machine curiosity and new ways of thinking", "How will future humans look at our science?", "AI's final form", and "Exploring space and beyond." This sits inside Wolfram's regular livestream cadence and treats AI epistemology, machine cognition, and the historical positioning of present-day science as a single bundle — a recurring Wolfram move that connects the Ruliad / Computational Metaphysics framework to operational claims about AI as a knowledge-production system.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
The April 3 livestream is the freshest Wolfram public engagement on the AI-knowledge axis to be added to the wiki since the Foundation/CAG/LLM material (PRS-11) and the Mullenweg frontier-labs critique (PROP-2026-04-18-001). It is particularly valuable because Wolfram is staking a position on what AI-mediated knowledge actually is — a question the wiki's master agent has been tracking as a Wolfram-distinctive thread. The "machine curiosity and new ways of thinking" segment is the most novel reportable strand: Wolfram is starting to talk about cognition styles that are *not* human-imitative but rulial-space-native, which is a meaningful extension of the rulial-space-of-minds thesis (PRS-03, PRS-05).

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: AI systems produce outputs that pass as "knowledge" yet do not appear to satisfy traditional epistemic standards (justification, source-tracking, reproducibility). Is AI output knowledge, and if so, what kind, and with what warrant?
  Resource: Wolfram's "What counts as knowledge with AI?" framing — treating AI-mediated outputs as moves through a slice of the Ruliad rather than as testimony from a knowledge-bearing agent, with the warrant question replaced by a question about the structure of the rulial-space path the AI traversed.
  Solution: A reframing of AI epistemology in which "knowledge with AI" is a status conferred by the *geometry of the rulial-space search* rather than by the AI's own credibility — making AI knowledge a second-order property of the human–AI pipeline, not of the AI alone. Bridges Wolfram framework to standard epistemology in a fresh way.
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: April 3 livestream topic listed by Wolfram livestream archive.

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: Most AI cognition is trained to imitate human styles of thought. Does this exhaust the space of cognition, or are there forms of "machine curiosity" and "new ways of thinking" that are native to the rulial space and orthogonal to human cognition?
  Resource: Wolfram's "machine curiosity and new ways of thinking" segment — argues that present-day AI is mining a small humanlike pocket of the Ruliad and that further progress requires AI systems whose curiosity and search dynamics are not constrained by human-imitation training.
  Solution: A research-program suggestion that "AI's final form" is not a more-fluent humanlike system but a system exploring genuinely non-humanlike rulial regions — operationalizing the rulial-space-of-minds thesis as a specific research target rather than a metaphor.
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: April 3 livestream topic list, "machine curiosity" and "AI's final form" segments.

- **Hawkins (Thousand Brains / Monty):** Wolfram's "AI's final form is non-humanlike rulial-space exploration" is a direct counter-position to Hawkins' biologically-constrained AI thesis (Hawkins PRS-06 through PRS-17 in the 2026-04-16 batch). A new CROSS candidate: "Two rival foundational research programs for what 'final-form AI' should look like — biologically constrained (Hawkins) vs. rulial-space-native (Wolfram)."

Both are gesturing at a relational/structural account of higher-level properties.
- **Friston (active inference as reasoning):** Wolfram's "machine curiosity" segment is structurally close to Friston's information-gain-as-curiosity formalism (Friston PRS-09, the "active inference and artificial reasoning" paper). Both define curiosity as a search property of the agent–world system, not a psychological state.
