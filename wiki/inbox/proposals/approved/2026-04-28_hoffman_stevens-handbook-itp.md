## Summary
Hoffman's authoritative handbook chapter on the Interface Theory of Perception (ITP), published as part of the 4th edition of Stevens' Handbook of Experimental Psychology and Cognitive Neuroscience (Wiley, 2026). The chapter consolidates ITP into a textbook-grade synthesis built on two formal results: the **Fitness-Beats-Truth (FBT) Theorem**, which proves that veridical perceptions are generically driven to extinction by equally complex non-veridical perceptions tuned to fitness functions, and the **Invention of Symmetry (IOS) Theorem**, which establishes that organisms can have perceptions of 3D space and 1-D time with perfectly coordinated, predictable actions even when no spacetime structure exists in objective reality. From these, Hoffman derives a new ITP-native theory of illusions (an illusion is *an unadaptive perception that most normal perceivers have* — not a mismatch with reality, since no perception ever matches reality on ITP) and a parallel reformulation of hallucinations.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is the canonical reference-work statement of ITP. The wiki currently captures ITP via PRS-03 (the empirical claim that perception tracks fitness, not truth) but has no record of either theorem under Hoffman's name, nor of the new ITP-internal accounts of illusion and hallucination. The chapter is also the first place where Hoffman uses the IOS theorem to **directly refute** the Shepard / Tenenbaum line that perceptual regularities (e.g., the Euclidean group of 3D space) are evolutionarily-internalized regularities of the objective world — a foundational claim of mainstream perceptual science. This is high-prestige placement (Stevens' Handbook is the standard reference), so the chapter's framing will shape how ITP is taught for the next decade.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: ITP is widely cited but its two foundational mathematical results — the FBT Theorem and the IOS Theorem — have not been integrated into a single, citable, peer-published exposition that mainstream perceptual scientists can engage with directly. As long as they remain dispersed across journal articles, ITP looks like a philosophical position rather than a proven mathematical claim.
  Resource: A single textbook-grade chapter in Stevens' Handbook (Wixted ed., 2026) that states both theorems, sketches their proofs, and applies them in sequence: FBT shows veridical perceptions are evolutionarily improbable; IOS shows that lack of veridicality is fully compatible with the predictable structured perception we actually have.
  Solution: ITP is now positioned not as one option among many but as the formally-grounded null hypothesis for any evolutionary theory of perception — the one that survives both the existence proof against truth-tracking and the existence proof for non-truth-tracking but action-coordinated interfaces.
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: "Together the IOS Theorem and FBT Theorem flatly contradict the claim that the regularities of our perceptions — regularities such as the Euclidean group of three-dimensional space — are evolutionarily internalized versions of regularities in the objective world."

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: The standard theory of illusion in perceptual science presupposes veridical perception as the baseline: an illusion is a perception that fails to match reality. ITP says no perception ever matches reality, which on the standard view would entail that *all* perceptions are illusions — a reductio that has been used as a standing objection to ITP.
  Resource: Hoffman's ITP-internal redefinition: *An illusion is an unadaptive perception that most normal perceivers have under specified conditions.* The Necker cube, depth illusions, and other classics are illusions not because they fail to match objective reality but because they fail to guide adaptive behavior in the niche where they occur.
  Solution: ITP is no longer vulnerable to the "everything is an illusion" reductio. It now generates a positive research program: empirical investigation of *which* perceptions guide adaptive behavior in specific niches, with illusions and hallucinations identified relative to that adaptive standard rather than to objective truth.
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: "Here it is — the ITP theory of illusions: An illusion is an unadaptive perception that most normal perceivers have...the perception fails to guide adaptive behavior."

PRS-CANDIDATE-03:
  Problem: Conscious Realism's claim that spacetime is not fundamental needs an argument that does not depend on physics speculation (amplituhedron, scattering amplitudes) but emerges from perceptual science itself, so that perceptual scientists are forced to engage with it.
  Resource: The IOS Theorem applied to spacetime: organisms can have perceptions of 3D space and 1-D time with perfectly coordinated predictable actions in that perceived spacetime, even when objective reality has no such structure, provided the cardinality of objective states is sufficiently large. The theorem is not restricted to 3D space — it holds for arbitrary groups in any dimension.
  Solution: A perceptual-science-internal argument that spacetime is an interface artifact, decoupled from the (separate, more speculative) physics-internal arguments via the amplituhedron program. This gives Conscious Realism two independent routes to its central ontological claim — perceptual evolution and fundamental physics — strengthening the program's robustness to objections from either side.
  Confidence: Medium-High
  Evidence: "The IOS Theorem is not restricted to 3D space and 1-D time. The IOS Theorem holds for arbitrary groups of any dimension." Combined with the Reply 3 discussion of how predictable spacetime perception coexists with non-spacetime objective reality.

## Cross-Tradition Signals

- **Hawkins / reference frames** — Hawkins' cortical columns build reference frames that are *useful for action*, not veridical maps of objective reality. The IOS Theorem provides a formal underwriting of Hawkins' implicit assumption: a column can build a fully predictive, action-coordinated reference frame for an "object" even when objective reality contains nothing like that object. This deepens PRS-12 in the Hoffman wiki (Hawkins reference frames ARE Hoffman interfaces) by giving it a theorem-level grounding rather than a structural analogy. Flag for both Hawkins and Hoffman wikis.

- **Carroll / poetic naturalism** — The chapter's positive theory of illusion (unadaptive perceptions, not non-veridical ones) is precisely the move Carroll's poetic naturalism would have to make if it accepted ITP: physical talk and perceptual talk are *useful* at their respective scales, but neither has metaphysical priority. Productive tension here — Carroll insists the physical level is real; Hoffman now has a theorem-grade reason to deny it.

- **Arkani-Hamed / amplituhedron** — The IOS Theorem, generalized to arbitrary groups in any dimension, is the formal companion to the amplituhedron-derivation program: where amplituhedron physics shows spacetime can be derived *out of* a more primitive structure, IOS shows spacetime perception can persist *without* a more primitive spacetime. Together they bracket the ITP claim from above and below.

- **Levin / basal cognition** — Hoffman's reformulated theory of illusion as "unadaptive perception" works at any scale where there is an organism with adaptive behavior — including xenobots, anthrobots, and Levin's collective cellular agents. The chapter therefore extends ITP downward to basal cognition without modification, strengthening the Hoffman × Levin convergence already noted in CROSS-034.

- **Stump / Thomistic intellect** — Hoffman's evidence-based denial that perception delivers objective reality is structurally compatible with the Thomistic claim that intellect (not sense) is the seat of understanding. The chapter's machinery may be a usable resource for Stump's account of how intellect operates on a non-veridical sensory interface.
