## Summary
Second installment of Wolfram's new dialogue series with professional philosophers (following the Mark Jago Ep. 1, 2026-03-13 — see PRS-10). Wolfram speaks with Luz Christopher Seiberth (Potsdam), a Sellars scholar whose research program "Hypergraphing the Space of Reason" applies the hypergraph formalism — explicitly at Wolfram's (2021) suggestion that we can "get underneath" concepts by modelling their constituents — to the Sellarsian/Brandomian "space of reasons." Seiberth treats steps in the space of reasons as argument-map-based movements along contextually confined paths through a hypergraph; a "conceptual scheme" becomes a sub-area of the hypergraph acting as a contextual boundary for projecting classes of objects.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is the Wolfram framework migrating out of fundamental physics and into analytic philosophy of mind and language — the hypergraph is being used to model *inferential* structure (the pragmatist/inferentialist space of reasons), not just *physical* structure. It is the most explicit example yet of Wolfram's "rulial space = map of all possible minds and all possible disciplines" thesis (PRS-03, PRS-05) being operationalized by a non-physicist in a rival intellectual tradition. It extends the Wolfram × philosophy engagement opened by the Templeton "Computational Metaphysics" grant (PRS-06) and the Mark Jago dialogue (PRS-10) from metaphysics into epistemology.

## Cross-Tradition Signals

- **Stump / MacIntyre (analytic philosophy, tradition-constituted rationality):** Seiberth's Sellarsian "space of reasons" is close cousin to MacIntyre's tradition-constituted rationality — both treat rationality as embedded in contextually bounded inferential practices rather than as a view-from-nowhere. If Seiberth's hypergraph-of-reasons can represent tradition-bounded inference, this is a direct formal model for what C2A2 is trying to build. Possible new CROSS candidate: "Is the hypergraph of the space of reasons the formal structure of tradition-constituted rationality?" Bridges Wolfram ↔ Stump/MacIntyre.
- **Hoffman (trace logic / conscious-agent networks):** Hoffman's trace logic (CROSS-032, CROSS-033) generalizes Markov blankets via structures on graphs; Seiberth's conceptual-scheme-as-sub-area-of-hypergraph generalizes conceptual containers via structures on hypergraphs. Two independent uses of graph/hypergraph combinatorics to model boundaries of cognitive/inferential activity.
- **Carroll (Bayesian confirmation / methodology):** The dialogue series is itself a response to CROSS-016 / the confirmation-standard challenge — Wolfram is now showing the program can engage rigorously with external rational scrutiny, which is part (but not all) of what Carroll demands.
- **Kastrup (consciousness-first ontology):** Seiberth's "Arguments as Vehicles for the Space of Intelligence" (LSE, forthcoming talk) treats arguments themselves as substrate-independent vehicles — structurally parallel to Kastrup's treatment of minds as dissociated instances of a shared structure.
- **Levin:** Seiberth uses hypergraph substructure to get underneath a high-level concept ("the space of reasons") the way Levin uses bioelectric substructure to get underneath morphogenetic form. The same philosophical move — metaphysical substrate as graph — now runs across three traditions (Wolfram physics, Seiberth inference, Levin morphogenesis).
