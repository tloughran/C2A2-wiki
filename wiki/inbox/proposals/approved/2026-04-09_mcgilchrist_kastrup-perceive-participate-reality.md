## Summary
Published April 7, 2026 on With Reality in Mind — a structured dialogue between Iain McGilchrist and Bernardo Kastrup in which both argue for idealism while clarifying where they diverge. Both agree that consciousness is primary and that "emergence" as an explanation for mind from matter is empty hand-waving. Key disputed terrain: for Kastrup, time is an illusion; for McGilchrist, time is real — a continuous flow indicative of the unity of nature. On the "dashboard of perception" (Kastrup's metaphor for how consciousness represents reality), McGilchrist dissents: the metaphor is too mechanistic, implies a dashboard operator reading data that could be measured by a machine, whereas reality is not hidden behind a dashboard but is an experience in which we are embedded and in which we participate. Up to 80% of neurons are in the unconscious cerebellum, and there are well-documented cases of conscious experience in humans with virtually no cortex — both thinkers cite this as evidence that standard neuroscience is insufficient.
