## Summary
The UNIVERSE+ School "Exploring Positive Geometry" took place at the Les Houches School of Physics (French Alps) from March 30 to April 10, 2026. Arkani-Hamed taught the course component titled "Combinatorics and Geometry of Quantum Mechanics and Spacetime" — a two-week self-contained doctoral training program introducing positive geometry as a unifying language bridging particle physics, cosmology, and mathematics. The school is part of the ERC Synergy Grant UNIVERSE+ project led by Arkani-Hamed (IAS Princeton), Daniel Baumann, Johannes Henn, and Bernd Sturmfels.

  Resource: Positive geometry as a unifying mathematical framework — a language bridging scattering amplitudes (particle physics), cosmological correlators, and deep mathematical structures (combinatorics, algebraic geometry)
  Solution: A research program establishing that the correct computational language for fundamental physics is combinatorial-geometric rather than field-theoretic, with locality and unitarity emerging from geometry rather than being assumed
  Confidence: High

  Resource: Combinatorial geometry as the foundational language of quantum mechanics — developed through amplituhedron geometry and generalized to cosmological correlators
  Solution: A systematic reformulation showing that quantum mechanical amplitudes are volumes of combinatorially defined geometric objects, making the combinatorial structure of QM explicit and computable without reference to spacetime scaffolding
  Confidence: Medium
