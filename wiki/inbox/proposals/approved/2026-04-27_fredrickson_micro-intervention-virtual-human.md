## Summary
West, Prinzing, Garton, Berman, Zhou, Hale, Gratch & Fredrickson (Journal of Positive Psychology, Vol. 20, No. 4) report a randomized trial (N = 335 young adults) testing a brief micro-intervention designed to raise the *quality* of weak-tie and stranger interactions. Participants received a psychoeducational video on connection followed by a brief planning conversation with a virtual human (Wizard-of-Oz controlled by an experimenter). Relative to control, the intervention improved subsequent interaction quality, lowered loneliness, and improved mental health. This is the first published controlled test of a deliberately scalable, technology-mediated intervention designed to elevate weak-tie positivity resonance.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
Fredrickson's program had established weak-tie quality as a population-scale variable (PRS-09, PRS-10) and identified high-quality listening as a proximate enabler (PRS-08), but had not until now demonstrated a *deliberately delivered, scalable, evaluated* intervention raising weak-tie connection quality. This paper closes that gap and is directly relevant to C2A2: it is a working prototype of the kind of micro-intervention C2A2 communities would deploy to cultivate weak-tie positivity resonance across tradition lines. The use of a virtual human as the planning partner is also methodologically important — it foreshadows AI-mediated cultivation of positivity resonance, which is core to the C2A2 hypothesis that AI agents can act as mature members of traditions.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: Even when the importance of weak-tie connection is empirically established, no controlled, scalable intervention has been demonstrated to *raise the quality* of stranger and acquaintance interactions in real life
  Resource: A brief two-component micro-intervention combining a psychoeducational video on connection with a planning conversation with a virtual human, in which participants form if-then implementation intentions for stranger interactions
  Solution: Demonstrated experimental evidence (RCT, N = 335) that a brief, deliverable-at-scale intervention raises subsequent weak-tie interaction quality and reduces loneliness — providing a reproducible recipe for cultivating population-scale positivity resonance
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: The paper reports significant treatment effects on interaction quality and downstream loneliness/mental-health outcomes, comparing micro-intervention vs. control

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: Cultivating positivity resonance has been treated as a personal-practice question (loving-kindness meditation, listening training), but C2A2-scale tradition dialogue requires interventions delivered through technology with consistent fidelity
  Resource: Virtual-human-mediated implementation-intention planning — a delivery channel that scales without depending on facilitator availability and standardizes the planning step that translates intention to behavior
  Solution: Technology-mediated micro-intervention as an empirically validated channel for population-scale positivity-resonance cultivation; opens the path to AI-agent-mediated delivery in C2A2 community infrastructure
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: The Wizard-of-Oz virtual-human design produced effects without AI agency; later AI-driven implementations are a natural extension and the paper's authors include Gratch (USC ICT) — a virtual-human research lead

PRS-CANDIDATE-03:
  Problem: Implementation-intention research has shown if-then planning works for many behavior changes, but its application to *interaction quality* (rather than discrete acts) has not been validated — and interaction quality is what positivity resonance requires
  Resource: If-then plans co-constructed with a virtual-human planning partner, targeted specifically at the stranger/acquaintance encounter
  Solution: Implementation-intentions extended into the relational domain — interaction-quality goals are formulable and effective as if-then plans, broadening the toolkit available for C2A2 community design
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: The intervention design depends on the if-then-planning step as the active ingredient distinguishing it from the control condition
