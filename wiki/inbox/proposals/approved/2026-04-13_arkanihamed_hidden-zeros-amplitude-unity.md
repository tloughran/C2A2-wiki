## Summary
Arkani-Hamed and collaborators discover that tree-level scattering amplitudes in Tr(φ³) theory have a hidden pattern of zeros — the amplitudes vanish when certain non-planar Mandelstam invariants are set to zero, a property invisible in standard Feynman diagram calculations. More remarkably, these hidden zeros reveal that the amplitudes for colored scalars, pions (NLSM), and gluons (Yang-Mills) are all unified: the pion and gluon amplitudes are secretly contained within the scalar amplitude structure. Published in Journal of High Energy Physics, October 2024.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This paper deepens the case that standard physics formalism (Feynman diagrams) obscures deep structural features of reality. The "hidden zeros" are invisible in the conventional formulation but become manifest in geometric/combinatorial approaches — reinforcing the amplituhedron program's claim that the correct language of fundamental physics is geometric, not field-theoretic. The unification of scalars, pions, and gluons through this hidden structure suggests the mathematical organization of particle physics is far more unified than the Standard Model formalism reveals.
