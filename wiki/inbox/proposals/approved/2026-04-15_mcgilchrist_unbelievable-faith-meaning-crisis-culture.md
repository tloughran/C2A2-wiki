## Summary
McGilchrist appeared on Premier Unbelievable with host Luke Martin for an extended interview covering faith, meaning, AI, and the crisis of modern culture. He explored how the brain's hemispheric structure shapes our perception of reality, arguing that modern life's increasing fragmentation is a symptom of left-hemisphere dominance. Crucially, he discusses what beauty, nature, worship, and the sacred can offer in response — positioning these not as optional luxuries but as essential modes of right-hemisphere engagement that re-ground human cognition in relational reality. The conversation also addresses AI, Christianity, suffering, music, and the kind of attention needed to live well in a fragmented world.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This interview extends McGilchrist's program into explicitly theological and spiritual territory — not as a private belief but as a consequence of his neuroscience. If the right hemisphere discloses relational reality and the sacred is a feature of that disclosure, then worship and contemplative practice are not subjective preferences but epistemically necessary modes of engagement. This directly strengthens PRS-06 (embodied tradition formation) by providing a neurological account of why spiritual practices matter for knowledge. For C2A2, it supports the claim that tradition-formation requires embodied, contemplative engagement — and that AI agents, however capable of processing information, may lack access to this mode of knowing.
