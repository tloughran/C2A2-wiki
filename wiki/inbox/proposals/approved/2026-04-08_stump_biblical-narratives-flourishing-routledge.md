## Summary
A December 2025 Routledge volume co-edited by Stump and Judith Wolfe (University of St Andrews), bringing together accomplished philosophers and theologians to demonstrate how biblical narratives contribute to our understanding of human flourishing. The volume's thesis — developed across multiple chapters by different contributors — is that narrative theology provides a form of *philosophical knowledge* about flourishing that is irreducible to propositional argument. This is the collaborative successor to Stump's solo *Grains of Wheat*, broadening the project to include contemporary analytic theology across traditions (Protestant, Catholic, Jewish contributors). The methodological innovation is treating narrative cognition — knowledge conveyed by and through stories — as a rigorous contribution to analytic theology and the philosophy of human flourishing.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This volume positions narrative epistemology as a research program in its own right within analytic theology, with Stump as its leading architect. It introduces the concept of "narrative cognition" as a philosophical category applicable to inter-tradition learning — directly relevant to how the C2A2 project envisions second-first-language competence in rival traditions. Traditions may transmit their deepest insights precisely through narrative structures that propositional summaries cannot replicate.
