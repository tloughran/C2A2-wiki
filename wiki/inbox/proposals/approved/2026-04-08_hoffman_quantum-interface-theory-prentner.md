## Summary
Robert Prentner — Hoffman's longtime collaborator — published this paper in the Journal of Consciousness Studies (Volume 33, Issue 1, 2026, pp. 194–210) formally extending Hoffman's Conscious Agent Theory into quantum mechanics. The paper argues that core quantum-mechanical phenomena — entanglement, superposition, and decoherence — are artifacts of the interface: they are features of the adaptive desktop, not properties of the underlying conscious agent dynamics. The paper sketches a formalism of "quantum conscious agents" as the mathematical foundation for a Quantum Interface Theory (QIT), treating perception as a quantum instrument. Hoffman promoted the paper via his X account and a podcast with Prentner.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is the most significant formal advance in Hoffman's research program since the "Fusions of Consciousness" paper (2023). It directly attacks a foundational assumption of physics — that quantum mechanics describes reality — and reinterprets QM as interface phenomena. This is precisely the direction Hoffman's program has been heading since PRS-02 (physics–consciousness unification). Published in 2026, it is not yet in the wiki.
