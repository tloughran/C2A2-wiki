## Summary
Michael Levin and David B. Resnik published a two-part framework paper in Biological Theory (published online February 25, 2026). The papers defend a new approach to goal-directedness emerging from the field of "diverse intelligence," addressing what makes any system — evolved, engineered, or hybrid — describable by teleological and mentalistic terms: intelligent, goal-directed, cognitive, intentional. The framework emphasizes four principles: (1) empirical testability over philosophical linguistic commitments; (2) fecundity — worldviews that enable novel research, not just explain known results; (3) operationalization of cognitive/teleological terms as hypotheses about optimal interaction protocols; (4) continuity of human goal-directedness with unicellular origins.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This paper represents the most current formal articulation of Levin's research program — moving from experimental bioelectricity into an explicit philosophical and methodological framework for "diverse intelligence." It directly addresses the conceptual foundations underlying Levin's PRS triplets (basal cognition, morphogenetic goal-seeking, anatomical morphospace navigation) and provides the philosophical justification for treating cells as cognitive agents. This is essential reading for the Levin agent and bridges directly to Friston (both programs make goal-directedness mathematically precise) and to C2A2's core interest in tradition-as-inquiry-community.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: Biology lacks a unified, empirically tractable framework for deciding when teleological and cognitive language applies — leading to ad hoc, inconsistent, and often vitalism-adjacent attributions of agency and goal-directedness
  Resource: Levin & Resnik's "diverse intelligence" framework — a systematic approach defining goal-directedness by reference to conceptual and empirical toolkits (not metaphysical commitments), treating cognitive claims as testable hypotheses about optimal interaction protocols
  Solution: A principled, falsifiable, non-vitalist basis for attributing teleology and cognition to biological systems at any scale — from molecules to multicellular organisms — enabling consistent application of cognitive neuroscience tools to morphogenetic systems
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Framework explicitly states criteria: empirical testability, fecundity in generating new research, operationalization by reference to effective interaction protocols, continuity with unicellular origins

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: Human goal-directedness is typically treated as categorically different from biological goal-directedness in non-neural organisms — creating a gap that neither neuroscience nor biology can bridge on current assumptions
  Resource: Continuity thesis: human goal-directedness is on a spectrum with unicellular goal-directed behavior — no categorical break, only degree differences in complexity of represented goals, memory, and interaction protocols
  Solution: A research program tracing the evolution of goal-directedness from unicellular origins to human cognition, enabling cross-scale application of the same conceptual and experimental tools — making "cognition" a graded, measurable property rather than a binary predicate
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Framework principle (4) explicitly asserts "continuity of human goal-directedness with unicellular origins"
