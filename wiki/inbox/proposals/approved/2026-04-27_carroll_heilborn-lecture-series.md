## Summary
Sean Carroll delivered the 2026 Heilborn Lecture Series at Northwestern University over three days (April 22, 23, 24). Lecture 1 ("Explaining the Arrow of Time," April 22) reprises and updates his Past Hypothesis program — entropy increase as a cosmological boundary condition. Lecture 2 ("Extracting the Universe from the Wave Function," April 23) is the technical centerpiece: Carroll argues that classical spacetime, locality, and the Born rule should be derivable from basic quantum principles, with explicit consequences for emergent spacetime and quantum gravity. Lecture 3 ("The Many Worlds of Quantum Mechanics," April 24, public) restates the MWI case for a general audience. Together the series is the most consolidated public statement of Carroll's "spacetime-from-Hilbert-space" research program in 2026.
