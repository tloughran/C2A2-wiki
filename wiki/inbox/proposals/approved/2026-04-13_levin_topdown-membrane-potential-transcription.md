## Summary
Cervera, Levin, and Mafe model the multicellular interplay between bioelectricity and protein transcription using a top-down perspective. Their simulations yield three predictions: (i) shifts in membrane potential enable transitions between gene expression states, (ii) depolarized cells cannot control distinct gene expressions as effectively as polarized cells, and (iii) community effects permit extension of single-cell bioelectric control to the multicellular level. Published in Scientific Reports, volume 16 (2026).

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This paper provides computational modeling support for one of Levin's most important empirical claims: that bioelectric signals operate as a top-down control layer over gene expression. The three predictions formalize the mechanism by which membrane voltage acts as morphogenetic "software" — directly supporting PRS-07 (bioelectric reprogramming as therapeutic paradigm) and PRS-01 (morphogenetic control via bioelectric circuits). The community-effects prediction (iii) is particularly significant: it models how single-cell bioelectric control scales to multicellular collective behavior, bridging the gap between individual cell states and tissue-level morphogenetic goals.
