## Summary
Two-hour Legacy Interview released April 23, 2026 by MINDinMIND, in which Karl Friston joins psychoanalysts Mark Solms and Jeremy Holmes (with host Jane O'Rourke) to translate FEP and active inference into the language of clinical practice. The conversation explicitly bridges three vantage points: Friston's neuroscience (uncertainty management, prediction, active inference), Solms' neuropsychoanalytic framework, and Holmes' attachment-informed psychotherapy. Major novel content: Friston articulates how minds form *symptoms* (as stable but suboptimal predictive states) and how relationship — not just intervention — becomes the mechanism of change under FEP.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is the most thorough public articulation to date of FEP-as-clinical-framework, and crucially it is *tri-vocal*: Friston himself, a psychoanalyst (Solms), and an attachment theorist (Holmes) jointly construct the bridge. The wiki currently has strong coverage of FEP as physics-of-mind and FEP as computational psychiatry, but less on the **relational** dimension of FEP — how active inference operates between agents, and how that operation is the mechanism of therapeutic change. This interview provides primary material for that missing axis.

  Problem: How do mental symptoms form, persist, and resolve under active inference — and why are they resistant to direct correction?

  Confidence: Medium
