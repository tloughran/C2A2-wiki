## Summary
Arkani-Hamed, Baumann, Hillman, Joyce, Lee, and Pimentel show that differential equations governing cosmological correlators can be derived from simple combinatorial rules ("kinematic flow") defined purely in terms of boundary data — without any reference to bulk time evolution. Yet these boundary-defined rules reproduce the physics of time evolution exactly. The authors argue this suggests the existence of an "autonomously defined mathematical structure from which cosmological correlations, and the time evolution of the associated spacetime, emerge." Published in Physical Review Letters 135, 031602 (2025).

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is arguably Arkani-Hamed's most philosophically significant result since the amplituhedron. The amplituhedron showed that particle interactions can be computed without spacetime; this paper shows that cosmological time itself can be derived from a timeless combinatorial structure. It extends the post-spacetime program from particle physics to cosmology — the emergence of time from something deeper is no longer a hope but a demonstrated mathematical fact (in a simplified setting). Published in PRL, the flagship physics journal, giving it maximum credibility.
