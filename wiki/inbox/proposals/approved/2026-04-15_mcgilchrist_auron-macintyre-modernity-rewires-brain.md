## Summary
McGilchrist appeared on The Auron MacIntyre Show on March 14, 2026, arguing that the popular characterization of the left hemisphere as "logical and rational" and the right as "emotional and creative" is "almost the inverse of the truth." He reframes the hemispheric divide as a difference in modes of attending: the left hemisphere offers narrow, focused, manipulative attention while the right hemisphere provides broad, contextual, living engagement. He argues that modernity has systematically rewired human cognition toward left-hemisphere dominance, producing extraordinary technical competence but diminishing meaning. The conversation specifically addresses how modern institutions — media, education, technology — reinforce this imbalance.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This interview is significant because McGilchrist presents his hemispheric thesis to a political-cultural audience (MacIntyre's conservative media platform), explicitly connecting the divided-brain framework to the crisis of modernity — not just as neuroscience but as cultural diagnosis. The claim that modernity "rewires" the brain toward left-hemisphere patterns extends PRS-01 (hemispheric asymmetry and cultural impoverishment) into an active, ongoing process rather than a static condition. For C2A2, this reinforces the urgency of the tradition-accelerator: if modern institutions are actively deepening left-hemisphere dominance, the pedagogical tools C2A2 builds must actively counteract this, not merely present information.
