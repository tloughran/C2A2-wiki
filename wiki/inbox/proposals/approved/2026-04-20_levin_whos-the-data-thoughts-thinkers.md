## Summary
Levin essay on thoughtforms.life proposing a *thoughts-are-thinkers* continuum — the view that cognitive content (thoughts, patterns, goals) and cognitive agents (thinkers) do not occupy separate ontological categories but lie on a single continuum. Applies this framing to developmental bioelectricity: the bioelectric *pattern* in embryonic tissue is not merely data-for-a-thinker but is itself a low-grade thinker. This collapses the distinction between "what the cell knows" and "the cell that knows it," providing a principled account of how agency can be distributed across scales.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
Ontological clarification of Levin's core diverse-intelligence claim. Previous statements left room for reading bioelectric patterns as merely informational substrates for higher-level agents; this essay argues the patterns *themselves* have (minimal) agentic status. Strongly relevant to the network's open questions about whether agency is a binary or scalar property, and to the formal relationship between Hoffman's trace-logic agents, Friston's Markov-blanket systems, and Levin's cognitive-cell-collectives.
