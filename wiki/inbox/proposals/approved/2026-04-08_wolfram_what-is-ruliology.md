## Summary
Wolfram formalizes "ruliology" as a distinct scientific discipline: the systematic empirical study of all possible simple programs in the computational universe — asking not "what can we compute?" but "how do systems following particular rules actually behave?" The essay is presented as a computational notebook, where code, exposition, and computation are co-equal. Wolfram situates ruliology as the foundational methodology beneath the Physics Project, the Ruliad, and his entire research program, drawing on 30+ years of archived research notebooks.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is the methodology paper for the Wolfram program — defining the discipline that undergirds every other claim. Without understanding ruliology as an empirical method (actual enumeration, observation, and analysis of the computational universe), Wolfram's Physics Project can appear to be pure speculation; with it, it is a systematic science of possible programs. Any serious engagement with Wolfram's tradition must pass through this methodological grounding.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: What is the scientific discipline that encompasses the study of the computational universe? Physics, mathematics, and computer science each capture partial aspects of how simple rules produce complex behavior, but no unified discipline has existed for the systematic study of all possible computational rules.
  Resource: Ruliology — the empirical science of enumerating, executing, and analyzing the behavior of all possible simple programs in the computational universe, conducted through Wolfram Language computational essays.
  Solution: Ruliology provides the methodological foundation that unifies the Physics Project (what rules produce spacetime?), the Ruliad (what is the entangled limit of all rules?), and computational metaphysics (what does the space of all rules ultimately constitute?). It establishes that these are empirical, not merely speculative, programs.
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: "The fundamental question of ruliology is: if one sets up a system to follow a particular set of simple rules, what will the system do? These are pure, abstract questions of basic science." — Wolfram (2026)
