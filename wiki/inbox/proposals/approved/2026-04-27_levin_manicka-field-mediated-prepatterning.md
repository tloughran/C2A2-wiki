## Summary
Manicka & Levin (Cell Reports Physical Science, 2025) provide a computational and theoretical account of how the electrostatic *field* — not just local cell-cell signaling — contributes to morphogenetic prepatterning by enhancing the complexity of membrane potential patterns through a coarse-grained projection. Two optimized models exhibit contrasting "mosaic" and "stigmergic" pattern-coding strategies; the stigmergic model recapitulates the qualitative developmental sequence of bioelectric craniofacial prepatterning observed in *Xenopus* frog embryos. The paper introduces a **synergetics-based mechanism** that bridges single-cell bioelectric state and tissue-level morphogenetic field.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is a foundational paper for the field-theoretic side of Levin's program. The wiki currently has rich coverage of bioelectric *signaling* and bioelectric *patterns*, but less on the formal *field* — the mathematical object that integrates signaling across the tissue. Manicka & Levin formalize that field and demonstrate it can do real explanatory work (recapitulating empirical craniofacial development). The "stigmergic" pattern-coding strategy they identify — where the field encodes the prepattern indirectly via cell-environment coupling rather than directly via cell-state — is a conceptually significant move that connects to swarm cognition and externalist theories of mind.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: Bioelectric *signaling* has been well-characterized at the cell-cell level, but a formal account of the bioelectric *field* — the macroscopic object that organizes morphogenesis — has been missing
  Resource: Synergetics-based coarse-graining: a mathematical framework that projects fine-grained membrane voltage patterns into a field representation that exhibits emergent prepattern complexity
  Solution: Formal derivation that the electrostatic field actively contributes to morphogenetic prepatterning — not merely as a passive readout of cell states but as a generative factor enhancing pattern complexity
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Two optimized computational models reproduce qualitative craniofacial prepattern sequences observed empirically in *Xenopus*

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: How does morphogenetic information get encoded — directly in cell states or indirectly in the cell-environment interaction?
  Resource: Comparison of "mosaic" vs "stigmergic" pattern-coding strategies in the optimized models
  Solution: The stigmergic strategy — where information is stored in the field-mediated environment rather than in individual cells — better matches empirical observation, suggesting morphogenesis uses external scaffold encoding analogous to swarm cognition
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: Stigmergic model uniquely recapitulates the developmental sequence observed in vivo
