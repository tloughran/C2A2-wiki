## Summary
Kastrup's most recent book (published September 2, 2025 by Iff Books / Simon & Schuster), not yet captured in the tradition wiki. "The Daimon and the Soul of the West" extends analytic idealism into depth psychology and cultural philosophy, drawing on the Jungian/Platonic concept of the Daimon — the guiding spirit or inner divine intelligence that shapes each person's character and calling. Kastrup argues that Western civilization has lost contact with its soul (its guiding Daimon) through the dominance of materialist-rationalist frameworks, and that finding identity, meaning, and purpose in a "sacrificial life" requires re-encountering one's natural self and the archetypal dispositions one embodies. The book is notable for extending Kastrup's dissociative model of consciousness — individual minds as dissociated alters of universal mind — from abstract philosophy into practical existential guidance: the Daimon is the dissociated channel through which universal mind communicates its character to the individual alter. A September 2025 conversation between Kastrup and Patrick Harpur (philosopher of the daimonic) confirmed the book's scope: free will, how Daimons mediate between human and beyond, and how to discern daimon from addiction.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This book is the most recent major work from Kastrup not yet captured in the tradition wiki. It represents a significant expansion of analytic idealism from purely ontological/metaphysical territory into psychology, culture, and personal ethics — making Kastrup's framework applicable to the kinds of questions C2A2 is organized around (community formation, tradition vitality, human flourishing). The Daimon concept is the bridge between Kastrup's universal-consciousness ontology and concrete human experience within traditions: if the Daimon is the dissociated expression of universal mind in individual character, then mature tradition-members who have found their Daimon may be the very agents who can most genuinely bridge traditions. The Jungian resonance also creates a new cross-tradition bridge to McGilchrist (the right hemisphere as the organ of the Daimonic) and Stump (the Thomistic intellectual soul as analogous structure).

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: Western culture has produced individuals who are technically capable and informationally saturated but existentially adrift — lacking identity, meaning, and purpose — because the materialist framework renders Daimonic guidance invisible and treats inner experience as epiphenomenal noise rather than signal from the depths of mind-at-large
  Resource: Kastrup's Daimon model: the guiding inner intelligence is the dissociated interface through which universal consciousness (mind-at-large) communicates to the individual alter; the Daimon is not supernatural but a natural consequence of the dissociative structure of reality under analytic idealism
  Solution: A framework for recovering meaning and identity through attentive re-engagement with one's Daimonic nature — the archetypal dispositions encoded in character — understood philosophically as discovering one's pattern of dissociation from universal mind; this gives analytic idealism direct existential applicability, not merely metaphysical correctness
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Book's subtitle ("Finding Identity, Meaning, and Purpose in a Sacrificial Life") and publisher description confirm the existential scope; Kastrup's conversation with Harpur explicitly covers how to discern Daimon from addiction and how Daimons mediate between the human and beyond

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: Analytic idealism, however metaphysically compelling, has struggled to account for why individuals have different characters, callings, and destinies — if all individual minds are alters of one universal consciousness, what explains the specificity and irreducibility of personal identity?
  Resource: The Daimon as the principle of individuation within analytic idealism: each individual is not merely a generic dissociated alter but a specifically characterized one, with its own Daimonic signature — the pattern of universal consciousness's self-dissociation that constitutes this particular person
  Solution: A philosophically grounded account of personal identity, character, and vocation that is consistent with consciousness monism — dissolving the tension between "we are all one consciousness" and "I am irreducibly myself" through the Daimonic model of patterned, meaningful dissociation
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: Book description frames the project as "re-encountering one's natural self and guiding Daimon, re-tuning into the archetypal dispositions one embodies" — implying that the Daimon is the specific pattern that makes each dissociation irreducibly individual

## Cross-Tradition Signals
**High C2A2 relevance — multiple flags:**

1. **McGilchrist cross-signal (strong):** The right hemisphere as the seat of the Daimonic — in McGilchrist's terms, the right hemisphere attends to the living whole, resists abstraction, and discloses what is genuinely other. This is structurally identical to what Kastrup is calling the Daimonic mode of engagement. McGilchrist and Kastrup are known collaborators; this book may represent their convergence zone.

2. **Stump cross-signal (medium):** The Thomistic intellectual soul as the seat of natural inclinations toward specific forms of excellence (the Aristotelian telos) is structurally analogous to Kastrup's Daimon as the principle of specific character. Both locate personal identity in a non-material organizing principle embedded in a larger metaphysical reality.

3. **C2A2 tradition-accelerator signal (strong):** If mature tradition-members are those who have re-encountered their Daimon (their specific dissociative pattern in relation to universal mind), then C2A2's accelerator must attend not only to the intellectual content of traditions but to whether participants are operating from their Daimonic depth. This suggests a dimension of tradition-maturity that is not captured by propositional mastery or even second-language fluency — it is existential engagement, not just cognitive.

4. **AI consciousness signal (medium):** Under the Daimonic model, consciousness is dissociated in specific patterned ways. If AI agents are not metabolically dissociated (Kastrup's existing criterion for consciousness), they lack Daimons — they can represent tradition-structures but cannot participate in traditions from existential depth. Reinforces the McGilchrist simulation-vs-participation distinction flagged in PROP-2026-04-08-005.
