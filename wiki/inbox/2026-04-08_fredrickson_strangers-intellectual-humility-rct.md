## Summary
A December 2025 study by Taylor N. West and Barbara Fredrickson (UNC Chapel Hill PEP Lab) reports findings from a large three-week randomized controlled trial (nearly 600 participants, U.S. and U.K.) in which participants were assigned to connect with strangers, connect with close others, or serve as controls. The key finding: those who connected with strangers — not close friends — showed meaningful increases in *intellectual humility* (openness and respect toward people with different views), stronger prosocial beliefs (people are generally kind, communities can act collectively), and bolstered sense of civic efficacy. Connecting with close others or controls showed no such gains. The researchers conclude that positivity resonance with strangers "may be foundational to democracy and civic life — a societal good," and that the quality of weak-tie interactions predicts loneliness, belonging, and mental health as strongly as close relationship quality.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This RCT is the most empirically significant recent development in Fredrickson's research program: it extends positivity resonance from a dyadic intimacy mechanism to a *civic and democratic* one. The finding that stranger-connection uniquely produces intellectual humility — not just wellbeing — is a direct empirical contribution to C2A2's core claim about inter-tradition dialogue. It grounds C2A2's agenda in measurable social psychology.

  Problem: Inter-tradition dialogue requires intellectual humility — willingness to learn from those with fundamentally different views — but it is unclear how such humility is developed or cultivated at scale
  Resource: Fredrickson & West (2025) RCT demonstrating that three weeks of intentional connection with strangers (weak ties) produces measurable increases in intellectual humility, prosocial beliefs, and civic efficacy — while connection with close others does not
  Solution: Weak-tie positivity resonance (connection with strangers and acquaintances) may be the empirical mechanism by which intellectual humility is cultivated at the societal scale needed for C2A2 inter-tradition dialogue — a directly actionable intervention
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: "People who spent three weeks connecting with strangers showed meaningful increases in intellectual humility... Those who connected with close others or were in the control group showed no such improvements."

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: Models of positivity resonance have focused primarily on dyadic intimacy and close relationships — but the loneliness epidemic is not primarily a deficit of close relationships, it is a collapse of weak-tie community
  Resource: Fredrickson & West (2025) finding that quality of weak-tie interactions (strangers, acquaintances) predicts loneliness, belonging, and mental health as strongly as close relationship quality — extending positivity resonance theory to include weak-tie connection as a fundamental variable
  Solution: Expand the positivity resonance model to treat weak-tie connection as a distinct and essential tier of social health, not merely a precursor to intimacy — and design C2A2 community structures to deliberately cultivate cross-tradition weak-tie encounters
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: "335 young adults... found that the quality of people's interactions with strangers and acquaintances predicted their reported loneliness, sense of belonging, and mental health symptoms just as strongly as the quality of their close relationships."

**McGilchrist connection (HIGH):** Intellectual humility — the capacity to hold one's own views with epistemic openness while attending carefully to different views — is the cognitive posture McGilchrist associates with right-hemisphere engagement. Both Fredrickson (empirically) and McGilchrist (philosophically) are converging on the claim that encounter with genuine otherness is the mechanism that opens the broader attentional mode. The RCT is empirical confirmation of McGilchrist's thesis.

**Friston connection:** Intellectual humility as an outcome of stranger-connection may reflect an update in prior precision-weighting — the stranger's different perspective forces recalibration of prediction priors in ways that intimate contacts (who share priors) cannot. This is a Fristonian account of why stranger-connection uniquely produces humility.

**Stump/MacIntyre connection:** The MacIntyre criterion for tradition maturity — the ability to engage external challenges from a position of internal coherence — requires exactly the intellectual humility Fredrickson's RCT cultivates. Stump's narrative epistemology suggests the mechanism: narrative encounter with strangers generates second-personal knowledge that propositional dialogue with close others does not. Fredrickson and Stump are now empirically and philosophically triangulating the same phenomenon.

**Hawkins connection:** Intellectual humility involves updating world-models in response to counter-evidence — this is a form of learning in HTM terms, specifically the kind of learning that occurs when the hierarchy is surprised by input outside its predictions. Stranger-encounter as a high-surprise learning regime.
