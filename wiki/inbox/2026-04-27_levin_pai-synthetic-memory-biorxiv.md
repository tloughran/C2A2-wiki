## Summary
Pai, Traer, Sperry, Zeng & Levin (bioRxiv, March 2026) demonstrate that basal Xenobots — autonomously motile constructs derived from Xenopus embryonic ectodermal explants — exhibit memory of brief chemical exposures (Xenopus embryonic cell extract and ATP) that manifests as distinct, specific, and persistent changes in behavior, physiology, and gene expression. The paper provides multi-level evidence (behavioral assays + physiological recordings + transcriptional profiling) that synthetic living constructs without evolutionary selection histories nonetheless implement learning-like processes, pushing the empirical envelope on substrate-independent cognition.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is a direct empirical advance on Levin's core program: it shows memory — long thought to require neural circuitry shaped by evolutionary history — emerging in a de novo biological construct that has none. It extends the basal cognition framework from "agency without neurons" to "memory without selection history," tightening the case that information-processing capacities are properties of bioelectric/transcriptional networks per se rather than of evolved biological lineages.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: Memory has been theoretically tied to either (a) neural substrates or (b) evolved organisms whose memory mechanisms were shaped by selection — leaving open whether memory is intrinsic to living material or requires that historical scaffolding
  Resource: Multi-level assay of basal Xenobots (behavioral + physiological + transcriptional) before and after chemical exposure to embryonic cell extract and ATP
  Solution: Empirical demonstration that synthetic living constructs without evolutionary history implement specific, persistent memory traces — generalizing memory as a property of bioelectric/transcriptional networks rather than an exclusive feature of evolved nervous systems
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Distinct behavioral state changes after brief chemical exposure correlated with measurable physiological and transcriptional signatures specific to the stimulus (per bioRxiv abstract)

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: How do we operationalize and detect memory in non-standard biological systems where conventional behavioral assays may not apply?
  Resource: Triangulation methodology — behavioral + physiological + transcriptional readouts cross-validating each other as evidence of a memory trace
  Solution: A reproducible multi-level protocol for attributing memory to novel biological constructs that meets Levin & Resnik's "diverse intelligence" framework criteria (testable hypotheses about interaction protocols)
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Methodological structure of the Pai et al. study explicitly combines three independent evidence streams
