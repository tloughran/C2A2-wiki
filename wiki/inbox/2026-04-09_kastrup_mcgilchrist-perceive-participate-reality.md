## Summary
Published April 7, 2026 on With Reality in Mind — a structured dialogue between Bernardo Kastrup and Iain McGilchrist comparing their idealist frameworks. Both dismiss emergence (matter → mind) as philosophically empty. Kastrup defends his "dashboard of perception" model — individual human consciousness as a screen or interface presenting representations of a larger universal consciousness, not unlike Hoffman's interface theory but grounded in a different metaphysics. McGilchrist pushes back on the dashboard metaphor as too mechanistic. Kastrup holds that time is illusory — a phenomenal representation within universal consciousness — while McGilchrist treats time as ontologically real. Both cite cases of conscious experience with minimal cortical tissue as evidence for the primacy of consciousness over neural correlates.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is the first recorded systematic comparison between Kastrup's analytic idealism and McGilchrist's hemispheric idealism. For Kastrup's tradition, it tests the dissociation model in a philosophically demanding dialogue: if individual alters of universal consciousness represent reality via a dashboard/screen, and if McGilchrist's participatory account is correct that genuine encounter with reality is irreducible to representation, then Kastrup faces a question about whether his individual alters can genuinely participate in traditions or only simulate participation. This connects directly to the AI membership question (PROP-2026-04-08-007, PROP-2026-04-08-004) and to Kastrup's psychopathology model (FINDING-009). The time debate is especially significant for the Kastrup tradition: if time is illusory, the dissociated alters' sense of temporal narrative is phenomenologically real but ontologically a representation — with implications for how traditions, institutions, and cross-generational communities are grounded in Kastrupian idealism.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: Kastrup's "dashboard of perception" metaphor — individual minds as screens presenting representations of universal consciousness — faces the objection that representation implies a mechanistic data-reading relation, missing the qualitative "participation" and genuine encounter that characterize lived conscious experience
  Resource: The McGilchrist counter-proposal: perception is participatory, not representational — the perceiver is embedded in a field of consciousness, and the encounter is a real two-way contact, not a one-way reading of a display. McGilchrist and Kastrup agree on consciousness-primacy but differ on the directionality and intimacy of the consciousness-perception relation.
  Solution: Kastrup's analytic idealism may need refinement to distinguish two modes of "screening": (a) the left-hemisphere mode — data-reading, representation, abstraction — which is genuinely dashboard-like; and (b) the right-hemisphere mode — participation, encounter, embeddedness — which is the mode McGilchrist identifies as ontologically primary. This would make Kastrup's framework internally incorporate McGilchrist's hemispheric distinction at the phenomenological level.
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: McGilchrist's April 7, 2026 critique of the dashboard metaphor; Kastrup's published response (same dialogue); their areas of agreement and divergence on this point are documented in the conversation
