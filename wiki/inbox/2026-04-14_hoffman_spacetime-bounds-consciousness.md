## Summary
Hoffman introduces a mathematical framework for determining whether spatially extended systems — ant colonies, human populations, cloud-hosted AI, brain-computer hybrids — can support unified conscious experience. He formalizes two conditions: "Chord" (all ingredients of a subjective experience must be simultaneously true at one instant and causally exchange influence within time window θ, constraining system diameter D ≤ κvθ where v is signal speed and κ depends on architecture) and the weaker "Arpeggio" (each ingredient need only occur somewhere within the window). The paper derives size limits for conscious systems and argues that certain architectures structurally forbid Chord while satisfying Arpeggio.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is a major extension of Hoffman's Conscious Realism program. Previously, conscious agent theory described the dynamics of consciousness but left open the question of scale — could any system of any size be conscious? This paper introduces physics-derived constraints on consciousness, directly engaging with spacetime structure rather than treating it purely as an interface artifact. It advances Active Research Question #2 in the Hoffman wiki ("What is the complete mathematical theory of conscious agent networks?") by adding spatial and temporal boundary conditions. It also has direct implications for Active Research Question #5 ("AI consciousness") by constraining what kinds of AI architectures could in principle support unified conscious experience.

## Cross-Tradition Signals
- **Carroll/Arkani-Hamed connection (strong):** Hoffman is now directly engaging with spacetime physics — using signal speed (c) and spatial diameter as constraints on consciousness. This moves Conscious Realism closer to testable physics and deepens the Hoffman↔Carroll productive tension: Carroll insists spacetime is fundamental; Hoffman treats it as interface but now shows that even within his framework, spacetime structure constrains what consciousness can do. The D ≤ κvθ bound is a physics equation inside a consciousness-first ontology.
- **Levin connection:** The paper's analysis of whether ant colonies and cellular collectives can have unified consciousness directly engages Levin's work on collective intelligence. Levin's bioelectric networks operate at scales and speeds that may satisfy Chord — this paper provides the mathematical tools to evaluate that claim.
- **Hawkins connection:** Hawkins' 150,000 cortical columns achieve consensus through electrochemical voting within tight temporal windows — the Chord condition may explain WHY cortical columns must be physically close and signal-fast to produce unified experience. The Thousand Brains model is implicitly a system that satisfies Chord.
- **Kastrup connection:** Hoffman's framework addresses a key challenge for idealism — if consciousness is fundamental, why does it appear bounded by physics? The Chord/Arpeggio distinction offers Kastrup a tool: perhaps "dissociation" (Kastrup's key mechanism) operates under Arpeggio rather than Chord constraints.
- **Friston connection:** Active inference agents must integrate information in real-time to minimize free energy — the Chord condition may be a necessary prerequisite for any system performing genuine active inference, linking Hoffman's consciousness bounds to Friston's information-processing bounds.
