## Summary
Sean Carroll and Achyuth Parola propose a new classification of emergence based on dynamics (states evolving with time) rather than subjective criteria like "novelty" or "surprise." They define Type-0 (featureless), Type-1 (local), and Type-2 emergence, with explicit attention to mereology (decomposition of systems into parts). The paper argues that higher-level entities described by Type-1 and Type-2 emergence are "real" in the sense of Dennett's "real patterns." Submitted to a volume on Real Patterns (ed. Tyler Milhouse), forthcoming from MIT Press.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is Carroll's most rigorous philosophical articulation of emergence to date — the concept that sits at the heart of his poetic naturalism. By grounding emergence in dynamics rather than subjective surprise, Carroll provides a framework that can be used to evaluate every consciousness-first and top-down-causation claim in the C2A2 network. This paper makes Carroll's implicit methodological standard explicit and testable.
