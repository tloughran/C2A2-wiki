## Summary
In a recent Q&A session (With Reality In Mind / Nour, 21 April 2026), Kastrup advances a sharpened version of his realism criterion: *explanation* — not predictive accuracy, not computational simulation — is what tracks reality. He distinguishes computational equivalence (a model that *predicts* a phenomenon) from explanatory adequacy (a model that *accounts for why* the phenomenon must be the way it is), and argues that conflation of the two is the central error of computational metaphysics. The session positions explanatory primacy as a constraint on which metaphysics can be true: only frameworks that reduce the explanatory gap qualify as realism candidates.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is Kastrup making the *epistemological* commitment of analytic idealism explicit: idealism is preferred not because it is more parsimonious in the bare predictive sense, but because it is more *explanatory* — fewer brute facts, less appeal to mystery. That move sharpens the analytic-idealism research program by tying its truth conditions to a specific, debatable epistemic norm (explanation > computation), which the program can then defend or revise. It is exactly the kind of program-defining move MacIntyre's framework rewards.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: Computational and predictive models of reality (including ruliological / physicalist programs) routinely substitute simulation for explanation — leaving the question of whether they track reality unanswered.
  Resource: An explicit demarcation criterion — *explanation* (accounting for why the phenomenon must be so) vs. *computation* (predicting that the phenomenon is so) — as the test for whether a metaphysics is realism-eligible.
  Solution: An epistemological filter that sharpens the analytic-idealism program: idealism is preferred because it minimizes the residual brute-fact / explanatory-gap remainder, not because of greater predictive scope.
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Kastrup's framing in the 21 April 2026 Q&A.

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: How do idealist metaphysics engage with computational programs (Wolfram, ruliology, computational physicalism) on their own terms?
  Resource: The explanation/computation distinction as a shared discussion-frame — it lets idealists and computationalists locate their actual disagreement at the epistemological layer (what counts as understanding) rather than talking past each other at the ontological layer.
  Solution: A productive bridge structure for cross-tradition dialogue between Kastrup and computational metaphysics programs — promising for C2A2 inter-tradition work.
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: The Q&A's explicit address to the "computation reshapes our understanding of physical possibility" framing prevalent in Wolfram-style programs.
