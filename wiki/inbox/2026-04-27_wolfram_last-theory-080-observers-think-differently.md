## Summary
Mark Jeffery interviews Stephen Wolfram on how observer characteristics (size, time-scale, multiway-mind topology) determine the structure of the physics that observer perceives. Wolfram works through three concrete thought experiments: (1) planet-sized brains for whom the finite speed of light forces a different conception of space and time — the "fiction of an instantaneous state of space" would be untenable; (2) molecule-sized brains for whom thermal motion would no longer look random, and the heat death of the universe would be a richly structured prospect; (3) "multiway minds" able to hold multiple branches of the multiway graph simultaneously, producing multiple parallel threads of experience. The pivotal question raised: does the Principle of Computational Equivalence force all observers to think the same way, or does observer-scale genuinely differentiate the cognitive style of an observer-class? Wolfram tilts toward the latter — observer-scale matters because we think the way we think because we are the way we are.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is the most concrete extension of Wolfram's Observer Theory the wiki has captured since the February 2026 "Metaphysics and the Ruliad" essay (PRS-06, PRS-07). It moves the rulial-space-of-minds thesis (PRS-03, PRS-05) from abstract claim to operationalizable thought experiment: each observer type is a *specific path through rulial space*, and Wolfram shows what physics looks like when that path is varied along one parameter (size, time-scale, multiway-coherence). It also tightens the connection between Wolfram's observer theory and the question of cognitive style — making this episode a direct upstream input to the "machine curiosity / new ways of thinking" thread captured in PROP-2026-04-27-003 (today's earlier Wolfram proposal). Together #080 and the April 3 livestream show Wolfram converging on a single research target: the systematic study of non-human-imitative observer-classes inside the Ruliad.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: The rulial-space-of-minds thesis (PRS-03, PRS-05) is conceptually compelling but has lacked concrete worked examples. Without specific observer-classes to point at, "different observers sample the Ruliad differently" remains a slogan rather than a research agenda.
  Resource: Wolfram's three-parameter observer-class taxonomy (Last Theory #080): scale (molecule → planet), temporal coherence (instantaneous-state vs. light-delay-dominated), and multiway-coherence (single thread vs. multi-branch experience). Each parameter generates a specific, describable physics for the observer-class that holds it.
  Solution: A first-pass operational map of the rulial-space-of-minds: observer types are points in (scale × temporal-coherence × multiway-coherence) space, and each generates a distinct effective physics. This converts PRS-03 / PRS-05 from a programmatic claim into a research agenda — one can now ask "what other parameters foliate observer-space?" and "what physics does each foliation produce?"
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: Direct from the episode: planet-brain → finite-c-dominated spacetime; molecule-brain → non-random thermal motion / interesting heat death; multiway-brain → parallel threads of experience.

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: Does the Principle of Computational Equivalence (PCE) imply that all sufficiently complex observers think the same way, making observer-class differentiation a mere surface phenomenon? If yes, the rulial-space-of-minds thesis collapses; if no, the thesis is substantive.
  Resource: Wolfram's argued resolution in #080: PCE applies to the *computational capability* of observers but not to the *experienced physics* of observers. Two observers can be computationally equivalent in the strong sense and still inhabit incommensurable physics because they sample the underlying computation differently.
  Solution: A clarified two-level reading of PCE: (i) computational-capability level — all observers above the universality threshold are equivalent in what they can in principle compute; (ii) experiential-physics level — observers above the threshold can still inhabit profoundly different effective worlds depending on their sampling parameters. The rulial-space-of-minds thesis lives at level (ii).
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: Episode framing: "if we were much larger-scale, much smaller-scale or if we had multiway minds, then we'd think very differently" — Wolfram explicitly affirming variation despite PCE.
