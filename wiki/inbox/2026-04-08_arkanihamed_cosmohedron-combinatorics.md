## Summary
A new paper by Ardila-Mantilla, Arkani-Hamed, Figueiredo, and Vazão proves that the faces of the cosmohedron — a polytope proposed as the geometric object underlying the cosmological wavefunction for Tr(φ³) theory — are in bijection with Matryoshka structures (nested polygon subdivisions). The paper elucidates the combinatorial structure of cosmohedra, generalizes them to a broader class of "X in Y" polytopes (where a polytope from family X is chiseled at each vertex of a polytope Y), and identifies a new application: UV divergences in loop-integrated Feynman amplitudes. Submitted March 3, 2026; last revised March 5, 2026.
