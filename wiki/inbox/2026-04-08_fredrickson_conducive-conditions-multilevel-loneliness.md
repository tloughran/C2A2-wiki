## Summary
Fredrickson's most recent colloquium presentation (documented at UBC Psychology and a 2025 Positive Education Forum) advances a major new multi-level framework for the conditions that enable or inhibit positivity resonance. The framework spans individual-level factors (anticipated discrimination, high-quality listening, sensory attunement), collective-level factors (civic engagement, relational mobility), and structural-level factors (economic inequality, the built environment). The explicit motivation is translating basic science on positivity resonance into actionable, equitable interventions to address the loneliness epidemic. Fredrickson describes this as "the latest evolution of her research program on the emotion of love." The companion empirical work establishes that high-quality listening — as a specific learnable behavior — boosts sensory connection and perceived safety in ways that are directly conducive to positivity resonance emergence between strangers.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This multi-level framework is a significant theoretical advance: Fredrickson is now explicitly theorizing the *structural* conditions that enable or block love, not just the interpersonal ones. This makes positivity resonance a theory of social architecture — which communities, practices, and built environments enable love to emerge — directly applicable to C2A2's design questions about what conditions enable inter-tradition dialogue.

  Solution: A hierarchical intervention framework: address structural barriers to resonance first (built environment, inequality), then collective-level enablers (civic engagement programs), then individual skills (listening quality training) — enabling equitable positivity resonance at population scale

  Problem: High-quality listening has been identified as conducive to positivity resonance but its mechanism — why it works and how to train it — has not been specified within the positivity resonance framework
  Resource: Fredrickson lab finding (2025): high-quality listening boosts real-time sensory connection and perceived safety — these are identified as the proximate mechanisms by which listening behavior enables positivity resonance to emerge between strangers
  Solution: Listening quality training as a targeted, learnable intervention for increasing positivity resonance between strangers — with sensory attunement and psychological safety as the measurable intermediate outcomes
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: "High-quality listening behaviors linked to social connection between strangers" (PMC 2025); "High-quality listening is a functional behavior that can boost real-time sensory connection and perceived safety, each of which is conducive for the emergence of positivity resonance" (Fredrickson lab)
