## Summary
Robert Chis-Ciure and Michael Levin published this two-part argument in Synthese (2025), extending Levin's earlier "Cognition all the way down" framework into a formal operationalization. The paper introduces a novel "problem space lexicon" and a quantitative search efficiency metric, defined as the decimal logarithm of the ratio between the cost of a random walk and the cost of a biological agent's actual policy in navigating a problem space — measuring how many orders of magnitude of dissipative work an agentic policy saves relative to a maximal-entropy search. Empirical models for amoeboid chemotaxis and barium-induced planarian head regeneration show that even "simple" organisms are from two-hundred- to sextillion-fold more efficient than random walk baselines, under conservative assumptions.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This paper delivers the formal mathematical operationalization that Levin's research program has been building toward: a way to measure intelligence as a graded, empirically quantifiable property of any system, not a binary predicate. It directly resolves PRS-04 (cognition substrate problem) by providing the mathematical scaffolding that makes "basal cognition" scientifically falsifiable. It also gives the C2A2 system a formal basis for "tradition vitality" tracking — the MacIntyre criterion (vital programs generate many new questions) can now be formally modeled as a measure of the "search efficiency" of a research tradition in problem space.
