## Summary
Published March 20, 2026 on Seen & Unseen, this interview explores McGilchrist's intellectual genealogy — the thinkers who shaped his divided-brain thesis. He discusses Henri Bergson's distinctions and Leszek Kołakowski's work, showing how both correspond broadly to his left-right hemisphere framework. McGilchrist traces how his neuroscientific findings were anticipated by philosophers who recognized the two modes of attending to reality long before modern neuroscience confirmed them empirically.
