## Summary
Fredrickson presented at UBC a new multi-level framework for understanding what enables positivity resonance. The framework identifies conducive conditions at three levels: individual-level (anticipated discrimination, high-quality listening), collective-level (civic engagement, relational mobility), and structural-level (economic inequality, the built environment). This represents a significant evolution of the positivity resonance program from dyadic psychology to a multi-scale social science.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is a major theoretical advance — Fredrickson is scaling positivity resonance from a dyadic phenomenon to a multi-level systems phenomenon. For C2A2, this is crucial: the conditions for productive inter-tradition dialogue are not just interpersonal but structural and collective. This framework gives the C2A2 project a way to think about what institutional and environmental conditions make tradition-crossing dialogue possible.

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: Positivity resonance research has focused on individual/dyadic interactions — but what structural and collective conditions enable or inhibit these micro-moments of connection at scale?
  Resource: A multi-level framework identifying conducive conditions at individual, collective, and structural levels of analysis
  Solution: Positivity resonance is not just a psychological event but a multi-scale phenomenon shaped by civic engagement, economic inequality, relational mobility, and the built environment — enabling intentional design of resonance-conducive communities
  Confidence: High
  Evidence: UBC colloquium explicitly presented the multi-level framework as the latest evolution of her research program

PRS-CANDIDATE-02:
  Problem: Economic inequality and anticipated discrimination suppress positive social interactions — but the mechanism linking structural factors to micro-moments of connection is unclear
  Resource: Fredrickson's finding that community trust predicts the emotional quality of interactions with strangers but not close others
  Solution: Structural factors (inequality, discrimination) act as filters on positivity resonance with weak-tie and stranger interactions — the connections most relevant to inter-tradition dialogue
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: Related research on community trust and stranger interactions from Fredrickson's lab

## Cross-Tradition Signals
- **Levin connection (strong)**: Multi-level conducive conditions map directly onto Levin's multi-scale agency — just as bioelectric signals create conducive conditions for morphogenetic goal-directedness at different scales, social structures create conducive conditions for positivity resonance at different scales
- **Friston connection**: Collective-level conducive conditions for positivity resonance are essentially shared priors in the active inference framework — civic engagement and relational mobility shape the generative models that communities use to predict each other's behavior
- **C2A2 direct relevance**: This framework provides actionable design principles for C2A2 communities — what structural conditions must be in place for productive inter-tradition dialogue? Fredrickson's multi-level analysis gives the project a concrete research agenda
