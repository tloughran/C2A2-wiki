## Summary
On the *Life with Machines* podcast (Episode 14), Jeff Hawkins makes the case that current large-model AI is not intelligent in the sense that brains are intelligent — it lacks the sensorimotor, reference-frame-anchored learning loop that, on his Thousand Brains theory, is constitutive of cortical understanding. He frames the gap not as a parameter-count problem but as a missing architectural ingredient: the absence of grid-cell-style reference frames over which models of the world are continuously composed and recomposed during action. The episode reiterates and operationalizes the Thousand Brains program in direct dialogue with the present LLM moment.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This is Hawkins addressing the most-watched question in AI today (are LLMs intelligent?) directly from the Thousand Brains stance. It sharpens the *contrast claim* of his program — that intelligence is not a function of pattern-matching scale, but of reference-frame-based modeling executed through a sensorimotor loop. For our wiki this provides current-moment positioning of the Hawkins program against the most-funded alternative paradigm in AI, which is essential for tracking the program's track record under McIntyrean criteria.
