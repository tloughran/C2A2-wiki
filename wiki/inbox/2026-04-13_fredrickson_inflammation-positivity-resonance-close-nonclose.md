## Summary
A 2025 study by West, Jolink, Feldman, Alvarez, Cardenas, Fredrickson, and Muscatell published in Emotion. The study (N = 55) examined whether systemic inflammation — induced via influenza vaccination and measured through interleukin-6 (IL-6) levels — is associated with anticipated and experienced positivity resonance (shared, kind-hearted positive affect) with close versus non-close others. Participants provided blood samples before and after vaccination, completed a writing task about anticipated positivity resonance, and reported experienced positivity resonance across eight momentary assessments. The study tests the emerging hypothesis that inflammation may selectively enhance social approach toward close (safe) others while potentially dampening it toward non-close (less predictable) others — linking the immune system directly to the quality and target-selectivity of social connection.

## Why This Matters for This Tradition
This paper extends Fredrickson's positivity resonance framework into psychoneuroimmunology, establishing a biological mechanism (inflammation) that differentially modulates the capacity for resonance depending on relational closeness. It provides the first evidence within Fredrickson's framework that the body's immune state shapes not just general sociality but specifically the *target selectivity* of positivity resonance — suggesting that biological states gate who we can connect with. This is a significant deepening of the embodiment dimension of Fredrickson's theory: love is not merely biological in its signatures (vagal tone, oxytocin) but biological in its preconditions (immune state determines who resonance can reach).

## Candidate PRS Triplets

PRS-CANDIDATE-01:
  Problem: Fredrickson's model establishes that positivity resonance has biological correlates (vagal tone, oxytocin, neural synchrony) but does not specify how the body's physiological state modulates the *availability* or *target selectivity* of resonance — who can we connect with, and when?
  Resource: West, Jolink, Feldman et al. (2025): inflammation (IL-6 levels post-vaccination) is associated with differential anticipated and experienced positivity resonance with close vs. non-close others — suggesting immune state as a gating mechanism for resonance target selectivity
  Solution: The body's inflammatory state is a biological boundary condition on positivity resonance: inflammation may narrow the social radius of resonance toward familiar, safe others and away from strangers. This implies that population-level chronic inflammation (from stress, inequality, poor health) could systematically constrain the capacity for weak-tie positivity resonance
  Confidence: Medium
  Evidence: Study design measures IL-6 before and after influenza vaccine and correlates with positivity resonance reports differentiated by relationship closeness; published in Emotion (APA)

## Cross-Tradition Signals
**Fredrickson × Fredrickson internal signal:** This paper bridges two other recent Fredrickson findings captured in the wiki. The "Trust a Stranger" paper (PROP-2026-04-13-008) shows community trust mediates stranger-connection quality; this paper shows inflammation mediates stranger-connection biology. Together, they suggest a dual-pathway model: both psychological (trust) and biological (inflammation) conditions gate positivity resonance with strangers. Economic inequality may operate through both pathways — degrading trust AND increasing chronic inflammation via stress.

**Friston connection (STRONG):** Inflammation as a modulator of social approach selectivity maps directly onto active inference. Under inflammation (a signal of threat/vulnerability), the generative model increases precision on self-protective priors — the organism expects danger from unpredictable agents (strangers) and seeks the lower-surprise environment of familiar others. This is a Fristonian account of why sickness narrows social reach.

**Levin connection:** Inflammation as a systemic signal that reorganizes social behavior parallels Levin's bioelectric signals that reorganize morphogenetic behavior — both are body-wide signals that shift collective agent dynamics toward defensive/conservative configurations.

**Stump connection (speculative):** Stump's account of suffering as a condition that can either isolate or drive toward deeper union finds an empirical analog here. Inflammation (a form of bodily suffering/stress) appears to narrow the circle of connection — but Stump would argue that this narrowing can be transcended through grace/virtue. The question of whether practices (meditation, listening, virtue cultivation) can override inflammation's social-narrowing effect is a testable prediction that bridges Stump and Fredrickson.

**C2A2 design implication:** If chronic inflammation narrows the social radius of positivity resonance, then C2A2 communities engaged in inter-tradition dialogue (inherently stranger-to-stranger) may need to attend to participants' physical health and stress levels as a precondition for productive encounter — not just intellectual preparation.
