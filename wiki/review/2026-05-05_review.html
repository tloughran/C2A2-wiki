MICHAEL LEVIN · PAPER · 2026-03-17
Behavioral, Physiological, and Transcriptional Mechanisms of Memory in a Synthetic Living Construct
https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.03.17.712168v1
SUMMARY
Pai, Traer, Sperry, Zeng & Levin (bioRxiv, March 2026) demonstrate that basal Xenobots — autonomously motile constructs derived from Xenopus embryonic ectodermal explants — exhibit memory of brief chemical exposures (Xenopus embryonic cell extract and ATP) that manifests as distinct, specific, and persistent changes in behavior, physiology, and gene expression. The paper provides multi-level evidence (behavioral assays + physiological recordings + transcriptional profiling) that synthetic living constructs without evolutionary selection histories nonetheless implement learning-like processes, pushing the empirical envelope on substrate-independent cognition.
WHY IT MATTERS
This is a direct empirical advance on Levin's core program: it shows memory — long thought to require neural circuitry shaped by evolutionary history — emerging in a de novo biological construct that has none. It extends the basal cognition framework from "agency without neurons" to "memory without selection history," tightening the case that information-processing capacities are properties of bioelectric/transcriptional networks per se rather than of evolved biological lineages.
CANDIDATE PRS TRIPLETS
CANDIDATE PRS 1 — High
P Memory has been theoretically tied to either (a) neural substrates or (b) evolved organisms whose memory mechanisms were shaped by selection — leaving open whether memory is intrinsic to living material or requires that historical scaffolding
R Multi-level assay of basal Xenobots (behavioral + physiological + transcriptional) before and after chemical exposure to embryonic cell extract and ATP
S Empirical demonstration that synthetic living constructs without evolutionary history implement specific, persistent memory traces — generalizing memory as a property of bioelectric/transcriptional networks rather than an exclusive feature of evolved nervous systems
Evidence: Distinct behavioral state changes after brief chemical exposure correlated with measurable physiological and transcriptional signatures specific to the stimulus (per bioRxiv abstract)
CANDIDATE PRS 2 — High
P How do we operationalize and detect memory in non-standard biological systems where conventional behavioral assays may not apply?
R Triangulation methodology — behavioral + physiological + transcriptional readouts cross-validating each other as evidence of a memory trace
S A reproducible multi-level protocol for attributing memory to novel biological constructs that meets Levin & Resnik's "diverse intelligence" framework criteria (testable hypotheses about interaction protocols)
Evidence: Methodological structure of the Pai et al. study explicitly combines three independent evidence streams
CROSS-TRADITION SIGNALS
- **Friston (strong):** Memory in basal Xenobots without evolutionary scaffolding is a clean test bed for FEP/active inference predictions about memory as predictive model maintenance — flag for Friston Agent. If memory traces in Xenobots can be modeled as Bayesian posteriors over embodied priors, this is a major bridge. - **Hawkins (medium):** The transcriptional component of memory in a non-neural substrate creates tension with Hawkins' cortical-column-as-memory-unit framing — does the cortical column theory of memory generalize, or is it one instantiation of a broader principle? - **Kastrup (speculative):** If memory does not require evolutionary history, the question of *whose* memory the Xenobot has becomes acute — does the dissociation/idealism framing offer a cleaner ontology than emergentist accounts? - **Stump (medium):** Memory of "exposure" in a created construct touches Thomistic questions about formation and the persistence of impressions in created beings — relevant to the substantial-form discussions in Stump's work.

https://www.cambridge.org/core/services/aop-cambridge-core/content/view/07A6C257…
SUMMARY
Stump's published version of her 2025 Maritain Lecture (Notre Dame) appears in the Cambridge journal *Religious Studies* (DOI prefix S0034412525). The paper takes recent neuroscience of the mirror-neuron system and other "collective neuroscience" findings as evidence that human persons are constitutively related to one another at the neural level, and argues that explaining what we are requires a metaphysics of *united somethings-or-others* — composite entities that include human persons as constituents without dissolving them. Stump deploys hylomorphism to ground philosophical and theological notions of "a people," communal guilt and shame, the church as the body of Christ, and human perfection as likeness to a triune God.
WHY IT MATTERS
This is Stump's most direct treatment to date of the Thomistic metaphysics of *collective entities* — exactly the conceptual pivot the C2A2 framework needs to ground its claims about traditions, communities, and second-first-language speakers as intelligible *kinds*. It moves Stump's program past individual hylomorphism (soul as form of body) into the metaphysics of supra-individual wholes that are nonetheless real, formed entities. Not yet captured in the wiki and arguably the most important new Stump piece for the C2A2 project.

S A hylomorphic metaphysics of the collective — peoples, communal guilt and shame, the church-as-body-of-Christ — in which the supra-individual whole has a real form that organizes its human constituents without dissolving their personhood
Evidence: Stump explicitly argues that mirror-neuron-system findings make possible "a kind of direct and immediate knowledge" between persons that requires metaphysical accommodation in a theory of what we are

STEPHEN WOLFRAM · PODCAST · 2026-03-05
The Last Theory Podcast #080 — How different observers think differently
https://lasttheory.com/podcast/080-how-different-observers-think-differently
SUMMARY
Mark Jeffery interviews Stephen Wolfram on how observer characteristics (size, time-scale, multiway-mind topology) determine the structure of the physics that observer perceives. Wolfram works through three concrete thought experiments: (1) planet-sized brains for whom the finite speed of light forces a different conception of space and time — the "fiction of an instantaneous state of space" would be untenable; (2) molecule-sized brains for whom thermal motion would no longer look random, and the heat death of the universe would be a richly structured prospect; (3) "multiway minds" able to hold multiple branches of the multiway graph simultaneously, producing multiple parallel threads of experience. The pivotal question raised: does the Principle of Computational Equivalence force all observers to think the same way, or does observer-scale genuinely differentiate the cognitive style of an observer-class? Wolfram tilts toward the latter — observer-scale matters because we think the way we think because we are the way we are.
WHY IT MATTERS
This is the most concrete extension of Wolfram's Observer Theory the wiki has captured since the February 2026 "Metaphysics and the Ruliad" essay (PRS-06, PRS-07). It moves the rulial-space-of-minds thesis (PRS-03, PRS-05) from abstract claim to operationalizable thought experiment: each observer type is a *specific path through rulial space*, and Wolfram shows what physics looks like when that path is varied along one parameter (size, time-scale, multiway-coherence). It also tightens the connection between Wolfram's observer theory and the question of cognitive style — making this episode a direct upstream input to the "machine curiosity / new ways of thinking" thread captured in PROP-2026-04-27-003 (today's earlier Wolfram proposal). Together #080 and the April 3 livestream show Wolfram converging on a single research target: the systematic study of non-human-imitative observer-classes inside the Ruliad.
CANDIDATE PRS TRIPLETS
CANDIDATE PRS 1 — High
P The rulial-space-of-minds thesis (PRS-03, PRS-05) is conceptually compelling but has lacked concrete worked examples. Without specific observer-classes to point at, "different observers sample the Ruliad differently" remains a slogan rather than a research agenda.
R Wolfram's three-parameter observer-class taxonomy (Last Theory #080): scale (molecule → planet), temporal coherence (instantaneous-state vs. light-delay-dominated), and multiway-coherence (single thread vs. multi-branch experience). Each parameter generates a specific, describable physics for the observer-class that holds it.
S A first-pass operational map of the rulial-space-of-minds: observer types are points in (scale × temporal-coherence × multiway-coherence) space, and each generates a distinct effective physics. This converts PRS-03 / PRS-05 from a programmatic claim into a research agenda — one can now ask "what other parameters foliate observer-space?" and "what physics does each foliation produce?"
Evidence: Direct from the episode: planet-brain → finite-c-dominated spacetime; molecule-brain → non-random thermal motion / interesting heat death; multiway-brain → parallel threads of experience.
CANDIDATE PRS 2 — Medium

Two observers can be computationally equivalent in the strong sense and still inhabit incommensurable physics because they sample the underlying computation differently.
S A clarified two-level reading of PCE: (i) computational-capability level — all observers above the universality threshold are equivalent in what they can in principle compute; (ii) experiential-physics level — observers above the threshold can still inhabit profoundly different effective worlds depending on their sampling parameters. The rulial-space-of-minds thesis lives at level (ii).
Evidence: Episode framing: "if we were much larger-scale, much smaller-scale or if we had multiway minds, then we'd think very differently" — Wolfram explicitly affirming variation despite PCE.
