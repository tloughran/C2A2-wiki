Behavioral, Physiological, and Transcriptional Mechanisms of Memory in a Synthetic Living Construct
https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.03.17.712168v1
SUMMARY
Pai, Traer, Sperry, Zeng & Levin (bioRxiv, March 2026) demonstrate that basal Xenobots — autonomously motile constructs derived from Xenopus embryonic ectodermal explants — exhibit memory of brief chemical exposures (Xenopus embryonic cell extract and ATP) that manifests as distinct, specific, and persistent changes in behavior, physiology, and gene expression. The paper provides multi-level evidence (behavioral assays + physiological recordings + transcriptional profiling) that synthetic living constructs without evolutionary selection histories nonetheless implement learning-like processes, pushing the empirical envelope on substrate-independent cognition.
WHY IT MATTERS
This is a direct empirical advance on Levin's core program: it shows memory — long thought to require neural circuitry shaped by evolutionary history — emerging in a de novo biological construct that has none. It extends the basal cognition framework from "agency without neurons" to "memory without selection history," tightening the case that information-processing capacities are properties of bioelectric/transcriptional networks per se rather than of evolved biological lineages.

SUMMARY

The pivotal question raised: does the Principle of Computational Equivalence force all observers to think the same way, or does observer-scale genuinely differentiate the cognitive style of an observer-class? It directly addresses the under-developed biology side of PRS-03 (Research Agenda Extensions) and gives Wolfram's framework a first-class biological vocabulary. It also positions the program to engage Levin's morphogenesis on Wolfram's own home turf — adaptive computation — rather than only by analogy.

CANDIDATE PRS 1 — Medium
P What distinguishes living systems from arbitrary computational systems? Wolfram's Physics Project derives universal physics from hypergraph rewriting, but life-as-computation has lacked a formal characterization that explains why some computations are alive and most are not.
R The "rulial ensemble" — a constrained ensemble of rules within rulial space, where the constraints are those that achieve and sustain organismic purpose. Combined with "bulk orchestration": the empirical observation that molecular and cellular processes are actively organized rather than statistically random.
S Life is a localized, history-dependent occupation of rulial space — an ensemble of rules selected, over evolutionary time, for the property that running them on each other produces a self-sustaining, self-correcting computational coherence. The rulial ensemble is to biology what a single rule is to physics.
Evidence: Wolfram introduces cellular automata rule ensembles as idealized genotypes whose behavior idealizes phenotypic development, and argues that "by thinking in terms of the rulial ensemble, one may be able to develop a foundational theory of bulk orchestration and adaptive systems, most notably biology."
CANDIDATE PRS 2 — Medium
P Computational irreducibility (PRS-04) seems to forbid prediction — yet biological organisms reliably reach developmental targets. How can adaptive, goal-directed systems exist within a universe where most computations are unpredictable?
R Bulk orchestration as the answer: organisms do not predict computational futures, they constrain them — the rulial ensemble is precisely the set of rules whose joint operation funnels otherwise irreducible computation into reliable, organism-scale outcomes.
S Biological reliability is not the negation of computational irreducibility but its harnessing. Selection acts on rule-ensembles whose collective behavior achieves bulk orchestration despite irreducibility at the substrate level. This reframes "agency" (PRS-04) as the local taming of irreducibility through rulial-ensemble structure.
Evidence: The essay's framing of cellular automata ensembles as adaptive systems where rule-level irreducibility coexists with phenotype-level orchestration.
