## Research Program Overview
**Thinker:** Michael Levin (Tufts University, Allen Discovery Center)
**Core claim:** Intelligence, agency, and goal-directedness are substrate-independent. Bioelectric signaling networks in non-neural tissue exhibit forms of cognition that challenge brain-centric definitions of mind.
**Paradigm:** Basal cognition, morphogenetic intelligence, collective cellular agency

**Summary (from RC Pilot document):**
Michael Levin is the central scientific figure in the Resurrecting Civility pilot document, whose work anchors the biological dimension of the emerging Conscious Realism (CR) paradigm. His principal contribution is the discovery that bioelectric signals — ion flows, membrane voltage gradients, and gap junction networks — constitute a second genetic code governing not just cell identity but the large-scale shape and goal of an entire organism. Where molecular biology asks "which genes?", Levin asks "what pattern is the body trying to achieve, and how does it know?" Organisms are not assembled bottom-up from genes but are pulled top-down toward an anatomical attractor state encoded in bioelectric memory. His xenobots and anthrobots demonstrate that biological cells can be reprogrammed into entirely novel body plans with no precedent in evolution. Later work frames morphogenesis in explicitly cognitive and teleonomic terms: bodies have goals, pursue them through feedback control, and exhibit agency at every level of organization.
