## Triplets

PRS-01:
  Label: P1 (Text, pp.41–53) — Ontological closure vs. emergence
  Problem: Physicalism cannot easily account for consciousness, agency, or meaning without either eliminating them or invoking explanatory gaps
  Resource: Poetic naturalism: multiple levels of description (particles, organisms, persons) are all legitimate stories told about the same physical substrate
  Solution: A fully naturalist account of mind, meaning, and value that requires no ontological additions to physics, while preserving the validity of higher-level descriptions
  Date Added: 2026-04-03
  Source: Resurrecting Civility — RC Pilot GPT Tome / Document Explorer
  Confidence: High

PRS-02:
  Label: P2 (Text, pp.44–47) — Quantum interpretation
  Problem: The measurement problem in quantum mechanics creates apparent discontinuities that tempt non-physical interpretations (consciousness causes collapse, etc.)
  Resource: Many-Worlds Interpretation: the wavefunction never collapses; all outcomes occur in branching Everettian worlds
  Solution: A philosophically principled resolution of the measurement problem that invokes no consciousness, no hidden variables, and no new physics — just the unmodified Schrödinger equation
  Date Added: 2026-04-03
  Source: Resurrecting Civility — RC Pilot GPT Tome / Document Explorer
  Confidence: High

PRS-03:
  Label: P3 (Text, pp.47–53) — Rival theory evaluation
  Problem: Novel physical theories (Wolfram Physics, Quantum Holonomy Theory, Conscious Realism) claim to unify physics but lack empirical traction
  Resource: Bayesian theory confirmation: novel theories must demonstrate tangible explanatory gains over established physics before warranting acceptance
  Solution: A methodological standard that keeps physics honest: welcome novel frameworks but require them to recover known physics and generate new predictions
  Date Added: 2026-04-03
  Source: Resurrecting Civility — RC Pilot GPT Tome / Document Explorer
  Confidence: High

PRS-04:
  Label: P4 (Implicit) — Agency without dualism
  Problem: Accounts of human agency often require either dualism (a non-physical will acting on the body) or eliminativism (agency is an illusion)
  Resource: Carroll's compatibilism: agency is a useful high-level description of physical processes; free will is compatible with determinism if properly understood
  Solution: A deflationary but non-eliminativist account of agency: persons really do act, but acting is a pattern in the physical substrate, not a violation of it — preserving moral responsibility without dualism
  Date Added: 2026-04-03
  Source: Resurrecting Civility — RC Pilot GPT Tome / Document Explorer
  Confidence: Medium

PRS-05:
  Label: P5 (Implicit) — Science–religion dialogue
  Problem: Science and religion appear to conflict because both make claims about the same domain (reality); naive harmony is intellectually dishonest
  Resource: Poetic naturalism's distinction between core ontology (physics) and useful stories (meaning, value, purpose) — religion traffics in the latter
  Solution: A framework where science and religion can coexist without either capitulating: science owns the core ontology, religion provides coherent stories about meaning at the human level
  Date Added: 2026-04-03
  Source: Resurrecting Civility — RC Pilot GPT Tome / Document Explorer
  Confidence: Medium

PRS-06:
  Label: P6 (Implicit, via document contrast) — Physicalism's explanatory limit
  Problem: Carroll's framework has no resources for explaining why there is something rather than nothing, or why mathematical structures are empirically instantiated
  Resource: Carroll's own use of the 'why is there something?' question as an edge of physics — acknowledging limits
  Solution: An acknowledged boundary condition that opens space for Thomistic engagement: Carroll's naturalism, rigorously applied, reaches its own limit precisely where Thomistic metaphysics begins
  Date Added: 2026-04-03
  Source: Resurrecting Civility — RC Pilot GPT Tome / Document Explorer
  Confidence: High

PRS-07:
  Label: P7 (PROP-2026-04-07-004) — Observer-centric quantum gravity
  Problem: Quantum mechanics and general relativity remain unreconciled after decades of effort, with no convergence on a theory of quantum gravity
  Resource: A new observer-centric framework (Harlow et al.) — treating the observer as a classical, mathematically well-defined entity within the theory, with new rules for observer-system interactions
  Solution: Rather than deriving the observer from quantum mechanics, build the observer into the foundations — a "precise Copenhagen" that may resolve the measurement problem while naturally incorporating gravitational effects
  Date Added: 2026-04-07
  Source: Mindscape #349 — Daniel Harlow on What Quantum Gravity Teaches Us About Quantum Mechanics (March 30, 2026)
  Confidence: Medium
