  Problem: Physicalism cannot easily account for consciousness, agency, or meaning without either eliminating them or invoking explanatory gaps
  Resource: Poetic naturalism: multiple levels of description (particles, organisms, persons) are all legitimate stories told about the same physical substrate
  Solution: A fully naturalist account of mind, meaning, and value that requires no ontological additions to physics, while preserving the validity of higher-level descriptions

  Label: P2 (Text, pp.44–47) — Quantum interpretation

  Resource: Many-Worlds Interpretation: the wavefunction never collapses; all outcomes occur in branching Everettian worlds
  Solution: A philosophically principled resolution of the measurement problem that invokes no consciousness, no hidden variables, and no new physics — just the unmodified Schrödinger equation

PRS-04:
  Label: P4 (Implicit) — Agency without dualism
  Problem: Accounts of human agency often require either dualism (a non-physical will acting on the body) or eliminativism (agency is an illusion)
  Resource: Carroll's compatibilism: agency is a useful high-level description of physical processes; free will is compatible with determinism if properly understood
  Solution: A deflationary but non-eliminativist account of agency: persons really do act, but acting is a pattern in the physical substrate, not a violation of it — preserving moral responsibility without dualism
  Date Added: 2026-04-03
  Source: Resurrecting Civility — RC Pilot GPT Tome / Document Explorer
  Confidence: Medium

PRS-05:
  Label: P5 (Implicit) — Science–religion dialogue
  Problem: Science and religion appear to conflict because both make claims about the same domain (reality); naive harmony is intellectually dishonest
  Resource: Poetic naturalism's distinction between core ontology (physics) and useful stories (meaning, value, purpose) — religion traffics in the latter
  Solution: A framework where science and religion can coexist without either capitulating: science owns the core ontology, religion provides coherent stories about meaning at the human level
  Date Added: 2026-04-03
  Source: Resurrecting Civility — RC Pilot GPT Tome / Document Explorer

PRS-07:
  Label: P7 (PROP-2026-04-07-004) — Observer-centric quantum gravity
  Problem: Quantum mechanics and general relativity remain unreconciled after decades of effort, with no convergence on a theory of quantum gravity
  Resource: A new observer-centric framework (Harlow et al.) — treating the observer as a classical, mathematically well-defined entity within the theory, with new rules for observer-system interactions
  Solution: Rather than deriving the observer from quantum mechanics, build the observer into the foundations — a "precise Copenhagen" that may resolve the measurement problem while naturally incorporating gravitational effects

PRS-08:
  Label: P8 (PROP-2026-04-08-004) — Intelligence as multidimensional across species
  Problem: Carroll's poetic naturalism needs a positive account of what "mind" tracks across different biological systems — not just that mind-language is a valid higher-level description, but what feature of complex systems it describes
  Resource: Erica Cartmill (Santa Fe Institute) on comparative cognitive science: intelligence is multidimensional, non-linear across species — some numerical competencies present in chimpanzees, social intelligence (play, teasing, humor) as a distinct cognitive axis separate from problem-solving
  Solution: Carroll's emergence thesis gains empirical specificity: minds are not points on a single axis from simple to complex, but multi-dimensional systems where different capacities emerge independently — supporting the view that consciousness/agency are emergent real patterns, not just useful fictions
  Date Added: 2026-04-16
  Source: Mindscape #346 "Erica Cartmill on How Human and Animal Minds Think and Play" (Carroll, March 9, 2026); PROP-2026-04-08-004
  Confidence: Medium

PRS-09:
  Label: P9 (PROP-2026-04-08-005) — Biological agency as naturalist research program
  Problem: Standard physicalist accounts of organisms treat them as passive products of selection pressure acting on random variation — but this appears to eliminate genuine biological agency
  Resource: Jessica Riskin's historical re-reading of Lamarck's creative agency (PROP-2026-04-08-005): organisms possess an internal agentive force that is not reducible to external selection or random mutation — they are active participants in life, not passive recipients
  Solution: A naturalistic framework for biological agency that goes beyond compatibilism without invoking dualism — organisms as genuine agents within Carroll's physicalism, grounded in historical precedent that predates modern physics
  Date Added: 2026-04-16
  Source: Mindscape #348 "Jessica Riskin on Jean-Baptiste Lamarck and Life as Creative Agency" (Carroll, March 23, 2026); PROP-2026-04-08-005
  Confidence: Medium

PRS-10:
  Label: P10 (PROP-2026-04-09-003) — Observer problem in Many-Worlds interpretation
  Problem: Many-Worlds interpretations of quantum mechanics struggle to define "observer" without circularity or infinite regress — Witten's critique holds that knowing one's measurement outcome requires a meta-observer, who requires another meta-observer, with no principled stopping point
  Resource: Carroll's response in April 2026 AMA: the observer is defined functionally within the wavefunction via decoherence — there is no external observer required; "knowing an outcome" is itself a physical correlation between memory state and environmental state
  Solution: A fully physicalist account of observation, knowledge, and memory within Many-Worlds that dissolves Witten's regress by treating all observer-states as internal physical correlations in the wavefunction — at the cost of requiring that "experience" is fully described by physical correlations, which the Consciousness Cluster denies
  Date Added: 2026-04-16
  Source: Mindscape AMA "April 2026 — Sean Carroll's Mindscape" (April 6, 2026); PROP-2026-04-09-003
  Confidence: High

PRS-11:
  Label: P11 (PROP-2026-04-13-010) — Emergence classification and ontological taxonomy
  Problem: The concept of "emergence" is used loosely across philosophy and science — some mean epistemic convenience, others mean genuine ontological novelty, and the ambiguity undermines productive cross-tradition dialogue about consciousness, agency, and causation
  Resource: Carroll & Parola's dynamics-based classification of emergence (2024): Type-0 (featureless), Type-1 (local), Type-2 (with new ontological elements); grounded in state spaces and evolution rules rather than subjective judgments
  Solution: A precise, physics-inspired taxonomy that lets rival traditions specify exactly what kind of emergence they are claiming — forcing clarity on whether consciousness is Type-1 emergent (reducible in principle) or Type-2 (requiring genuinely new ontological ingredients), providing shared vocabulary for C2A2 network's central debate
  Date Added: 2026-04-16
  Source: "What Emergence Can Possibly Mean" (Carroll & Parola, 2024, arxiv: 2410.15468); PROP-2026-04-13-010
  Confidence: High
