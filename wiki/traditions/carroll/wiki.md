## Research Program Overview
**Thinker:** Sean Carroll (Johns Hopkins / Santa Fe Institute; Mindscape podcast)
**Core claim:** Physics is complete at the Core Theory (quantum field theory + general relativity) level; higher-level descriptions are real even if not fundamental; many-worlds solves the measurement problem; consciousness is emergent, not fundamental
**Paradigm:** Many-Worlds Interpretation, poetic naturalism, emergence, Core Theory completeness

**Summary (from RC Pilot document):**
Sean Carroll is the tradition's foil and philosophical conscience — the most rigorous defender of physicalist naturalism in the network, whose arguments must be engaged before any consciousness-first or non-physicalist program can claim scientific credibility. His poetic naturalism holds that multiple levels of description (particles, organisms, persons, values) are all legitimate stories told about the same physical substrate — none is more real than another, but all must be grounded in a physical reality that is described at the fundamental level by quantum field theory. His Many-Worlds Interpretation resolves the measurement problem without consciousness, hidden variables, or wavefunction collapse — an important negative result for Conscious Realism (Hoffman), which needs the observer to be physically significant. In the RC Pilot document, Carroll represents the principal philosophical opposition to the CR program. His Bayesian theory confirmation criterion — novel theories must demonstrate tangible explanatory gains over established physics before warranting acceptance — is the methodological standard that programs like Wolfram Physics, Conscious Realism, and Kastrup's idealism must meet. Carroll's work also features prominently on the Mindscape podcast, which is a major secondary source for several program agents.

## Paradigm Assessment
Carroll's program is **the methodological spine of the Post-Spacetime Cluster and the primary philosophical opposition to the Consciousness Cluster**. **UPDATE 2026-04-07:** Carroll's serious engagement with Harlow's observer-centric quantum gravity framework (Mindscape #349) marks a significant development. The network's most conservative voice is now actively exploring frameworks where the observer is mathematically special — a position structurally convergent with Wolfram (observer-dependent Ruliad sampling) and Hoffman (conscious agent primacy). This does not mean Carroll has abandoned physicalism, but the direction of travel is toward observer-dependence in ways that narrow the gap between the Post-Spacetime and Consciousness Clusters. His Bayesian confirmation criterion is essential for the C2A2 network — it is the methodological standard that keeps the network honest and forces each program to demonstrate genuine explanatory gains, not merely conceptual elegance. The Carroll × Hoffman tension and the Carroll × Wolfram methodological challenge are the network's two most philosophically productive conflict zones. Carroll's program is also the most publicly accessible (Mindscape podcast) and so is the best entry point for secondary source material.
