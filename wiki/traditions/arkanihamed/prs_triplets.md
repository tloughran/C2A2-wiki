PRS-08:
  Label: P8 (PROP-2026-04-13-012) — Hidden structure and particle unification
  Problem: Different particle types (scalars, pions, gluons) appear as distinct entities in the Standard Model formalism — there is no known reason why their scattering amplitudes should be related at the level of mathematical structure
  Resource: Hidden zeros in Tr(φ³) amplitudes (Arkani-Hamed et al., JHEP 2024): when non-planar Mandelstam invariants are set to zero, the scalar amplitudes reveal a pattern that secretly encodes pion (NLSM) and gluon (Yang-Mills) amplitudes within the same mathematical object
  Solution: A unification of apparently distinct particle types at the level of amplitude structure — scalars, pions, and gluons are manifestations of the same deeper mathematical entity, visible only when hidden zeros are exposed; Standard Model's apparent diversity conceals deeper unity
  Date Added: 2026-04-16
  Source: "Hidden zeros for particle/string amplitudes and the unity of colored scalars, pions and gluons" (Arkani-Hamed et al., arxiv: 2312.16282); PROP-2026-04-13-012
  Confidence: High
