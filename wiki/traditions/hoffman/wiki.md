## Research Program Overview
**Thinker:** Donald Hoffman (UC Irvine)
**Core claim:** Perception is a fitness-tuned interface, not a window on reality; consciousness is fundamental; conscious agents form networks that give rise to spacetime and matter as perceptual constructs
**Paradigm:** Interface Theory of Perception (ITP), Conscious Realism, Conscious Agent Networks

**Summary (from RC Pilot document):**
Donald Hoffman is the philosophical linchpin of the RC Pilot document, providing the metaphysical framework within which Levin's biology, Friston's active inference, Hawkins's neuroscience, and Wolfram's computational physics are all situated. His Interface Theory of Perception mathematically proves that natural selection drives organisms to perceive fitness-relevant information, not objective reality — perception is a species-specific interface optimized for survival, not truth. His Conscious Realism goes further: consciousness, not matter, is ontologically primary; the physical world is what the network of conscious agents constructs as a shared interface. His collaboration with Arkani-Hamed on whether the amplituhedron can be derived from conscious agent mathematics represents the program's highest-risk, highest-reward research direction. The RC Pilot positions Hoffman as providing the missing metaphysical framework for recovering Thomistic philosophy as a living tradition: if consciousness is primary, Thomism's insistence on the primacy of intellect over matter is not anachronistic metaphysics but cutting-edge science.
