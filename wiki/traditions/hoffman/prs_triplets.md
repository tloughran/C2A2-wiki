PRS-17:
  Label: P17 (PROP-2026-04-28-001) — ITP-internal redefinition of illusion and hallucination
  Problem: Standard theory of illusion presupposes veridical perception as the baseline (an illusion fails to match reality); ITP says no perception ever matches reality, which on the standard view appears to entail "all perceptions are illusions" — long used as a reductio against ITP
  Resource: Hoffman's ITP-native redefinition: an illusion is an unadaptive perception that most normal perceivers have under specified conditions (Necker cube, depth illusions, etc., are illusions because they fail to guide adaptive behavior in the niche where they occur, not because they fail to match objective reality); parallel reformulation for hallucinations
  Solution: ITP escapes the "everything is an illusion" reductio and gains a positive research program — empirical investigation of which perceptions guide adaptive behavior in specific niches, with illusions and hallucinations identified relative to that adaptive standard rather than to objective truth; extends without modification to basal-cognition organisms (xenobots/anthrobots) where adaptive standards are well-defined
