## Research Program Overview
**Thinker:** Jeff Hawkins (Numenta)
**Core claim:** The neocortex consists of ~150,000 cortical columns each building a complete world model with reference frames; intelligence is predictive modeling, not pattern matching; understanding requires world models
**Paradigm:** Hierarchical Temporal Memory (HTM), Thousand Brains Theory, reference frames, temporal prediction

**Summary (from RC Pilot document):**
Jeff Hawkins is the tradition's neuroscientist of intelligence architecture, offering a detailed, empirically grounded account of how the human neocortex actually works — as a hive-mind of 150,000 cortical columns, about 1% of which are active at any one time, each building its own complete world model based on sensorimotor experience. Consensus emerges through electrochemical voting. The RC Pilot positions Hawkins as providing direct evidence for the Levin-style view that multiple cognitive agents (cortical columns) constitute a single unified self that is conscious of itself but not of its parts. His Thousand Brains model is simultaneously the most empirically grounded account of neocortical function and the most concrete instantiation of Levin's multi-scale cognition thesis at the neural level. Hawkins deliberately avoids claims about consciousness — he is not a Conscious Realist — but his architecture provides the most detailed implementation of how a network of sub-agents can produce unified intelligent behavior. For the RC Pilot, Hawkins's work demonstrates that distributed, multi-agent cognitive architecture is the empirical reality of human intelligence, not a speculative analogy.
