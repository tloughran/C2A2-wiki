  Solution: Cultural and philosophical recovery of right-hemisphere modes of attention: phenomenological, relational, embodied knowing prior to and founding analytical abstraction

  Solution: A model of dialectical engagement pairing right-hemisphere openness (inhabiting the whole of another tradition) with left-hemisphere analysis (identifying structural rules of disagreement) — the neurological basis for second-first-language learning

PRS-04:
  Label: P4 (Implicit) — Multi-agent integration and proof of concept
  Problem: Conscious realism posits that individual conscious agents integrate into higher-order agents, but the mechanism of that integration in human cognition is underdetermined
  Resource: McGilchrist's corpus callosotomy data: when hemispheres are severed, each operates as a separate conscious agent; when integrated, a single unified self emerges — empirical proof of conscious agent fusion
  Solution: Split-brain evidence supports Conscious Realism's mathematical claim that agent networks form higher-order agents — providing empirical grounding for a theoretical claim that had lacked it
  Date Added: 2026-04-05
  Source: Resurrecting Civility — RC Pilot GPT Tome / Document Explorer
  Confidence: Medium

PRS-05:
  Label: P5 (Implicit) — Attention as ontological act
  Problem: Contemporary epistemology treats attention as a cognitive function rather than an ontological act — missing that the mode of attending constitutes the kind of world that shows up
  Resource: McGilchrist's claim that attention is world-constituting: the mode of attention (right vs. left hemisphere) shapes not just what we see but what kind of world is disclosed
  Solution: A philosophy of attention as ontological action — compatible with Friston's precision-weighted prediction (attention as evidence weighting) and Hoffman's interface theory (attention as fitness-tuned perception)
  Date Added: 2026-04-05
  Source: Resurrecting Civility — RC Pilot GPT Tome / Document Explorer
  Confidence: Medium

PRS-06:
  Label: P6 (Implicit) — Embodied tradition formation

  Resource: McGilchrist's account of embodied cognition: right-hemisphere knowledge is skill-based, contextual, and person-involving — not merely propositional
  Solution: Tradition formation requires deliberate cultivation of right-hemisphere engagement: mentorship, shared practice, contextual immersion — not information transfer alone; implications for C2A2's tradition-accelerator design
