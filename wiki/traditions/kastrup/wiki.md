## Research Program Overview
**Thinker:** Bernardo Kastrup (Essentia Foundation; PhD philosophy and computer science)
**Core claim:** Consciousness is the only thing that exists; matter is the extrinsic appearance of mental processes; individual beings are dissociated alters of a single universal consciousness
**Paradigm:** Analytic idealism, dissociative identity model, consciousness as ground

**Summary (from RC Pilot document):**
Bernardo Kastrup is the tradition's analytic idealist philosopher, bringing rigorous philosophical argument to a position that most analytic philosophers dismiss — that consciousness is ontologically primary and the physical world is its expression, not its cause. His analytic idealism is distinguished from older idealist positions by its use of contemporary philosophy of mind tools and its engagement with quantum mechanics, neuroscience, and biology on their own terms. The dissociative identity disorder model is his key move: individual beings are not separate substances but dissociated points of view within a single universal consciousness, the same way that alters in DID have their own perspective without being separate minds. The RC Pilot document positions Kastrup as the tradition that most directly confronts Carroll's physicalism and provides the metaphysical grounding for both Hoffman's Conscious Realism and the Thomistic framework (Stump) that the RC project uses. CROSS-005 (love as ontological re-integration) and CROSS-001 (consciousness as substrate-independent) are both significantly advanced by Kastrup's program.
