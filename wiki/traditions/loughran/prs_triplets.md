## Triplets

PRS-01:
  Label: P1 — The silo problem
  Problem: Intellectual traditions operate in isolation; researchers within one paradigm rarely engage seriously with the internal logic of rival paradigms, producing parallel monologues rather than genuine dialogue
  Resource: Multi-agent AI architecture in which each agent inhabits a single tradition's logic and is tasked with identifying connections, tensions, and bridging opportunities with other agents
  Solution: The Synergistic Coil — a structured methodology for staging inter-tradition encounters through AI agents that can learn each tradition as a second first language (MacIntyre)
  Date Added: 2026-04-29
  Source: C2A2 Project Architecture; Resurrecting Civility RC Pilot
  Confidence: High

PRS-02:
  Label: P2 — Scaling intellectual civility
  Problem: Productive disagreement requires intellectual virtues (patience, honesty, willingness to revise) that are difficult to cultivate and do not scale naturally
  Resource: AI agents that can model intellectual virtues procedurally — agents tasked with steelmanning rival positions, identifying genuine common ground, and flagging where disagreement is substantive rather than terminological
  Solution: A technology for resurrecting civility — the multi-agent architecture as a practice (MacIntyre) whose internal goods include cross-traditional insight and whose characteristic virtue is productive disagreement
  Date Added: 2026-04-29
  Source: Resurrecting Civility RC Pilot, Introduction
  Confidence: Medium

PRS-03:
  Label: P3 — The integration problem
  Problem: Cross-disciplinary synthesis typically collapses into either reductive unification (forcing everything into one framework) or shallow eclecticism (cherry-picking from multiple frameworks without respecting their internal logic)
  Resource: Rohr's non-dual awareness and MacIntyre's tradition-constituted rationality — integration that holds tensions rather than resolving them prematurely, while maintaining each tradition's evaluative standards
  Solution: The Coil's methodology is neither synthesis nor eclecticism but structured encounter — traditions meet on their own terms, and the integrator's role is to identify where one tradition's resources solve another tradition's problems
  Date Added: 2026-04-29
  Source: C2A2 Project Architecture; cross-program analysis methodology
  Confidence: Medium

PRS-04:
  Label: P4 — Can AI agents participate in traditions?
  Problem: MacIntyre's account of tradition-constituted rationality assumes human participants with embodied experience, narrative identity, and moral formation — can AI agents genuinely participate or only simulate participation?
  Resource: The empirical track record of C2A2 agents producing cross-program insights (CROSS-001 through CROSS-054) that no individual human researcher had articulated
  Solution: The question may be pragmatic rather than metaphysical — if agents produce genuine insights by inhabiting traditions, the functional result may matter more than the ontological status of their participation; but this remains an open question with implications for Levin (substrate-independence of cognition) and Hoffman (consciousness as fundamental)
  Date Added: 2026-04-29
  Source: C2A2 meta-analysis; connection to Levin PRS-04
  Confidence: Speculative

PRS-05:
  Label: P5 — Four models of cultural exchange
  Problem: Three existing models of cultural exchange — aesthetic (willing intrinsic good to one party), moral (willing intrinsic good to multiple parties), and prescientific (willing improvement in the craft of exchange) — each prove inadequate from within and point beyond themselves, but lack a systematic forum for encounter among rival traditions
  Resource: MacIntyre's tradition-constituted rationality + PRS triplet methodology for articulating traditions + concept inventories and application tests from physics education + backward design of curriculum + assembly theory as basis for quantifiable prediction
  Solution: A fourth sapiential model — a new master science operating as an accelerator/detector complex for traditions, built at theoretical (CHANGE equation), experimental (tradition interaction protocol), computational (ML-supervised tradition modeling), and institutional levels
  Date Added: 2022-07-01
  Source: Loughran, "Four Models of Cultural Exchange: Aesthetic, Moral, Prescientific and Sapiential," ISME Summer Conference 2022
  Confidence: High

PRS-06:
  Label: P6 — The CHANGE equation
  Problem: No quantifiable basis exists for predicting outcomes of full-information exchanges among rival traditions; without quantifiability, tradition interaction cannot claim to be a science capable of falsification and progress
  Resource: Assembly theory (information content as fundamental quantity of the universe) + tradition-constituted rationality + concept inventories + application tests + mature member agreement protocols
  Solution: PA(1,2) = c * h * a * n * g — the probability of person A converting in a full-information interaction of rival traditions (1,2) is the product of normalized complexity ratio (c), history of interaction (h), access to traditions (a), nurture (n), and genetic propensities (g)
  Date Added: 2022-07-01
  Source: Loughran, "Four Models of Cultural Exchange," ISME 2022; see also "Toward a Computational Natural Law Defense of the Sanctity of Human Life," DCEC Fall 2021
  Confidence: Medium

PRS-07:
  Label: P7 — The 40-step accelerator/detector algorithm
  Problem: MacIntyre acknowledges that full tradition interaction — achieving second-first-language competency and mutual assessment — might require a "Herculean effort" spanning centuries
  Resource: 16 years of NSF program review including 7 with instructional technology startups + backward design best practices + parallel processing methodology from tournament-style athletic contests + educational technology at scale
  Solution: A two-phase startup algorithm — pilot phase (prepare two traditions in series via 8-step protocol including mature member agreement, PRS history, concept inventories, application tests, backward-designed curriculum; then interact in 10-step series) followed by full run phase (4-6 traditions prepared in parallel, interacted in tournament-style pairings, with results promulgated via community-curated social media and long-form podcasting)
  Date Added: 2022-07-01
  Source: Loughran, "Four Models of Cultural Exchange," ISME 2022, Figure 2 and accompanying text
  Confidence: Medium
