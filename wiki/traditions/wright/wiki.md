## Research Program Overview
**Thinker:** N.T. Wright (University of St Andrews, emeritus)
**Core claim:** The biblical narrative is a five-act drama (creation, fall, Israel, Jesus, church) in which the present community is called to improvise faithfully within the unfinished final act; critical realism provides an epistemology that avoids both naive positivism and postmodern relativism; the resurrection of Jesus is a historical event with cosmological implications
**Paradigm:** Critical realism, narrative theology, new perspective on Paul, inaugurated eschatology, faithful improvisation

**Summary (from RC Pilot document):**
N.T. Wright is the tradition's narrative theologian and historian — the thinker who provides the C2A2 project with a model of how communities inhabit and extend stories they did not originate. His critical realism occupies a precise epistemological position between Carroll's poetic naturalism and Kastrup's idealism: reality exists independently of our knowing it, but all knowing is perspectival, tradition-embedded, and subject to revision. This aligns him closely with MacIntyre's tradition-constituted rationality while adding an explicitly historical and narrative dimension. Wright's most distinctive contribution is the five-act hermeneutic: if the biblical story is an unfinished drama, then the task of the present community is not to repeat the past but to improvise faithfully — to act in ways consistent with the story's trajectory while responding to circumstances the original authors could not have anticipated. This model maps directly onto the C2A2 project's methodology: each agent inherits a thinker's research program and must extend it into new territory (cross-program connections, novel proposals) while remaining faithful to the program's internal logic. Wright's work on Paul and the concept of inaugurated eschatology — the claim that the decisive event has already occurred but its full implications are still unfolding — also provides a temporal framework for understanding how research programs develop: the key insight may already be present in the founding texts, but its significance only becomes visible through the patient work of elaboration and cross-traditional encounter.

- **MacIntyre**: Wright's critical realism and five-act hermeneutic are a narrative-theological application of MacIntyre's tradition-constituted rationality; both reject the view from nowhere
- **Rohr**: Wright's inaugurated eschatology (already/not-yet) resonates with Rohr's order-disorder-reorder; both describe a present that is shaped by a decisive past event whose implications are still unfolding
