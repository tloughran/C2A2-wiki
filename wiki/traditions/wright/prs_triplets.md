## Triplets

PRS-01:
  Label: P1 — The epistemological false dichotomy
  Problem: Modern thought oscillates between naive positivism (objective facts speak for themselves) and postmodern relativism (there are no facts, only interpretations), leaving no viable middle ground
  Resource: Critical realism — the philosophical position that reality exists independently of the knower, but all knowing is perspectival, tradition-embedded, and revisable
  Solution: An epistemology that takes evidence seriously without pretending to a view from nowhere; knowing is always from within a story, but stories can be tested against reality
  Date Added: 2026-04-29
  Source: The New Testament and the People of God (1992), Part II
  Confidence: High

PRS-02:
  Label: P2 — Faithful improvisation
  Problem: How does a community extend a tradition into circumstances its founders never anticipated, without either rigid repetition or unfaithful innovation?
  Resource: The five-act drama hermeneutic — the biblical narrative as an unfinished play in which the present community must improvise the final act
  Solution: Faithful improvisation requires deep immersion in the preceding acts (the tradition's internal logic) and creative responsiveness to the present scene; this is neither fundamentalism nor liberalism but a third way
  Date Added: 2026-04-29
  Source: The New Testament and the People of God, Chapter 5; The Last Word (2005)
  Confidence: High

PRS-03:
  Label: P3 — Inaugurated eschatology
  Problem: How should a community understand its present moment — as a continuation of the past, a break from it, or something else entirely?
  Resource: The theological concept of inaugurated eschatology — the decisive event has already occurred, but its full implications are still being worked out
  Solution: The present is neither mere repetition nor blank slate; it is the space between the decisive insight and its full elaboration — a framework applicable to research programs as well as theology
  Date Added: 2026-04-29
  Source: Surprised by Hope (2008); Paul and the Faithfulness of God (2013)
  Confidence: High

PRS-04:
  Label: P4 — Resurrection as historical and cosmological
  Problem: Modern historiography excludes in principle any event that does not conform to naturalistic assumptions, making certain historical questions undecidable by methodological fiat
  Resource: Wright's historical argument for the bodily resurrection of Jesus as the best explanation of the available evidence, using the same criteria applied to any other historical claim
  Solution: If the evidence warrants it, historical method must be willing to consider explanations that exceed the naturalistic framework — a direct challenge to Carroll's Core Theory completeness
  Date Added: 2026-04-29
  Source: The Resurrection of the Son of God (2003)
  Confidence: Medium
