## Research Program Overview
**Thinker:** Stephen Wolfram (Wolfram Research)
**Core claim:** The universe is a hypergraph evolving by rewriting rules; the ruliad is the entangled limit of all possible computations; spacetime and quantum mechanics emerge from computational irreducibility
**Paradigm:** Ruliad, hypergraph physics, computational irreducibility, observer theory

**Summary (from RC Pilot document):**
Stephen Wolfram is the tradition's computational physicist, whose Wolfram Physics Project proposes that the universe is a hypergraph of relationships that evolves according to simple rewriting rules, with spacetime and quantum mechanics both emerging as observer-dependent samplings of this underlying computational structure. His key concept of computational irreducibility — that the behavior of many systems cannot be predicted without running them — provides both a limitation on scientific prediction and a new kind of explanation: complexity from simplicity. His rulial space (the space of all possible computational rules) is the most ambitious conceptual framework in the network: it maps the landscape of all possible minds, all possible universes, and all possible physical laws as a unified computational object. The RC Pilot document connects Wolfram to Levin (bioelectric field as a morphogenetic ruliad?), to Hoffman (observer-dependent spacetime as a conscious-agent interface?), and to Carroll (does Wolfram Physics meet the Bayesian confirmation standard?). Wolfram's program also provides the computational framework within which AGI systems could be located — as entities navigating rulial space, not merely executing algorithms.
