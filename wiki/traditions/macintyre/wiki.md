## Research Program Overview
**Thinker:** Alasdair MacIntyre (University of Notre Dame, emeritus)
**Core claim:** Rational inquiry is always tradition-constituted; moral reasoning requires narrative coherence within a living tradition; the Enlightenment project of grounding morality in tradition-independent reason has failed, and the choice is between Aristotelian-Thomistic virtue ethics and Nietzschean emotivism
**Paradigm:** Tradition-constituted rationality, narrative identity, practice-virtue-institution triad, epistemological crisis and recovery

**Summary (from RC Pilot document):**
Alasdair MacIntyre is the tradition's philosopher of rationality itself — the thinker who provides the epistemological framework within which the entire C2A2 project makes sense. His central argument, developed across After Virtue, Whose Justice? Which Rationality?, and Three Rival Versions of Moral Enquiry, is that all rational inquiry is conducted from within a tradition, and that traditions can be rationally evaluated by their capacity to solve their own internal problems, survive epistemological crises, and render rival traditions intelligible on their own terms. This is the methodological foundation for C2A2's multi-tradition architecture: each agent operates within a tradition (Levin's basal cognition, Friston's active inference, Hoffman's conscious realism, Stump's Thomism) and the project's value lies not in collapsing them into one but in staging the encounters MacIntyre describes — moments where one tradition's resources solve another tradition's problem. MacIntyre's practice-virtue-institution triad also provides the sociological grammar for understanding how intellectual communities sustain themselves: practices generate internal goods, virtues sustain practices, and institutions sustain both but risk corruption. The RC Pilot document's Synergistic Coil is, in MacIntyrean terms, a practice whose internal goods include cross-traditional insight and whose virtues include intellectual honesty, patience with incommensurability, and the willingness to revise premises under rational pressure.
