**Paradigm:** Analytic Thomism, hylomorphism, final causality, MacIntyre's tradition-constituted rationality

**Summary (from RC Pilot document):**
Eleonore Stump is the tradition's philosopher, bringing Aquinas and MacIntyre into direct contact with the natural sciences assembled around the RC Pilot document. Her core contribution is the recovery of Thomistic hylomorphism — the doctrine that form and matter are co-constitutive of substance — as a philosophical framework capable of accommodating the cognitive turn in biology and neuroscience without reducing it. Where modern philosophy abandoned final causality (Mayr's 'teleonomy'), Stump rehabilitates it in analytic terms: organisms act toward forms not yet realized, and this is not mysticism but metaphysics recoverable within a naturalistic research program. Her second contribution is the demonstration that Thomism meets MacIntyre's criterion for tradition vitality — it has engaged analytic philosophy, cognitive science, and evolutionary biology on their own terms without dissolving into them. The RC Pilot document uses Stump to bridge the theological and scientific wings of the project, particularly through the connection between Thomistic final causality and Levin's morphogenetic teleology, and between Thomistic union (the highest good) and Fredrickson's positivity resonance (love as biophysiological coupling).
