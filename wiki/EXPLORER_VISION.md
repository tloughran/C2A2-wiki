## The Core Idea

The RC Explorer is an **accelerator-detector system** — the full architecture of which is advanced by agents, both AI and human. The entire system needs to be laid out inside this explorer. What follows is the intended sequence and logic of each component.

---

## 1. Community Explorer *(First Tool — already built)*

The starting point is not the sociogram. The sociogram is far downstream. The beginning is: **who is there to talk to?**

The Community Explorer enables communities to find one another and quickly see what others stand for and what they are pursuing. It structures that information as:

- **Goals** — what the community is actually trying to accomplish by way of action
- **Problems** — what they encounter on the way to those goals
- **Resources** — the resource pools they draw on to overcome those problems
- **Solutions** — what they actually arrived at to overcome those problems

This PRS-structured community profile is essential. The Community Explorer is already built and should be the very first thing in the explorer — not buried.

---

## 2. AI Heartbeat *(Second Tool — needs UI rebuild)*

The second path is AI education — specifically, authentic infrastructure education. A **Heartbeat Pool** was built in February (assembled in Code, pre-Cowork) that lets people stay current with reputable AI learning sources. The fundamentals work; the UI needs a rebuild.

The system:
- Notifies users of important AI developments
- Summarizes them in ways relevant to each user, based on what that user has said matters to them
- Is free to surface things it thinks are important, even without being asked

**Navigation proposal:** A **Tools menu** containing Community Explorer, AI Heartbeat, and further tools to come.

---

## 3. Dialogue and Exchange *(Future Phase)*

Once communities know one another and are AI-equipped — pursuing their own goals and interacting with others pursuing theirs — the next phase is dialogue. The invitation is to learn from one another, and perhaps to compete in some sense: a **contest of ideas**. Not a game. Rational inquiry. Letting ideas into the open. Freedom of speech, properly understood — not only an American thing, but a deeply American expression of it.

---

## 4. The Sociogram *(Perspective Tool — existing)*

There has to be a way to **climb into a perspective**. That is what the Sociogram in the wiki explorer does. It is a tool for accelerating the growth of a particular tradition — one that doesn't yet have many people who fully agree on it, but is unmistakably a tradition. Currently: 15 representative thinkers (Aquinas as a 16th is the next addition; many more to come).

This tool should be available to any community — not just this one. To hold a dialogue, you need to be able to enter not only your own perspective but another's. The Sociogram is that tool for entering another perspective. You have to put a team on the field, and that's what the wiki explorer does: it puts a team on the field.

---

## 5. PRS Triplets *(Cross-cutting Tool)*

The PRS (Problem / Resource / Solution) triplet structure is not merely a display format — it is a **tool for communities**. It has been in use since the Community Explorer and applies across the entire system. PRS triplets categorize thinking and the exchange of thought among thinkers. Most of the thinkers in the current wiki are within a single emerging tradition; Carroll sits as a productive foil — someone who thought deeply about a different tradition (the physical) and is present to teach from outside.

---

## 6. Agents Tab *(Inside View — existing)*

The Agents Tab provides visibility into the tools that build this particular knowledge and interaction enterprise. It is an inside view of the infrastructure, not primarily a user-facing feature.

---

## 7. Generalization / Mirroring *(Future Phase — Tools Section)*

Eventually: a version of all this that is **scraped off and configurable**. You put in your own fundamental tools, your own thinkers, design your own agents (or use the ones already designed), and mirror the system if you like it. This capability would sit under the Tools section — tools for communities interacting with one another.
