Strength of support: Moderate

Summary: Literature supports that core Thousand Brains principles (multiple parallel maps/modules, reference frame representations, associative learning) are conceptually transferable to AI. The architecture's emphasis on redundant parallel processing, distributed learning, and message-passing protocols aligns with existing multi-agent systems. However, transfer is not "intact"—implementation requires substantial adaptation (AI doesn't require actual sensorimotor embodiment, can optimize differently, doesn't need biological constraints). The principles transfer; the implementation details require redesign.

Caveats: "Conceptually intact" may overstate transferability—metaphors from neuroscience often require significant reinterpretation in AI. Thousand Brains emphasizes sensorimotor grounding that may not apply to abstract reasoning tasks. Empirical validation of transfer in non-sensorimotor domains is limited.

Recommendation: PARTIALLY-SUPPORTED
