    1. Agile/iterative development literature (Beck, Cohn): "good enough to checkpoint" is an accepted working norm for iterative progress.
    2. Lean startup literature (Ries): MVP checkpointing is endorsed for early-stage artifacts.

  Strength of support: Weak-Moderate
  
  Summary: Iterative and lean-development literature supports "good enough to checkpoint" as a legitimate working practice for in-progress artifacts. The criterion becomes problematic when (a) the artifact is user-facing and likely to be evaluated as "done" rather than "in progress" and (b) self-assessment is the only quality signal. The literature supports the general pattern but flags evaluator-producer separation as a quality safeguard that this checkpoint lacked.
  
  Caveats: Self-assessed adequacy has documented positivity bias. For user-facing artifacts, external evaluation or user-task completion testing is the stronger criterion.
