  Summary: Literature supports evidence-gated development as standard practice in research-informed design, particularly in high-stakes domains (conservation, policy, organizational learning). The critical variable for evidence effectiveness is timeliness — pausing to integrate findings before proceeding is documented as best practice. However, literature does not quantify optimal pause duration or address pause costs (delay, opportunity loss, stale evidence).
  
  Caveats: Support assumes evidence integration window is narrow enough that pause adds net value. If literature search takes weeks and implementation windows are days, pause may negate benefit. Literature focuses on external research integration; applicability to internal meta-cognitive pipeline less direct.
