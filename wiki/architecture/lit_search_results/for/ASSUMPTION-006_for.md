Summary: Literature supports that problems, solutions (research), and synthesis form natural phases in scientific progress. Kuhn's framework explicitly identifies "exemplary problem-solutions" as central to paradigm organization. Contemporary philosophy of science recognizes synthesis as distinct phase (meta-analyses, systematic reviews) that aggregates research findings. However, literature does not specifically validate the "PRS triplet" as the optimal or minimal structure—it treats these as recognizable phases but not necessarily as a complete tripartite system. Some frameworks add additional phases (e.g., critique, refinement, application).

Caveats: Different research domains may not follow PRS structure uniformly. Social sciences and humanities often have different progress models than natural sciences. The triplet structure may be domain-dependent rather than universal.
