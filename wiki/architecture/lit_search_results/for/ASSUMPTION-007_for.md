Summary: Literature on computational philosophy and AI epistemology supports that AI agents can instantiate research traditions IF they implement the requisite mechanisms. The "meaningful instantiation" requirement is addressed by computationalist philosophy (Bechtel & Abrahamsen), which shows mechanistic implementation suffices for genuine cognitive processes. Recent AI epistemology literature acknowledges AI as epistemic agents. However, existing literature does not provide empirical validation that current large language models or multi-agent systems successfully maintain the coherence and long-term persistence of traditions—this remains an open question. Literature addresses the theoretical possibility more than the practical implementation.

Caveats: Requires commitment to computational functionalism about knowledge and research traditions. Does not address whether AI agents can maintain traditions across training/deployment boundaries. Assumes traditions do not require human intentionality or institutional context to be "genuine."
