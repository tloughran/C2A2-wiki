Strength of support: Weak (contradicting evidence)

Summary: Literature on system development stages (particularly software evolution) strongly suggests that advancement is NOT monotonic. Stage models often serve as idealized frameworks rather than descriptions of actual progression. Real systems exhibit: (1) cyclic improvement within stages, (2) regressions due to bug discovery or requirement changes, (3) non-uniform stage durations, (4) occasional reversal to earlier stages. The assumption treats stages as monotonic progression; evidence shows this is unrealistic.

Caveats: C2A2 stages may differ from software development stages. However, general principle that complex systems show non-monotonic progress is well-established. Staged models are normative (how it should go) rather than descriptive (how it actually goes).
