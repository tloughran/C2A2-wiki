Summary: Literature supports the general principle that metacognitive oversight burden scales with decision complexity (dimensionality of decision space) rather than purely with agent count. Research on metacognitive calibration shows that as decisions become more multidimensional, additional oversight resources are needed. However, literature does not fully address the specific claim that adding agents does NOT increase oversight burden—there may be coordination complexity overhead that scales with agent count independently. The scaling relationship is more nuanced than the assumption suggests.

Caveats: Decision complexity and agent count may interact (more agents = more complex coordination decisions). Literature primarily addresses oversight in single-agent or small-team settings. Does not establish that oversight scales negligibly with agent count.
