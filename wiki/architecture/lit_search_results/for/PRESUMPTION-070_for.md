  Summary: There is some literature support for the general decomposability of virtue from metaphysics, and Stump's second-personal account has analogues that don't require her full hylomorphic anthropology. However, the strongest supporting case is at the genus level (virtue can be detached from metaphysics in general), not at the species level (Stump's specific virtue account survives demotion of *her* metaphysics). The assumption's claim is the species-level one.

  Caveats: (a) Stump scholarship typically reads her metaphysics and ethics as integrated; (b) "decomposable" admits degrees — partial vs. full; (c) the supporting analogues (MacIntyre, Slote, Darwall, Frankl) are not Stumpian and may not preserve her distinctive contributions.

  Recommendation: PARTIALLY-SUPPORTED (genus-level support exists; species-level support — that Stump specifically can be demoted on metaphysics with virtue intact — is weak)
