Summary: Literature provides some support for 2/3 as meaningful threshold, particularly in three-agent voting contexts. Mathematical analyses suggest 2/3 balances competing objectives (convergence, robustness, information value). However, research also shows threshold optimality depends on context (noise levels, consequence severity, task domain). The claim "meaningful" is supported but "optimal" or "universal" is not—different applications may benefit from different thresholds (e.g., 1/2, 3/4, unanimity).

Caveats: Optimal threshold depends on error costs, which are not specified in the assumption. Three-agent systems are small; scaling properties to larger systems uncertain. Literature does not specifically validate this threshold for research evaluation tasks.
