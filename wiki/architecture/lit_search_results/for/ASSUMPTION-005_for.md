Strength of support: Strong

Summary: Literature firmly establishes that traditions, research programmes, or paradigms are appropriate fundamental units for analyzing scientific progress. Lakatos's research programmes (hard core + protective belt + auxiliary hypotheses) provide well-developed framework for treating traditions as coherent evolving systems rather than discrete theories. Kuhn's paradigm concept, though controversial regarding incommensurability, demonstrates that scientists organize work within larger conceptual frameworks. Laudan's work extends these frameworks, showing they remain analytically useful even when refined. All major philosophy of science frameworks treat traditions or their equivalents as primary unit of analysis.

Caveats: Different philosophical schools (Popperian, Kuhnian, Lakatosian) define "tradition" differently in details. Some post-modern critiques question whether traditions have clear boundaries. Application to AI-instantiated traditions may require modification.
