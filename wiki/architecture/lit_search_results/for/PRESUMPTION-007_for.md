Summary: Literature strongly suggests that treating literature absence as evidence of novelty is dangerous. Publication bias means negative results, null findings, and contradictory evidence are systematically under-represented. Geographic, demographic, and methodological blind spots pervade literature. Absence of published research on a topic may indicate: (1) it's an active frontier, (2) it's been studied but not published, (3) it's been studied but results were negative/null and not disseminated, (4) it represents a systematic blind spot. Only rarely does absence confidently indicate true novelty. The presumption inverts the actual risk.

Caveats: Some domains have better publication tracking than others. Systematic reviews sometimes surface hidden literature. However, fundamental publication bias remains across all fields.
