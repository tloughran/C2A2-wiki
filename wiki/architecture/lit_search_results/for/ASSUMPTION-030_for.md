  Summary: Open-science and computational reproducibility literature consistently supports the idea that research artifacts should meet identified benchmarks before public release. The specific form of the benchmark (accuracy, reproducibility, internal validity, coverage) is domain-dependent, but the general principle — "don't release before you can say how to evaluate it" — is widely endorsed. For an interdisciplinary-synthesis system like C2A2, benchmark-gating is particularly important because unclear evaluation criteria can create false confidence in outputs. The assumption is well-aligned with established open-science norms.
  
  Caveats: Benchmark-gating can become a perpetual-stall mechanism if criteria are never concretized; open-science literature also warns against over-gating that prevents beneficial early sharing. The "criteria TBD" flag in the queue item is the risky part.
