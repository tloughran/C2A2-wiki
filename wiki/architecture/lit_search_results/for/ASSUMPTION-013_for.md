Strength of support: Moderate

Summary: Literature on analogical reasoning supports that structural correspondences are more reliable indicators of genuine connections than surface associations. Structure-mapping theory distinguishes between systematicity (deep relational correspondence) and literal similarity (attribute overlap). Genuine cross-tradition connections should exhibit high systematicity. However, literature also shows that retrieval of analogies is primarily surface-based (people find analogies via surface similarity), so early detection during proposal generation may include high false positive rate. The assumption is directionally supported (structure indicates genuineness) but may underestimate false positive problems in automatic detection.

Caveats: Detecting structural correspondence automatically is difficult (requires domain representation). Literature primarily addresses human analogy-making; AI systems may perform differently. Surface similarity highly correlated with relational similarity in real-world domains despite theoretical distinction.
