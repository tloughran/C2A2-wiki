  Strength of challenge: Weak-to-Moderate

  Summary: Institutional events are valid signals but noisier than paper publications. Funding-cycle and PR artefacts are documented as confounders. One launch event is one data point and the canonical recommendation is to weight institutional events lower than paper publications and to require multiple events for pattern detection. The challenge is not against tracking institutional events but against treating one event as research-program-level alliance signal without weighting.

  Specific risks: (a) PR / funding artefact mistaken for intellectual maturation; (b) single-event signal-inflation; (c) institutional-event signal weighted equivalently to paper-citation signal without empirical justification.

  Mitigations available: (a) Weight institutional events lower than paper publications; (b) require multiple events for pattern detection; (c) annotate funding source / PR context on each event; (d) treat the launch as input, not as primary signal.
